## Context

This maamar was delivered during **Shabbat Teshuva 1903-04** in the year **תרד"ע** (1903–04), as part of the ongoing hemshech (continuous discourse) that Rebbe Shalom DovBer of Lubavitch (the Rashab) delivered over two decades. It forms part of **חלק ראשון (Part 1)** of the hemshech.

The Hemshech Taarav is the longest chassidic discourse ever composed, spanning from 5662 (1901-02) through 5680 (1919-20). Each maamar continues the thread of the previous, building a comprehensive Kabbalistic-Chassidic system centered on the nature of divine light, the structure of the sefirot, and the mission of the Jewish soul.

## Deep Dive

### The Construct Being Built

Delivered on Shabbat Teshuva — the Shabbat between Rosh Hashanah and Yom Kippur — this maamar builds the hemshech's most detailed account of *teshuvah* (return/repentance) as a Kabbalistic mechanism: specifically, how the Or Chozer generated through teshuvah draws from the innermost dimension of *Moh sheb'Keter* (*Mo"s*) — the mouth of the beard of Arich Anpin — which is the level of divine being associated with the Thirteen Attributes of Mercy (*yud-gimmel midot harachamim*). Teshuvah is shown to be not merely moral reformation but the activator of the highest possible Or Chozer, one that reaches beyond the structures of the sefirotic chain entirely.

### The Opening Move

Shabbat Teshuva is the pivot of the High Holiday period: after the cosmic renewal of Rosh Hashanah (Malkhut renewed from Keter) and before Yom Kippur's maximum Or Chozer (the Thirteen Attributes of Mercy), this Shabbat holds the threshold. The Rashab opens with the established teaching from the previous Rosh Hashanah discourses that Keter is the *ממוצע* between the Emanator and the emanated — and asks: what specifically is the mechanism by which Yom Kippur's teshuvah reaches *higher* than the ordinary sefirotic chain? The answer will require the full anatomy of Keter's inner structure.

### The Argumentative Arc

- **Worlds of Tohu and Tikkun revisited**: The maamar opens the discussion of Olam haTikun's inadequacy: even the perfectly rectified system of Atzilut — with its balanced vessels capable of receiving divine light — cannot permanently fill the ontological gap in Malkhut (*miut halevanah*). The vessels of Tikkun provide what is called *dai machsoro* (adequate to his lack) but not complete filling. Complete filling of Malkhut requires not just more light within the sefirotic chain but light from *outside* the chain — from Keter's innermost depth.

- **The two types of drawing from Keter**: The maamar distinguishes between two modes of drawing from Keter: (1) through the *seder hishtalshelut* (the normal chain of descent), which operates via *Arich Anpin* as the will governing the sefirot — this is the ordinary Or Yashar that fills the sefirot but cannot fundamentally transform Malkhut's deficiency; and (2) through *Radla* (Reisha d'Lo Ityada — the unknowable head) and *Mo"s* (Mo sheb'Keter), which bypasses the chain of descent and delivers *etzmiut* (essential divine being) directly. This second mode is what teshuvah activates.

- **Why teshuvah reaches this level**: Teshuvah's Or Chozer uniquely accesses the *penimiyut* (innermost dimension) of Keter because teshuvah operates from a place prior to and deeper than the soul's structured faculties. Ordinary divine service operates from the soul's *orot megunlim* (revealed lights) — the cognitive and emotional capacities. Teshuvah operates from the soul's root, which precedes those capacities. Just as Or Chozer reaches higher than Or Yashar descends by traveling the same path in reverse *beyond its point of origin*, teshuvah reaches *before* the origin of the self's conscious life.

- **The Thirteen Attributes and the beard of Arich**: The Thirteen Attributes of Mercy function specifically through the *tikkunei dikna* (the configurations of the beard of Arich Anpin) because *dikna* (beard) is the sefirotic anatomical locus of *hamshakha b'derekh dilug* (transmission by skipping), i.e., the mode of extension that operates outside the normal chain of Sefirah-to-Sefirah descent. The Thirteen Attributes deliver divine forgiveness not through a graduated descent but through a direct skip from the highest divine being to the lowest vessel — enabling complete, rather than partial, repair of Malkhut.

- **Connecting to the souls of Israel**: The maamar emphasizes that the root connection established by teshuvah is not only the soul's connection to its divine source but also the root-bond of Israel's collective soul to the *moh shebeketer* — the shared root from which all Jewish souls originate. Teshuvah is not only vertical (soul to God) but also horizontally restorative, reconnecting each soul to the community of all souls at their common root.

### What It Gets To

The maamar arrives at the conclusion that teshuvah is the uniquely powerful Or Chozer mechanism precisely because it does not operate through the soul's ordinary faculties but through its root — which is bound to the level of *Mo"s* and *Radla*, the deepest aspects of Keter. The Thirteen Attributes of Mercy that God proclaims on Yom Kippur are the response to this Or Chozer: they are the mode of Or Yashar that comes not from Arich but from the unknowable head, delivering not ordinary divine blessing but essential divine being into the world. This is why teshuvah "reaches the divine throne" and why a penitent stands higher than a righteous person: the Or Chozer of teshuvah engages a source that even continuous righteousness does not regularly touch.

### How This Fits the Hemshech

The Shabbat Teshuva maamar completes the hemshech's analysis of the High Holiday period by mapping teshuvah onto the Or Chozer structure developed throughout the summer parashiyot. The progression is: (1) Va'etchanan — Torah study as Or Chozer through Chokhmah; (2) Eikev — mitzvot as Or Chozer through Netzach/Hod; (3) Ki Tetze — Torah's essential union as Or Chozer at the level of Yechidah; (4) Rosh Hashanah — communal prayer as Or Chozer reaching Keter; (5) Shabbat Teshuva — teshuvah as Or Chozer penetrating to Radla/Mo"s. Each stage engages a higher level of Or Ein Sof and requires a deeper level of the soul.
