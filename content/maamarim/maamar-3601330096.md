## Context

This maamar was delivered during **Parshat Vayechi 1909-10** in the year **העת"ר** (1909–10), as part of the ongoing hemshech (continuous discourse) that Rebbe Shalom DovBer of Lubavitch (the Rashab) delivered over two decades. It forms part of **חלק שני (Part 2)** of the hemshech.

The Hemshech Taarav is the longest chassidic discourse ever composed, spanning from 5662 (1901-02) through 5680 (1919-20). Each maamar continues the thread of the previous, building a comprehensive Kabbalistic-Chassidic system centered on the nature of divine light, the structure of the sefirot, and the mission of the Jewish soul.

## Deep Dive

### The Construct Being Built

This maamar constructs the deep relationship between hoda'ah (acknowledgment/praise), malchut, and the power of yad b'oref oyvecha (hand on the enemies' necks), demonstrating that when the inner light of the neshama is revealed through genuine acknowledgment of the divine — and when Yehuda (Malchut) is praised by his brothers — the light of Malchut achieves the power to overcome the kelipot from the inside out, not merely from the outside in.

### The Opening Move

The verse is Yaakov's blessing to Yehuda in Vayechi: "יהודה אתה יודוך אחיך ידך בעורף אויביך" — "Yehuda, your brothers will acknowledge you; your hand shall be on the neck of your enemies." The Rashab identifies a compound difficulty: (1) What is the meaning of "yoduchah" — acknowledgment, praise, thanksgiving? The brothers acknowledge/praise Yehuda, but why? (2) The verse says that because the brothers acknowledge him, "your hand shall be on the neck of your enemies" — implying that the brothers' acknowledgment is the cause of Yehuda's triumph over enemies. What is the causal connection between being praised by one's brothers and having dominion over enemies?

### The Argumentative Arc

- **"Yehuda" derives from hoda'ah (thanksgiving/acknowledgment)**: Leah named her fourth son Yehuda saying "this time I will acknowledge (odeh) God" — the very name encodes the essence of this son as the carrier of hoda'ah. In the hemshech's framework, hoda'ah is not merely gratitude but the specific mode of the soul's relationship to what transcends rational comprehension: when one cannot grasp or justify something but acknowledges its reality fully, that is hoda'ah. This maps onto the soul's relationship to Keter/Ein Sof.
- **Malchut is the sefirah of hoda'ah — and all Israel is called Yehudi**: the Midrash Rabbah notes that the brothers are called by Yehuda's name — "yehudi" — meaning all twelve tribes are ultimately identified through Yehuda. The Rashab reads this through the lens of Malchut: just as Malchut receives all the lights of the upper sefirot and "acknowledges" them (contains/reflects them), so Yehuda/Malchut is the point of integration at which all the brothers are gathered into a single people with a single name.
- **The structure of tefillah as revealed in the order of the siddur**: the source text develops an extended analysis of how prayer (tefillah) progresses through levels, beginning with Modeh Ani (pure acknowledgment), then Pesukei D'Zimra (cutting through the kelipot through praise), then Birkat Shema and Shema itself (intellect and love). The seder of tefillah mirrors the seder of the sefirot: ascending from Malchut (hoda'ah) through the middot (emotional arousal) to the mochin (intellectual illumination). This is Or Chozer in its ordered form.
- **"Yoduchah achecha" — when the brothers acknowledge Yehuda, Malchut receives light from all the higher sefirot**: the brothers represent the other sefirot (Reuven/Chesed, Shimon/Gevura, Levi/Tiferet, and so on). When they "acknowledge" Yehuda, they are channeling their respective lights into Malchut. This is the condition for Malchut achieving its full stature — not in isolation but when all the other sefirot are flowing into it.
- **"Yadcha b'oref oyvecha" — dominion over enemies as the result of Malchut's full illumination**: when Malchut receives the light of all the upper sefirot through the brothers' acknowledgment, it achieves the power to subdue the kelipot from within the system rather than merely from without. "Oref" (back of the neck) suggests an internal victory — catching the enemy from behind, overpowering what had been attacking from below. This is the structure of Or Chozer at full power: it rises to the upper sefirot, draws down their light into Malchut, and then that illuminated Malchut turns and overwhelms the opposing forces.
- **The "two half-forms" (shtei chatza'ot tzurot) that complete each other**: the source text cites the Maggid's teaching that Israel below and the divine above are like two half-forms that complete each other. Malchut (Israel) is incomplete without Ze'ir Anpin, and Ze'ir Anpin achieves its completion through Malchut's ascent. The dynamic of Yehuda being acknowledged by his brothers is the moment when this completion occurs — the two halves of the divine-human relationship are fully joined.

### What It Gets To

The maamar concludes that the "hand on the neck of enemies" is not a separate reward for being acknowledged but is the direct structural consequence: when Malchut is fully illuminated through the combined acknowledgment of all the sefirot-brothers, it achieves the natural power to subdue what opposes it. The inner logic is Or Chozer: Malchut's acknowledgment (hoda'ah) rises, draws down the combined light of all the upper sefirot, and that combined light expressed through Malchut becomes the power to overcome all opposition.

### How This Fits the Hemshech

This maamar is the culmination of the Bereishit-section maamarim in 5670, bringing together the threads of Or Chozer (from the Vayishlach katanti maamar), bold approach (from Vayigash), and the structure of tefillah-as-Or Chozer (from the 19 Kislev maamar) into a comprehensive account of how Malchut achieves its complete illumination. The sequence Vayishlach → 19 Kislev → Vayeshev → Miketz → Vayigash → Vayechi traces a complete arc: humility before divine grace → structure of prayer → tzaddik in concealment → light that transforms exterior darkness → bold approach that demands the highest light → acknowledgment that integrates all lights into Malchut. With Vayechi the hemshech transitions into the Shemot cycle, which will begin analyzing how this now-complete Malchut/Israel descends into the deepest exile (Egypt/Mitzrayim) and generates Or Chozer from that nadir.
