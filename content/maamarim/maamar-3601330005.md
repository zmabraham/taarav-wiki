## Context

This maamar was delivered during **Night of 2nd day of Shavuot 1901-02** in the year **תער"ב** (1901–02), as part of the ongoing hemshech (continuous discourse) that Rebbe Shalom DovBer of Lubavitch (the Rashab) delivered over two decades. It forms part of **חלק ראשון (Part 1)** of the hemshech.

The Hemshech Taarav is the longest chassidic discourse ever composed, spanning from 5662 (1901-02) through 5680 (1919-20). Each maamar continues the thread of the previous, building a comprehensive Kabbalistic-Chassidic system centered on the nature of divine light, the structure of the sefirot, and the mission of the Jewish soul.

## Summary

**Opening Question**: The verse 'He wraps Himself in light as a garment, He spreads the heavens like a curtain' (Psalms 104:2) — what is the relationship between 'wrapping in light' and 'spreading the heavens'? These seem to be two entirely different acts. And what does it mean that light is a garment for God?

**Central Concept**: The two dimensions of Torah as a 'garment' of divine light: the inner dimension (the divine wisdom itself, Or Ein Sof clothed in Torah) and the outer dimension (the revealed Torah as a 'curtain' that both conceals and reveals the divine). The relationship between Or Pnimi (inner light) and Or Mekif (surrounding/outer light) as applied to the structure of Torah.

**Resolution**: The 'light-garment' (ohr kashalma) represents the two levels of divine presence within Torah: the concealed Ein Sof-light (pnimiyut) and the revealed structure (chitzonyut). Just as the Atzilut-world serves as an intermediary between Ein Sof and the lower worlds — containing both the unlimited lights (orot) and the structured vessels (kelim) — Torah similarly mediates between infinite divinity and created existence through its two dimensions.

## Deep Dive

### The Construct Being Built

This maamar erects the framework of Atzilut as a *double intermediary* — simultaneously a garment (concealment) and a curtain (revelation) — by showing that the two images in Psalms 104:2 ("He wraps Himself in light as a garment, He spreads the heavens like a curtain") correspond to two distinct structural functions: Torah/Atzilut as Or Pnimi (inner light clothed in vessels) and as Or Mekif (surrounding/encompassing light spread across creation).

### The Opening Move

The maamar opens on the second night of Shavuot, the night after the Naaseh/Nishma discourse, picking up the theme of divine self-disclosure through Torah. Its opening verse — *Oteh or kashalma, noteh shamayim kayri'ah* — presents what seems like two unconnected images: God "wrapping Himself" in light (an intimate, inward act), and "spreading the heavens" (an outward, cosmic act). Why are they joined in a single breath? This syntactic intimacy is the maamar's engine: if God's light-garment and the stretched heavens belong together, they must share a structural logic that the maamar will unveil.

### The Argumentative Arc

- **The analogy of inner soul-powers in bodily organs** (drawn from the previous maamar) is now applied to the cosmic order. Each inner soul-power — intellect in the brain, vision in the eye — is delimited by its vessel; the vessel shapes how the power manifests. This is Or Pnimi: light that enters a vessel and is configured by it.

- **Adam Kadmon (Atzilut conceived as the primordial Human Form)** is introduced as the principle that even in the divine realm, lights are clothed in corresponding vessels: Chesed in the right arm, Gevurah in the left, Chochmah in the brain, etc. The Etz Chayyim's teaching that the Sefirot of Atzilut are אורות מתלבשים בכלים (lights clothed in vessels) is the foundation here. Each light has its fitting vessel and no other — the light of Chochmah inhabits only the vessel of Chochmah.

- **The crucial distinction is then made**: the lights of Atzilut are Or Pnimi, but even within Atzilut the full essence of the higher lights does not descend into the lower vessels. The Mohin (intellectual Sefirot, Chochmah-Binah-Da'at) only send their *radiance* (הארה) into the Middot (emotional Sefirot) — the essential Mohin remain in the three Rishonot (upper three). This maps onto the human analogy: the intellect that reaches the hand is only *practical intellect* (שכל מעשי), not the essence of intellect that illuminates the brain.

- **Atzilut as intermediary** (ממוצע, *memutza*) between Ein Sof and the lower worlds is then established. An intermediary must partake of *both* levels: (a) from the Ein Sof side — the Lights (Orot) of Atzilut, which derive from the Kav (the post-Tzimtzum conduit from Ein Sof) and therefore bear the quality of Ein Sof within Atzilut; (b) from the created-worlds side — the Vessels (Kelim) of Atzilut, which constitute a kind of boundary-capacity (כח הגבול), limited and distinct from each other (Chesed is Chesed, not Gevurah).

- **The two images of the verse are now resolved**: "wrapping in light as a garment" = Or Pnimi, the inner lights clothed in Atzilut's vessels — this is the intimate divine self-disclosure in structured form. "Spreading the heavens like a curtain" = Or Mekif, the encompassing light that spreads beyond Atzilut into Beriah-Yetzirah-Asiyah — this is the cosmic extension that simultaneously conceals and reveals. Both acts are one continuous gesture of divine self-expression, because Atzilut is the site where Or Pnimi and Or Mekif coexist.

- **The Lights of Atzilut are Ein Sof within Atzilut, but still bounded**: even the Lights are not purely infinite, since the Vessels exercise a limiting function (הגבלה) such that, for instance, the light of Chesed can extend infinitely within Atzilut yet remains in the direction of Chesed, not Gevurah. This "power of the boundary" without actual boundary (כח הגבול לא גבול ממש) is the distinctive mode of Atzilut.

### What It Gets To

The maamar concludes with a worked-out picture of Atzilut as the structural site where Or Pnimi and Or Mekif coexist in a way they cannot in lower worlds: the inner light is fully and truly clothed in specific vessels, while the surrounding light (the Kav's derivation from pre-Tzimtzum Ein Sof) overarches all. Together these correspond to the two images in the Psalm. The coexistence of Or Pnimi and Or Mekif within Atzilut is the template for understanding all subsequent structures in the hemshech.

### How This Fits the Hemshech

Building directly on maamar 4's establishment of Keter/Ratzon as Or Mekif, this maamar now maps Or Pnimi and Or Mekif onto the full structure of Atzilut, grounding the entire Lights-and-Vessels framework that will undergird the hemshech's analysis for years. The tension between the Lights' infinite character and the Vessels' limiting function — resolved here through the concept of the "power of the boundary" — becomes a recurring technical tool.
