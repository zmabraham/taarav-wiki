## Context

This maamar was delivered during **Night of 2nd day of Shavuot 1901-02** in the year **תער"ב** (1901–02), as part of the ongoing hemshech (continuous discourse) that Rebbe Shalom DovBer of Lubavitch (the Rashab) delivered over two decades. It forms part of **חלק ראשון (Part 1)** of the hemshech.

The Hemshech Taarav is the longest chassidic discourse ever composed, spanning from 5662 (1901-02) through 5680 (1919-20). Each maamar continues the thread of the previous, building a comprehensive Kabbalistic-Chassidic system centered on the nature of divine light, the structure of the sefirot, and the mission of the Jewish soul.

## Summary

**Opening Question**: The verse 'He wraps Himself in light as a garment, He spreads the heavens like a curtain' (Psalms 104:2) — what is the relationship between 'wrapping in light' and 'spreading the heavens'? These seem to be two entirely different acts. And what does it mean that light is a garment for God?

**Central Concept**: The two dimensions of Torah as a 'garment' of divine light: the inner dimension (the divine wisdom itself, Or Ein Sof clothed in Torah) and the outer dimension (the revealed Torah as a 'curtain' that both conceals and reveals the divine). The relationship between Or Pnimi (inner light) and Or Mekif (surrounding/outer light) as applied to the structure of Torah.

**Resolution**: The 'light-garment' (ohr kashalma) represents the two levels of divine presence within Torah: the concealed Ein Sof-light (pnimiyut) and the revealed structure (chitzonyut). Just as the Atzilut-world serves as an intermediary between Ein Sof and the lower worlds — containing both the unlimited lights (orot) and the structured vessels (kelim) — Torah similarly mediates between infinite divinity and created existence through its two dimensions.
