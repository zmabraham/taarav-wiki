## Context

This maamar was delivered during **Parshat Ki Tisa 1909-10** in the year **העת"ר** (1909–10), as part of the ongoing hemshech (continuous discourse) that Rebbe Shalom DovBer of Lubavitch (the Rashab) delivered over two decades. It forms part of **חלק שני (Part 2)** of the hemshech.

The Hemshech Taarav is the longest chassidic discourse ever composed, spanning from 5662 (1901-02) through 5680 (1919-20). Each maamar continues the thread of the previous, building a comprehensive Kabbalistic-Chassidic system centered on the nature of divine light, the structure of the sefirot, and the mission of the Jewish soul.

## Deep Dive

### The Construct Being Built

This maamar uses the drama of Ki Tisa — the breaking of the first Tablets, the sin of the golden calf, and Moses' petitioning to see G-d's "back" — to construct the hemshech's account of why there must be two stages of divine light-giving (two Tablets) and what the "back" of G-d (achoraim) is as a Kabbalistic-ontological category. The maamar establishes that the first Tablets represent Or Yashar in its undifferentiated, supra-rational mode (the hewn-by-G-d state), while the second Tablets represent Or Yashar as it passes through the mediation of human formation (hewn by Moses), and that this second mode is not inferior but is actually more integrated into the world.

### The Opening Move

The parasha opens with the census command (כי תשא — "when you lift the heads of the children of Israel"), immediately setting up the question of how a census — an act of counting that by definition creates division and individuation — can be performed without harm. The Rashab notes the Talmudic teaching that direct counting of Israelites brings plague, which is why the shekel mediation is required. This leads to the core question: what makes the soul susceptible to danger through direct enumeration, and how does the half-shekel function as a protective medium? Behind this lies the deeper question that dominates the parasha: why did the gold of the calf (direct material expression) shatter the divine gift, while the second Tablets — processed through human mediation — endured?

### The Argumentative Arc

- **Direct encounter and the danger of undifferentiated Or**: When the Or Ein Sof shines without mediation — as it did in the first Tablets written entirely by G-d — the lower reality cannot sustain it. This is not failure of the lower level but a structural incompatibility: undifferentiated Or Ein Sof, in the mode of Sovev (Encompassing), cannot be grasped and held by the individuated soul without shattering whatever individuated form the soul inhabits. The direct census (counting without shekel) is the same phenomenon — the Or that illuminates each person as infinitely equal (no person more than another in the divine view) dissolves the individual's capacity to exist as a separate vessel.
- **The chet ha'egel as premature materialization**: The golden calf represents the attempt to clothe Or Yashar directly in the most material Levush — gold, sensory form, the "you can see it" of the mixed multitude's demand. This is the catastrophic short-circuit: attempting to skip the entire hierarchy of Kelim and Levushim and land Or Yashar in a raw material object. The first Tablets shatter not because G-d "withdraws" but because the Or cannot remain in that broken vessel-structure.
- **The Reshimu and the second Tablets**: After the shattering, a Reshimu (impression) remains. This is the hemshech's technical point: even total apparent rupture does not destroy the Or's trace in the world. The Rashab connects this to the broader cosmological teaching about the Tzimtzum: when the Or withdrew, it left a Reshimu in the vacated space, and this Reshimu is the source of all subsequent vessel-formation. Moses' carved second Tablets (human hands forming the receptacle first, then G-d inscribing the Or) model the correct sequence: vessel before light.
- **Moses' request to see G-d's "back" (achoraim)**: Moses cannot see G-d's "face" (panim) — the direct Or — but is shown G-d's "back." The Rashab reads this as the ontological dimension of Or Chozer: the "back" is what remains after the Or has passed, the trace-dimension, the achoraim (posteriority) which corresponds to Malkhut as the last Sefirah — the terminal point of Or Yashar's descent that becomes the source of Or Chozer's ascent. Seeing G-d's back is thus seeing the mechanism of Or Chozer itself: the reflected light that makes divine presence intelligible to the created world.
- **The 13 Attributes of Mercy as the architecture of Or Chozer**: G-d's declaration of the Thirteen Attributes (Exodus 34:6-7) immediately follows the promise to show His "back." The Rashab treats these attributes as the specific configuration of Or Chozer — the exact channels through which divine mercy flows upward from the level of Malkhut and returns toward its source. Each of the thirteen (Rachum, Chanun, etc.) corresponds to a specific grade of Or Chozer in the hierarchy of the Sefirot.

### What It Gets To

The maamar concludes that the two-Tablet sequence is not a failure followed by a lesser replacement but a divinely intended two-stage revelation: first the supra-rational Or (direct, undifferentiated), then the mediated Or (vessel-formed, sustainable). The "back" of G-d — Or Chozer — is what makes the second revelation not a diminishment but a completion. Only when Or Yashar has "passed" (descended fully through the chain of worlds) does Or Chozer become visible, and it is in that Or Chozer that the Thirteen Attributes — the enduring structure of divine compassion — are inscribed.

### How This Fits the Hemshech

Ki Tisa is the hemshech's account of how rupture is built into the system rather than being an accident. The Tzimtzum, the shattering of the Vessels (Shevirat HaKelim), and the breaking of the Tablets are all instances of the same structural necessity: Or Yashar cannot dwell in fully actualized lower-world form without passing through the mediation of human/material vessel-formation. This maamar prepares the reader for the subsequent analysis of the Mishkan's completion (Vayakhel-Pekudei) as the moment when the vessel-formation process is finally complete enough to receive the Or without shattering.
