## Context

This maamar was delivered during **Night of 2nd day of Shavuot 1902-03** in the year **תער"ג** (1902–03), as part of the ongoing hemshech (continuous discourse) that Rebbe Shalom DovBer of Lubavitch (the Rashab) delivered over two decades. It forms part of **חלק ראשון (Part 1)** of the hemshech.

The Hemshech Taarav is the longest chassidic discourse ever composed, spanning from 5662 (1901-02) through 5680 (1919-20). Each maamar continues the thread of the previous, building a comprehensive Kabbalistic-Chassidic system centered on the nature of divine light, the structure of the sefirot, and the mission of the Jewish soul.

## Summary

**Opening Question**: In the context of the festival of Night of 2nd day of Shavuot 1902-03, the Rashab asks about the spiritual significance of the Torah's transmission at Sinai — specifically, what new dimension of divine revelation was made accessible through the giving of the Torah that was not accessible before, and how this relates to the ongoing hemshech's analysis of Or Yashar and Or Chozer.

**Central Concept**: The revelation at Sinai (Matan Torah) as a unique cosmic event in which Or Chozer ascended higher than had ever occurred previously, creating a new mode of divine-human relationship. The Torah itself is analyzed as a 'garment' that paradoxically both conceals and reveals the infinite divine light — making it accessible to finite minds while retaining its infinite quality.

**Resolution**: The giving of Torah created a permanent channel (צינור, tzinor) through which Or Chozer from the lowest world can reach the highest divine levels. Matan Torah established the template for the entire project of 'dwelling in the lower realms' (dira betachtonim) — the divine goal of having infinite light inhabit finite structure without destruction.

## Deep Dive

### Opening Kushya

The maamar opens with a double question on the first of the Aseres HaDibros: **"אנכי ה' אלקיך אשר הוצאתיך מארץ מצרים"** — why does the revelation at Matan Torah identify itself by referencing Yetzias Mitzrayim rather than the creation of heaven and earth? And more sharply: if "אנכי" refers to the essence of Ein Sof above all revelation, while "הוי'" refers to the revelation of divine light, what kind of disclosure was Matan Torah? If it was a disclosure of the Essence (אנכי), the name הוי' is superfluous; if it was only a disclosure of הוי', why invoke אנכי at all?

### The Argumentative Arc

To resolve this, the Rashab first builds a theoretical account of how divine light emerges from its concealed source. The key concept introduced is **אור זרוע** ("sown light") — drawn from a Midrash (Shemos Rabbah, Parshas Bo) that Avraham "began to illuminate," explained in the Zohar as referring to אור זרוע. The Rashab anchors this in the Zohar's formulation **"אנת הוא חכים ולא בחכמה ידיעא"**: Chochma and Binah as they exist in Atzilus are "Chochma ידיעא" — revealed, knowable — while the sefirot of Akudim and Adam Kadmon are still in the category of "לאו אינון נהורין," not yet in the mode of actual lights.

The transition from concealed to revealed light is then explained through an extended analogy: the sweetness of a fruit does not exist explicitly in the seed, nor in the spiritual "mazal" (the cosmic force that drives growth), yet it emerges through the process of planting. The sweetness of the mazal is purely spiritual — like the sweetness of a melody or an intellectual insight — and through the seed it becomes grossified (מתעבה) into tangible physical sweetness. The maamar states: **"המתיקות הרוחני שבמזל ה"ה מתעבה ומתגשם ע"י הגרעין הנזרע ונעשה מתיקות גשמי"**. This is precisely אור זרוע: the sefirot are not an emanation of the Essence the way water flows from one place to another (which depletes the source), but a radiation (הארה) that thickens into actual light — **"ואין התפשטות זו כמו התפשטות המים... שזהו התפשטות הארה בלבד"**.

From this the Rashab turns to Avraham, who revealed that the world is not a separate entity from Divinity but its grossified illumination — **"אל עולם"** meaning the world itself is Elokus. This is what drew down the disclosure of יגת"ד (Yud-Gimmel Tikkunei Dikna), and is why "until Avraham there was no old age" — i.e., no manifestation of the supernal beard-configurations that express how the divine light inhabits and constitutes the world.

### The Chiddush and Resolution

With this framework in place, the maamar resolves the opening question. **"מצרים בקדושה"** — Mitzrayim in holiness — refers to the bounded, contracted mode of divine light, where the Or comes dressed in limiting vessels (כלים). Matan Torah was the event of יציאה from those limits: a disclosure of שם הוי' not merely as the light operates within the structure of Atzilus, but as it relates to the Essence — **"גילוי שם הוי' כמו שהוא מצד בחי' אנכי"**. The formulation "אנכי ה' אלקיך אשר הוצאתיך" is thus precise: the light of הוי' was revealed from the vantage point of the Essence (אנכי), which is why this disclosure could effect a genuine יציאה from all constriction. Creation is not mentioned in the Dibur because creation corresponds to שם שדי — the "די" of limitation — while Matan Torah corresponds to the revelation that transcends that limitation from within it.
