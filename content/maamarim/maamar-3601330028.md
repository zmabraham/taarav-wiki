## Context

This maamar was delivered during **Parshat Chayei Sara 1902-03** in the year **תער"ג** (1902–03), as part of the ongoing hemshech (continuous discourse) that Rebbe Shalom DovBer of Lubavitch (the Rashab) delivered over two decades. It forms part of **חלק ראשון (Part 1)** of the hemshech.

The Hemshech Taarav is the longest chassidic discourse ever composed, spanning from 5662 (1901-02) through 5680 (1919-20). Each maamar continues the thread of the previous, building a comprehensive Kabbalistic-Chassidic system centered on the nature of divine light, the structure of the sefirot, and the mission of the Jewish soul.

## Deep Dive

### The Construct Being Built

This maamar advances the sefirot-analysis of maamar 27 into a third meaning of *Sefirah*: from *sapir* (sapphire, brilliance) — the vessel's quality of *translucency* or *luminosity*. The construct here is the distinction between the sefirot-vessels as *revealing* instruments (not concealing ones) and the *levushim* (garments) which genuinely conceal. The Rashab builds a precise typology: kelim, orot, and levushim are three distinct ontological categories, not two — and Sara's life-narrative in Chayei Sara provides the exegetical map.

### The Opening Move

The parasha opens with the unusual phrase *"Vayihy'u chayei Sara meah shanah ve-esrim shanah ve-sheva shanim"* — the years of Sara's life are counted in three separate units (100, 20, 7) rather than as a single sum (127). Rashi's famous gloss explains this as indicating that at 100 she was as sinless as at 20, and at 20 as beautiful as at 7 — the three units represent three distinct qualities of her life that were each fully preserved. The Rashab uses this tripartite structure to introduce the third meaning of sefirah and its implications for the typology of kli, or, and levush.

### The Argumentative Arc

- **Sefirah as sapir (brilliance/translucency)**: The third meaning derives from the Zohar's description of the divine light shining *through* the sefirot as through a transparent sapphire. The key quality is *translucency*: a translucent medium does not add its own color to the light passing through it but allows the light to shine with full clarity. This distinguishes the sefirot-vessels from *levushim* (garments), which inherently add their own character and thereby obscure the light they contain.

- **Kelim as meyakhdim (unifiers) rather than masikhim (concealers)**: The maamar's central argument is that the sefirot-kelim, precisely because they are sapphire-like, do not conceal the divine light — they *particularize* it without distorting it. The kli of Chesed allows the Or Ein Sof to shine as loving-kindness; the kli of Gevurah allows it to shine as strength. Each kli gives the light a specific face (*panim*) without deducting from its essential nature. This is entirely different from a levush, which by its own opacity creates a new layer of concealment.

- **The three modes of Sara's life as three ontological levels**: The Rashab maps Sara's three age-groups onto three ontological categories: the 7-year-old's beauty = the *or* (light) dimension, which is pure, undifferentiated, essential; the 20-year-old's blamelessness = the *kli* dimension, which gives the or a specific form without concealing it; the 100-year-old's wisdom = the synthesis — a being in whom *or, kli*, and the relationship between them have reached full integration. Sara as a whole figure represents the fully actualized sefirot-system in which all three dimensions are present and mutually supporting.

- **Levush vs. kli — the critical distinction**: The maamar pauses to establish this distinction formally. A *levush* (garment) is a separate entity that is not of the same substance as the wearer — it covers and conceals. A *kli* (vessel) shares the substance of what it contains — it is a differentiated expression of the same reality. The intellect and emotions are *kelim* of the soul, not *levushim* — they reveal the soul's specific character. The letters of thought and speech (*machashavah u-dibbur*) are *levushim* — they are external to the soul's substance and inherently capable of concealing it.

- **Implications for Atzilut vs. lower worlds**: This distinction is mapped onto the world-structure. The sefirot of Atzilut are kelim — translucent instruments through which Or Ein Sof shines without concealment. The *Partzufim* of Beriyah, Yetzirah, and Asiyah involve increasing degrees of levush — the divine light is progressively more concealed by the non-Atzilut vessel-structures. This explains why Atzilut is called the world of *giluy* (revelation) and the lower worlds are worlds of *hester* (concealment).

- **Torah and mitzvot as the sapphire-kli structure**: The maamar connects this to divine service: Torah study and mitzvot function as the sapphire-kli of the lower worlds — they are the Or Pnimi structures through which divine light can shine even within the concealing environment of Beriyah through Asiyah. Properly performed Torah and mitzvot achieve, in the lower worlds, what the sefirot-kelim achieve naturally in Atzilut: transparent revelation.

### What It Gets To

The maamar has completed the threefold sefirah-typology: mispar (limit/form) from maamar 27's kelim analysis, sippur (narration/revelation) from the orot analysis, and sapir (translucency/brilliance) from the present maamar's distinction of kli from levush. Together these three meanings describe the full ontological function of the sefirot: they bound (mispar), they reveal (sippur), and they do so transparently without concealment (sapir). Sara's three-fold life is the human embodiment of this complete sefirot-integration.

### How This Fits the Hemshech

Maamar 27 introduced the *mispar/sippur* distinction. This maamar adds the *sapir* dimension, completing the foundational typology of sefirot-function. With the kli/levush distinction now formally established, the hemshech has the tools to map every level of reality — from Atzilut to Asiyah — in terms of how much divine light is being revealed vs. concealed at each level. This mapping will be applied systematically in subsequent maamarim to explain the mechanism of divine service as the process of converting levush-concealment into kli-revelation.
