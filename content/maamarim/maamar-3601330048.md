## Context

This maamar was delivered during **Parshat Shoftim 1902-03** in the year **תער"ג** (1902–03), as part of the ongoing hemshech (continuous discourse) that Rebbe Shalom DovBer of Lubavitch (the Rashab) delivered over two decades. It forms part of **חלק ראשון (Part 1)** of the hemshech.

The Hemshech Taarav is the longest chassidic discourse ever composed, spanning from 5662 (1901-02) through 5680 (1919-20). Each maamar continues the thread of the previous, building a comprehensive Kabbalistic-Chassidic system centered on the nature of divine light, the structure of the sefirot, and the mission of the Jewish soul.

## Deep Dive

### Opening Kushya

The maamar opens with a double question from the posuk: **"צדק צדק תרדוף למען תחי' וירשת"** — why does the Torah repeat the word *tzedek* (justice) twice, and why does fulfilling this double-tzedek yield the two further outcomes of *tikhye* (life) and *virashta* (inheritance)? The opening also asks what the connection is between this verse and the preceding command, *"שופטים ושוטרים תתן לך"*. These questions frame the entire maamar.

### Conceptual Argument, Step by Step

The Rashab grounds the answer in the hemshech's earlier discussion of *שלשה קשרין* — the three bonds connecting Israel, Torah, and the Holy One. The key distinction introduced is that **התקשרות** (bonding/linkage) requires two genuinely distinct entities, whereas where two things are not truly separate — like light and its source — one speaks of **דבקות** (cleaving) rather than hitkashtut. This sets up the central tension: the Zohar states *"אורייתא וקוב"ה כולא חד"* (Torah and the Holy One are entirely one), yet the hemshech had been describing a *hitkashtut* of Torah with God — which implies separateness. How can both be true?

The maamar resolves this by distinguishing **חיצוניות התורה** (the outer dimension of Torah) from **פנימיות התורה** (its inner dimension). The outer dimension consists of the revealed letters and laws — *"מה שניתן בהתגלות ולכאו"א הן האותיות"* — which, though rooted in divine wisdom, dress themselves in discrete worldly subjects like "המחליף פרה בחמור" (one who exchanges an ox for a donkey) or laws of *ona'ah*. This outer Torah, including even its spiritual form in the worlds of Beriah and Yetzirah, remains in the category of the distinct and can therefore be spoken of as *mithkashret* (linked) to God.

**Mitzvot** are then compared and contrasted: they function as *"אברין דמלכא"* — limbs through which physical objects become actual vessels for the divine (the parchment of tefillin, the lulav), a relationship the source calls *"איהו וגרמוהי חד"*. Torah, by contrast, is not an enclothing in physical objects at all; at the level of its inwardness it is *"איהו וחיוהי חד"* — the unity of God with His very life-force.

The pnimiyut haTorah is then identified with **העונג שבתורה** — the delight within Torah, which transcends *hasagah* (comprehension) entirely. Even the pleasure experienced while grasping a concept is still within the category of intellectual apprehension; but the essential delight of the soul in Torah — *"נכספה וגם כלתה נפשי"* — is beyond settling into vessels and constitutes the true *"אורייתא וקוב"ה כולא חד ממש"*.

The maamar then notes (perek 182) that even within pnimiyut haTorah there is a revealed and a concealed dimension: the *nigleh she'al pi sod* (the kabbalistic revealed layer) corresponds to the *sa'arot* (hairs) of Z"A, while the truly hidden is the *mohin* (intellect) of Z"A itself — inaccessible even in mystical disclosure.

### Chiddush and Resolution

This framework resolves the opening double-tzedek. The two instances of *tzedek* correspond to the two dimensions of Torah — outer (*hitkashtut*, the bonded-distinct) and inner (*yihud atzmut*, essential unity). Pursuing both — the disciplined engagement with Torah law in its exoteric and esoteric forms — yields the two results: *tikhye* (life, the vitality of pnimiyut) and *virashta* (inheritance, the portion in the outer structure). The maamar thus arrives at the chiddush that the complete path of Torah service requires holding both modalities: the structured, letter-bound engagement that forges linkage, and the interior delight that dissolves separation entirely.
