## Context

This maamar was delivered during **Shabbat Teshuva 1905-06** in the year **עתר"ו** (1905–06), as part of the ongoing hemshech (continuous discourse) that Rebbe Shalom DovBer of Lubavitch (the Rashab) delivered over two decades. It forms part of **חלק ראשון (Part 1)** of the hemshech.

The Hemshech Taarav is the longest chassidic discourse ever composed, spanning from 5662 (1901-02) through 5680 (1919-20). Each maamar continues the thread of the previous, building a comprehensive Kabbalistic-Chassidic system centered on the nature of divine light, the structure of the sefirot, and the mission of the Jewish soul.

## Deep Dive

### Opening Kushya

The maamar opens from the verse "אנכי אנכי הוא מוחה פשעיך למעני" (Isaiah 43:25 — "I, I am He who blots out your transgressions, for My own sake"). The Rashab presses two difficulties immediately. First, the phrase "למעני" (for My own sake) demands explanation: Rashi glosses it as referring to God's Name — not to the person's merit — yet the Gemara itself asks "מה איכפת לי' להקב"ה" ("what does it matter to the Holy One") whether one slaughters from the throat or the nape? If God gains nothing from human conduct, in what sense can sin or forgiveness matter to Him at all? Second: at Mattan Torah (Sinai), the word אנכי appeared once; here it appears doubled — "אנכי אנכי" — what is the significance of this repetition?

### Conceptual Argument Step by Step

To resolve these questions, the Rashab builds on a framework already established earlier in the hemshech: the threefold structure of יש, אין, ויש — two kinds of Yesh (being) with Ayin (nothingness) as the intermediary between them.

The first Yesh is בחי' יש אמיתי, the true being of the Atzmus Ein Sof (God's very essence), which is the only genuine reality. The second Yesh is the created world — יש הנברא. The Ayin between them itself divides in two: (1) the Ayin of the true Yesh — the divine Or (light) as it clings to its source, existing in a state of utter self-nullification, so that "האור גופא הוא בבחי' אין בעצמו שאינו בבחי' תפיסת מקום בעצמו כלל" — the light itself is in a state of Ayin because it has no independent standing; and (2) the Ayin of the created Yesh — the level that serves as the transitional source for created existence, but is itself not truly Ayin in the first sense.

The maamar then introduces the two divine perspectives called דעת עליון (Da'at Elyon — the upper knowledge) and דעת תחתון (Da'at Tachton — the lower knowledge). Da'at Elyon is the perspective from above: Ein Sof is the sole reality; everything else is literally "כלא חשיב" — as nothing. Da'at Tachton is the perspective from below: here the created world registers as real and God appears concealed. The divine light that clings to its source operates according to Da'at Elyon; the light that enters the vessels and becomes the source for created existence operates according to Da'at Tachton — it conceals itself for the sake of creation ("מתעלם ומסתתר בשביל ההתהוות"), and precisely this concealment is what allows sin to take hold there.

An analogy develops this point: intellect (שכל) drawn through the faculties of will (רצון) and thought reveals that the hidden, essential power of intellect — the כח השכל ההיולי — can only be accessed through intense effort and resistance (on the model of striking a flint stone, צור החלמיש, where fire is latent but never exhausted). Similarly, the Or Chozer (reflected light) that arises from below through labor reaches deeper into the divine source than the Or Yashar (direct light) alone can express.

### The Chiddush and Resolution

The doubled "אנכי אנכי" signals two levels of Keter: the outer dimension of Keter, which is the source of Chokhmah — echoed in the single "אנכי" of Sinai ("אורייתא מחכמה נפקת") — and the inner dimension of Keter, פנימיות הכתר, which is the true Ayin Amiti, the level the Rashab identifies as "מי שאנכי — דלא ידע ליה בר איהו בלחודוהי" (known only to Himself alone). This Pnimiyut ha-Keter stands entirely beyond the hishtalshelut (chain of worlds) and beyond any concealment. Sin has no grip there: "אין ההעלמות דהעולם מעלימים שם." Forgiveness — מחיית הפשעים — flows specifically from this level, because it is the only level at which no concealment exists to give sin its hold.

Teshuva draws down this level: "ממעמקים קראתיך ה'" — from the inner depths of the soul one reaches the inner depths of divinity. The "למעני" is thus not a vague assertion of divine self-interest but a precise metaphysical claim: only from Atzmus that is wholly above any stake in creation can forgiveness be truly unconditional. The doubled Anochi resolves both questions simultaneously — the repetition marks the passage from outer Keter to Pnimiyut ha-Keter, and it is from this Pnimiyut, accessed through teshuva, that God wipes away transgression entirely.
