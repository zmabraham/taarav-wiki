## Context

This maamar was delivered during **Parshat Tazria-Metzora 1909-10** in the year **העת"ר** (1909–10), as part of the ongoing hemshech (continuous discourse) that Rebbe Shalom DovBer of Lubavitch (the Rashab) delivered over two decades. It forms part of **חלק שני (Part 2)** of the hemshech.

The Hemshech Taarav is the longest chassidic discourse ever composed, spanning from 5662 (1901-02) through 5680 (1919-20). Each maamar continues the thread of the previous, building a comprehensive Kabbalistic-Chassidic system centered on the nature of divine light, the structure of the sefirot, and the mission of the Jewish soul.

## Deep Dive

### The Construct Being Built

This maamar uses the tzara'at (skin affliction) laws of Parshat Tazria-Metzora to build the hemshech's analysis of how the Or's presence differs across the four worlds (Atzilut, Beriah, Yetzirah, Asiyah), with particular focus on the critical distinction between the Kelim (vessels) of Atzilut and the Kelim of Beriah. The construct is that in Atzilut the vessels grasp the Or's mahut (essence/quality), while in Beriah the vessels grasp only the Or's metziyut (existence/presence) — the light's being-there rather than what-it-is. This distinction, grounded in the Torah's use of the name "Adam" for the afflicted person, explains why spiritual blemish (tzara'at) at the level of the highest human type (Adam) requires the specific ritual of priestly diagnosis and quarantine.

### The Opening Move

The maamar opens with the verse: "אָדָם כִּי יִהְיֶה בְעוֹר בְּשָׂרוֹ שְׂאֵת כוּ' וְהוּבָא אֶל אַהֲרֹן הַכֹּהֵן" — "A person (adam) when there is in the skin of his flesh a white mark... and he shall be brought to Aaron the Kohen." Four questions are raised: (1) Why does the Torah use the word "adam" — the highest of the four human designations (adam, ish, gever, anosh) — in the context of skin affliction, which seems to concern the lowest (skin/flesh) aspect of the person? (2) What is the significance of "in the skin of his flesh" (be'or besaro) — why specify both skin and flesh? (3) What are the three marks (se'et, sapachat, baheret) and what do they represent? (4) Why specifically must the affliction be brought to Aaron the Kohen — what is the Kohen's specific function here?

### The Argumentative Arc

- **The prior context — Or's garbing in the vessels across BYA**: The maamar recalls the analysis from the preceding maamarim (particularly d"h "Vayhi Bayom HaShemini"): the tzimtzum causes the Or to be garbed (mitlabesh) in the Kelim at each level. The question had been: in what sense does the Kli in Atzilut "receive" the Or differently from the Kli in Beriah? The answer being developed is that in Atzilut, the Kli grasps the Or's mahut — its qualitative essence, what-it-is — while in Beriah, the Kli grasps only the Or's metziyut — its bare existence, that-it-is.
- **The mashal of the sage and the student**: The Rashab deploys the analogy (which he has established in earlier maamarim) of a great sage communicating an idea: for another great sage, a single phrase is sufficient — the listener grasps the full quality and texture of the idea from the phrase, not merely the fact that the idea exists. For a lesser student, many words are needed, and even then the student grasps only that this idea is present and true without grasping its inner quality. In Atzilut, the Kelim are the great-sage type: they grasp the Or's mahut. In BYA, the Kelim are the lesser-student type: they grasp only the metziyut.
- **"Adam" as the Atzilut-level human**: The Rashab reads the four human names as corresponding to four levels of the soul-in-Sefirot: anosh corresponds to Asiyah/Malkhut, gever to Yetzirah/Yesod, ish to Beriah/Tiferet, and adam to Atzilut/Chokhmah-Keter. "Adam" specifically denotes the soul at the level where it grasps the Or's mahut — the Atzilut-dimension of the human being. The affliction of tzara'at at the "adam" level is therefore not a physical skin disease in the ordinary sense but a spiritual blemish at the highest soul-level: a disruption in the Atzilut-type Kli's capacity to grasp and unify with the Or's mahut.
- **Skin and flesh — chitzoniyut and pnimiyut of the Kli**: "Be'or besaro" (in the skin of his flesh): the "skin" (or) is the chitzoniyut (outer face) of the Kli — the functional-operational dimension through which the Or acts outward; the "flesh" (basar) is the pnimiyut (inner face) of the Kli — the dimension that grasps and unifies with the Or's mahut. The tzara'at appears "in the skin of his flesh" — precisely at the interface between the outer and inner Kli-faces, the junction point between the Kli's capacity to receive (pnimiyut) and its capacity to radiate outward (chitzoniyut). This is the exact point where the Or-Kli relationship either unifies or fragments.
- **The three marks and their Sefirot-correspondence**: Se'et (elevation), sapachat (spreading/dependent mark), and baheret (brightness): the three marks of tzara'at correspond to three grades of spiritual disruption at the adam-level. Se'et corresponds to the disruption of Chokhmah's reception of Or (the highest Sefirah-vessel being unable to maintain the Or's mahut-level); sapachat to Binah's disruption (the Or spreading beyond its proper vessel-containment, becoming diffuse); baheret to Keter's disruption (the Or appearing with a brightness that the vessel cannot bear without opacity — the paradox of being blinded by excess light).
- **Aaron the Kohen as the restoring mechanism**: Aaron represents the attribute of Chesed (loving-kindness) — specifically, the directed outflow of Or Yashar that can re-establish the damaged Or-Kli relationship. Only the Kohen can diagnose tzara'at because only the Chesed-function can distinguish between a disrupted Or-Kli interface (impurity requiring quarantine) and a complete absence of Or (such as an entirely white person — tahor, pure, because the disruption has resolved into a different state). The Kohen's priestly function is thus the hemshech's model of the repair (tikkun) mechanism for Or-Kli disruption.

### What It Gets To

The maamar concludes that tzara'at at the "adam" level is the Torah's paradigm for what happens when the highest-capacity human Kli (adam = Atzilut-level soul) loses its capacity to grasp the Or's mahut rather than mere metziyut. The ritual of quarantine and eventual purification is the structured process of Or-Kli repair: isolating the disrupted interface, allowing the Or's natural pressure to reconstitute the Kli, and reintegrating the restored Kli-Or relationship into the community.

### How This Fits the Hemshech

Parshat Tazria-Metzora provides the hemshech with its account of what happens at the human-spiritual level when the Or-Kli relationship breaks down. Having established in Shemini what the fully functional Or-Kli structure looks like (the tenth crowns of the eighth day), the Rashab now maps the failure modes — the three types of tzara'at corresponding to three types of Or-Kli disruption at the Atzilut soul-level. This negative mapping (what goes wrong) deepens the understanding of the positive structure (what goes right in the Mishkan) and prepares the ground for the Yom Kippur analysis in Acharei Mot, where the Cohen Gadol's inner-Mishkan service is the supreme repair of all such disruptions at the cosmic level.
