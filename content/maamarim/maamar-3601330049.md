## Context

This maamar was delivered during **Parshat Ki Tetze 1902-03** in the year **תער"ג** (1902–03), as part of the ongoing hemshech (continuous discourse) that Rebbe Shalom DovBer of Lubavitch (the Rashab) delivered over two decades. It forms part of **חלק ראשון (Part 1)** of the hemshech.

The Hemshech Taarav is the longest chassidic discourse ever composed, spanning from 5662 (1901-02) through 5680 (1919-20). Each maamar continues the thread of the previous, building a comprehensive Kabbalistic-Chassidic system centered on the nature of divine light, the structure of the sefirot, and the mission of the Jewish soul.

**Central Concept**: This maamar continues the hemshech's development of the relationship between Or Yashar (direct divine light) and Or Chozer (reflected light), applying it to the specific occasion of Parshat Ki Tetze 1902-03. The Rashab develops the Kabbalistic framework of Sefirot and Partzufim to illuminate the Torah's discussion of the relevant theme.

**Resolution**: The chiddush reached in this maamar shows how the specific quality of divine service associated with ש"פ תצא, תער"ג corresponds to a specific aspect of the Or Chozer dynamic — revealing how the human soul's action in the lowest world (Asiyah) draws divine light from the highest Keter-level, thereby advancing the hemshech's central theme of divine habitation in the lower realms (dira betachtonim).

## Deep Dive

### The Construct Being Built

This maamar builds a fundamental distinction between two modes of divine-human union — the union achieved through *mitzvot* and the union achieved through *Torah* — and maps these onto two different formulations in the Zohar regarding the nature of Israel's relationship with God. The central kabbalistic edifice is the contrast between *ihu v'garmohei chad* (He and His instruments/attributes are one — a relational unity) and *ihu v'chayohi chad* (He and His life/essence are one — an intrinsic unity), showing that Torah operates at a categorically higher register of union than mitzvot.

### The Opening Move

The verse *ki tetze lamilchama al oyvecha* (when you go out to war against your enemies) presents an immediate structural paradox: the opening phrase ("when you go out to war") suggests human autonomy and initiative, while the next phrase ("and YHVH your God delivers them into your hand") suggests the gift comes from above. Then *v'shavita shivyo* — "and you will take captives of him" — implies the captive belongs to the enemy, yet the text says *shivyo* (his captivity). Why the combination of human initiative and divine gift? And why is the object of capture described as belonging to the enemy?

### The Argumentative Arc

- **The Zohar's two formulations reconciled**: The maamar opens by reconciling the apparent contradiction between two Zoharic statements. In one place the Zohar says "three are bound to each other: the Holy One, Torah, and Israel" — implying *hitkashrut* (binding together of separate things). In another it says "Torah and the Holy One are utterly one" — implying identity, not merely connection. The Rashab shows these describe two aspects: the exoteric dimension of Torah (*chitzoniut haTorah*) operates in the mode of *hitkashrut* because it speaks of created beings and bounded realities (even Torah in Gan Eden and in Atzilut remains in the mode of *hasagah*, comprehension, and thus retains separateness). The esoteric dimension of Torah (*penimiyut haTorah*) — specifically the dimension of *oneg* (delight) within Torah — operates in the mode of *chad mamash* (complete identity).

- **Mitzvot and Torah as two levels of union**: Mitzvot achieve the union of *ihu v'garmohei chad* — God and His manifest attributes/vessels are one — which is the union at the level of action and extension. Torah achieves *ihu v'chayohi chad* — God and His essential vitality are one — which is union at the level of being. The kabbalistic mapping is precise: the union of mitzvot is like *ihhu and garmohei* (God and the "body" of divine expression), while the union of Torah is like *ihu and chayohi* (God and the inner life/essence).

- **The war-analogy decoded**: *Ki tetze lamilchama* — "going out to war" under one's own initiative — represents the Or Chozer generated from human effort, specifically the mode of mitzvah-performance. *V'natan YHVH Elokecha b'yadecha* — God delivering the enemy — represents the Or Yashar that descends in response. *V'shavita shivyo* — taking the enemy's captive — means extracting and elevating the sparks (*nitzotzot*) embedded in the enemy's domain: the klipah's own spiritual substance is "taken captive" and returned to holiness. The captive belongs to the *enemy* precisely because these sparks originally fell from the world of Tohu into the domain of the klipot.

- **Chitzoniut and penimiyut of Torah**: The hidden-revealed structure (satum v'galuy) within Torah is itself internally differentiated. The revealed dimension of Kabbalistic wisdom (such as is studied in Gan Eden and in Atzilut) is in fact the *chitzoniut* of penimiyut — the external aspect of the inner Torah. The true *penimiyut* is the *oneg* (delight) that transcends comprehension entirely and in which the identification of Torah with God is not a relational claim but an ontological one.

- **Future revelation and the purging of kelipat nogah**: The maamar concludes by linking this distinction to the eschatological: the full reward of mitzvot is not available in this world (*s'khar mitzvah b'hai alma leika*) because the light that is the essence of each mitzvah can only shine in full once the kelipat nogah — currently operational as a metaphysical filter — is fully dissolved. At that point, the *penimiyut* of each mitzvah's light will be revealed without mediation.

### What It Gets To

The maamar arrives at a precise taxonomy of divine-human union and its conditions. Torah-union (essential, intrinsic, beyond separation) is ontologically superior to mitzvot-union (relational, instrumental, though genuinely unitive), yet both are real and necessary. The war-verse encodes the full Or Yashar/Or Chozer cycle: human initiative (Or Chozer), divine response (Or Yashar), and the extraction of embedded sparks (*birur nitzotzot* of Tohu) that constitutes the actual content of *dira betachtonim*. The future revelation awaits the dissolution of the final ontological screen.

### How This Fits the Hemshech

This is one of the most conceptually dense maamarim in this section of the hemshech. It advances two critical fronts: (1) it distinguishes Torah-union from mitzvot-union, providing the hemshech with a differential account of how different modes of divine service engage different levels of Or Ein Sof; (2) it integrates the *birur nitzotzot* (rectification of sparks from Tohu) framework into the Or Chozer dynamic, showing that Or Chozer is not merely a metaphysical reflection but an active salvage operation — the reintegration of broken divine light scattered through history and materiality.
