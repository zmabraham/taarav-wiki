## Context

This maamar was delivered during **Shabbat Shel Pesach 1909-10** in the year **העת"ר** (1909–10), as part of the ongoing hemshech (continuous discourse) that Rebbe Shalom DovBer of Lubavitch (the Rashab) delivered over two decades. It forms part of **חלק שני (Part 2)** of the hemshech.

The Hemshech Taarav is the longest chassidic discourse ever composed, spanning from 5662 (1901-02) through 5680 (1919-20). Each maamar continues the thread of the previous, building a comprehensive Kabbalistic-Chassidic system centered on the nature of divine light, the structure of the sefirot, and the mission of the Jewish soul.

## Deep Dive

### The Construct Being Built

The Shabbat of Pesach is the intersection of two supreme revelatory occasions: Shabbat (the weekly revelation of Or Yashar's encompassing light in a world of rest) and Pesach (the historical irruption of the Sovev Kol Almin into the tzimtzum-world). This maamar develops the hemshech's account of what happens when the Sovev's Pesach-revelation is encountered in the Shabbat frame — specifically, how the two modes of revelation (the Shabbat's Or of menucha/rest and the Pesach's Or of chipazon/haste) interact and what their combination reveals about the highest level of divine light, the Or Ein Sof in its Atzmut (essential) dimension.

### The Opening Move

The haftarah for Shabbat Shel Pesach (Ezekiel 37) describes the vision of the valley of dry bones (tziyat ha-atzmot): Ezekiel is shown a valley full of desiccated human bones and is told to prophesy that G-d will breathe life back into them, reconstituting the dead. The Rashab raises the central question: why is this haftarah specifically chosen for the intermediate Shabbat of Pesach? What is the connection between Pesach's exodus and the resurrection of the dead? The vision of reconstitution from utter death (not from illness, not from weakness, but from disarticulated dry bones — the most extreme possible distance from life) must correspond to some essential aspect of Pesach's revelatory event.

### The Argumentative Arc

- **Dry bones as the paradigm of tzimtzum-exhausted matter**: The bones in Ezekiel's vision are "yeveshim me'od" — exceedingly dry. All moisture (the fluid of life, associated in Kabbalistic thought with the Chesed-flow of Or Yashar) has been removed. These bones are not merely lifeless but are the paradigm of matter that has passed through and beyond the entire range of graduated Or Yashar — receiving it all, using it all up, and having nothing left. They represent Asiyah (the world of action/matter) at its terminal point: after all the Or has descended through the chain (hishtalshelut) and been absorbed, what remains?
- **The Pesach-revelation as the Sovev's action on the terminally depleted**: The Rashab argues that Pesach's geula addresses precisely this condition — not the repair of a world still in process (which Mimaleh/Or Yashar can manage) but the revival of a world that has exhausted the graduated light entirely. Egypt (Mitzrayim — the straits/constriction) is the endpoint of the tzimtzum's downward work: matter so contracted that no Or Yashar can penetrate it further. The Sovev Kol Almin's irruption at the exodus is thus the paradigm of divine action on the terminally constricted — life infused from utterly outside the graduated system.
- **Shabbat's menucha and the Sovev**: Shabbat's rest (menucha) is the suspension of the graduated, sequenced, time-structured flow of Or Yashar. On Shabbat the Mimaleh's work ceases and only the Sovev's encompassing presence sustains the world. The Rashab notes that Shabbat of Pesach places the Sovev's Shabbat-presence in direct conjunction with the Sovev's Pesach-presence — two modes of the same Or, one rhythmic and weekly (Shabbat) and one historically unique (Pesach). Their conjunction is not additive but multiplicative: the Shabbat of Pesach accesses a dimension of the Sovev that neither Shabbat alone nor Pesach alone can reach.
- **The ruach (breath of life) and the Atzmut**: When G-d breathes the ruach (spirit/breath) into the reconstituted bones in Ezekiel's vision, the Rashab identifies this ruach with the Atzmut (essential being) of Ein Sof — the dimension that is not even Sovev (which is still a relational category: it encompasses the worlds, however transcendently) but is prior to any relationship with created beings whatsoever. The ruach that revives the dry bones comes from the Atzmut precisely because only the Atzmut is not diminished by the tzimtzum's deepest constrictions — it has no relationship to the created framework at all and can therefore act upon what is utterly beyond the reach of Or Yashar and even of the Sovev-as-encompasser.
- **The connection to the Omer-counting (Sefirat HaOmer)**: The Shabbat of Pesach falls within the Omer-period (which begins on the 16th of Nissan). The Rashab connects this: the counting of the Omer (49 days from Pesach to Shavuot) is the graduated, sequential, time-structured process of Or Chozer — the ascent from the Pesach-revelation through all 49 sub-levels of soul-refinement toward the Shavuot-giving of Torah. The Shabbat of Pesach, falling at the very beginning of this counting-process, is the point at which the Atzmut's irruption (Pesach) is given a graduated structure for human absorption (the Omer count). The Shabbat-frame provides the menucha (rest, integration-point) within which the Pesach-Or begins to be absorbed into the counting-process.

### What It Gets To

The maamar establishes that the highest revelatory events (Pesach, resurrection, the Atzmut's irruption) are not merely more intense versions of the graduated Or Yashar but are qualitatively different in kind — they operate from a divine dimension that stands entirely outside the tzimtzum-governed world. The Shabbat of Pesach is the annual experience point at which this qualitatively different Or becomes accessible through the double-Sovev of Shabbat-plus-Pesach.

### How This Fits the Hemshech

The Shabbat Shel Pesach maamar introduces the Atzmut as a third term beyond Or Yashar and Or Chozer — a dimension of G-d's being that is not even the Encompassing but the absolutely transcendent. This will become crucial in subsequent maamarim as the hemshech works toward its ultimate claim: that the goal of creation (dira betachtonim) is not merely the dwelling of the Sovev below but the manifestation of the Atzmut itself in the lower world — which requires the full circuit of Or Yashar, Or Chozer, and finally the Atzmut's own irruption through acts like the exodus and the future redemption.
