## Context

This maamar was delivered during **2nd day of Rosh Hashana 1903-04** in the year **תרד"ע** (1903–04), as part of the ongoing hemshech (continuous discourse) that Rebbe Shalom DovBer of Lubavitch (the Rashab) delivered over two decades. It forms part of **חלק ראשון (Part 1)** of the hemshech.

The Hemshech Taarav is the longest chassidic discourse ever composed, spanning from 5662 (1901-02) through 5680 (1919-20). Each maamar continues the thread of the previous, building a comprehensive Kabbalistic-Chassidic system centered on the nature of divine light, the structure of the sefirot, and the mission of the Jewish soul.

## Deep Dive

### The Construct Being Built

This maamar builds the Kabbalistic account of Keter as the true *ממוצע* (intermediary) between the Emanator and the emanated, specifically by resolving the paradox of how a single entity can bridge the absolute ontological gap between *etzmus ha-maatzil* (the essence of the Emanator) and *hanetzalim* (the emanated sefirot). The resolution hinges on the unique structure of Keter: its upper dimension is genuinely of the Emanator's being, while its lower dimension is genuinely of the sefirot's register — yet these two dimensions coexist within a single "level" called Keter precisely because they are united by the specific mode of *ממוצע* that neither level alone could provide.

### The Opening Move

The Zohar on Parashat Beshalach asks: "Shir hama'alot mimaamakim — from the depths I call You, YHVH." This psalm is prescribed for recitation during the Ten Days of Repentance (*Aseret Yemei Teshuva*), but the Zohar notes that it is attributed to no individual ("Shir hama'alot" without specifying who said it) because *kol bnei alma zemin lemeimar* — all beings in the world will eventually say it. The Zohar explains that *mimaamakim* refers to praying from the *ikara de'kol ikarin* — "the root of all roots." The maamar asks: what is the difference between *b'khol libi derashtikha* ("with all my heart I sought You," which Psalm 119 attributes to David) and *mimaamakim* ("from the depths")? If David prayed with his whole heart, what more could "from the depths" add?

### The Argumentative Arc

- **The paradox of Keter as intermediary**: The maamar begins by acknowledging the problem with calling Keter the *ממוצע*: Keter's upper dimension (Atik Yomin/Keter she'b'Keter) is genuinely divine essence, while Keter's lower dimension (Arich Anpin/Chokhmah sheb'Keter) is genuinely the root of the sefirot. But these two dimensions are themselves in an *ein aroch* (incomparable) relationship to each other — so Keter is internally divided by the same unbridgeable gap it was supposed to bridge. How can this work?

- **The resolution through third-category ontology**: The Rashab's answer is that a true *ממוצע* cannot simply be a composite of the two extremes; it must be a third category (*madrega acheret*) that participates genuinely in both without being reducible to either. In Keter this is achieved: Keter's upper dimension, while of the divine essence, is not the etzmus itself (it is described as *hinatkat ha-atzmus* — "a displacement of the essence"); and Keter's lower dimension, while the root of the sefirot, is not the sefirot themselves. This double "not-quite" is what makes Keter the genuine intermediary — it bridges through participatory incompleteness from both sides.

- **Keter's two modes of counting**: This explains a known paradox: sometimes Keter is counted in the ten sefirot, sometimes it is not. When counted, it is counted from its lower dimension's affiliation with the sefirot; when not counted, it is from its upper dimension's affiliation with the divine essence. A single entity participating in both counting-systems precisely enacts the intermediary function.

- **"With all my heart" vs. "from the depths"**: *B'khol libi* — praying with the full capacity of the conscious emotional core — is a powerful human act but remains within the registered capacities of the soul's *orot megunlim* (revealed lights). *Mimaamakim* — from the depths — corresponds to the *mekorot kol meorin* (the root of all roots), which is the *Yechidah*, the highest soul-level that is bound directly to Keter/Atik and is not accessible through ordinary emotional or intellectual prayer. The *eitan* (root/depth) from which Rosh Hashanah prayer draws is precisely this level of soul that ordinarily lies beyond the reach of the praying self.

- **Why all beings will say this psalm**: The psalm being destined for all beings (not just Israel) reflects the universal dimension of the Keter-mediation: when Keter's function as intermediary is fully revealed in the messianic future, the connection between all existence and the divine source will be transparent at every level of reality, not just the Jewish soul's relationship to God.

### What It Gets To

The maamar arrives at a precise account of why Rosh Hashanah prayer draws from a depth inaccessible at other times of year: the Yamim Noraim create the conditions for the *Yechidah* soul-level — normally beyond the range of articulated prayer — to become the operative register of divine service. When prayer draws from this depth, it reaches the corresponding divine level: Keter/Atik, the *ikkara de'kol ikkarin*, the root of all roots. This is the maximum possible Or Chozer from the human side, and the divine response is the maximum possible Or Yashar: a renewal of Malkhut from Keter's highest depth.

### How This Fits the Hemshech

This is one of the hemshech's most technically sophisticated maamarim on Keter as intermediary. It advances the hemshech's central metaphysical project by resolving the most acute version of the Ein Sof-sefirot gap problem: not just asking how the infinite relates to the finite, but how a single entity (Keter) can genuinely bridge entities that are in *ain aroch* (no-comparison) relationship even to each other. The *mimaamakim* framework then gives this metaphysics immediate devotional application: the "depth" of prayer on Rosh Hashanah is the Yechidah accessing the Keter-level, completing the Or Chozer circuit at its most transcendent point.
