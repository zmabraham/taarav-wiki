## Context

This maamar was delivered during **Parshat Teruma 1909-10** in the year **העת"ר** (1909–10), as part of the ongoing hemshech (continuous discourse) that Rebbe Shalom DovBer of Lubavitch (the Rashab) delivered over two decades. It forms part of **חלק שני (Part 2)** of the hemshech.

The Hemshech Taarav is the longest chassidic discourse ever composed, spanning from 5662 (1901-02) through 5680 (1919-20). Each maamar continues the thread of the previous, building a comprehensive Kabbalistic-Chassidic system centered on the nature of divine light, the structure of the sefirot, and the mission of the Jewish soul.

**Central Concept**: This maamar continues the hemshech's development of the relationship between Or Yashar (direct divine light) and Or Chozer (reflected light), applying it to the specific occasion of Parshat Teruma 1909-10. The Rashab develops the Kabbalistic framework of Sefirot and Partzufim to illuminate the Torah's discussion of the relevant theme.

**Resolution**: The chiddush reached in this maamar shows how the specific quality of divine service associated with ש"פ תרומה, העת"ר corresponds to a specific aspect of the Or Chozer dynamic — revealing how the human soul's action in the lowest world (Asiyah) draws divine light from the highest Keter-level, thereby advancing the hemshech's central theme of divine habitation in the lower realms (dira betachtonim).

## Deep Dive

### The Construct Being Built

This maamar constructs the critical fourfold taxonomy of Orot, Kelim, Levushim, and Heikhalot (lights, vessels, garments, palaces), distinguishing the two types of relationship an Or can have with the reality it enters — either unifying with it (as Kli) or inhabiting it without merging (as Levush) — and showing how this distinction maps onto the difference between Atzilut and the worlds of Beriah-Yetzirah-Asiyah.

### The Opening Move

The maamar opens from a precise textual anomaly in Shemot 25:1-3: G-d issues three separate formulations — "they shall take for Me a teruma" (ויקחו לי תרומה), "from every person whose heart impels him you shall take My teruma" (מאת כל אשר ידבנו לבו תקחו את תרומתי), and then "this is the teruma" (וזאת התרומה). Three distinct instances of teruma, each phrased differently: one is spoken about in the third person (ויקחו), one in the second person (תקחו), and one with the demonstrative "this." Additionally, the middle formulation introduces voluntary impulse (ידבנו לבו — whose heart impels him) despite the fact that the half-shekel levy was obligatory, not voluntary. A further puzzle: the Midrash Rabbah connects the verse to "for I gave you a good acquisition" (כי לקח טוב נתתי לכם — Proverbs 4:2) and explains that when G-d sold the Torah to Israel, He was, as it were, sold with it — "take for Me" means "take Me." What is the connection between Torah and teruma, and how does Torah-acquisition entail G-d's own presence?

### The Argumentative Arc

- **The fourfold schema introduced**: The Rashab recalls the framework established in prior maamarim: there are four grades — Orot (lights), Kelim (vessels), Levushim (garments), and Heikhalot (palaces). The Kli relationship is defined by unification: the vessel receives the Or and, through that reception, shapes and reveals it — the vessel's pnimiyut (inner face) becomes one with the Or, creating form. The Levush relationship is one of functional enclosure without identity: the Or inhabits the garment to produce effects beyond its own essential nature, while remaining concealed rather than revealed.
- **The Atzilut/BYA mapping**: In Atzilut, because the world is structured as unity (achdut), the relationship of Or to its medium is properly Kli — the medium genuinely receives, unifies with, and reveals the Or. In the worlds of Beriah-Yetzirah-Asiyah, the media are structurally of the nature of Levushim — the Or inhabits them but does not merge; it acts through them without being revealed by them.
- **Interior vs. exterior of the Kli**: Within Atzilut itself there is a sub-distinction. The pnimiyut (inner face) of the Kli is what unifies with and reveals the Or — this is the Kli proper. The chitzoniyut (outer face) of the Kli does not receive or reveal the Or per se but channels the Or's power outward to act upon what is beyond the Kli's own essence. This outer face is, in a precise technical sense, a Levush even within Atzilut. It is through this outer face that the Sefirot extend into one another (hishtalshelut) and that Partzufim encloth one another.
- **The three terumot decoded**: The three terumot correspond to the three modes of divine giving at three levels. The first (ויקחו לי — "take Me") is the Torah itself — the Or that, when genuinely received, brings G-d's own presence, because Torah-study at its deepest is the pnimiyut-Kli relationship: the soul unifies with the Or and G-d is, as it were, "taken along." The second (מאת כל אשר ידבנו לבו — from the one whose heart impels him) is the voluntary material offering — Or operating through the chitzoniyut of the Kli, channeling divine energy through the will-impulse (ידבנו לבו) of the individual, which is why it can be voluntary: it depends on whether the heart's outer face is open. The third (וזאת התרומה — this is the teruma) is the material specification of Mishkan construction materials — the Or operating fully in the mode of Levush, acting through matter to build the divine dwelling.
- **Why ויקחו (third person) then תקחו (second person)**: The shift from third person (as if speaking about Israel from outside) to second person (addressing them directly) mirrors the shift from Or Yashar (which descends from a vantage that is above and outside the recipient) to Or Chozer (in which the divine address turns to face the human directly). The third-person phrasing signals the Or's originating transcendence; the second-person signals the Or's entry into direct relation.
- **The Midrash's chiddush — Torah as G-d's self-sale**: The Midrash's claim that G-d was "sold along with" the Torah is not metaphor but ontological claim. Torah belongs to the category of pnimiyut-Kli: a vessel so perfectly attuned to the Or that the Or cannot be separated from it. In all other terumot (chitzoniyut-Kli or Levush), the Or can be distinguished from its medium. In Torah, the Or and its vehicle are identical — hence "ויקחו לי," take Me.

### What It Gets To

The maamar arrives at a precise structural conclusion: the three terumot are not simply three collections but three modalities of divine self-giving, corresponding to the inner Kli (Torah), the outer Kli (voluntary heart-impulse), and the Levush (material Mishkan construction). This establishes that the Mishkan's construction is not a "descent" of holiness into matter but the third term in a triadic light-vessel-garment structure, each term necessary to the others.

### How This Fits the Hemshech

This maamar is a pivotal structural chapter within Part 2 of Taarav. The fourfold Orot/Kelim/Levushim/Heikhalot taxonomy, and especially the inner/outer Kli distinction, becomes foundational equipment for everything the hemshech does subsequently when analyzing the Mishkan, the Partzufim, and the mechanism by which Or Yashar and Or Chozer actually meet in the physical world. The identification of Torah as pnimiyut-Kli (carrying G-d's presence intrinsically) will reappear wherever the hemshech treats Torah-study as itself a cosmological act rather than merely a religious practice.
