## Context

This maamar was delivered during **Parshat Ki Tetze 1901-02** in the year **תער"ב** (1901–02), as part of the ongoing hemshech (continuous discourse) that Rebbe Shalom DovBer of Lubavitch (the Rashab) delivered over two decades. It forms part of **חלק ראשון (Part 1)** of the hemshech.

The Hemshech Taarav is the longest chassidic discourse ever composed, spanning from 5662 (1901-02) through 5680 (1919-20). Each maamar continues the thread of the previous, building a comprehensive Kabbalistic-Chassidic system centered on the nature of divine light, the structure of the sefirot, and the mission of the Jewish soul.

**Central Concept**: This maamar continues the hemshech's development of the relationship between Or Yashar (direct divine light) and Or Chozer (reflected light), applying it to the specific occasion of Parshat Ki Tetze 1901-02. **Resolution**: The chiddush reached in this maamar shows how the specific quality of divine service associated with ש"פ תצא, תער"ב corresponds to a specific aspect of the Or Chozer dynamic — revealing how the human soul's action in the lowest world (Asiyah) draws divine light from the highest Keter-level, thereby advancing the hemshech's central theme of divine habitation in the lower realms (dira betachtonim).

## Deep Dive

### The Construct Being Built

This maamar constructs the foundational distinction between *or* (light) and *shefa* (effluence/flow) as two categorically different modes of divine self-extension — and then leverages that distinction to explain the paradox of *Ki Tetze*: how the warrior who "goes out" from a position of inner dwelling gains power precisely through the going-out. The kabbalistic edifice is the theory of Or Mekif (encompassing light) as the source of all inner-world vitality, which will anchor the hemshech's argument for the next many maamarim.

### The Opening Move

The maamar opens with a textual-grammatical puzzle: why does Deuteronomy 20:1 use the phrase *ki tetze* ("when you go out") to war, rather than *ki telech* ("when you go")? And why does the verse simultaneously attribute victory to God (*"and the Lord your God gives them into your hands"*) while also describing Israel going out under their own initiative? The Midrash Rabbah's gloss is cited: *"ein omrim 'tze' ela le-mi she-sharuya bi-fnim"* — one says "go out" only to someone who dwells inside. This locates the paradox: the power to fight *outside* derives from habitation *inside*. The entry point matters because it maps precisely onto the Or Mekif / Or Pnimi distinction the hemshech has been building since its opening maamarim.

### The Argumentative Arc

- **Or vs. Shefa distinguished**: The maamar revisits the distinction established in earlier chapters (perakim): *shefa* (effluence) involves the actual transmission of a thing's substance — the giver is "seized" (*tafisa*) in the relationship because something of their essence flows into the receiver. *Or* (light), by contrast, is purely a revelation of the source (*giluy min ha-atzem*) — it does not constitute a new entity but only makes the source present. Because it is not substantive, it cannot be "grasped" (*eino b'vehinat tafisa ve-hitlabshut*).

- **Or Mekif's paradox**: Though Or Mekif (surrounding/encompassing light) does not *clothe* itself within worlds, it nonetheless *acts* within them — it is the very root of all revelation and all vital increase. The source text explicitly states: *"kol atzam mekor ha-hit'havut d'khol ha-olamot hu mi-bhinat or ha-makif"* — the prime source of the existence of all worlds is from the encompassing light. This overturns any assumption that "surrounding" means "inactive."

- **Or Mekif and Or Pnimi in the chain**: Or Pnimi (inner light) distributes itself differentially across the levels of the four worlds (ABYA), diminishing with each descent. Or Mekif, by contrast, surrounds all worlds equally (*mekif lakol b'shaveh*) — from the highest level of Adam Kadmon down to the lowest reality of Asiyah. This "equality" of the encompassing light is the sign of its belonging to an altogether different register than the inner-world gradient.

- **The Kitzur formulation**: The source's kitzur (summary) crystallizes the point: Or Mekif acts within the worlds not through *hitlabshut* (garbing/investment) but through *makif* (encompassing) — it is the origin of all inner light and all vital increase, yet it itself never enters the *kli* (vessel). Its action in the world is precisely *because* it does not become embedded.

- **Applied to Ki Tetze**: The one who "goes out to war" is one who *dwells inside* (in the Or Pnimi context of the inner divine dwelling). The "going out" — the encounter with external opposition — is enabled and empowered by the surrounding Or Mekif. Just as the Or Mekif produces all inner vitality without itself descending into vessels, the warrior who has internalized the divine *pnim* can engage the external domain of *kinim* (opponents in the world of Asiyah) and prevail precisely because the power derives from above and beyond the battlefield.

### What It Gets To

By the end of this maamar the hemshech has secured a critical structural claim: Or Mekif is not merely an abstract ontological category but the active *source* of all creative vitality in the worlds, while simultaneously remaining transcendent to them. This resolves the verse's paradox — divine victory accompanies human initiative not despite God's transcendence but *because* of it. The mode of divine involvement when the Jew "goes out" is Or Mekif, which is unlimited and operates equally at every level.

### How This Fits the Hemshech

Earlier maamarim established the distinction between Or Mekif and Or Pnimi at the level of abstract definition. This maamar makes the first systematic move toward showing their *functional* relationship — demonstrating that the encompassing light is not merely "outside" but is the very generative root of the inner light. This argument will be the fulcrum on which the hemshech later builds its account of how Tzimtzum relates to Or Ein Sof, and how the soul's highest dimension (Yechida, associated with Or Mekif) is the true source of all its lower powers.
