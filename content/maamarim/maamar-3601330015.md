## Context

This maamar was delivered during **Parshat Va'etchanan 1901-02** in the year **תער"ב** (1901–02), as part of the ongoing hemshech (continuous discourse) that Rebbe Shalom DovBer of Lubavitch (the Rashab) delivered over two decades. It forms part of **חלק ראשון (Part 1)** of the hemshech.

The Hemshech Taarav is the longest chassidic discourse ever composed, spanning from 5662 (1901-02) through 5680 (1919-20). Each maamar continues the thread of the previous, building a comprehensive Kabbalistic-Chassidic system centered on the nature of divine light, the structure of the sefirot, and the mission of the Jewish soul.

## Deep Dive

### The Construct Being Built

This maamar, delivered on Shabbat Nachamu (the Shabbat of Consolation following Tisha B'Av), builds the relationship between *Ahavat Yisrael* (God's love for Israel) and the structural dynamic of Or Chozer — establishing that the Jewish soul's capacity to elicit divine consolation and redemption is rooted in the soul's own Or Chozer nature as the *behinah* (dimension) of Malkhut that ascends back to Keter, and that the Shema's declaration of divine unity is the concentrated expression of this Or Chozer return.

### The Opening Move

Parshat Va'etchanan contains the *Shema* (Deuteronomy 6:4) — "Hear O Israel, the Lord is our God, the Lord is One" — and the first paragraph of the Shema. The maamar's entry is likely the question Moses's supplication (Va'etchanan: "I pleaded with God") raises about the relationship between prayer as Or Chozer and divine response as Or Yashar: Moses pleaded 515 times (the numerical value of va'etchanan) to enter the Land, and God declined. Yet the chapter of Va'etchanan is followed immediately by the great consolations and declarations of divine love. Why does the highest Or Chozer prayer (Moses's) not produce the expected result? And what does the Shema's declaration of unity have to do with the Or Yashar/Or Chozer dynamic?

### The Argumentative Arc

- **The Shema as Or Chozer declaration**: the Shema's proclamation that "the Lord is One" (*Hashem Echad*) is analyzed not merely as theological assertion but as a structural act: it is the soul's declaration that the Or Chozer it sends upward — its total orientation toward the divine — unifies the multiplicity of divine light-expressions (the Sefirot, the worlds, the Names) back into their single Source. This is the *yichud elyon* (upper unification) — Or Chozer at the level of Chochmah, returning the many to the One.

- **The distinction between Yichud Elyon and Yichud Tachton**: *Yichud Elyon* (the upper unification — "the Lord is our God") corresponds to the Or Chozer that ascends from the world back through the Sefirot to Keter; *Yichud Tachton* (the lower unification — "the Lord is One") corresponds to the recognition that even the material world contains no other power — the *ein od milvado* (there is nothing besides Him) which the soul proclaims in the second clause. Together they form the complete Or Chozer circuit: from below (material world) through all intermediaries to the single Ein Sof source.

- **The six-letter Shema and the six directions**: the tradition of elongating the *dalet* of *Echad* to the count of six is interpreted through the six Sefirot of Zer Anpin (Chesed through Yesod), which correspond to the six spatial directions. The Or Chozer of the Shema declaration traverses all six directions — unifying the divine presence in all spatial directions of the created world and returning them to their single source. This is Or Chozer operating not just vertically (from below to above) but comprehensively through all dimensions of creation.

- **Va'etchanan (Moses's 515 prayers) and the Or Chozer limit**: the maamar addresses why even the most concentrated Or Chozer (Moses's 515-prayer ascent) did not achieve the desired result (entry to the Land). The principle: Or Chozer reaches as high as Or Yashar has first descended. Moses's level — aspaklaria ha-me'irah — corresponded to the Or Yashar of Chochmah; his Or Chozer was the most powerful human Or Chozer possible. But the entry into the Land required a *further descent* of Or Yashar into the physical domain (dira betachtonim) that had not yet been enabled — and that enabling would come through the entire people's service in the Land rather than through Moses alone. The Or Chozer cannot precede the Or Yashar that will meet it.

- **The consolation of Shabbat Nachamu**: the maamar engages the haftarah's opening "Nachamu nachamu ami" — "Be consoled, be consoled My people" — as the divine Or Yashar that descends in response to the Or Chozer of Israel's suffering and return. The double language (nachamu nachamu, consoled consoled) corresponds to the two dimensions of the consolation: the Or Yashar restoration of the outer light that was withdrawn at the Temple's destruction, and the Or Mekif revelation of a level of divine light that was not present even when the Temple stood — the hidden light of Geulah (Redemption) that is higher than the Temple's Or Pnimi.

### What It Gets To

The maamar establishes that the Shema is the paradigmatic Or Chozer act of the Jewish soul — the daily return of the many to the One — and that it operates through both the yichud elyon (upper unification via the first clause) and yichud tachton (lower unification via the second clause) to complete a comprehensive Or Chozer circuit through all levels of reality. Moses's prayer establishes the limit of Or Chozer: it cannot exceed the level of Or Yashar that has been established to meet it.

### How This Fits the Hemshech

Following the Devarim maamar's analysis of Moses as Or Yashar conduit, this maamar introduces the Or Chozer dimension of the human soul's divine service through the Shema. The hemshech thus establishes, by the Nachamu maamarim, the complete Or Yashar/Or Chozer circuit as it operates through human consciousness: Or Yashar descends through Moses/Chochmah into Torah; Or Chozer ascends through the Shema declaration, seeking to return the many to the One. The circuit is not yet complete (Geulah has not come) but its structure is now fully articulated.
