## Context

This maamar was delivered during **Parshat Vayera 1903-04** in the year **תרד"ע** (1903–04), as part of the ongoing hemshech (continuous discourse) that Rebbe Shalom DovBer of Lubavitch (the Rashab) delivered over two decades. It forms part of **חלק ראשון (Part 1)** of the hemshech.

The Hemshech Taarav is the longest chassidic discourse ever composed, spanning from 5662 (1901-02) through 5680 (1919-20). Each maamar continues the thread of the previous, building a comprehensive Kabbalistic-Chassidic system centered on the nature of divine light, the structure of the sefirot, and the mission of the Jewish soul.

## Deep Dive

### The Construct Being Built

This maamar builds the most precise account thus far of *Chokhmah sheb'Keter* (the wisdom-dimension of Keter, also called *Chokhmah Stimaah*/hidden wisdom) as the true *ממוצע* (ontological intermediary) between the Emanator and the emanated sefirot — by resolving the structural paradox of how Chokhmah she'b'Keter (abbreviated *Ch"S*) can simultaneously be: (a) the source/root of *Chokhmah d'Atzilut*, and (b) utterly concealed from and beyond the level of Chokhmah d'Atzilut. The resolution involves distinguishing between the *chitzoniut* (outer dimension) of Ch"S — which connects to Chokhmah d'Atzilut through the *shearot* (hairs/filaments) of *mazzal* — and the *penimiyut* (inner dimension) of Ch"S — which contains the full being of *penimiyut Atik* and is accessed only through *Or Chozer d'teshuvah*.

### The Opening Move

The verse: "And YHVH said: Shall I conceal from Abraham what I am doing? For Abraham will surely become a great and mighty nation." The Rashab opens with the sharpest possible paradox: God says *asher ani oseh* — "what I am doing." The word *asiyah* (doing/making) implies *revelation*, since every act of making is a disclosure of something from potential to actual. How can there be concealment (*hakisui*) of something that is inherently revelatory? And the Midrash adds: "God gave the world to Abraham" — this is somehow implied by "shall I conceal from Abraham?" What is the connection between concealment and being given the world?

### The Argumentative Arc

- **The two types of concealment in Ch"S**: The maamar establishes that Chokhmah sheb'Keter has two distinct types of inner hiddenness (*histarus*). The first type: *hasatum raq l'gabei hamekablim* — concealed only relative to the receivers, because their vessels are too small; but in principle the light is *b'erech hamekablim* (in a relatable register to them) and could in principle be received if their vessels were enlarged. This is a relative, receiver-dependent concealment. The second type: *hasatum b'atzmo* — the concealment is intrinsic to the source itself, not dependent on the receivers' limitations; it is the *he'elem ha'atzmus* (the essential concealment) — the light is intrinsically beyond the category of revelation to any vessel, ever.

- **The mem-samech analogy from the Zohar**: The Zohar (Parshat Teruma) uses the forms of the Hebrew letters *mem* and *samech* to illustrate these two types. The closed *mem* (מ) is sealed in a *marbua* (square) — it has corners, indicating that the concealment still has some *achizah* (grip, relation) to the rectangular space of disclosed existence. The closed *samech* (ס) is sealed in an *igul* (circle) — it has no corners, no grip, no edge that touches the rectangular world of defined existence. The samech-type concealment is the *he'elem ha'atzmus* — perfectly round, perfectly self-contained, touching nothing outside itself.

- **Ch"S as genuine intermediary**: The prior maamarim established that the full Keter (Atik + Arich) cannot function as a true intermediary because Atik is too utterly beyond the sefirot and Arich is too clearly of the sefirot's register. Now the Rashab refines: the true intermediary is specifically *Ch"S* because it is genuinely of the category of *Chokhmah* (which is the sefirot's register) while being simultaneously concealed in its essence. It is *Chokhmah* — yet *Chokhmah d'lo ityada* (Chokhmah that is unknowable). This double-nature — "it is wisdom, yet entirely hidden wisdom" — is the precise structure that enables mediation: it shares the *mahut* (essence-type) of the sefirot (it is Chokhmah) while containing the *atzmus* (being) of the Emanator (through penimiyut Atik embedded in it).

- **Chitzoniut Ch"S — connecting through the shearot**: The outer dimension of Ch"S connects to Chokhmah d'Atzilut through a mode called *shearot* (hairs/filaments): the Zohar teaches *Abba yonekh mimazzala* — the "father" (Chokhmah d'Atzilut) draws sustenance from the *mazzal* (the 13th tikun of the beard, *Mazzal v'Notzer Chesed*). This mode of transmission is by *dilug* (skipping/leaping) — not a graduated descent through the chain but a non-hierarchical transfer. Because the transmission is by dilug, Chokhmah d'Atzilut comes into existence as a *metzia* (found thing, as if discovered), utterly unaware of its source. This explains the verse "and Chokhmah — from *ayin* shall it be found (*timmatzei*)": *ayin* is both the "nothingness" of Keter and the mode of discovery (*metziah*) — found as if from nowhere.

- **Penimiyut Ch"S and Or Chozer**: The inner dimension of Ch"S — *penimiyut Atik* embedded there — is entirely inaccessible through the downward channel of Or Yashar. It can only be reached through the *Or Chozer* of genuine teshuvah, which is the *ohr hahozer* that ascends from below through the sefirotic chain and, by passing through the *chitzoniut* of Ch"S, is drawn upward into the *penimiyut* Ch"S. This is why the Zohar says the 13 Attributes of Mercy emanate from *Moha* (the brain/Mo"s of Keter): these attributes, which are the divine response to teshuvah, come from *penimiyut* Ch"S, not from the external dimensions of the divine system.

- **"Giving the world to Abraham"**: The Midrash's statement that God gave the world to Abraham is now explained: Abraham's *mesirut nefesh* in the furnace and his total dedication to God's will (*nitziv al ahavato, standing firm in his love*) generated the maximum possible Or Chozer — reaching into *penimiyut* Ch"S. This gave Abraham a rootedness in the deepest level of divine being, which then flowed outward as his ownership of the world: one whose root is in *penimiyut* Ch"S has access to the source from which all worlds are sustained, and in that sense the world "belongs" to him as his inheritance.

- **Concealment and the koach hayeridah**: The paradox of concealment-within-disclosure (*hakisui*/*asiyah*) is resolved: "what I am doing" (*asher ani oseh*) refers to the Or Yashar mode of disclosure through the sefirotic chain — but "shall I conceal from Abraham" (*hamekhashe ani m'Avraham*) refers to the *penimiyut* Ch"S that is intrinsically concealed even within what is being made. The act of creation (asiyah) is the disclosure of Or Yashar; but within that disclosure, the *penimiyut* remains concealed — it is the *sod YHVH l'yereiav* (the secret of God for those who fear Him) that Abraham's unique relationship to God allows him to access.

### What It Gets To

The maamar arrives at the definitive formulation of Ch"S's dual structure and its role as the true ontological bridge between Ein Sof and the sefirot. Its outer dimension (chitzoniut) enables Chokhmah d'Atzilut to come into existence at all, through the non-hierarchical filament of *mazzal*. Its inner dimension (penimiyut) is the site where *penimiyut Atik* — the most transcendent divine being accessible through any created pathway — is embedded, and it is this inner dimension that teshuvah/Or Chozer uniquely reaches. Abraham's relationship to God is paradigmatic because it reaches both dimensions: through his ongoing service (outer Ch"S, shearot) and through his self-sacrifice (inner Ch"S, Or Chozer). His "ownership of the world" is the natural consequence of being rooted at the source that sustains all worlds.

### How This Fits the Hemshech

This maamar is a culminating synthesis for this phase of the hemshech. It pulls together: (1) the Keter-as-intermediary analysis from the Rosh Hashanah maamarim; (2) the korban/ketoret distinction from Sukkot; (3) the Noach maamar's account of Or Makif from birur nitzotzot; (4) the Lech Lecha analysis of koach hahafla'ah/koach hayeridah — and integrates them all into the precise account of Ch"S's chitzoniut/penimiyut structure. Having established this, the hemshech can now proceed to the extended analysis of how human souls are rooted in Ch"S and what this implies for the nature and possibility of the *dira betachtonim*.
