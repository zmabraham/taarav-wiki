## Context

This maamar was delivered during **Parshat Ki Tavo 1903-04** in the year **תרד"ע** (1903–04), as part of the ongoing hemshech (continuous discourse) that Rebbe Shalom DovBer of Lubavitch (the Rashab) delivered over two decades. It forms part of **חלק ראשון (Part 1)** of the hemshech.

The Hemshech Taarav is the longest chassidic discourse ever composed, spanning from 5662 (1901-02) through 5680 (1919-20). Each maamar continues the thread of the previous, building a comprehensive Kabbalistic-Chassidic system centered on the nature of divine light, the structure of the sefirot, and the mission of the Jewish soul.

## Deep Dive

The maamar opens from the verse אני לדודי ודודי לי הרועה בשושנים (Shir HaShirim 6:3), noting its well-known Roshei Teivot for Elul. But the Rashab immediately presses a double question: why does an earlier verse state דודי לי ואני לו — placing the Beloved first — while here אני precedes? And what precisely is meant by הרועה בשושנים in each occurrence? These are the opening kushyot that drive the entire discourse.

To answer, the maamar draws on what it explicitly labels as having been taught previously (מה שנת"ל): the union of אור וכלי (light and vessel) requires that they exchange their natures. By essence, אור is אין and כלי is יש — opposites that cannot interface. The solution is that the אור moves מאין ליש (descends into manifest being) and the כלי moves מיש לאין (surrenders its own substantiality). The maamar maps this onto human divine service: נשמה is מ"ה (the divine name associated with אין and elevation) while גוף and נפש הבהמית are ב"ן (associated with יש and descent).

The same polarity governs תורה ותפלה. Torah is מ"ה, characterized by המשכה מלמעלמ"ט — drawing down from above — and its Bitul is ביטול עצמי, a self-nullification that is absolute because the light itself comes from on high. Prayer is ב"ן, characterized by העלאה מלמטה למעלה — ascent from below — and its Bitul is ביטול היש alone, the creature's own self-effort. The maamar traces the entire seder HaTefillah as a progression from below upward: מודה אני as הודאה כללית without any השגה, through פסוקי דזמרה which cuts the קוצים of the נפש הבהמית, through ברכת יוצר and ק"ש with its יחוד, arriving finally at שמונה עשרה as ביטול במציאות.

The chiddush: because prayer is purely the creature's own ascent, even its highest level — ביטול במציאות — is still technically only ביטול היש. Torah, flowing from above, generates ביטול עצמי and even elevates the ביטול היש it produces into ביטול עצמי.

With this, the opening questions resolve. דודי לי ואני לו — Beloved first — corresponds to Nissan and Yetziat Mitzrayim, where the divine initiative (אתעדל"ע) preceded Israel's readiness. אני לדודי — Israel first — corresponds to Tishrei and Elul, the season of teshuvah, where the human ascent (אתעדל"ת) precedes and draws down the divine response. The phrase הרועה בשושנים — שושנה being the כנסת ישראל/מלכות — refers in each case to the illumination of Malchut that follows: in one instance through Torah's downward draw, in the other through the teshuvah of Elul that reveals מלכות in its שורש ומקור העצמי, as expressed in the phrase ממשיכים בחי' התגלות המל' משרשה ומקורה בבחי' עצמות א"ס ב"ה.
