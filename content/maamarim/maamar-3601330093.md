## Context

This maamar was delivered during **Parshat Vayeshev 1909-10** in the year **העת"ר** (1909–10), as part of the ongoing hemshech (continuous discourse) that Rebbe Shalom DovBer of Lubavitch (the Rashab) delivered over two decades. It forms part of **חלק שני (Part 2)** of the hemshech.

The Hemshech Taarav is the longest chassidic discourse ever composed, spanning from 5662 (1901-02) through 5680 (1919-20). Each maamar continues the thread of the previous, building a comprehensive Kabbalistic-Chassidic system centered on the nature of divine light, the structure of the sefirot, and the mission of the Jewish soul.

## Deep Dive

### Opening Kushya

The maamar opens with a cluster of textual difficulties on the verse "וישב יעקב בארץ מגורי אביו בארץ כנען." The Rashab asks: we already know from the preceding pasuk "ויבא יעקב אל יצחק אביו" that Yaakov arrived home — so why does the Torah repeat "וישב יעקב"? Why are there two uses of "ארץ" in one verse? And why add "בארץ כנען" after "מגורי אביו" makes the location obvious? Most pointedly: "מגורי" implies the language of גירות (temporary sojourning), as in "אשר גר שם אברהם ויצחק" — yet "וישב" denotes permanent settlement (התיישבות), which is the opposite of גירות. He adds the Midrash's remark that "בקש יעקב לישב בשלוה" — Yaakov merely sought to dwell — which seems to contradict the plain meaning that he did dwell.

### Conceptual Argument: Two Modes of Drawing Down

The maamar uses this textual knot as the entry point for a major structural distinction that has been running through the hemshech: the difference between המשכה מלמטה למעלה (drawing from below upward, via the creature's effort) and המשכה מלמעלה למטה (drawing from above downward, from G-d's own initiative).

The first mode — למטלמ"ע — operates through הכנת הכלי: the vessel must be purified and prepared, and the light that descends is proportional to that preparation. The light itself "נתצמצם להיות בערך אל התחתון." This is illustrated by the mashal of a person climbing a mountain: he needs strength, knowledge of the hidden paths, and properly fitted garments, and he can only ascend as far as his own preparation carries him — reaching, at most, "עד שרשו ומקורו."

The second mode — מלמעלמ"ט — is an emanation that proceeds "מצ"ע," entirely from G-d's own will, unconditioned by the vessel's readiness: "ההמשכה היא שלא לפ"ע כלי המקבל כלל." This is the mode of Matan Torah, where the revelation "היה שלא ע"י העלאת מ"ן" and indeed the Jewish people were not yet prepared vessels for such light.

### Key Chassidic Concepts

- **ממלא כל עלמין / סובב כל עלמין (Mema"k / Sova"k)** — G-d's immanent light that fills creation in graduated measure vs. the transcendent light that surrounds all worlds equally. Gan Eden's revelation is from ממכ"ע, proportioned to the recipient; the revelation of תחיית המתים will be from סובב כל עלמין — עצמות א"ס — and therefore unconditioned.
- **רצוא ושוב** — the pulsating movement of the soul's ascent and return; the Avot served G-d through this mode alone, and their service reached only to "הארה לבד" ("ריחות היו").
- **אור פנימי / אור מקיף** — the distinction between light that dwells within vessels and light that transcends them; the revelation of תחה"מ will be "בבחי' אור פנימי" even though the vessels are not commensurate with the light.

### The Chiddush: Gan Eden vs. Resurrection

The Rashab's resolution hinges on this distinction. Gan Eden operates on the למטלמ"ע principle — hence "מי יעלה בהר ה'" requires "נקי כפים ובר לבב," ascent is gradual (ממדרגא לדרגא), and each level requires prior immersion in the Nahar DiNur (טבילה בנה"ד) to shed one's previous level. By contrast, תחיית המתים will be a revelation of עצמות א"ס that descends without regard to vessel-readiness — "ויהיה הגילוי לכל ישראל בנשמות בגופים הגם שאינם בגדר כלי."

### Resolution of the Peshat

"וישב יעקב" — the word ישיבה (settlement, rest) denotes the mode of תחיית המתים, where there are no further upward movements, only inner illumination. "בארץ מגורי אביו" — מגורי is read as לשון אוצר, a hidden treasure-chamber: the concealed עצמות א"ס that will be revealed in the future, called "מגורי אביו" because "לעתיד יאמרו ליצחק כי אתה אבינו." "בארץ כנען" refers to the revelation of גן עדן (מלכות). The Torah records both because Yaakov longed for both — but truly he only "sought" (בקש) the higher one, since that ultimate revelation "אמיתית הגילוי הזה יהי' לעתיד כו'."
