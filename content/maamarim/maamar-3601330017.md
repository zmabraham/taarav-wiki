## Context

This maamar was delivered during **Parshat Shoftim 1901-02** in the year **תער"ב** (1901–02), as part of the ongoing hemshech (continuous discourse) that Rebbe Shalom DovBer of Lubavitch (the Rashab) delivered over two decades. It forms part of **חלק ראשון (Part 1)** of the hemshech.

The Hemshech Taarav is the longest chassidic discourse ever composed, spanning from 5662 (1901-02) through 5680 (1919-20). Each maamar continues the thread of the previous, building a comprehensive Kabbalistic-Chassidic system centered on the nature of divine light, the structure of the sefirot, and the mission of the Jewish soul.

## Deep Dive

### The Construct Being Built

This maamar builds the relationship between *Tzaddik* (righteous judge) and *mishpat* (justice) as sefirot-structural categories — establishing that the Tzaddik (Yesod) is the conduit that channels the Or Yashar from the Mohin (Chochmah-Binah-Da'at) and Middot (Chesed-Gevurah-Tiferet) into the Malkhut (the domain of practical law and worldly reality), and that the judicial system (shoftim ve-shotrim — judges and officers) corresponds to the sefirot-structure through which Or Yashar achieves its most complete expression in the lower world prior to the full revelation of Geulah.

### The Opening Move

Parshat Shoftim opens with the commandment to appoint judges: "Judges and officers shall you appoint in all your cities... and they shall judge the people with righteous judgment" (Deuteronomy 16:18). The maamar's entry is the phrase *tzedek tzedek tirdof* — "Justice, justice shall you pursue" (16:20) — and the question: why the doubled *tzedek* (justice)? The Talmud and Zohar derive multiple laws from the doubling, but the maamar asks the structural question: what is the Kabbalistic import of "justice twice"? The doubling of tzedek hints at two levels of justice — one deriving from Or Yashar (the divine standard of justice, absolute) and one from Or Chozer (the human court's application of that standard below), and the maamar's task is to show how these two levels relate.

### The Argumentative Arc

- **Shoftim (judges) as Chochmah-Binah-Da'at (Mohin)**: the judges represent the intellective faculty — the capacity to understand a case, weigh its merits, and reach a principled conclusion. Mishpat (judgment/justice) emanating from the judges is Or Yashar at the level of Mohin: the divine light of truth and discernment flowing through the intellectual faculty of the Sefirot.

- **Shotrim (officers/enforcers) as the Middot (emotional Sefirot)**: the officers who carry out the judges' rulings represent the motivating, activating emotional faculty — they give the Mohin-judgment its force and application. Chesed and Gevurah (the poles of the emotional Sefirot) correspond to acquittal and condemnation; Tiferet corresponds to the balanced ruling that weighs both.

- **The Tzaddik (Yesod) as the conduit from Mohin+Middot to Malkhut**: the *tzaddik* of the verse *tzedek tzedek tirdof* is Yesod in its Kabbalistic resonance — the Sefirah that gathers the Or Yashar from all the Sefirot above it and channels it into Malkhut (the world of practical implementation). The doubling of *tzedek* corresponds to the Yesod's dual function: (1) receiving the Or Yashar from above (tzedek from the Mohin and Middot) and (2) transmitting it into the Malkhut below (tzedek as what Malkhut receives). The pursuit of justice (*tirdof*) is the Or Chozer aspect: the human judge's striving upward toward the divine standard.

- **The king as Malkhut**: the command to appoint a king (Deuteronomy 17:15) follows immediately from the judicial section, and the maamar reads this sequentially: judges (Mohin) → officers (Middot) → king (Malkhut) — a complete sefirot-chain of governance. The king is the comprehensive expression of Malkhut, the final recipient and practical embodiment of the Or Yashar chain from the top of the divine-human governance structure to its earthly expression. "Justice, justice shall you pursue" — the Tzaddik (Yesod/Moshe) is the one who pursues the transmission of justice from above to below.

- **The Levitical priests and prophets** (also legislated in Shoftim) correspond to the right column (Chesed, Kedushah) and the Or Mekif dimension — the prophetic voice is the Or Mekif that speaks from above the structured sefirot-chain to correct or supplement the Or Pnimi of the judicial system when necessary. The false prophet who misleads is the analog of Bilam — Or Chozer access through uncalibrated vessels — showing the maamar is in dialogue with the Balak maamar (no. 11).

- **Tzedek tzedek — the doubled justice and Geulah**: the two tzadeks of the verse are interpreted eschatologically: the first tzedek is the present-world justice administered through the sefirot-chain of shoftim-shotrim-melech; the second tzedek is the Geulah-era justice when Or Mekif descends fully into Or Pnimi — when the encompassing light of the Messianic revelation fills the vessels of the entire cosmic structure without diminishment. "Pursue justice" (tirdof) is the ongoing Or Chozer work that draws the second tzedek closer.

### What It Gets To

The maamar establishes that the commandment to appoint judges and officers is not merely civil governance but the institutionalization of the sefirot-chain of Or Yashar in the human social domain — Mohin (judges), Middot (officers), Malkhut (king), with the Tzaddik/Yesod as the conduit connecting them. The doubled *tzedek* points to the tension between present-world justice (Or Pnimi, partial) and Geulah-justice (Or Mekif, complete), and the verb *tirdof* (pursue) names the Or Chozer work of the Jewish people as the engine of the second tzedek's arrival.

### How This Fits the Hemshech

As the final maamar of Part 1 (first year of Taarav, spanning Shavuot through the early-Elul parashiyot), this maamar completes the hemshech's initial sweep by arriving at the theme that will dominate the discourse's later years: the relationship between the present-world Or Pnimi structure and the eschatological Or Mekif revelation. Having traversed the full sefirot-architecture from Keter's internal structure (maamarim 4-6) through the mechanics of Or Yashar descent (7-10) and the dynamics of Or Chozer ascent through human service (11-17), Part 1 closes with the promise built into the doubled *tzedek*: the complete revelation is being pursued, and the pursuit is itself already the beginning of the arrival.
