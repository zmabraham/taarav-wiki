## Context

This maamar was delivered during **Parshat Lech Lecha 1903-04** in the year **תרד"ע** (1903–04), as part of the ongoing hemshech (continuous discourse) that Rebbe Shalom DovBer of Lubavitch (the Rashab) delivered over two decades. It forms part of **חלק ראשון (Part 1)** of the hemshech.

The Hemshech Taarav is the longest chassidic discourse ever composed, spanning from 5662 (1901-02) through 5680 (1919-20). Each maamar continues the thread of the previous, building a comprehensive Kabbalistic-Chassidic system centered on the nature of divine light, the structure of the sefirot, and the mission of the Jewish soul.

## Deep Dive

### The Construct Being Built

This maamar builds a highly refined account of the two structural powers within *Chokhmah* (divine wisdom): the *koach hahafla'ah* (the power of abstraction/elevation) and the *koach hayeridah* (the power of descent/concretization). These two powers are shown to be internally interdependent (*da beda tali* — each depends on the other) in a precise dialectical relationship: the more deeply a thinker penetrates the essence of a concept through abstraction, the more powerfully that concept can be descended and expressed in concrete terms — and vice versa. The maamar then applies this bilateral-Chokhmah structure to the question of Abraham's spiritual transformation in Lech Lecha, and to the distinction between the inner incense-altar's ketoret and the Yom Kippur ketoret in the Holy of Holies.

### The Opening Move

The verse "Go for yourself (*lech lecha*) from your land and from your birthplace" presents the famous double-phrasing: the word *lech* (go) followed by *lecha* (for yourself). The plain reading adds "for yourself" as an intensifier or as a promise of personal benefit. But the Rashab notes: why not simply say "go from your land" (*lech mei'artzecha*)? The duplication signals a Kabbalistic doubling of movement — a going *outward* and a going *inward* simultaneously. The Midrash reinforces this through its analogy: Abraham was like a perfume bottle (*tzlochit shel apopalsemon*) sealed tightly and placed in a corner — its fragrance was not spreading. When it was moved (*taltul*), its fragrance spread. Why does movement release fragrance? And what does this have to do with the name's greatness (*shim'kha mitgadel*)?

### The Argumentative Arc

- **The two powers of Chokhmah established**: The maamar develops the two powers of Chokhmah that were introduced in earlier sections. The *koach hahafla'ah* (power of elevation/abstraction): the ability to grasp a concept in its utmost purity and depth, stripped of all concrete garbing — to apprehend the subject *in essence* rather than through its surface manifestations. The *koach hayeridah* (power of descent/expression): the ability to bring that essence down into concrete forms — parables, examples, simplified explanations — so that it can be received by minds operating at lower levels.

- **Their interdependence**: The critical insight is that these two powers are not opposed but mutually constituting. The depth of the descent depends on the height of the ascent: only one who has grasped the subject at its most abstract (koach hahafla'ah) can produce explanations that genuinely convey the subject's essential content rather than mere surface forms. Conversely, the depth of abstraction one can achieve is measured by the quality of descent one can generate — since the ability to truly clothe a concept in multiple different forms of garbing shows that one possesses the concept in its pre-garbed, essential form. This dialectic maps precisely onto the Or Yashar (descent/expression) and Or Chozer (ascent/abstraction) polarity, now applied within the faculty of Chokhmah itself.

- **Abraham's transformation as activation of Or Makif**: Abraham prior to Lech Lecha was the *apopalsemon* sealed in a corner — his Or (divine apprehension) was present but not diffusing outward because he remained in his native context of Ur Kasdim. The divine command *lech lecha* — go for yourself, from yourself — initiates Abraham's koach hayeridah: his spiritual reality is released into active expression by being displaced from its comfortable container. The fragrance (Or Makif) that had been sealed now diffuses outward. This explains why the name's greatness (*shim'kha mitgadel*) follows the journey: the power of koach hayeridah is evidenced precisely in the *shem* (name/expression) — the ability to make the inner reality publicly known.

- **The lech lecha as double movement**: The doubled language is now read as encoding both powers simultaneously: *lech* (go outward) — koach hayeridah, the descent into new environments and encounters; *lecha* (to yourself) — koach hahafla'ah, the accompanying ascent into one's deepest essence that makes genuine expression possible. True movement in the world requires both simultaneously: an outward engagement that is simultaneously an inward deepening.

- **Ketoret distinction**: The maamar returns to the ketoret (incense) framework from the previous maamarim and refines it. The ketoret on the inner golden altar (daily, in the Heikhal) corresponds to the dimension of *ratzoh v'dauv* — the longing movement of Or Chozer — that operates within the Or Yashar framework (still within *seder hishtalshelut*, as the Heikhal corresponds to Atzilut). The ketoret on Yom Kippur in the Holy of Holies (Kodesh haKodashim) corresponds to the Or Chozer that penetrates *beyond* the sefirotic chain entirely — it engages the *penimiyut* of Keter (Moha sheb'Keter/Mo"s) that stands beyond Atzilut. The Yom Kippur ketoret is thus the Or Chozer of the koach hahafla'ah at its maximum: complete abstraction from all particular relationships and direct contact with the divine essence.

### What It Gets To

The maamar establishes that Lech Lecha is the narrative archetype of the koach hahafla'ah/koach hayeridah dynamic: Abraham's journey is the paradigmatic instance of a soul that achieves its maximum or exactly *by* disengaging from its comfortable context and entering into new encounters. The Or Makif (the fragrance) that was sealed is released precisely through movement — through the Or Chozer of genuine displacement. This gives the hemshech a biographical/historical dimension: the patriarchs' journeys are not merely personal odysseys but structural instances of the cosmic Or Yashar/Or Chozer dynamic being enacted through human lives.

### How This Fits the Hemshech

This maamar significantly advances the hemshech by: (1) introducing the Chokhmah-internal dialectic (koach hahafla'ah / koach hayeridah) as the analogue of Or Chozer/Or Yashar at the level of divine wisdom itself; (2) grounding this structure in the narrative of Abraham, establishing the hemshech's connection to the patriarchal biography; (3) completing the ketoret typology by distinguishing daily ketoret (Or Chozer within hishtalshelut) from Yom Kippur ketoret (Or Chozer beyond hishtalshelut). These three moves together complete the summer/fall semester's argument and set the stage for the extended analysis of Chokhmah's inner structure that will follow.
