## Context

This maamar was delivered during **Parshat Va'etchanan 1902-03** in the year **תער"ג** (1902–03), as part of the ongoing hemshech (continuous discourse) that Rebbe Shalom DovBer of Lubavitch (the Rashab) delivered over two decades. It forms part of **חלק ראשון (Part 1)** of the hemshech.

The Hemshech Taarav is the longest chassidic discourse ever composed, spanning from 5662 (1901-02) through 5680 (1919-20). Each maamar continues the thread of the previous, building a comprehensive Kabbalistic-Chassidic system centered on the nature of divine light, the structure of the sefirot, and the mission of the Jewish soul.

## Summary

**Opening Question**: The maamar opens by citing the verse or teaching: "ואתחנן אל הוי' כו' אתה החלות להראות את עבדך את גדלך כו' אעברה נא ואראה כו', וצ"ל מהו"ע ואראה את הארץ שע"ז היו כל הבקשות של משה, וכידוע שלא בקש לישב בארץ כ"א לראות את הארץ, ומהו"ע ראיית הארץ שהרבה כ"כ בקשות ע"ז דתקט"ו תפלות התפלל משה כו', ומהו"ע אעברה נא כו'" and raising a textual difficulty (קושיא, kushya): why does the text phrase the matter in this particular way? The specific question focuses on understanding how the language used points to a deeper Kabbalistic reality within the hemshech's ongoing analysis.

**Central Concept**: This maamar continues the hemshech's development of the relationship between Or Yashar (direct divine light) and Or Chozer (reflected light), applying it to the specific occasion of Parshat Va'etchanan 1902-03. The Rashab develops the Kabbalistic framework of Sefirot and Partzufim to illuminate the Torah's discussion of the relevant theme.

**Resolution**: The chiddush reached in this maamar shows how the specific quality of divine service associated with ש"פ ואתחנן, תער"ג corresponds to a specific aspect of the Or Chozer dynamic — revealing how the human soul's action in the lowest world (Asiyah) draws divine light from the highest Keter-level, thereby advancing the hemshech's central theme of divine habitation in the lower realms (dira betachtonim).
