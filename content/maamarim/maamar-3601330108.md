## Context

This maamar was delivered during **Parshat Tzav, Shabbat HaGadol 1909-10** in the year **העת"ר** (1909–10), as part of the ongoing hemshech (continuous discourse) that Rebbe Shalom DovBer of Lubavitch (the Rashab) delivered over two decades. It forms part of **חלק שני (Part 2)** of the hemshech.

The Hemshech Taarav is the longest chassidic discourse ever composed, spanning from 5662 (1901-02) through 5680 (1919-20). Each maamar continues the thread of the previous, building a comprehensive Kabbalistic-Chassidic system centered on the nature of divine light, the structure of the sefirot, and the mission of the Jewish soul.

## Deep Dive

### Opening Kushya

The maamar opens with a triple question on the verse "צַו אֶת אַהֲרֹן... זֹאת תּוֹרַת הָעֹלָה הִיא הָעֹלָה עַל מוֹקְדָהּ" (Vayikra 6:2). First, why does the Torah say "היא העולה" — "it is the olah" — after already stating "זאת תורת העולה"? Second, if only a single law (that the limbs may be offered all night) is given here, how can this be called "the entire Torah of the olah"? Third, why the word "צו" (command) rather than the usual "דבר" or "אמור"? A fourth question accompanies a midrash cited from Bahya: the phrase "היא העולה על מוקדה" is interpreted to refer to the wicked kingdom (מלכות הרשעה) that "exalts itself" — and will ultimately be given "ליקידת אשא" (to the fire of Gehinnom, Daniel 7). The maamar therefore must explain both the plain sense and this midrashic reading in a unified framework.

### The Conceptual Argument

The maamar first revisits an earlier discussion of "ועבדתם את ה' אלקיכם" — that avodah is tzorech gavo'ah, a need from Above, and that this applies to the drawing-down of constricted (מצומצם) divine light to vivify the worlds. But the Rashab pushes deeper: that is only a lower level. The ultimate purpose is to draw down "בחי' עצמות אוא"ס שלמעלה מעולמות" — the level of Ein Sof's very Essence, which transcends the entire chain of worlds (hishtalshelut). This Essence corresponds to shem Havayah in its deepest sense — "הוי' הוא הי' הוה ויהי' כאחד," above all time and place. The soul's root is there: "חלקי ה' אמרה נפשי" — souls descend precisely to draw that revelation into the world ("נר ה' נשמת אדם").

The maamar then builds through the structure of the Kav (the primordial line post-tzimtzum): the Kav creates spiritual space and time (maala u'mata, kadima v'ikhur) within the worlds, but the Essence of Ein Sof is entirely beyond — "אני ה' לא שניתי." All concealments and limitations belong only to the Kav's light, not the Essence. The goal of avodah is therefore not merely the descending light, but that "אוא"ס שלפני הצמצום יאיר בגילוי לאחר הצמצום" — the pre-tzimtzum light should shine revealed after tzimtzum.

### Key Chassidic Concepts

- **עצמות אוא"ס** (Atzmus Ein Sof) — the Essence beyond all worlds and Sefirot
- **צמצום** (Tzimtzum) — the primordial contraction that creates the Kav
- **סובב כל עלמין / ממלא כל עלמין** (Sovev / Memaleh) — transcendent vs. immanent divine presence
- **אתעדל"ת / אתעדל"ע** — arousal from below producing corresponding arousal from Above
- **בוצינא דקרדוניתא** (Botzina deKardinuta) — the primordial "dark flame," gevurot of Atik, the source of consuming fire

### Resolution and Chiddush

The maamar resolves all three kushyot together. "תורת העולה" refers to Torah study, through which one draws down from Atzmus Ein Sof — "זאת תורת העולה שע"י התורה הוא המשכת העצמות." "היא העולה" refers to tefillah — the upward elevation (ha'ala'ah) that must precede and enable that drawing-down: "היא העולה — ההעלאה דתפלה, ותורת העולה — ההמשכה ע"י התורה." The phrase "צו את אהרן... מיד ולדורות" is explained via Penei Yehoshua: "מיד" refers to the korbanot of the Mikdash; "לדורות" refers to Torah study, since studying the Torah of an offering is as if one offered it. As for the midrash — "היא העולה זו מלכות הרשעה... על מוקדה — ויהיבת ליקידת אשא" — this fire is the Botzina deKardinuta, gevuros d'Atik, which is Atzmus Ein Sof itself. When that Essence is revealed, the forces of the sitra achra, which drew sustenance only from the concealing "nartik" (sheath), are consumed: "הקב"ה מוציא חמה מנרתיקה — צדיקים מתרפאין בה ורשעים נכוין בה." The chiddush: the Torah and tefillah of Israel draw down the very Essence — and that same revelation is the source of both healing for the righteous and the ultimate nullification of evil.
