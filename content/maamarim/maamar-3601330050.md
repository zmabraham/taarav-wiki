## Context

This maamar was delivered during **Parshat Ki Tavo 1902-03** in the year **תער"ג** (1902–03), as part of the ongoing hemshech (continuous discourse) that Rebbe Shalom DovBer of Lubavitch (the Rashab) delivered over two decades. It forms part of **חלק ראשון (Part 1)** of the hemshech.

The Hemshech Taarav is the longest chassidic discourse ever composed, spanning from 5662 (1901-02) through 5680 (1919-20). Each maamar continues the thread of the previous, building a comprehensive Kabbalistic-Chassidic system centered on the nature of divine light, the structure of the sefirot, and the mission of the Jewish soul.

## Deep Dive

### Opening Kushya

The maamar opens with the paired verses of Parshat Ki Tavo: "את ה' האמרת היום להיות לך לאלקים... ולשמור חוקיו ומצותיו" and "וה' האמירך היום להיות לו לעם סגולה ולשמור כל מצותיו." The Rashab invokes the Or HaChaim's difficulty: both verses describe a mutual act of praise — Israel praises God, God praises Israel — but why does the praise of God toward Israel include the phrase "ולשמור כל מצותיו"? Israel's commitment to keep the mitzvot is a reasonable component of Israel's praise of God. But what is the meaning of Israel's mitzvah-observance as the content of God's praise of Israel? This is the opening kushya: "מהו"ע ולשמור כו'" in the context of God's accolade toward the Jewish people.

### Argumentative Arc

The maamar links this kushya to the Gemara (Berakhot) teaching: "אתם עשיתוני חטיבה אחת בעולם אף אני אעשה אתכם חטיבה אחת בעולם" — Israel declares God's oneness (שמע ישראל... ה' אחד) and God in turn makes Israel one (ומי כעמך ישראל גוי אחד). The challenge is to understand the inner connection between these two unities.

To address this, the Rashab traces the structure of divine light (אור) through hishtalshelut (the chain of worlds). Drawing on the Etz Chaim, he explains that the progressive descent of Or Ein Sof through the sefirot — from Keter to Chokhmah to Binah and so on — is not a simple emanation but involves a genuine "ריחוק ממקורו" (distancing from the source). Each level of descent causes the light to become more particular and defined: "כל מה שיורד ונמשך ה"ה מתרחק יותר ממקורו." This distancing is paradoxically what enables existence — "להיות האור בבחי' מציאות" — since undifferentiated light cannot be received by the worlds.

The Zohar's description of "פלא" (from the verse כי עשית פלא עצות מרחוק) is then explained as a "כסוייא ולבושא לאתלבשא נהורא סתימא" — the concealment and garment that allows the hidden supernal light to clothe itself and be received at each lower level.

### Key Chassidic Concepts

- **ביטול במציאות / ביטול היש** (bittul bimetziut / bittul hayesh): The maamar distinguishes two modes of nullification. The higher is yichuda ila'ah (יחו"ע) — the awareness that "כולא קמי' כלא" (all is as nothing before Him), which flows from the sefira of Chokhmah and the name מ"ה. The lower is yichuda tata'ah (יחו"ת), the bittul available through intellectual contemplation (התבוננות) in da'at tachton (ד"ת).

- **דעה עילאה / דעה תתאה** (da'at elyon / da'at tachton): The upper knowledge that all created existence is truly naught cannot be grasped by creatures from within themselves — "כל השגה היא יש" — since comprehension is itself a mode of existence. Da'at tachton — awareness that divine vitality sustains all existence — can be reached through contemplation, but the deeper da'at elyon is given from above.

- **תושב"כ ותושבע"פ** (Written Torah / Oral Torah): The maamar develops a sustained parallel: Written Torah corresponds to Chokhmah and שם מ"ה (the name of bittul), conveying da'at elyon directly to the divine soul (נה"א). Oral Torah corresponds to Binah — it clothes itself in legal reasoning, questions and answers (קושיות וסתירות), to draw down and purify the animal soul (נה"ב). This maps onto the three functions of מלמד / דרבון / מרדע drawn from the Yerushalmi (Sanhedrin, Perek Chelek).

### Chiddush and Resolution

The maamar arrives at a precise resolution of both the Or HaChaim's question and the Gemara's parallel. The mutual "chativa" (unique oneness): when Israel keeps the mitzvot — especially through the bittul of Shema, "למסור נפשו באחד" — they access and reveal the dimension of Chokhmah within their souls, the sefira where Ein Sof truly resides: "בחכ' שורה אוא"ס אחד האמת שהוא לבדו הוא." This bittul constitutes the "ה' אחד" declaration in its deepest sense. In response, God's praise makes Israel "גוי אחד" — a unified people — because the inner oneness they reveal above is reflected back into their collective identity below. The mitzvah observance mentioned in God's praise is not incidental; it is precisely the mechanism by which Israel's unity (and God's oneness in the world) is actualized. "ולשמור כל מצותיו" is thus the content of Israel's highest dignity: their capacity to be the vessel through which ה' אחד becomes a living reality in the world.
