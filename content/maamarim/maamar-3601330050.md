## Context

This maamar was delivered during **Parshat Ki Tavo 1902-03** in the year **תער"ג** (1902–03), as part of the ongoing hemshech (continuous discourse) that Rebbe Shalom DovBer of Lubavitch (the Rashab) delivered over two decades. It forms part of **חלק ראשון (Part 1)** of the hemshech.

The Hemshech Taarav is the longest chassidic discourse ever composed, spanning from 5662 (1901-02) through 5680 (1919-20). Each maamar continues the thread of the previous, building a comprehensive Kabbalistic-Chassidic system centered on the nature of divine light, the structure of the sefirot, and the mission of the Jewish soul.

## Deep Dive

### The Construct Being Built

This maamar uses Parshat Ki Tavo — centered on the *bikkurim* (first-fruit offering) ceremony and its elaborate confession, the covenant on Mount Gerizim and Mount Eival, and the *tochechah* (series of blessings and curses) — to develop the hemshech's account of how the highest reaches of Or Ein Sof become concretely embedded in physical ritual acts. The construct is specifically about *bikkurim* as the paradigmatic instance of the Or Chozer movement: the first and best portion of the earth's fruit returned upward as an acknowledgment that all belongs to God, thereby drawing down the blessings that constitute *dira betachtonim*.

### The Opening Move

The parasha's opening ritual — bringing first fruits to the Temple, making a declaration that begins "my father was a wandering Aramean" (*arami oved avi*) — poses a structural question: what is the connection between the lowly historical origins of Israel (wandering, servitude) and the highest form of agricultural gratitude? The maamar opens by noting that the bikkurim ceremony uniquely combines physical act, verbal declaration, and historical memory — a simultaneous engagement of action, speech, and consciousness — which signals a comprehensive Or Chozer.

### The Argumentative Arc

- **Bikkurim as Or Chozer paradigm**: The bringing of first fruits is the act of returning to God the "first" (*reishit*) of all production — which Kabbalistically corresponds to Chokhmah (called *reishit*). By returning this first-and-highest portion, the farmer is performing a cosmic Or Chozer: lifting the Chokhmah-dimension of material productivity back to its source and thereby enabling a new Or Yashar to descend.

- **The declaration and its structure**: The verbal declaration (*mikra bikkurim*) traces Israel's trajectory from *arami oved avi* (the patriarch's lowliness and wandering) through Egyptian slavery and the Exodus to the land flowing with milk and honey. This narrative arc — from desolation upward to abundance — enacts the Or Chozer structure in historical form: the soul's descent into the depths of material existence generates, when acknowledged and declared, the maximum upward draw.

- **Mount Gerizim and Mount Eival**: The two mountains used for the covenant blessing-and-curse ceremony are read as corresponding to the two fundamental directions in the Or Yashar/Or Chozer system. Gerizim (blessing) corresponds to the Or Yashar that descends when the vessel is properly aligned; Eival (curse) corresponds to the contraction and concealment that results when the vessel is misaligned. The twelve tribes positioned on both mountains represent the complete sefirotic structure in its covenantal form.

- **The tochechah's Kabbalistic depth**: The curses of the *tochechah* are not punitive threats but descriptions of what happens to the sefirotic structure when Or Chozer fails to operate — when the material world no longer returns its "first fruits" to the divine source. The catalogue of curses thus maps the progressive failure of the *dira betachtonim* project across all domains of human life.

### What It Gets To

The maamar establishes that the bikkurim ceremony is the paradigmatic expression of the entire hemshech's theological project: physical matter (fruit of the earth) being consciously returned to its divine source through a ritual that combines deed, word, and memory. This triple register of Or Chozer — bodily, verbal, intellectual — is the most complete form of the ascending movement. The blessings that follow are not arbitrary rewards but the natural consequence of a completed sefirotic circuit.

### How This Fits the Hemshech

At this point in the annual cycle, the hemshech is building toward the High Holiday discourses that will soon follow (Rosh Hashanah, Shabbat Teshuva). Ki Tavo provides the agricultural and covenantal grounding for those discourses: the bikkurim ceremony is the *avodah* (service) that prepares the ground for the cosmic renewal of Rosh Hashanah. The hemshech needs this passage to show that Or Chozer operates not just in the rarified domains of mystical prayer or Torah study, but in the most elemental transactions between human beings and the physical earth they inhabit.
