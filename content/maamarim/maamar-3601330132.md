## Context

This maamar was delivered during **Parshat Ki Tetze 1909-10** in the year **העת"ר** (1909–10), as part of the ongoing hemshech (continuous discourse) that Rebbe Shalom DovBer of Lubavitch (the Rashab) delivered over two decades. It forms part of **חלק שני (Part 2)** of the hemshech.

The Hemshech Taarav is the longest chassidic discourse ever composed, spanning from 5662 (1901-02) through 5680 (1919-20). Each maamar continues the thread of the previous, building a comprehensive Kabbalistic-Chassidic system centered on the nature of divine light, the structure of the sefirot, and the mission of the Jewish soul.

## Deep Dive

### Opening Question

The maamar opens on the verse from Tehillim 27: **"אחת שאלתי מאת ה'... שבתי בבית ה' לחזות בנועם ה'"** — "One thing I ask of G-d... to dwell in the house of G-d to behold the pleasantness of G-d." The Yalkut records that G-d challenged David: you said "one thing" yet you are asking for many things. David replied: "ממך למדתי" — I learned this from You — for You said "only to fear G-d" yet then opened many commandments. The Maggid of Mezeritch explained that David truly requested only one thing: "שבתי בבית ה'," and from that single request, "לחזות בנועם ה'" would follow automatically.

The maamar then presses three interlocking kushyot: (1) If only one thing is truly being asked, why the appeal to "ממך למדתי"? (2) How does "שבתי" automatically generate "לחזות בנועם ה'"? (3) What is the connection between "שבתי בבית ה'" and the verse "ועתה ישראל מה ה' שואל... כי אם ליראה"?

### The Conceptual Argument

The maamar builds on a prior discussion (from earlier discourses in the hemshech) establishing that the **עונג** (divine delight/pleasure) present in the creation of worlds is specifically **הארת העונג שבחכמה** — a mere radiance of delight as it surfaces in Chochmah — not the **עצמות התענוג** (the essence of delight itself). This distinction anchors the entire structure.

The maamar then develops the principle of **נעוץ תחילתן בסופן וסופן בתחילתן** (abbreviated: נתב"ס וסוב"ת — from Sefer Yetzirah): beginning and end are mutually embedded. Using the analogy of a benefactor's act of chesed, the maamar shows that the initial stirring of the will to give is directed toward the actual act of giving — **"סוף מעשה עלה במחשבה תחלה"** (the completion of the deed was foremost in thought). The beginning exists entirely for the sake of the completed act.

The crucial chiddush then emerges: while the relationship of beginning-to-end means the completed act ascends back to the source of the initial impulse (i.e., to Rosh — the beginning of Chochmah), this alone only returns the act to **הארת העונג**. But when the creative work is accomplished with consummate beauty and fullness — **"וירא אלקים את כל אשר עשה והנה טוב מאד"** — when the receiver genuinely receives the שפע in a proper כלי — the completed act ascends to **עצמות התענוג** itself, beyond where mere reflected pleasure can reach.

The maamar proves this with a phenomenological observation: when one sees one's work accomplished in its finest form, or sees a recipient truly receive what was given, one is gripped by a deeper delight than the original wish produced — **"מתענג בעצם נפשו בבחי' תענוג עצמי"** — because that moment touches the very essence of the soul's delight, not merely its particular expression.

### Key Chassidic Concepts

- **עונג עצמי / הארת העונג** — essential delight vs. radiated/particular delight
- **עצמות עתיק** — the innermost stratum of Atik Yomin, source of essential pleasure
- **סוף מעשה עלה במחשבה תחלה** — the teleological priority of the completed act
- **נעוץ תחב"ס וסוב"ת** — the mutual embedding of beginning and end
- **כלי המקבל** — the vessel of the receiver, whose quality determines whether essential delight is aroused
- **סעודתא דעתיקא** — the Shabbat morning meal as the occasion of revealed essential delight (Atik), contrasted with Friday night's סעודתא דחקל תפוחין (which reveals only particular/radiated pleasure)

### Resolution and Chiddush

The maamar resolves all three questions through this framework. "שבתי בבית ה'" refers to the soul's creation of proper **כלים** — letters of Torah and prayer, and the elevation of all physical acts לשם שמים — so that the שפע is received in a truly prepared vessel. This corresponds to **סוף מעשה** at its finest. And when the כלים are truly good, the completion of the act ascends not merely to the beginning of thought but **למעלה מתחלת המחשבה** — above the beginning of thought — into **עצמות התענוג**, which is "לחזות בנועם ה'."

The appeal "ממך למדתי" is thus precise: just as G-d asks only one thing (יראה/ביטול) from which all else flows, David asks only for שבתי — the creation of good vessels — knowing that from this alone, the highest revelation will follow of itself. The single request generates the multiple outcomes not through addition but through the inner logic of how **סוף מעשה** unlocks **עצמות** — the essence that no particular desire or radiated light could reach on its own.
