## Context

This maamar was delivered during **Parshat Yitro 1909-10** in the year **העת"ר** (1909–10), as part of the ongoing hemshech (continuous discourse) that Rebbe Shalom DovBer of Lubavitch (the Rashab) delivered over two decades. It forms part of **חלק שני (Part 2)** of the hemshech.

The Hemshech Taarav is the longest chassidic discourse ever composed, spanning from 5662 (1901-02) through 5680 (1919-20). Each maamar continues the thread of the previous, building a comprehensive Kabbalistic-Chassidic system centered on the nature of divine light, the structure of the sefirot, and the mission of the Jewish soul.

## Deep Dive

### The Construct Being Built

This maamar — on the Sinai revelation — builds the concept of Ma'amad Har Sinai (the standing at Sinai) as the supreme historical instance of Or Yashar descending from the level of Keter-of-Atzilut directly into the collective national vessel of Israel, and analyzes how the Ten Commandments' structure encodes the complete framework of Or Yashar and Or Chozer — the first five corresponding to Or Yashar (divine-to-human) and the second five corresponding to Or Chozer (human-to-divine).

### The Opening Move

The parasha opens with Yitro — an outsider, a Midianite priest — hearing "everything that God did for Moses and for Israel His people" and coming to join them. The Rashab identifies the structural significance: why does the Torah place the account of Yitro's conversion and the establishment of the judicial system immediately before Matan Torah (the giving of the Torah at Sinai)? What is the connection between Yitro's arrival from the nations and the divine disclosure of the Torah? And more pointedly: why is the parasha that contains the Ten Commandments named after a convert?

### The Argumentative Arc

- **Yitro represents the elevation of the "sparks" (nitzotzot) from all the nations that must be gathered before the Torah can be given**: in the hemshech's framework, the divine light that descended into the nations during the primordial shattering of the vessels (shevirat ha-kelim) must be partially reintegrated before the Or Pnimi of Torah can be fully disclosed. Yitro, the exemplary convert who gathered whatever divine sparks were present in Midian and brought them to the camp of Israel, is the precondition for Matan Torah precisely because his arrival signals that the necessary pre-Sinai elevation of sparks has occurred.
- **"Vayishma Yitro" (Yitro heard) — hearing as the vessel for Or Yashar that precedes sight**: the parasha's opening word is "vayishma" (and he heard). In the hemshech's sensory Kabbalah, hearing (shemia) corresponds to Bina — the level of the divine intellect that processes and comprehends what Chokhmah (sight/flash of insight, corresponding to "seeing") perceives. Yitro's hearing represents the preparatory vessel: before Israel can "see" the divine (Sinai is described in terms of visual revelation — "v'chol ha-am ro'im et ha-kolot," the people saw the voices), Yitro establishes the hearing-vessel of Bina.
- **The ten utterances at Sinai as the complete structure of Or Yashar and Or Chozer**: the hemshech has been building toward this analysis. The first five commandments, which govern the human-divine relationship, correspond to Or Yashar flowing from the upper five sefirot (Keter, Chokhmah, Bina, Chesed, Gevura) into the human receptor. The second five, governing human-to-human relationships (lo tirtzach, lo tin'af, etc.), correspond to Or Chozer flowing from the lower five sefirot (Tiferet, Netzach, Hod, Yesod, Malchut) back upward through human ethical action. The Ten Commandments are thus the complete circuit in a single, unified disclosure.
- **"Anochi Hashem Elokecha" — the first commandment as the disclosure of Or Pnimi at its most essential**: the opening word "Anochi" is identified in the Talmud as an acronym for "ana nafshi ketavit yehavit" — "I have written and given My self." The Rashab develops this: Anochi represents the disclosure of the divine "Anochi" (the first-person singular, the innermost self-awareness of the infinite) within the vessel of human language and law. This is Or Pnimi at its most penetrating — not merely divine light entering the world but the divine "I" becoming the animating principle of the human "I."
- **The thunder and lightning at Sinai as the simultaneous operation of Or Yashar and Or Chozer**: "Vayhi kolot u-verakim" — there were thunderclaps and lightning. In the hemshech's framework, lightning (barak) corresponds to Or Yashar — the sudden, direct flash from above; thunder (kol) corresponds to Or Chozer — the reverberant, extended sound that follows and is generated by the lightning's impact. At Sinai, both operate simultaneously: the lightning of Or Yashar strikes the national vessel, and the thunder of Or Chozer rises from that impact — the two modes of divine light in perfect simultaneous expression.
- **Yitro's advice about delegating judgment as the institutionalization of the hishtalshelut structure**: Yitro's suggestion to appoint judges at multiple levels (officers of thousands, hundreds, fifties, tens) is not merely administrative wisdom but the institutionalization of the seder hishtalshelut — each level receiving from the one above and transmitting to the one below. The judicial system is the earthly analogue of the chain of worlds, ensuring that divine wisdom (Torah) reaches every individual through a graduated structure of transmission.

### What It Gets To

The maamar concludes that Parshat Yitro frames the Sinai revelation as the culmination of a process that required both the elevation of the nations' sparks (Yitro's conversion) and the establishment of a graduated structure of transmission (the judicial system), before the Or Pnimi of the Ten Commandments could be disclosed. The Anochi of Sinai is the most concentrated expression of Or Pnimi available within the seder hishtalshelut — the divine "I" making itself fully present within the national vessel of Israel.

### How This Fits the Hemshech

The Yitro maamar brings the 5670 (1909-10) sequence of Shemot-cycle maamarim to a culmination. The arc of Shemot → Va'era → Bo → Beshalach → Yitro traces the complete journey: names preserved in exile (Shemot) → distinction between Or Makif and Or Pnimi (Va'era) → Or Pnimi penetrating the deepest kelipah (Bo) → Or Chozer achieving its maximal expression in song and establishing the divine throne (Beshalach) → the direct disclosure of the divine "Anochi" within the national vessel (Yitro/Sinai). This sequence constitutes the hemshech's analysis of how Or Yashar and Or Chozer operate at the national-historical scale, providing the macro-level complement to the personal-avodah analysis of the Bereishit-cycle maamarim. The subsequent maamarim will continue to develop the implications of the Sinai disclosure — particularly how the structure of the Ten Commandments maps onto the specific sefirot and how this shapes the service of the neshama in each of the lower worlds.
