## Context

This maamar was delivered during **Parshat Yitro 1909-10** in the year **העת"ר** (1909–10), as part of the ongoing hemshech (continuous discourse) that Rebbe Shalom DovBer of Lubavitch (the Rashab) delivered over two decades. It forms part of **חלק שני (Part 2)** of the hemshech.

The Hemshech Taarav is the longest chassidic discourse ever composed, spanning from 5662 (1901-02) through 5680 (1919-20). Each maamar continues the thread of the previous, building a comprehensive Kabbalistic-Chassidic system centered on the nature of divine light, the structure of the sefirot, and the mission of the Jewish soul.

## Deep Dive

### Opening Questions (Kushyot)

The maamar opens with a cluster of textual difficulties on the verse "וישמע יתרו כהן מדין חותן משה את כל אשר עשה אלקים למשה ולישראל עמו כי הוציא ה' את ישראל ממצרים." Three questions are posed: (1) Why does the Torah identify Yitro as "כהן מדין" — a Midianite priest — which is hardly a compliment to Moshe's father-in-law? (2) Why state "חותן משה," since this is already well known? (3) Why does the verse seem to divide into three separate clauses — "וישמע יתרו," "את כל אשר עשה אלקים," and "כי הוציא ה'" — as if describing three distinct events? Added to these is the Zohar's startling claim (Yitro 67b) that מתן תורה happened specifically through Yitro's acknowledgment: "כאשר אמר יתרו עתה ידעתי כי גדול ה' מכל אלקים כדין אסתלק ואתייקר קוב"ה ביקרי' עילא ותתא ולבתר יהיב אורייתא." Could Moshe and six hundred thousand Jews not have merited the Torah without a Midianite priest's confession?

### The Conceptual Architecture Built

To answer these questions, the maamar builds an elaborate structure distinguishing four levels: **אורות** (lights), **כלים** (vessels), **לבושים** (garments), and **היכלות** (palaces). Vessels (כלים) are like matter and form — the light is unified with the vessel as soul with body, so that "גילוי האור הוא כפי אופן הכלי דוקא." Garments (לבושים) are categorically different: they clothe the light but do not receive it internally; they can be exchanged, they have an independent existence, and they conceal more than they reveal. The letters of thought (אותיות המחשבה) are the paradigmatic garment — they clothe the intellect without being its vessels, and unlike the brain's physical matter (חומר המוח), they do not live from the light they carry. Speech-garments are even more external: one can speak the opposite of one's thought precisely because speech is a fully separated garment. Applied cosmologically: the sefirot of Atzilut are כלים, unified with divine light; the worlds of Beriah, Yetzirah, and Asiyah are progressively separated לבושים; the היכלות are concealments entirely, like riddles (חידות) that hide rather than reveal.

### The Resolution and Chiddush

The chiddush turns on the equation: Yitro = חכמה דקליפה. Moshe embodies חכמה דקדושה — whose essence is ביטול, nullification — while Yitro as "כהן מדין" (כהן מלשון מדון, strife) represents the polar opposite: חכמה whose essence is ישות, self-assertion. When Yitro declared "עתה ידעתי כי גדול ה' מכל אלקים," the חכמה of the kelipot was converted to holiness: "כאשר דחכ' דקלי' נתהפך לקדושה נמשך עי"ז תוס' אור בחכ'." This addition of light in Chokhmah enabled Torah to descend — "אורייתא מחכ' נפקת" — since any downward flow requires first an increase above. The three clauses of the verse thus map onto the four ontological levels: "וישמע יתרו" = the essential supernal light (above vessels entirely, as at Kri'at Yam Suf); "את כל אשר עשה אלקים למשה ולישראל" = lights clothed in vessels (אלקים = vessels); "כי הוציא ה' את ישראל ממצרים" = the exit from the concealments and היכלות. And "חותן משה" points to the mechanism — ביום חתונתו זה מתן תורה — Yitro's acknowledgment precipitated the "descent" (חות דרגא) of Torah-Chokhmah, making him the agent of that ירידה.
