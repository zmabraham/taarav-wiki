## Context

This maamar was delivered during **Parshat Pinchas 1901-02** in the year **תער"ב** (1901–02), as part of the ongoing hemshech (continuous discourse) that Rebbe Shalom DovBer of Lubavitch (the Rashab) delivered over two decades. It forms part of **חלק ראשון (Part 1)** of the hemshech.

The Hemshech Taarav is the longest chassidic discourse ever composed, spanning from 5662 (1901-02) through 5680 (1919-20). Each maamar continues the thread of the previous, building a comprehensive Kabbalistic-Chassidic system centered on the nature of divine light, the structure of the sefirot, and the mission of the Jewish soul.

## Deep Dive

### The Construct Being Built

This maamar builds the framework of how a *zealous act performed outside the normative halakhic structure* (Pinchas's slaying of Zimri and Kozbi) can nonetheless constitute a supreme expression of divine will — by analyzing the category of *kana'ut* (zealotry) as an instance of Or Mekif (encompassing light) that bypasses the graduated Or Pnimi structure of ordinary halakhic process, and thereby establishes the conditions under which the supra-rational dimension of Keter can override the rational-discursive dimension of the Sefirot below it.

### The Opening Move

Parshat Pinchas opens with God's covenant of peace granted to Pinchas as reward for his act of zealotry in the previous parasha: "Pinchas son of Elazar son of Aaron the priest turned away My anger from the children of Israel when he acted zealously for My sake among them" (Numbers 25:11). The halakhic paradox is sharp: Pinchas killed a Jewish tribal leader without trial, without warning, without rabbinic sanction — and yet not only is he not punished, he receives the eternal covenant of priesthood. The Talmud (Sanhedrin 82a) notes that had Pinchas asked whether the act was permitted, the Sanhedrin could not have told him so — yet the act was retrospectively validated. What is the structure of an act that is *simultaneously outside the halakhic system and supremely expressing it*?

### The Argumentative Arc

- **The Or Mekif / Or Pnimi distinction** is now applied to the two modes of divine will's expression: Or Pnimi (inner light) expresses divine will through the structured gradation of Torah law — its rulings, its logic, its halakhic process. Or Mekif (encompassing light) expresses the same divine will from a level that *transcends* and *encompasses* that structure. Kana'ut (zealotry) is an Or Mekif act: it does not emerge from the inner discursive halakhic process but from an immediate, total alignment with the divine will at the Keter level — Ta'anug and Ratzon — before that will has been mediated through the graduated Sefirot.

- **Why Pinchas could not have been told to do it**: a ruling, by definition, is a product of the Or Pnimi dimension — it is Chochmah-Binah-Da'at applied to a case through the proper vessel-structure of legal reasoning. But the act that Pinchas performed was of a category that cannot pass through those vessels without being transformed by them. Had it been articulated as a ruling, it would have become an Or Pnimi act and lost the Or Mekif quality that made it efficacious. The act's very validity depended on its origin in the unmediated divine will.

- **The covenant of peace (brit shalom)** as reward: the hemshech uses the "covenant of peace" not merely as a narrative reward but as a structural indicator. Peace (*shalom*) in Kabbalah denotes the reconciliation of the Sefirot-hierarchy — the right column (Chesed) and the left column (Gevurah) brought into alignment by the central column (Tiferet). Pinchas, who acted from beyond the structured system, received a covenant that *re-integrates* him into the system at its highest level — the covenant of priesthood (Keter Kehunah) that was established in the opening maamar's analysis of the three crowns.

- **Zimri's act as the inverted parallel to Bilam**: drawing on the preceding maamar's analysis of Bilam's Or Chozer access through uncalibrated vessels, Zimri's public transgression with Kozbi is analyzed as the same structural failure: accessing the divine vitality embedded in the physical act through a vessel of *kelipah* (unholiness/separation) rather than holiness. Pinchas's act of kana'ut is the Or Mekif correction that closes the breach — redirecting the Or Chozer that Zimri had inverted back toward its proper source.

- **The gradation of the Sefirot and the exception-structure**: the maamar develops the principle that within the graduated Or Pnimi system, *every rule is accompanied by an exception-structure that derives from above the system*. Halakha recognizes the category of *hora'at sha'ah* (temporary exceptional ruling) and *kana'ut* (zealotry beyond law's reach) precisely because the divine will is not exhausted by the Or Pnimi structure it has created — Keter retains its Or Mekif presence above and around the system it generated. The system is real but not absolute.

### What It Gets To

The maamar establishes that Or Mekif (encompassing light, Keter-level will) is not merely the static envelope surrounding the Or Pnimi system — it is capable of *active intrusion* into the Or Pnimi domain when the covenantal vessels of the lower structure have been breached. Kana'ut is the human mirroring of this divine capacity: an act that bypasses the graduated system to express the Keter-level will directly. This establishes the category of *supra-rational divine service* that the hemshech will increasingly analyze as the discourse proceeds toward the theme of dira betachtonim (divine habitation below).

### How This Fits the Hemshech

Completing the trilogy of Korach-Balak-Pinchas in Part 1, this maamar establishes the *exception structure* of the Or Mekif dimension relative to the Or Pnimi chain — showing that Keter's encompassing light is not merely a theoretical level but an active force capable of overriding the graduated structure in specific conditions. This is essential preparation for the hemshech's later treatment of how divine light reaches all the way into the physical world (dira betachtonim) without being fully mediated by the sefirot-chain.
