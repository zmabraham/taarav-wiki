## Context

This maamar was delivered during **Parshat Pinchas 1902-03** in the year **תער"ג** (1902–03), as part of the ongoing hemshech (continuous discourse) that Rebbe Shalom DovBer of Lubavitch (the Rashab) delivered over two decades. It forms part of **חלק ראשון (Part 1)** of the hemshech.

The Hemshech Taarav is the longest chassidic discourse ever composed, spanning from 5662 (1901-02) through 5680 (1919-20). Each maamar continues the thread of the previous, building a comprehensive Kabbalistic-Chassidic system centered on the nature of divine light, the structure of the sefirot, and the mission of the Jewish soul.

## Deep Dive

### The Construct Being Built
This maamar — on Parshat Pinchas — develops the role of zealotry (קנאות) as a mode of divine service that bypasses the intellect-mediated structure entirely, locating Pinchas's act as a paradigm of the "leap" from the lowest world directly to the highest level of divine will (Keter), and connecting this to the Musaf (additional) sacrifices of the festivals whose enumeration occupies the bulk of the parasha.

### The Opening Move
The maamar opens with the theological puzzle of Pinchas's act: he killed Zimri in the grip of zealous passion (קנאי), without being commanded, without deliberation — an act that violated the normal halakhic process (one who asks about such an act is refused a ruling; only the spontaneous passionate response is valid). The Rashab enters through the paradox: how can an act that bypasses all deliberative and intellectual process be the highest form of divine service? What does it reveal about the relationship between the divine will and the intellect-mediated service structure?

### The Argumentative Arc
- The hemshech has established that Or Yashar descends through the intellect-Sefirot (Chochmah-Binah) before reaching the emotional Sefirot (Chesed-through-Yesod) and then the action-level (Malkhut-Asiyah). Mediated divine service (prayer, Torah study, deliberate mitzvah-performance) follows this descending path and generates an Or Chozer that ascends through the same pathway.
- But there is a mode of divine will that precedes the intellect-mediation: the Keter-as-pure-will (רצון עליון שלפני השכל), which drives the Kav itself. This level cannot be accessed by passing through the intellect — it can only be accessed by an act that itself bypasses the intellect and directly enacts the divine will as will.
- Pinchas's zealotry is analyzed as precisely this: he acted from רצון עליון that is above his personal intellect — in that moment, his personal cognitive processes were suspended and the pure divine will acted through him. This is why the act is only valid when spontaneous — the moment it becomes deliberate it descends into the intellect-level and loses its access to the Keter-will.
- The Musaf sacrifices (additional offerings on Shabbat and festivals) represent the same dynamic within the structured service of the Temple. Musaf = "additional" — beyond what the base service requires. This "additionalness" corresponds to the Or that descends from Keter above the normal hishtalshelut flow, adding light to the world-system from above the system's own self-generating capacity.
- The tribal leader Pinchas receives a "covenant of peace" (ברית שלום) — which the hemshech reads as the installation of a permanent access-point at the Keter-will level for his descendants: the priestly Pinchas-lineage permanently carries the capacity to activate Keter-level Or Chozer in their service, not just episodically but as a heritable spiritual structure.
- The connection to the festival Musaf offerings: just as Pinchas's act was "additional" (beyond the normal service-structure), so the Musaf opens an "additional" Or Yashar from Keter. The numerous festival Musaf offerings (Rosh Chodesh, Pesach, Shavuot, etc.) each correspond to a different dimension of the Keter-will's expression through time — each festival activating a different aspect of the pre-intellectual divine will.

### What It Gets To
The maamar establishes two things: (1) that there is a mode of divine service (zealotry/spontaneous self-sacrifice) that bypasses the intellect-structure and directly activates Keter-level Or Chozer; and (2) that this mode is institutionalized in the Musaf-offering structure as a regular, calendrically-organized access to Keter-level Or Yashar. Pinchas's individual act becomes the archetype of a systemic feature.

### How This Fits the Hemshech
This maamar extends the hemshech's analysis of Or Yashar levels by showing that Keter-level Or Yashar can be accessed not only through the "chok" pathway (obedience without reason, analyzed in Chukat) but also through the "kana'ut" pathway (spontaneous passionate action that bypasses all deliberation). Together, these two pathways establish the full range of Keter-access mechanisms — one through pure will expressed as law, one through pure will expressed as passion — that the Jewish people carry as their permanent inheritance.
