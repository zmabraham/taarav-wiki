## Context

This maamar was delivered during **Parshat Pinchas 1902-03** in the year **תער"ג** (1902–03), as part of the ongoing hemshech (continuous discourse) that Rebbe Shalom DovBer of Lubavitch (the Rashab) delivered over two decades. It forms part of **חלק ראשון (Part 1)** of the hemshech.

The Hemshech Taarav is the longest chassidic discourse ever composed, spanning from 5662 (1901-02) through 5680 (1919-20). Each maamar continues the thread of the previous, building a comprehensive Kabbalistic-Chassidic system centered on the nature of divine light, the structure of the sefirot, and the mission of the Jewish soul.

## Deep Dive

### Opening Question

The maamar opens with a double kushya on the verse: **"פנחס בן אלעזר... השיב את חמתי מעל בני ישראל... לכן הנני נותן לו את בריתי שלום"**. Two things demand explanation: (1) What precisely is the act of "השיב את חמתי" — what does "turning back the wrath" mean at a metaphysical level? (2) Why is the reward specifically "בריתי שלום" — what is the inner logic connecting this reward to that act? The maamar further sharpens the difficulty via the Midrash: Pinchas is called "משיב חימה בן של משיב חימה" — son of Aharon who turned back wrath through the ketores (incense). What connects these two acts of "turning back," and how does the ketores relate to ברית שלום?

### The Conceptual Architecture

To answer, the maamar draws on the Tikkunei Zohar formula **"איהו וחיוהי חד, איהו וגרמוהי חד"** — two distinct modes of unity between Ein Sof and the sefirot. The maamar identifies: *גרמוהי* (lit. "His bones") = the *כלים* (vessels) of the sefirot, rooted in **כח הגבול** — the power of limitation within Ein Sof that makes finite existence possible; *חיוהי* = the *אורות* (lights), rooted in **כח הבל"ג** — Ein Sof's power of the unlimited, which is the manifestation of the עצמות itself.

The key distinction: the *כח הבל"ג* is the dimension of divine self-disclosure ("להיותו בחי' גילוי העצמות"), while the *כח הגבול* is the dimension of concealment that enables created beings to experience themselves as independently existing — **"הרגשת מציאותם בעצמן הוא מבחי' כח הגבול"**. Both powers originate from the עצמות, but they occupy radically different levels: "בחי' איהו שמתייחד עם חיוהי הוא גבוה ונעלה במדרי' הרבה יותר מבחי' איהו שמתייחד עם גרמוהי."

### The Arc Through Nadav, Avihu, and Pinchas

The maamar pivots to Nadav and Avihu: their sin was **רצוא ללא שוב** — a soul-consuming yearning ("כלות הנפש") for divine unity that was entirely correct in its direction but lacking the complementary return (שוב) into Torah and mitzvot. Their ratzo accessed the dimension of *איהו וחיוהי* — pure light, no limit — but without anchoring in גרמוהי/כלים, the act became destructive: the soul departed but the body remained intact.

Pinchas corrected this: his act was **מסירת נפש בפועל ממש** (literal self-sacrifice in action) — which touches the same level of self-nullification — but it was performed as a *mitzvah* (קידוש השם), meaning it retained "אחיזה בפנימי'" (inner grounding). This is ratzo with shuv: the כלות reached the level of עצמות while remaining channeled through Torah's structure.

### The Chiddush: יסוד שבעצמות

The reward *ברית שלום* is not merely honor — it is the bestowal of **יסוד שבעצמות**: the aspect of Yesod (the connecting-unifying sefirah) that belongs to the עצמות itself and reaches all the way down to the lowest realities. The maamar identifies this with **"וברית שלומי לא תמוט"** (Isaiah 54), distinguishing it from the covenant of the Avot (the "mountains that will move") which drew only from the *seder hishtalshelut* (the ordered chain of worlds). The Avot drew divine revelation so that the world would not conceal G-dliness; only *matan Torah* — and the act-mode it embodies — draws the עצמות itself into the lowest place: **"אתהפכא חשוכא לנהורא"**, the transformation of darkness into light, which is possible only through the power of the עצמות: "זהו בכח העצמות דוקא." The Torah is itself called *ברית שלום* ("אם לא בריתי יומם ולילה"), and through Torah the draw of the עצמות is made permanent — this is Pinchas's inheritance and the inner logic of his reward.
