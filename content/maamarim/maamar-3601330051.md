## Context

This maamar was delivered during **Night of 2nd day of Rosh Hashana 1903-04** in the year **תרד"ע** (1903–04), as part of the ongoing hemshech (continuous discourse) that Rebbe Shalom DovBer of Lubavitch (the Rashab) delivered over two decades. It forms part of **חלק ראשון (Part 1)** of the hemshech.

The Hemshech Taarav is the longest chassidic discourse ever composed, spanning from 5662 (1901-02) through 5680 (1919-20). Each maamar continues the thread of the previous, building a comprehensive Kabbalistic-Chassidic system centered on the nature of divine light, the structure of the sefirot, and the mission of the Jewish soul.

## Summary

**Opening Question**: On Rosh Hashanah (Night of 2nd day of Rosh Hashana 1903-04), the Rashab addresses the tension between divine kingship (מלכות, Malchut) and divine transcendence: if God is truly infinite and beyond all relationship with creation, how can there be a meaningful 'coronation' (הכתרה) of God as king? What does it mean to declare 'the Lord reigns' when God transcends all categories of governance?

**Central Concept**: The distinction between God's essential being (עצמות, Atzmus) and God's revelation as king (מלכות, Malchut) — Malchut is the Sefirah through which the infinite becomes relationally accessible to created beings without compromising the divine transcendence. The Rashab develops how the concept of 'king' points precisely to the aspect of divinity that interfaces with those it governs.

**Resolution**: The 'coronation' of Rosh Hashanah refers not to a change in divine being but to a heightened revelation of Malchut — the dimension of divine kingship that is always present but becomes more accessible to finite perception on this day. The Or Chozer of the collective soul's prayer on Rosh Hashanah uniquely draws down this revelation.

## Deep Dive

### The Construct Being Built

Delivered on the night of the second day of Rosh Hashanah 5664 (1903), this maamar marks the hemshech's transition into a new year (תרד"ע) and builds the Kabbalistic account of divine Malkhut as the Sefirah that makes divine kingship possible. The central construct is the distinction between God's *etzem* (essence/being) — which is beyond all relational categories — and God's revelation as *Melech* (king) through the Sefirah of Malkhut, which is precisely the Sefirah that constitutes the relational interface between the infinite and the finite. The "coronation" of Rosh Hashanah is not a change in divine being but an annual intensification of Malkhut's accessibility to created beings.

### The Opening Move

The opening tension is philosophical and devotional simultaneously: if God is truly infinite and transcends all limitation, how can there be a meaningful "coronation" of God as king (*haktarat hamelech*)? Kingship is an inherently relational category — it implies subjects, governance, and a kind of condescension to the level of those governed. Yet God's infinity seems to exclude all such relations. The question sharpens: is the declaration "YHVH Malakh" (God reigns) a statement about God's essence or about God's appearance? And if the latter, what is the theological status of that appearance?

### The Argumentative Arc

- **Etzmus vs. Malkhut**: The maamar draws the fundamental distinction between God's *atzmus* (essential being), which is entirely beyond relation, and God's Malkhut, which is the Sefirah through which the infinite becomes relationally accessible. Malkhut is called "the kingdom of heaven" because it is the plane at which divine sovereignty becomes perceptible — not because God becomes less than infinite there, but because Malkhut is the mode in which infinity *presents itself* as governable, relatable, and responsive to created beings.

- **Ein melech belo am — no king without a people**: The Talmudic/Kabbalistic principle that kingship requires subjects is turned to Kabbalistic use: the created world is not merely the backdrop for divine sovereignty but its enabling condition in the modality of Malkhut. The worlds of ABYA create the "population" that makes divine kingship meaningful at the Malkhut level. This is not a limitation on God but the structure of *hamshacha* (drawing down) — God's infinite light accepting a relational form in order to be genuinely present to created beings.

- **Rosh Hashanah as annual Malkhut-renewal**: The *haktarah* ceremony of Rosh Hashanah — in which the shofar sounds serve as a coronation fanfare, and Israel's prayers collectively re-enthrone God as king — is the annual moment when the Or Chozer of collective human consciousness draws down a renewed revelation of Malkhut. This is not merely liturgical performance but a genuine cosmic event: the capacity of divine Malkhut to permeate created existence is renewed at the root.

- **Or Chozer of communal tefillah**: The collective prayer of Israel on Rosh Hashanah functions as the most potent form of Or Chozer in the annual cycle: it is the gathered ascending movement of all Jewish souls simultaneously, drawing from a depth (*maamakim*) that no individual could reach alone. The "depth" in the night/second day setting is significant — the night represents the hiddenness of Or Chozer, the drawing from what is not yet revealed.

- **The Second Day's specific quality**: The second day of Rosh Hashanah carries a distinct Kabbalistic quality: it corresponds to the more hidden of the two aspects of the renewal, the *or chashuv* (hidden light) that precedes even the first day's disclosure. Delivering the maamar on the night of the second day positions this teaching at the most concealed node of the annual divine-human encounter.

### What It Gets To

The maamar concludes that the Rosh Hashanah coronation is a real event in the life of Malkhut de-Atzilut: the collective Or Chozer of Jewish prayer on this day draws down a revelation of Malkhut that is qualitatively different from its ordinary mode of presence. This renewed Malkhut then flows outward through the days of the year as the source of all divine governance, blessing, and judgment. The year's moral-cosmic infrastructure is set at this moment.

### How This Fits the Hemshech

This is the first maamar of the new year 5664, and it marks a significant thematic pivot. The summer parashiyot (Va'etchanan through Ki Tavo) developed the Or Yashar/Or Chozer dynamic in terms of Torah study, mitzvot, and social institutions. The Rosh Hashanah discourses begin the hemshech's extended analysis of Malkhut itself — the terminal Sefirah of Or Yashar's descent and the originating point of Or Chozer's ascent. This shift from the *avodah* (what we do) to the *Sefirah* (the divine structure we are engaging) marks a new analytical layer in the hemshech's progressive deepening.
