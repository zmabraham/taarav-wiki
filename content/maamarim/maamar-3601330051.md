## Context

This maamar was delivered during **Night of 2nd day of Rosh Hashana 1903-04** in the year **תרד"ע** (1903–04), as part of the ongoing hemshech (continuous discourse) that Rebbe Shalom DovBer of Lubavitch (the Rashab) delivered over two decades. It forms part of **חלק ראשון (Part 1)** of the hemshech.

The Hemshech Taarav is the longest chassidic discourse ever composed, spanning from 5662 (1901-02) through 5680 (1919-20). Each maamar continues the thread of the previous, building a comprehensive Kabbalistic-Chassidic system centered on the nature of divine light, the structure of the sefirot, and the mission of the Jewish soul.

## Summary

**Opening Question**: On Rosh Hashanah (Night of 2nd day of Rosh Hashana 1903-04), the Rashab addresses the tension between divine kingship (מלכות, Malchut) and divine transcendence: if God is truly infinite and beyond all relationship with creation, how can there be a meaningful 'coronation' (הכתרה) of God as king? What does it mean to declare 'the Lord reigns' when God transcends all categories of governance?

**Central Concept**: The distinction between God's essential being (עצמות, Atzmus) and God's revelation as king (מלכות, Malchut) — Malchut is the Sefirah through which the infinite becomes relationally accessible to created beings without compromising the divine transcendence. The Rashab develops how the concept of 'king' points precisely to the aspect of divinity that interfaces with those it governs.

**Resolution**: The 'coronation' of Rosh Hashanah refers not to a change in divine being but to a heightened revelation of Malchut — the dimension of divine kingship that is always present but becomes more accessible to finite perception on this day. The Or Chozer of the collective soul's prayer on Rosh Hashanah uniquely draws down this revelation.

## Deep Dive

The maamar opens with a double kushya on the pasuk "תקעו בחדש שופר בכסה ליום חגינו" (Tehillim 81:4). First: why does the verse say "בחדש" (in the month) without specifying which month — only afterward supplying the cryptic sign "בכסה" (when the moon is covered)? The Midrash Rabbah (Emor 29) compounds the difficulty through R. Berekhya's question: every month has a new moon — so what distinguishes this one? The maamar then focuses on what appears redundant: if "בכסה" already identifies Rosh Hashanah, why add "ליום חגינו" (on the day of our festival)? The Midrash answers: "שהחדש נכסה ויש בו חג וחגו בן יומו" — this is the month that is covered and whose festival falls on the very day of concealment. The question the maamar presses: what is the theological significance of the festival being specifically on the day of hiding?

To answer, the maamar pivots to Isaiah 25:1 ("ה' אלקי אתה ארוממך... כי עשית פלא עצות מרחוק אמונה אומן") and a Zohar (Balak 193b) that maps the divine names: "ה' אלקי" encompasses Chochmah and Binah, "אתה" denotes Chesed and Malkhut. Together these three words span the entire chain of Atzilut.

The maamar then builds its central structure around the concept of a "ממוצע" — an intermediary between the Ein Sof and the Netzalim (emanated sefirot). The Netzalim exist in a dimension of form and likeness (the "ציור אדם" of Ezekiel's vision), while the Atzmus of the Ein Sof is "כי לא אדם הוא" — utterly beyond any such form. Because their distance is absolute (אין ערוך), a mediating level is required that contains something of both: from the Atzmus side, the level of "כשב"כ" (Kesher sheb'Keter — the essential Will in Keter, which has no reason whatsoever, "אין טעם לרצון כלל"); from the Netzalim side, the "ח"ס" (Chochmah Setima of Atik/Arich) which serves as the root and source for the unfolding sefirot.

A key intermediate argument concerns the difference between ענג (oneg/delight) and רצון (ratzon/will). Both are aspects of etzem (selfhood), but delight is more interior — it does not spread outward like Binah-rooted שמחה (joy). Yet delight is still perceptible (e.g., "צהבו פניו" — a person's face brightens). Chochmah can conceal even delight, because as an "כח עצמי" (essential faculty) it can contain what is equally essential. This is the function of "פלא" — when Chochmah ascends toward the concealment of Keter, it takes on the quality of total hiddenness ("כמו כי יפלא ממך דבר") rather than its normal mode of illuminating Binah ("אאלפך חכ'" — Aleph, the teaching mode).

The chiddush and resolution: "בחדש" means renewal — the drawing down of "גילוי אור חדש מבחי' עצמות א"ס" (revelation of genuinely new light from the level of the Atzmus of Ein Sof). "בכסה" specifies that this new light originates precisely from the concealed upper tier of the Keter-intermediary — not from the level that already stands in relation to the Netzalim, but from what remains fully hidden. The shofar's cry — the inward voice of teshuvah ("צעקת הלב") — is the vehicle: "ע"י התשובה ממשיכים בחי' תענוג העצמי ועי"ז הרצון מחדש בחי' אור חדש ממש." The festival coinciding with the day of concealment ("חגו בן יומו... ביום הכסה דוקא") signifies that the very level which is hidden — "כסה" — is itself what descends into revelation on this day.
