## Context

This maamar was delivered during **2nd day of Shavuot, at the meal 1902-03** in the year **תער"ג** (1902–03), as part of the ongoing hemshech (continuous discourse) that Rebbe Shalom DovBer of Lubavitch (the Rashab) delivered over two decades. It forms part of **חלק ראשון (Part 1)** of the hemshech.

The Hemshech Taarav is the longest chassidic discourse ever composed, spanning from 5662 (1901-02) through 5680 (1919-20). Each maamar continues the thread of the previous, building a comprehensive Kabbalistic-Chassidic system centered on the nature of divine light, the structure of the sefirot, and the mission of the Jewish soul.

## Summary

**Opening Question**: In the context of the festival of 2nd day of Shavuot, at the meal 1902-03, the Rashab asks about the spiritual significance of the Torah's transmission at Sinai — specifically, what new dimension of divine revelation was made accessible through the giving of the Torah that was not accessible before, and how this relates to the ongoing hemshech's analysis of Or Yashar and Or Chozer.

**Central Concept**: The revelation at Sinai (Matan Torah) as a unique cosmic event in which Or Chozer ascended higher than had ever occurred previously, creating a new mode of divine-human relationship. The Torah itself is analyzed as a 'garment' that paradoxically both conceals and reveals the infinite divine light — making it accessible to finite minds while retaining its infinite quality.

**Resolution**: The giving of Torah created a permanent channel (צינור, tzinor) through which Or Chozer from the lowest world can reach the highest divine levels. Matan Torah established the template for the entire project of 'dwelling in the lower realms' (dira betachtonim) — the divine goal of having infinite light inhabit finite structure without destruction.

## Deep Dive

### Opening Question (Kushya)

The maamar opens with a verse from Yeshayahu: **"ואשים דברי בפיך ובצל ידי כסיתיך לנטוע שמים וליסוד ארץ"** ("I have placed My words in your mouth... to plant the heavens and to found the earth"). The Rashab cites the Yerushalmi that "ואשים דברי בפיך" refers to Torah study — and then asks the sharp question: **"מהו"ע שע"י התו' יהי' לנטוע שמים כו', הלא השמים והארץ כבר נבראו ונשתכללו"** — what does it mean that through Torah one "plants the heavens," when the heavens and earth already exist and were already perfected? Even granting the Radak's reading that the verse refers to the future ingathering of exiles (קיבוץ גליות), the problem deepens: Torah is given now, so how does present Torah-study bring about an entirely new world?

### The Conceptual Argument

The maamar's resolution turns on a precise distinction between two types of divine unity (יחודים): the yichud of **ישראל סבא ותבונה** (Yisrael Saba and Tevunah — the "lower father and mother," או"א תתאין), which sustains and renews existing worlds but produces only what was already latent in the root; and the yichud of **אבא ואמא עילאין** (the supernal father and mother, או"א עילאין), which draws down genuinely new light — **"להמשיך אור חדש ממש."**

To build toward this, the Rashab maps the entire spectrum of the Sefirot of Mochin and Midot. He distinguishes Yaakov (below the Chazeh — emotions that are felt, arising from shortened intellectual "reason," the טעם of the emotion alone) from Yisrael (above the Chazeh — emotions still clothed in expanded intellect, strong but imperceptible). Above both is **ישראל סבא** — entirely in the realm of Mochin, referring not to emotional arousal at all but to the **הטיית השכל**, the inclination or drawing of the intellect itself toward chesed or din, without any felt sensation.

The Rashab then analyzes the **שערי בינה** ("gates of Binah") — the fifty gates — as the mechanism by which supernal intellect opens outward: inner gates (תרעין עילאין) through which Chochmah inclines toward a particular direction while still fully luminous; outer gates (תרעין תתאין) through which this flows into the emotional Sefirot with some concealment of the original light. This is all the domain of או"א תתאין (Yisrael Saba and Tevunah).

### Chiddush and Resolution

Torah, the maamar concludes, operates specifically at the level of **או"א עילאין** — the supernal Abba and Imma — whose yichud draws down genuinely new light. "שמים וארץ" in the verse refers to Chochmah and Binah, and specifically to their supernal dimension. The existing heavens and earth — created and sustained through the seder hishtalshelus (the chain of worlds) — are only the level of או"א תתאין, which is "בחי' הארה לבד." Through Torah, one accesses **"שמים חדשים וארץ חדשה"** — the new heavens and earth that are the light of או"א עילאין. This is what the Radak meant by קיבוץ גליות: in the future, **"פנימיות אבא פנימיות עתיק"** — the innermost of Abba reflecting the innermost of Atik — will be revealed, producing **"נשמות חדשות"** (new souls) drawn from that innermost root. Torah-engagement now is the cause: **"וז"ש ואשים דברי בפיך... לנטוע שמים"** — the "planting" is the drawing down of this entirely new, supernal light through Torah study, establishing the foundation for the world that is yet to be revealed.
