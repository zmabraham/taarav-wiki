## Context

This maamar was delivered during **Parshat Re'eh 1903-04** in the year **תרד"ע** (1903–04), as part of the ongoing hemshech (continuous discourse) that Rebbe Shalom DovBer of Lubavitch (the Rashab) delivered over two decades. It forms part of **חלק ראשון (Part 1)** of the hemshech.

The Hemshech Taarav is the longest chassidic discourse ever composed, spanning from 5662 (1901-02) through 5680 (1919-20). Each maamar continues the thread of the previous, building a comprehensive Kabbalistic-Chassidic system centered on the nature of divine light, the structure of the sefirot, and the mission of the Jewish soul.

**Opening Question**: The maamar opens by citing the verse or teaching: "ראה אנכי נותן לפניכם היום ברכה כו' את הברכה אשר תשמעו כו', וצ"ל מה"ע אומרו לפניכם דהול"ל נותן לכם מהו לפניכם כו'" and raising a textual difficulty (קושיא, kushya): why does the text phrase the matter in this particular way? The specific question focuses on understanding how the language used points to a deeper Kabbalistic reality within the hemshech's ongoing analysis.

**Central Concept**: This maamar continues the hemshech's development of the relationship between Or Yashar (direct divine light) and Or Chozer (reflected light), applying it to the specific occasion of Parshat Re'eh 1903-04. The Rashab develops the Kabbalistic framework of Sefirot and Partzufim to illuminate the Torah's discussion of the relevant theme.

**Resolution**: The chiddush reached in this maamar shows how the specific quality of divine service associated with ש"פ ראה, תרד"ע corresponds to a specific aspect of the Or Chozer dynamic — revealing how the human soul's action in the lowest world (Asiyah) draws divine light from the highest Keter-level, thereby advancing the hemshech's central theme of divine habitation in the lower realms (dira betachtonim).

## Deep Dive

### The Construct Being Built
This maamar constructs a precise distinction between the two modes of divine image in which Adam was created — "בצלמינו" (our form) and "כדמותינו" (our likeness) — correlating them to the inner and outer dimensions of the Kabbalistic vessels (כלים), and thereby establishing the mechanics of how Or and Kli (light and vessel) genuinely unify.

### The Opening Move
The maamar opens on Parshat Re'eh with a dual textual difficulty: (1) Why does God say "לפניכם" (before you) rather than simply "לכם" (to you)? — a word that implies externality and distance rather than direct gift. (2) Why does the Torah phrase the blessing as "אשר תשמעו" (that you will hear) rather than the conditional "אם תשמעו" (if you hear) used for the curse? The Rashab then imports a Midrashic analogy — God offering Israel "two cups," likening נעשה ונשמע (we will do / we will hear) to two vessels — and sharpens the question: if "nishma" (hearing/study) is ultimately for the sake of "na'aseh" (action), why treat them as two cups of equal standing?

### The Argumentative Arc
- **The two dimensions of the vessel.** Building on the previous chapter's analysis, the Rashab distinguishes פנימיות הכלי (inner vessel) from חיצוניות הכלי (outer vessel). The inner vessel corresponds to "בצלמינו" — the essential form stamped into the very substance of the light, like letters within pure intellect before they become articulated thought. The outer vessel corresponds to "כדמותינו" — a form derived from and resembling the source, but standing apart as an independent structure.
- **Where true unity occurs.** Because the inner vessel (פנימיות) is itself engraved within the light (חקוקה בעצם האור), it is the site of authentic אחדות — the light and the vessel are not two things joined but one reality. The outer vessel, by contrast, is where letters of thought become perceptible as distinct elements; here light and vessel remain in a relationship of two.
- **Yichuda Ila'ah and Yichuda Tata'ah.** "בצלמינו" maps onto יחוד עליון (higher unity): the true perspective that below is genuinely nothing and only above truly exists — a nullification that is absolute (ביטול במציאות דכולא קמי' כלא חשיב). "כדמותינו" maps onto יחוד תחתון (lower unity): the world is granted a real existence, but that existence is entirely derivative of, and sustained by, the divine source.
- **The Atzilut analogy: "understanding a matter from within a matter."** The Rashab deploys a striking epistemological analogy. When a mind grasps a new insight not through an external parable (which yields a categorically different cognition) but through direct inner derivation from a previous insight — the new understanding shares continuity of substance with the old. This is structurally unlike the creation of Beriah from Atzilut (a categorical break through a Parsa), and it mirrors the relationship of the Netzutzot of Atzilut to their source: real newness, but not a wholly other entity. The Nitzulim (emanated beings) thus have a mode of knowing that reaches מהות האין — the essential nullity of their own being — rather than mere awareness of divine existence.
- **The vessels of Atzilut and their ביטול.** Because the Nitzulim arise through this inner derivation rather than categorical discontinuity, their self-knowledge carries an awareness of their own nullity within the Ein Sof. They are like fish whose vitality exists precisely within the water of their source — unlike land creatures who live only when separated. Their ביטול is therefore ביטול במציאות (nullification of substantive existence), not merely ביטול היש (subordination of a self-subsisting entity).
- **Resolution of the two cups.** "נעשה" corresponds to outer-vessel activity — the performed mitzvah as a structured outer form. "נשמע" corresponds to inner-vessel receptivity — the posture of hearing that aligns the inner form of the soul with the light itself. The blessing is phrased "אשר תשמעו" (not conditionally) because the inner vessel's alignment with its divine source is not a contingency imposed from without; it is the soul's own essential constitution. "לפניכם" rather than "לכם" signifies that the blessing is placed before them as something they must face and internalize — it operates at the level of inner form, not merely external acquisition.

### What It Gets To
The maamar arrives at the principle that genuine בצלמינו — the inner form chiseled into the substance of the light — constitutes the real locus of Or-Kli unification. The higher unity (יחוד עליון) is not a theological abstraction but the experiential mode of those whose ביטול is intrinsic rather than imposed. The hemshech's architecture of vessels is here given its philosophical depth: vessels are not passive containers awaiting light from outside but structures whose inner dimension is itself constituted by light.

### How This Fits the Hemshech
At this point in Taarav (Part 1, תרד"ע), the hemshech has been systematically working through the internal structure of Or and Kli. This maamar makes the decisive move of mapping the inner/outer vessel distinction onto the two modes of divine image (צלם/דמות) and then onto the two modes of cosmic yichud. This establishes an anthropological and Atzilutic foundation for the next phase of the discourse: if inner-vessel alignment is the site of true unity, then the question of how human divine service (עבודה) reaches that inner stratum — and thereby draws Or from the Keter-level — becomes the hemshech's living question going forward.
