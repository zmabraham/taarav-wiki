## Context

This maamar was delivered during **Parshat Shelach 1909-10** in the year **העת"ר** (1909–10), as part of the ongoing hemshech (continuous discourse) that Rebbe Shalom DovBer of Lubavitch (the Rashab) delivered over two decades. It forms part of **חלק שני (Part 2)** of the hemshech.

The Hemshech Taarav is the longest chassidic discourse ever composed, spanning from 5662 (1901-02) through 5680 (1919-20). Each maamar continues the thread of the previous, building a comprehensive Kabbalistic-Chassidic system centered on the nature of divine light, the structure of the sefirot, and the mission of the Jewish soul.

## Deep Dive

### The Construct Being Built

This maamar, anchored in the spies episode of Parshat Shelach, constructs a profound analysis of the ratzo v'shov (running and returning) dynamic of divine light, introducing the concept of the renewal of light (chidush ha-or) through the mechanism of ascent and return to source. The maamar is notable for its extended development of why Or Chozer is necessary not merely as a "reflection" but as the means by which the light itself is constantly renewed and strengthened in its descent.

### The Opening Move

The maamar opens with the command "שלח לך אנשים ויתורו את ארץ כנען" — "Send for yourself men, that they may spy out (v'yaturu) the land of Canaan." The Rashab focuses on two textual difficulties: (1) the phrase "לך" (for yourself) appears superfluous — Moses was commanded to send the spies, so why specify it was "for" Moses personally? and (2) the word "ויתורו" (they shall spy/scout) — the Rashab cites the Midrash in Shir HaShirim Rabbah that interprets this verb in light of Kohelet's phrase "לדרוש ולתור בחכמה" (to seek and to explore with wisdom), reading "tur" as "to make a tzir (envoy/ambassador) through wisdom" and "to leave over" (yoter) in wisdom. What does it mean to "make an ambassador through wisdom" and to "leave over" through wisdom in the context of the spying mission?

### The Argumentative Arc

- **The light's intrinsic ratzo**: The Rashab returns to and deepens an earlier established principle (referenced in the text as "as previously explained"): the divine light (or v'chayyut) in creation has an intrinsic character of ratzo v'shov (running and returning). The ratzo (running, yearning upward) is not an external impulsion but emerges from the nature of the light itself. Since the light is only a "shimmer" (haaraha) — not the divine essence itself but an emanation from it — it is, by nature, infinitely below its source and thus has an intrinsic yearning to return and be absorbed back into that source. The shov (returning, flowing outward into manifestation) corresponds to the act of manifestation, which is itself a "descent" from the light's perspective.
- **The second reason for ratzo — renewal**: Here the maamar introduces a crucial second reason for the ratzo (ascent). Beyond the light's inherent yearning for its source, there is a functional necessity: the light must ascend to its source periodically in order to be renewed. Just as blood that flows outward from the heart continuously without returning would grow weak, the divine light that flows into the world without periodically returning to its source would "fade" in its vitality. The ratzo is therefore the mechanism of constant renewal — the light returns to its source, is "recharged" (mitcḥaddesh), and flows outward again with renewed strength.
- **The hidden/primordial light (or ha-ganuz) as the renewing source**: The Rashab invokes the Midrashic concept of the "or ha-ganuz" — the primordial light created on the first day of creation, hidden away for the righteous in the future (Talmud Chagiga 12a, Bereishit Rabbah 11). This primordial light, which shone so that "one could see from one end of the world to the other," is identified as the source that renews all the lights of the world-system. The daily morning prayer "u-v'tuvo mechadesh b'khol yom tamid ma'aseh bereishit" (in His goodness He renews daily the work of creation) refers precisely to this: the or ha-ganuz, though "hidden," is the force that continuously renews all lights in the hishtalshelut. Without this renewal from the primordial source, the lights of the world would be consumed.
- **The Yesod/Tov structure of renewal**: The Rashab analyzes the sefirah of Yesod under the aspect of "tov" (goodness) — the attribute of unconditional giving. Yesod is the conduit through which the upper lights flow into the lower worlds, and unlike Chesed (which gives from love and therefore may have limits tied to relationship), Tov gives unconditionally from its own nature. This is why renewal flows through Yesod: the or ha-ganuz, channeled through Yesod-Tov, constantly replenishes the world-system not as a response to the world's merit but from the inherent nature of divine goodness to overflow.
- **"Tur" as the ambassadorial function of Or Chozer**: Returning to the parasha's "ויתורו," the Rashab reads the spies' mission as an allegory for Or Chozer. The spies were sent "for Moses" (לך) — that is, for the sake of the divine level that Moses represents (Chokhmah, the first flash of revelation). Their task was to "scout" the land — to descend into the material world (Eretz Canaan as the lowest world, Asiyah) and return with a "report." This return with a report is precisely Or Chozer: the consciousness that descends into materiality and brings back the "intelligence" of that realm upward to its divine source. The tragedy of the actual spies is that they failed at this mission — they descended but, instead of bringing a report of the divine potential in the material world, they brought back fear and resistance. Their Or Chozer was corrupted. Caleb and Joshua, who returned with a positive report, represent the successful Or Chozer.
- **"For yourself" — the personal dimension**: The "לך" (for yourself) indicates that the mission of Or Chozer is ultimately self-referential in the deepest sense: it benefits the divine source itself (Moses/Chokhmah/Ein Sof) by bringing renewed light back upward. The descent into materiality and return is not charity extended to the lower world — it is the means by which the upper level itself is renewed.

### What It Gets To

The maamar concludes that Or Chozer is not a secondary or derivative process but the mechanism by which all divine light is continuously renewed. Without the periodic ascent (ratzo) to the divine source, the light that flows into the world would progressively diminish and ultimately extinguish. The spying mission — in its ideal form, as embodied by Caleb and Joshua — is the archetype of the soul's descent into material reality followed by the ascent with renewed spiritual intelligence, which is itself the renewal of the divine light in the world.

### How This Fits the Hemshech

This maamar marks a significant development in the hemshech's treatment of Or Chozer. Previous maamarim established Or Chozer as the upward ascent of human service that "draws" divine light; this maamar adds the dimension that the light itself requires the Or Chozer cycle for its own renewal. This shifts the framing from "humans need to draw divine light downward" to "the divine light itself requires the cycle of descent and ascent in order to remain vital." The or ha-ganuz as the universal renewing source becomes a key concept in the hemshech's subsequent development.
