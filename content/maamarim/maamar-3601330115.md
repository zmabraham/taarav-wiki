## Context

This maamar was delivered during **Parshat Behar-Bechukotai 1909-10** in the year **העת"ר** (1909–10), as part of the ongoing hemshech (continuous discourse) that Rebbe Shalom DovBer of Lubavitch (the Rashab) delivered over two decades. It forms part of **חלק שני (Part 2)** of the hemshech.

The Hemshech Taarav is the longest chassidic discourse ever composed, spanning from 5662 (1901-02) through 5680 (1919-20). Each maamar continues the thread of the previous, building a comprehensive Kabbalistic-Chassidic system centered on the nature of divine light, the structure of the sefirot, and the mission of the Jewish soul.

## Deep Dive

### Opening Kushya

The maamar opens with a double question on the verse "כִּי תָבֹאוּ אֶל הָאָרֶץ... וְשָׁבְתָה הָאָרֶץ שַׁבָּת לַה'" (when you come to the land... the land shall rest a Shabbat to G-d). The Rashab notes two difficulties: (1) the phrase "כי תבאו... ושבתה" implies that the rest begins immediately upon entering — yet the verse itself then requires six years of agricultural work before the seventh year of rest. How can cessation precede labor? (2) More fundamentally — why is the שביתה (cessation) attributed to הארץ, the land itself? The prohibition applies to people who work the land; the other prohibited agricultural labors of shevi'it are forbidden to people, not to soil. What is the meaning of the land's own rest?

### Step-by-Step Argument

The maamar draws on the hemshech's prior discussions of פרסאות (partitioning-screens within the world of Atzilut) and the relationship between מוחין (intellect-sefirot: Chokhmah and Binah) and מדות (emotive sefirot: Chesed through Yesod). These two groupings are fundamentally different in essence — מוחין are in a mode of ביטול (self-nullification and cleaving upward), while מדות are in a mode of יש (selfhood, directed outward toward objects of love or fear). Because they are "מהותים נבדלים בעצם," a פרסא must exist between them — specifically the screen that emerges from the outer dimension of Binah, which acts as the Keter of Zer Anpin and allows the quality of intellect to be "born" into emotional form without simple continuity.

The Rashab then develops three levels of the כהן archetype drawn from Zohar Yitro (דס"ז ע"א): "אית מלך לעילא... וכהן משמש תחותי'." The כהן's function is double — העלאת מ"ן (elevation of feminine waters, arousal from below) and המשכה (drawing down from above). Three כהן-levels correspond to: (1) רב חסד דאריך, which bridges Ein Sof to Atzilut; (2) חסד דז"א, which draws Binah's light through the פרסא into Zer Anpin; (3) חסד דבריאה (Micha'el), which elevates the worlds of Beriah-Yetzirah-Asiyah to the Malkhut of Atzilut and draws that light downward. Each כהן operates through a לבוש (garment-screen) — the פרסא is simultaneously a concealer and the vehicle of transmission.

### Key Chassidic Concepts

- פרסא (partition-screen) — conceals the higher level while enabling birth of the lower
- מוחין ומדות — intellect and emotions as ontologically distinct levels of soul
- העלאת מ"ן / המשכה — the two-directional movement of divine service
- שביתה ונייחא — rest as the state of Malkhut when light is drawn into it
- זריעה (sowing) — metaphor for Torah and mitzvot as acts of drawing divine light into Malkhut

### Chiddush and Resolution

The maamar's resolution is precise: "ארץ הוא בחי' מל'" — the land is the sefirah of Malkhut. The Midrash notes that the land is called ארץ because "רצתה לעשות רצון קונה" — it perpetually wills to fulfill its Maker's will. This perpetual רצוא (longing-upward) is Malkhut's defining state: because the light of Ein Sof does not normally shine within it in revelation, it is always reaching upward. "וְשָׁבְתָה הָאָרֶץ" — the land's rest — means: when divine light is drawn into Malkhut, it achieves שביתה ונייחא, a state of settled rest. Hence "כי תבאו אל הארץ... ושבתה הארץ" does not promise immediate agricultural cessation — it means: when you bring the light into the land (Malkhut) through six years of זריעה (Torah and mitzvot, as in "אור זרוע לצדיק"), the land will achieve its שביתה. The six years precede the שביתה causally, not temporally: "שש שנים תזרע שדך... ובשנה השביעית שבת שבתון יהי' לארץ" — labor is the vehicle by which light reaches Malkhut, and Malkhut's rest is the result.
