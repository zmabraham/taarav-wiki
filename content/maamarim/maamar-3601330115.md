## Context

This maamar was delivered during **Parshat Behar-Bechukotai 1909-10** in the year **העת"ר** (1909–10), as part of the ongoing hemshech (continuous discourse) that Rebbe Shalom DovBer of Lubavitch (the Rashab) delivered over two decades. It forms part of **חלק שני (Part 2)** of the hemshech.

The Hemshech Taarav is the longest chassidic discourse ever composed, spanning from 5662 (1901-02) through 5680 (1919-20). Each maamar continues the thread of the previous, building a comprehensive Kabbalistic-Chassidic system centered on the nature of divine light, the structure of the sefirot, and the mission of the Jewish soul.

## Deep Dive

### The Construct Being Built

This closing maamar of the Vayikra sequence develops the hemshech's account of the temporal structures — Shemita (the sabbatical seventh year) and Yovel (the fiftieth/jubilee year) — as the cosmic-scale counterparts of the weekly Shabbat and the Omer-count's culmination at Shavuot. The construct being built is the relationship between two modes of divine service: chukkim (supra-rational statutes, whose logic is inaccessible to the rational mind) and mishpatim (rational laws). The maamar argues that the ultimate form of Or Chozer — what "im bechukotai telekhu" (walking in My statutes) produces — is not merely the ascent of Or Chozer to its source but a new mode of revelation from above: the Or that descends in response to supra-rational service is categorically different from the Or that descends in response to rational service.

### The Opening Move

The maamar opens with the double focus: Parshat Behar ("ה' דִּבֶּר אֶל מֹשֶׁה בְּהַר סִינַי" — G-d spoke to Moses on Mount Sinai) introducing the Shemita and Yovel laws; and Parshat Bechukotai ("אִם בְּחֻקֹּתַי תֵּלֵכוּ" — if you walk in My statutes). The Rashab raises the structural question that Rashi identified (cited in his famous comment on Behar's opening): why does the Torah need to specify that the Shemita law was given on Sinai? All laws were given on Sinai — why is this specification necessary only here? The Rashab transforms this halakhic-exegetical question into the maamar's metaphysical inquiry: what is it about Shemita specifically that requires the Sinai-specification — i.e., what about Shemita's nature connects it to the unique revelatory character of the Sinai-event rather than to ordinary Moshe-transmission?

### The Argumentative Arc

- **Shemita as the weekly Shabbat extended to cosmic scale**: The weekly Shabbat is, in the hemshech's terms, the annual re-setting of the Or Yashar/Or Chozer circuit at the scale of one week: six days of Or Yashar descending through human labor, then Shabbat's cessation allowing the Sovev's encompassing presence to be directly experienced. The Shemita is this same dynamic at the scale of seven years: six years of agricultural activity (Or Yashar entering the land through human labor) followed by the seventh year of complete agricultural cessation — the "Shabbat of the land" in which the land itself enters the Sovev's encompassing presence.
- **Yovel (Jubilee) as the Shavuot of the seven-year cycles**: Just as the 49-day Omer count culminates in the 50th-day Shavuot (the revelation of Torah = Sefirot HaGnuzot made accessible through the Sefirot HaGeluyot), the seven cycles of Shemita (7x7 = 49 years) culminate in the 50th-year Yovel. The Yovel is the Shavuot of the agricultural-cosmic time-scale: the year in which all accumulated Or Chozer — from seven cycles of Shemita — reaches its maximal concentration and draws down from the Sefirot HaGnuzot a revelation that transcends all the ordinary Or Yashar/Or Chozer circulation.
- **Chukkim vs. mishpatim in terms of Or**: Mishpatim (rational laws) operate through the intellect-vessel (Binah-Chokhmah Kelim): the mind grasps the law's logic, applies it through the emotive faculties (Chesed-Gevurah-Tiferet), and the action draws Or Yashar through the Kli-chain in the expected graduated manner. Chukkim (supra-rational statutes) operate differently: because they are not graspable by the intellect-Kli, they pass through the intellect without being filtered by it. They reach the will-level (Keter/Ratzon) directly — and from there they draw down not the filtered Or Yashar of the Kli-chain but the unfiltered Sovev-level Or that passes through without the Kli's form-imposition.
- **"Im bechukotai telekhu" — walking in statutes as continuous Or Chozer**: The word "telekhu" (you shall walk) rather than "tishmeру" (you shall observe) or "ta'asu" (you shall do) is the Rashab's primary textual hook. "Walking" in the statutes implies continuous motion — not a static compliance but a dynamic, ongoing engagement. This walking is Or Chozer in its most sustained, continuous mode: not the episodic burst of the Omer-count or the annual Yom Kippur, but a perpetual upward movement that never stops. The reward-promise of Bechukotai ("I will give your rains in their proper time") follows from this continuous Or Chozer because continuous Or Chozer draws a continuous Or Yashar in response — the "rains" (divine abundance flowing downward like rain) are the Or Yashar that the continuous walking-upward draws.
- **Sinai specification as the Chok-level of revelation**: Returning to the opening question — why specify that Shemita was given on Sinai: Sinai was the one event in history when the divine Or descended in its full unfiltered Sovev-mode into the material world (the world was the vessel; G-d "came down" on the mountain; Israel heard G-d's voice directly). Sinai is the Yovel of history — the 50th-level revelation in the sequence of all revelations. By specifying that Shemita was given "at Sinai," the Torah teaches that Shemita partakes of the Sinai-type revelation: it is not a rational-law that the mind grasps and applies (mishpat) but a supra-rational statute (chok) whose Or comes directly from the Sovev-level, bypassing the graduated Kli-chain. Rashi's question has its answer: Shemita is at Sinai to tell you it is Sovev-class revelation, not Mimaleh-class.
- **The blessing of the land as the circuit's completion**: Bechukotai's promise: "I will give your rains in their season, the land will give its produce, and the tree of the field will give its fruit." The agricultural blessing is the hemshech's paradigm for the complete Or Yashar/Or Chozer circuit operating at the scale of the land: the land (Eretz Yisrael as the paradigmatic lower world — aretz from ratzah, "will") receives Or Yashar through the rain, processes it through the seven-year Shemita cycle (the full 49-level Kli traversal), and in the Yovel year returns all the accumulated Or Chozer back to its source, drawing down in response the full Sovev-revelation. The agricultural abundance of the land in the years of Bechukotai-compliance is the physical-material sign of the complete circuit's operation.

### What It Gets To

The maamar concludes that Sefer Vayikra — the entire book of the Mishkan-service — culminates in the Shemita/Yovel structure because this structure is the temporal scale at which the Or Yashar/Or Chozer circuit operates in the created world as a whole (not just in the individual Mishkan or the individual soul). The "walking in statutes" (chukkim) is the supra-rational Or Chozer that draws the Sovev-level Or Yashar — the same level of Or that irrupted at Sinai and at the Pesach-exodus. The closing parasha of Vayikra is thus the hemshech's summation of how divine dwelling in the lower world (dira betachtonim) is sustained not only in sacred space (Mishkan) but in sacred time (Shemita-Yovel) and in supra-rational practice (chukkim).

### How This Fits the Hemshech

Behar-Bechukotai closes Part 2 of Hemshech Taarav's second volume and completes the long Vayikra-sequence. The entire arc from Vayikra (korbanot as Or Chozer) through Tzav (esh tamid), Shemini (dual tzimtzum-effects), Tazria-Metzora (Or-Kli disruption), Acharei-Kedoshim (Yom Kippur repair), Emor (Omer-count and Sefirot HaGnuzot), and now Behar-Bechukotai (temporal-cosmic circuit) has built a comprehensive map of how the divine-human Or Chozer circuit operates across every dimension: spatial (Mishkan), ritual (korbanot), spiritual (soul-levels), temporal (sacred calendar), and agricultural-cosmic (Shemita-Yovel). The subsequent volumes of Hemshech Taarav will build on this complete structural map to address the deeper questions of how the Messianic era — the definitive and permanent dira betachtonim — will differ from the current partial and intermittent divine-dwelling below.
