## Context

This maamar was delivered during **Parshat Korach 1902-03** in the year **תער"ג** (1902–03), as part of the ongoing hemshech (continuous discourse) that Rebbe Shalom DovBer of Lubavitch (the Rashab) delivered over two decades. It forms part of **חלק ראשון (Part 1)** of the hemshech.

The Hemshech Taarav is the longest chassidic discourse ever composed, spanning from 5662 (1901-02) through 5680 (1919-20). Each maamar continues the thread of the previous, building a comprehensive Kabbalistic-Chassidic system centered on the nature of divine light, the structure of the sefirot, and the mission of the Jewish soul.

## Summary

**Opening Question**: The maamar opens by citing the verse or teaching: "ויקח קרח בן יצהר בן קהת כו', וארז"ל בגמ' סנהדרין דק"ט ע"ב קרח שנעשה קרחה בישראל, בן יצהר בן שהרתיח עליו את כל העולם כצהרים, וצ"ל מהו"ע מחלוקת קרח שעי"ז נעשה קרחה בישראל, ומהו"ע שנעשה קרחה כו', גם מהו"ע שהרתיח עליו כו' כצהרים" and raising a textual difficulty (קושיא, kushya): why does the text phrase the matter in this particular way? The specific question focuses on understanding how the language used points to a deeper Kabbalistic reality within the hemshech's ongoing analysis.

**Central Concept**: This maamar continues the hemshech's development of the relationship between Or Yashar (direct divine light) and Or Chozer (reflected light), applying it to the specific occasion of Parshat Korach 1902-03. The Rashab develops the Kabbalistic framework of Sefirot and Partzufim to illuminate the Torah's discussion of the relevant theme.

**Resolution**: The chiddush reached in this maamar shows how the specific quality of divine service associated with ש"פ קרח, תער"ג corresponds to a specific aspect of the Or Chozer dynamic — revealing how the human soul's action in the lowest world (Asiyah) draws divine light from the highest Keter-level, thereby advancing the hemshech's central theme of divine habitation in the lower realms (dira betachtonim).

## Deep Dive

### The Construct Being Built
This maamar uses the figure of Korach and his rebellion to build an analysis of the difference between the divine illuminations drawn down by the Patriarchs (אבות) and the illumination of Matan Torah — specifically arguing that the Patriarchs drew from ישסו"ת (the source-level of hishtalshelut), while Matan Torah draws from the level of "Shem Havayah itself" (אמיתי' שם הוי'), a dimension above the source of hishtalshelut entirely.

### The Opening Move
The maamar opens with three interlocking questions about the Korach narrative. First, what is the inner meaning of "Korach" making Israel "bald" (קרחה) — what is stripped away in a dispute of this nature? Second, why does Rashi note that Yaakov prayed not to be mentioned in Korach's rebellion, and where is Yaakov then mentioned? The answer — that Yaakov is mentioned in the Levites' genealogy when they minister on the Duchan (the priestly platform) — opens the maamar's key move: the connection between Korach's rebellion and the Levitical singing on the Duchan is not coincidental. Both concern the same fundamental question of which level of divine service is legitimate. Korach's error was to seize the wrong level — to claim access to a divine light that his vessel could not contain.

### The Argumentative Arc
- Building directly on the previous maamar (Beha'alotcha on ישסו"ת), the Rashab now deploys the inner/outer Binah distinction for a historical application: the Patriarchs' service drew from ישסו"ת = "outer Binah" = the source of hishtalshelut. This is represented by the divine name "El Shaddai" (אל שדי) — the name associated with the Patriarchs and associated in Kabbalah with Binah that "nests" in Beriyah (Binah hamikvenet baBeriyah), the source of the world-chain.
- The proof-text is the verse: "וארא אל אברהם אל יצחק ואל יעקב באל שדי ושמי הוי' לא נודעתי להם" — God appeared to the Patriarchs via El Shaddai, but the true meaning of the Shem Havayah was not made known to them. This is interpreted not as God withholding information but as an ontological statement: the illumination the Patriarchs drew — sublime as it was — did not penetrate to the level of Shem Havayah itself, which is the level of Atzmut (divine essence) that transcends hishtalshelut altogether.
- The distinction of El Shaddai: "שדי" is derived from "שאמר לעולמו די" — God who said to His world "enough!" This is the level that defines and limits the world-process, i.e., the "source of hishtalshelut" that determines how much divine light enters the chain of worlds. The Patriarchs drew from this limiting/defining level — which is why their illumination was a "revelation of Divinity in the world" (גילוי אלקות בעולם) but not a revelation of the essence (Atzmut) itself.
- The Patriarchs' illumination surpassed normal hishtalshelut in that they drew from the source-level (not from the product-of-hishtalshelut), but it remained bounded by the El-Shaddai structure. The example: before the sin of Adam, the Shekhinah was present in the lower realms — but this was not through normal hishtalshelut; it was a "supplementary light" (בחי' תוס' אור), an emergency illumination from above the normal system. The Patriarchs similarly drew an extraordinary illumination but not from the absolute essence.
- Korach's error is precisely analyzed in this light: Korach perceived that the Levitical level (Binah/ישסו"ת) is where the deepest service occurs, and he was right about this. But he confused the service of outer Binah (appropriate to Levi's level) with the service of inner Binah/pnimiyut haChochmah (appropriate only to Aharon's level of Kohen Gadol). His "grabbing" (ויקח קרח — Korach "took") was the seizure of a level that belonged to Aharon and could not be held by Korach's vessel without destruction.
- The rebellion "made Israel bald" (קרחה בישראל): when a vessel attempts to hold a light it cannot contain, the result is the stripping away of all covering — the scorching of the entire structure. This is the spiritual meaning of the earthquake that swallowed Korach's company: the light expanded beyond what the vessel could hold and the vessel was destroyed.
- Yaakov is not mentioned in Korach's rebellion because Yaakov represents the quality of tiferet (truth / בחי' אמת), which is precisely the quality of a vessel holding its appropriate light — neither more nor less. The Levites singing on the Duchan (their positive mention) is Yaakov's descendants finding the vessel-appropriate version of the same Binah-service that Korach attempted to seize at too high a level.

### What It Gets To
The maamar establishes a precise taxonomy of divine service levels: (1) The Patriarchs drew from the outer level of ישסו"ת (El Shaddai / source of hishtalshelut); (2) Aharon drew from pnimiyut haChochmah (the inner face of Wisdom, above the source of hishtalshelut); (3) Matan Torah established access to Shem Havayah itself (the Atzmut, above even pnimiyut haChochmah in terms of revealed essence). Korach's error was a vessel-level mismatch — drawing from the wrong register for one's specific role in the divine structure.

### How This Fits the Hemshech
This maamar performs a crucial synthetic move: it takes the hemshech's abstract distinctions (Or Yashar levels, inner vs. outer Binah, hishtalshelut vs. Atzmut) and applies them to a concrete historical narrative (the Patriarchs' service, Korach's rebellion, the Levitical singing), showing that these abstract distinctions have real consequences for what happens when beings at different levels attempt to access different registers of divine light. This sets up the subsequent maamarim (Chukat, Balak) which will deepen the analysis of the Patriarchs' specific achievement.
