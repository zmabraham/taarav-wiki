## Context

This maamar was delivered during **Parshat Noach 1903-04** in the year **תרד"ע** (1903–04), as part of the ongoing hemshech (continuous discourse) that Rebbe Shalom DovBer of Lubavitch (the Rashab) delivered over two decades. It forms part of **חלק ראשון (Part 1)** of the hemshech.

The Hemshech Taarav is the longest chassidic discourse ever composed, spanning from 5662 (1901-02) through 5680 (1919-20). Each maamar continues the thread of the previous, building a comprehensive Kabbalistic-Chassidic system centered on the nature of divine light, the structure of the sefirot, and the mission of the Jewish soul.

## Deep Dive

### The Construct Being Built

This maamar builds a comprehensive theory of *re'ach* (fragrance/scent) as a Kabbalistic category — specifically as the mode of Or Makif generated through the *birur nitzotzot d'tohu* (the refinement of sparks from the world of Tohu) — and uses this theory to explain why Noah's korban produces the divine response "I will not curse the earth again," while the Midrash says what God smelled was actually "the fragrance of Abraham ascending from the furnace" and "the fragrance of Chananiah, Mishael, and Azariah." The construct unifies the korban/ketoret distinction from the prior maamarim with the Tohu/Tikkun framework that is the hemshech's deepest structural axis.

### The Opening Move

The verse "And YHVH smelled the pleasing fragrance (*rei'ach ha-nichoach*) and said: I will not continue to curse the ground" presents a double puzzle. On the plain level, the pleasing fragrance is Noah's burnt offering — that much is explicit. Yet the Midrash says God was smelling not Noah's korban but the fragrance of Abraham rising from the fiery furnace and the fragrance of Chananiah, Mishael, and Azariah rising from Nebuchadnezzar's furnace. What does it mean that a future event's "fragrance" can be smelled in the present? And why does this future fragrance — rather than Noah's immediate offering — generate the decisive cosmic resolution?

### The Argumentative Arc

- **Fragrance as a Kabbalistic category**: The maamar first establishes what *re'ach* (scent/fragrance) means in Kabbalistic terms. Scent, the Rashab argues, originates specifically from things that are *chazak* (strong and pungent) — spices, sharp substances, the residue on the bottom of a cooking pot (*tzikei kadeirah*). This is not accidental: the *or* that generates fragrance is the *or* of *Tohu* — the immense, unbounded, vessel-breaking lights of the pre-Tikkun world that were too intense to be contained and fell into the lower realms. Their strength (*tokef*) is what makes them "aromatic" — they carry a potency that the more moderate, vessel-compatible lights of Tikkun do not possess.

- **Birur of Tohu and the genesis of fragrance**: The world of Tohu's lights, after the *shevirat hakelim* (breaking of the vessels), scattered as *nitzotzot* (sparks) into the lower worlds. The process of *birur* — gradually extracting and elevating these sparks — generates what Kabbalah calls *re'ach*: the Or Makif released when an intensely strong or light that was previously embedded in impure matter is liberated. The fragrance is not the spark itself but the *aura* of its release — an Or Makif that is irreducibly more powerful than the or pnimi dimension.

- **Shem MA and Shem BaN — two modes of birur**: The maamar introduces the critical distinction between two stages of refinement. *Birur milematat l'maalah* (refinement from below upward) — the mode of human spiritual effort — separates the holy from the unholy but does not yet integrate the extracted sparks into holiness. *Birur milema'ala l'matat* (refinement from above downward) — the mode of Shem MA's operation — fully integrates the sparks into Atzilut's holiness, transforming them from "extracted sparks" into actual *or d'Tikkun* (rectified light). The vessels of Atzilut correspond to *Shem BaN* (Bet-Nun = 52, the filling of the divine name YHVH) and the lights to *Shem MA* (Mem-Aleph = 45, the expansive divine name).

- **Abraham and the furnace as pure Or Makif**: The reason the Midrash says God smelled Abraham's fragrance rather than Noah's korban is that Abraham's experience in Nimrod's furnace was a maximally pure *birur* — a *mesirut nefesh* (self-sacrifice) that released an Or Makif of Tohu's lights precisely through the encounter with the most extreme external opposition (fire threatening bodily destruction). The Or Makif released at the moment of *mesirut nefesh* — when the soul's Tohu-dimension is completely exposed through the threat of annihilation — is the most potent form of fragrance available, bypassing the entire Or Yashar chain and reaching directly to the divine source.

- **The cosmic resolution of "I will not curse again"**: The divine commitment not to curse the earth again (*lo osif l'kallel*) is prompted not by Noah's formal korban (which represents the ordinary *seder hishtalshelut* service) but by the Or Makif of Tohu generated through the fiery-furnace experiences — because this Or Makif reaches a level of divine being that transcends the logic of *din* (judgment) that produced the flood. The world of Tohu's lights, when properly elevated, access the Or Ein Sof that precedes all differentiation into justice and mercy, and at that level there is no basis for the curse to continue.

### What It Gets To

The maamar arrives at a unified theory: *re'ach nichoach* (pleasing fragrance) in all its biblical and post-biblical occurrences refers specifically to the Or Makif released through the *birur* of *nitzotzot d'Tohu* — the liberation of pre-Tikkun divine light from its embedment in material opacity. This Or Makif bypasses the ordinary sefirotic chain (Or Yashar mode) and reaches the level of Or Ein Sof that precedes all cosmic history — which is why it has the power to permanently alter the divine relationship with the world rather than merely contributing to the chain's ongoing maintenance.

### How This Fits the Hemshech

This maamar is the hemshech's definitive statement on the Tohu/Tikkun dimension of Or Chozer. It establishes that the most powerful Or Chozer movements in history — Abraham in the furnace, Chananiah/Mishael/Azariah — are not simply human achievements but events in the cosmic narrative of Tohu's lights being recovered through human *mesirut nefesh*. This frames the entire hemshech's analysis of divine service within a historical teleology: the gradual birur of Tohu's lights through Israel's encounters with opposition and self-sacrifice is the actual substance of *dira betachtonim* being built across time.
