## Context

This maamar was delivered during **Parshat Miketz, Shabbat Chanukah 1902-03** in the year **תער"ג** (1902–03), as part of the ongoing hemshech (continuous discourse) that Rebbe Shalom DovBer of Lubavitch (the Rashab) delivered over two decades. It forms part of **חלק ראשון (Part 1)** of the hemshech.

The Hemshech Taarav is the longest chassidic discourse ever composed, spanning from 5662 (1901-02) through 5680 (1919-20). Each maamar continues the thread of the previous, building a comprehensive Kabbalistic-Chassidic system centered on the nature of divine light, the structure of the sefirot, and the mission of the Jewish soul.

## Deep Dive

### The Construct Being Built
This maamar — delivered on Shabbat Chanukah, Parshat Miketz — builds the argument that the Chanukah light (נר חנוכה) is a unique modality of Or Chozer in which the light generated from below is specifically categorized as light "not for use" (אסור להשתמש לאורו), illuminating how certain divine revelations operate outside the normal economy of cause-and-effect within the Sefirot-structure.

### The Opening Move
The maamar's entry point is the halakhic datum that one may not use the Chanukah light for any practical purpose — it is kindled only to publicize the miracle (פרסומי ניסא). The Rashab treats this legal restriction not as a mere rabbinic decree but as a window into the nature of the light itself: the Chanukah lights reveal a dimension of divine illumination that transcends the transactional structure of Or Yashar flowing down and Or Chozer ascending. The question is: what kind of divine light is, by its very nature, above utility?

### The Argumentative Arc
- The hemshech's established framework distinguishes between lights that enter the economy of the Sefirot (flowing through the Kav and vesting in Kelim, thus becoming "usable" by successively lower levels) and lights that remain transcendent even in their moment of revelation.
- The Chanukah miracle is located at the level of Gevurah d'Atik — a "strong" concealment at the highest reaches of Keter that normally does not penetrate into the lower Sefirot at all. The miracle was a momentary puncture of this concealment.
- The "purity" (טהרה) of the oil — the Hashmoneans finding one cruse with the Kohen Gadol's seal unbroken — corresponds to the level of Chochmah-Stima'ah (the concealed wisdom), which maintains its integrity even within a defiled environment. This is the inner dimension of Or Chozer at its most powerful: the soul-level that klipah cannot contaminate.
- The light that results from this level cannot be "used" because use implies incorporation into a transactional system; this light operates at a level prior to the Tzimtzum that enables transactional structure. It illuminates but does not integrate — hence the prohibition on use.
- The connection to Parshat Miketz: Yosef's dreams and his interpretation of Pharaoh's dreams represent a similar dynamic — a divine light (prophetic illumination) that descends into Egypt's total darkness, remains unabsorbed by the surrounding klipah, and ultimately overturns the host system. The "not for use" quality of the Chanukah light parallels the way Yosef's inner divine light could not be co-opted by the Egyptian system despite his immersion in it.

### What It Gets To
The maamar establishes a category of Or that exists within the world-system yet cannot be domesticated by it — a light whose very transcendence is the source of its revelatory power. This is the Chanukah chiddush applied to the hemshech's larger framework: not all Or Chozer operates within the normal ascending-descending circuit; some Or Chozer carries with it an excess that overflows the circuit entirely.

### How This Fits the Hemshech
This maamar advances the hemshech by distinguishing between two modes of Or Chozer: the ordinary or Chozer that participates in the balanced Or Yashar / Or Chozer exchange within hishtalshelut, and the extraordinary Or Chozer (exemplified by Chanukah) that erupts from a level above the Tzimtzum-conditioned system — a distinction that the subsequent maamarim will need when analyzing Matan Torah and the nature of Torah itself as a light that transcends the world-structure it illuminates.
