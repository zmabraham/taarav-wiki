## Context

This maamar was delivered during **2nd day of Shavuot 1901-02** in the year **תער"ב** (1901–02), as part of the ongoing hemshech (continuous discourse) that Rebbe Shalom DovBer of Lubavitch (the Rashab) delivered over two decades. It forms part of **חלק ראשון (Part 1)** of the hemshech.

The Hemshech Taarav is the longest chassidic discourse ever composed, spanning from 5662 (1901-02) through 5680 (1919-20). Each maamar continues the thread of the previous, building a comprehensive Kabbalistic-Chassidic system centered on the nature of divine light, the structure of the sefirot, and the mission of the Jewish soul.

## Summary

**Opening Question**: The powers of the soul are 'inner' (pnimi) — they manifest specifically in organs suited to them. The intellect manifests in the brain, not the hand. Yet a person's will governs all limbs equally. What is the nature of will that transcends the inner/specific-organ relationship that governs all other soul-powers?

**Central Concept**: The distinction between Keter (will/Ratzon) and the other Sefirot: the inner soul-powers (intellect, emotions) are each tied to specific organs/vessels, while Keter/will is unbounded and encompasses all without limitation. This explains why Keter is called a 'crown' — it rests atop and encompasses the entire head rather than being localized within it.

**Resolution**: Will (Keter) transcends the inner/outer (pnimi/mekif) distinction that applies to lower powers. While intellect and emotions enter and are limited by their respective organs, will operates from 'outside' all of them — governing without being contained. This is why Keter is uniquely capable of acting as the intermediary (ממוצע, mamtze'et) between Ein Sof and the Sefirot.

## Deep Dive

### The Construct Being Built

This maamar constructs a precise phenomenology of Ratzon (will) as the one soul-faculty that transcends the inner-power/specific-vessel (כח פנימי/כלי מיוחד) dyad that governs all other faculties — and derives from this the structural reason why Keter alone is capable of functioning as the cosmic intermediary (ממוצע) between Ein Sof and the Sefirot.

### The Opening Move

The maamar opens on the second day of Shavuot with a philosophical puzzle about the structure of the soul. Every inner soul-power requires a specific organ: intellect in the brain, sight in the eye, hearing in the ear — each power has its uniquely suited vessel and cannot truly inhabit another. Even when intellect seems to reach the hand (in skilled writing or craftsmanship), what is present there is only *practical intellect* (שכל מעשי), the outermost stratum of intellect, not its essence. Yet will — the capacity for desire and intention — seems to operate in all limbs equally: when a person wills to move their hand or foot, will is equally present everywhere. What is the nature of a soul-power that lacks a specific organ?

### The Argumentative Arc

- **The rule of inner faculties and specific vessels** is first demonstrated through the soul-body map: intellect inhabits the brain essentially; what reaches other limbs is only a derivative radiance (הארה). The Rambam's eight chapters (Shemonah Perakim) is cited in support of the view that even skilled hand-craft derives from an intellective power, not mere motor function — but that intellective power present in the hand is only the lowest layer of intellect, *sechel maasi*.

- **Will (Ratzon) is classified as Or Mekif (surrounding/encompassing light)** rather than Or Pnimi. The verse "as a shield, will crowns him" (כצנה רצון תעטרנו, Ps. 5:13) is read structurally: a shield surrounds from without and does not enter; it governs by encompassing. Every other faculty governs by *entering* its organ and being shaped by it; will governs by *surrounding* all organs without being shaped by any.

- **The analogy is then applied cosmically**: just as in the human soul Ratzon is the Or Mekif that encompasses all the Or Pnimi faculties (intellect, emotions, action), so in the divine structure Keter is the Or Mekif that encompasses all the Or Pnimi Sefirot beneath it (Chochmah through Malkhut). The Sefirot of Atzilut are lights clothed in vessels — Or Pnimi — while Keter remains as the encircling will that activates without being confined.

- **Keter's uniqueness as intermediary** is derived from this Or Mekif status: precisely because Keter is not defined by a specific vessel, it can maintain dual citizenship — simultaneously touching the Ein Sof above (which is also without vessel, also Or Mekif in its own right) and initiating the chain of Or Pnimi Sefirot below. No Sefirah below Keter could serve this function, because each is defined by and limited to its vessel.

- **The pre-will state and the awakening of will** are distinguished: before will is aroused even toward its own self, there is pure concealment — a state of no relationship, no directedness whatsoever. The moment will is aroused, even inwardly (without yet directing outward), it constitutes the first hint of a *shaykhus* (relationship/relevance) and thus the beginning of the chain of existence. This makes Keter the ontological hinge: the pre-will Ein Sof is absolutely transcendent; the awakening of Keter-Ratzon is the first event in the order of worlds.

### What It Gets To

The maamar establishes that Keter's capacity to serve as the intermediary between Ein Sof and the Sefirot is not accidental or merely positional (being the "top" Sefirah) but is structurally necessary: only an Or Mekif faculty that does not enter and is not defined by any vessel can bridge the absolutely transcendent (Ein Sof, which also transcends all vessels) and the immanent (the Sefirot, which are lights-in-vessels). The soul-body analogy is now fully mapped onto the cosmic structure.

### How This Fits the Hemshech

Where maamar 4 distinguished Ta'anug and Ratzon as Keter's two faces, and maamar 5 grounded Or Pnimi/Or Mekif in the structure of Atzilut, this maamar provides the *derivation* of why Keter must be Or Mekif from first principles — completing the conceptual triangle of Keter, Or Mekif, and intermediary function that underpins the entire hemshech's account of how infinite divine light becomes structured worlds.
