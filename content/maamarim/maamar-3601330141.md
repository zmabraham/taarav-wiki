## Context

This maamar was delivered during **Parshat Noach, Shabbat Rosh Chodesh 1905-06** in the year **עתר"ו** (1905–06), as part of the ongoing hemshech (continuous discourse) that Rebbe Shalom DovBer of Lubavitch (the Rashab) delivered over two decades. It forms part of **חלק ראשון (Part 1)** of the hemshech.

The Hemshech Taarav is the longest chassidic discourse ever composed, spanning from 5662 (1901-02) through 5680 (1919-20). Each maamar continues the thread of the previous, building a comprehensive Kabbalistic-Chassidic system centered on the nature of divine light, the structure of the sefirot, and the mission of the Jewish soul.

## Deep Dive

### The Construct Being Built

Delivered on a doubly-charged Shabbat — Parshat Noach coinciding with Shabbat Rosh Chodesh (the Shabbat that is also the New Moon of Cheshvan) — this maamar builds the hemshech's account of the relationship between the two Shabbat dimensions (the weekly cessation as Or Chozer's moment of reception) and the Rosh Chodesh dimension (the monthly renewal of Or Yashar, modeled on the moon's cycle of disappearance and reappearance). The Noach narrative provides the typological frame: the flood (mabul) as the total inundation of Or Yashar overwhelming the keilim (vessels) of creation, and the Teiva (ark) as the protected interior within which Or Chozer is preserved.

### The Opening Move

The double occasion generates a double opening: the Shabbat maftir (additional reading) from Numbers 28:9-10 ("U'v'yom haShabbat — On the Sabbath day...") and the Rosh Chodesh maftir (28:11-15) together with the Parshat Noach narrative create a three-way resonance. The opening paradox is structural: Shabbat is the cessation (shevita) from creative work, associated with Or Chozer's moment of maximal ascent; Rosh Chodesh is the renewal (chidush) of the moon, associated with Or Yashar's fresh descent into the monthly cycle; and Noach's flood is the destruction of the created order followed by the covenant (rainbow) that ensures its preservation. How do these three themes — cessation, renewal, and catastrophe-into-covenant — cohere in a single discourse?

### The Argumentative Arc

- **Shabbat as the weekly Or Chozer apex**: Building on the Bereishit maamar's account of creation's six days as Or Yashar's progressive descent into the physical Yesh, the Rashab develops Shabbat as the moment when that descent ceases (shevita) and the Or Chozer generated by the week's creative work ascends. But more specifically, Shabbat is the moment when the oneg (divine pleasure) that was concealed throughout the week's labor becomes revealed: as the source text for adjacent maamarim establishes, "v'nireh ha'oneg k'mo shahu b'peshitut" — the pleasure appears in its simple, undifferentiated form once the work of creation is complete.
- **Rosh Chodesh as the monthly Or Yashar renewal**: The moon's cycle models Or Yashar's monthly rhythm: the moon wanes to invisibility (Or Yashar at maximum concealment, corresponding to the tzimtzum) and then renews itself (Or Yashar resuming its descent in fresh, undiminished potency). The Kabbalistic tradition identifies the moon with Malchut and the sun with Tiferet (or Zeir Anpin), so the solar-lunar relationship models the Or Yashar (sun/Zeir Anpin) and Or Chozer (moon/Malchut) relationship. Rosh Chodesh is specifically the moment when Malchut (the moon) begins its new cycle of receiving Or Yashar — the Or Chozer that was generated by the previous month's avodah now descends as new Or Yashar into the coming month.
- **The Teiva (ark) as the protected vessel of Or Chozer during cosmic inundation**: The mabul (flood) is read through the hemshech as an image of what happens when Or Yashar descends with such overwhelming force that the normal keilim (vessels) of creation cannot contain it — the structures of hishtalshelut (the chain of worlds) are "flooded" by excess Or Yashar. The Teiva is the protected interior — a vessel strong enough to survive this excess by being enclosed (teiva = also "word" in Hebrew, suggesting the protective enclosure of Torah-language). Noach, as the soul that enters the Teiva, represents the Or Chozer that survives the cosmic inundation by retreating into its innermost enclosure — atzilus (the depth of self-nullification before God) rather than expansive engagement with the overwhelmed world outside.
- **The rainbow covenant as the permanent Or Yashar/Or Chozer balance**: The covenant's sign — the rainbow (keshet) — is the Rashab's image of the permanent, stable relationship between Or Yashar (the sunlight) and Or Chozer (the reflective medium of water-droplets that breaks the light into its constituent colors). The rainbow appears specifically when sun and rain are simultaneously present: Or Yashar (sun) and Or Chozer's medium (water/rain) coexist in a dynamic equilibrium. The divine promise "I will never again destroy all life" is the promise that Or Yashar will never again descend with such force as to make the keilim of Or Chozer incapable of containment — that the rainbow-balance is permanent.
- **Shabbat-Rosh-Chodesh synthesis**: The double occasion's unity is that both Shabbat and Rosh Chodesh are moments when the Or Yashar/Or Chozer circuit "resets" — Shabbat weekly, Rosh Chodesh monthly. The Noach narrative provides the cosmic backstory for why this circuit needs periodic resetting: the flood demonstrated what happens when Or Yashar and Or Chozer fall out of balance (Or Yashar overwhelming Or Chozer). The Shabbat and Rosh Chodesh cycles are the practical divine institutions that prevent this imbalance from recurring — they are, in the hemshech's framework, the temporal expressions of the rainbow covenant.

### What It Gets To

The maamar concludes that the two sacred times (Shabbat, Rosh Chodesh) and the Noach narrative converge on a single principle: the Or Yashar/Or Chozer circuit requires periodic moments of reset and renewal (shevita and chidush) to maintain its proper balance. Without Shabbat's weekly cessation (allowing Or Chozer to ascend fully before the next week's Or Yashar descent begins), and without Rosh Chodesh's monthly renewal (allowing Malchut to receive fresh Or Yashar after its period of concealment), the system would inevitably tend toward the flood-condition of imbalanced Or Yashar domination. The Teiva-principle (protective interiority) is the soul's individual mechanism for maintaining this balance during periods of overwhelming divine light — and it is the model for how Or Chozer is preserved even when the outer world of keilim cannot contain the Or Yashar.

### How This Fits the Hemshech

The Noach/Rosh-Chodesh maamar bridges the cosmic scale of Bereishit (the creation of Yesh from Ayin) with the patriarchal scale of Lech Lecha and Vayera (Avraham's individual engagement with divine service). The flood-narrative provides the hemshech with its most vivid image of what Or Yashar looks like when it exceeds the capacity of the created keilim — and the rainbow covenant provides the assurance that this excess will never again be permitted to destroy the Or Chozer infrastructure of creation. This prepares directly for Lech Lecha's account of Avraham as the first human being to develop the interior capacity (Teiva-principle) strong enough to contain and reflect Or Yashar without being overwhelmed by it.
