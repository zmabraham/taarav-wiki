## Context

This maamar was delivered during **Parshat Eikev 1901-02** in the year **תער"ב** (1901–02), as part of the ongoing hemshech (continuous discourse) that Rebbe Shalom DovBer of Lubavitch (the Rashab) delivered over two decades. It forms part of **חלק ראשון (Part 1)** of the hemshech.

The Hemshech Taarav is the longest chassidic discourse ever composed, spanning from 5662 (1901-02) through 5680 (1919-20). Each maamar continues the thread of the previous, building a comprehensive Kabbalistic-Chassidic system centered on the nature of divine light, the structure of the sefirot, and the mission of the Jewish soul.

## Deep Dive

### The Construct Being Built

This maamar builds the framework of *birurim* (refinements/clarifications) — the process by which divine sparks scattered within the physical world through the *shevirat ha-kelim* (breaking of the vessels) are elevated back to their source through physical mitzvot performed in the material world, establishing that this elevation (Or Chozer through physical action) is the unique capacity of the *eikev* (heel) dimension of the Jewish soul, and therefore the reason why mitzvot seemingly minor ("trampled by the heel") carry cosmic weight.

### The Opening Move

Parshat Eikev opens with the promise that if Israel fulfills "these laws" — including, specifically, those which Israel *tramples with their heel* (*eikev tishm'un*) — God will fulfill His covenant and abundantly bless them. The word *eikev* (heel) is the maamar's entry. The Midrash and the Zohar highlight that Eikev refers to mitzvot that people treat as unimportant — mitzvot that get "trampled." Why should such mitzvot receive the greatest reward? And why does the reward for these "minor" mitzvot consist specifically of material blessings (grain, wine, oil, the fruits of the land)? The structural question is: what is the relationship between the *eikev* (lowest point of a thing — the heel) and the material reward that follows from it?

### The Argumentative Arc

- **The *eikev* as the lowest point — and therefore the point of contact with the lowest world**: in the sefirot-anatomy, the foot/heel (yesod and malkhut's external expression) is the level at which the spiritual chain makes its final contact with the physical domain. The "heel" mitzvot are those that operate at this juncture — where the Or Yashar chain has descended as far as it goes and must transition into material action. Far from being minor, these are the most consequential structurally, because they complete the Or Yashar's descent and initiate the Or Chozer ascent.

- **The birurim (refinements) as the unique work of Or Chozer through physical action**: the divine sparks that fell into the physical world through shevirat ha-kelim are embedded in material objects — food, clothing, money, the natural world. When a Jew performs a physical mitzvah (eating matzah, wearing tzitzit, giving charity) with proper intention, the divine spark embedded in that physical object is "refined" (elevated) and returned to its source. This is Or Chozer at the most concrete level: not the Shema's intellectual unification (analyzed in the previous maamar) but physical action that draws Or Chozer upward through material objects.

- **Why "trampled" mitzvot carry more weight**: the sparks embedded in the most coarse, seemingly mundane physical objects require more powerful Or Chozer to be released, because they have fallen further from their source. The "heel" mitzvot — mitzvot involving material things that seem beneath spiritual notice — are the ones that engage the deepest-buried sparks. The reward consists of material blessings precisely because the spiritual work of birur (refinement) returns the physical-world sparks to their source, and this spiritual ascent generates corresponding material abundance as the physical domain becomes more transparent to the divine light.

- **The *eikev* of the Jewish soul**: the maamar develops the idea that just as the human body's heel is the point of greatest contact with the earth, the *eikev*-dimension of the Jewish soul (its lowest, most body-engaged aspect — the Nefesh HaBahamit's interface with the physical world) is the point where birur work is performed. This is paradoxically the most powerful point of Or Chozer, because it draws the Or Chozer from the deepest material embedding upward. Higher soul-levels (Neshamah, Chayah) operate in their own elevated domains; the Nefesh's *eikev* does the groundwork.

- **The chain from Mohin to Middot to speech/action** is brought in: the Or Yashar descends from Chochmah (Mohin) through the Middot (emotional Sefirot) to Malkhut/speech and finally to physical action. Each transition involves diminishment of the Or (as established in the Chukat-Matot maamarim). But the physical action, though it receives the most diminished Or Yashar, is precisely the point at which Or Chozer is generated — the turning of the light back toward its source. The *eikev* moment is the inversion-point of Or Yashar into Or Chozer.

### What It Gets To

The maamar establishes that physical mitzvot at the "heel" level (eikev) are the primary engine of birur (refinement of scattered sparks) and thus of Or Chozer at its most material register. Material blessings are not an incidental reward but the natural consequence of releasing trapped divine sparks from their physical embedding — when the sparks ascend, the physical world is purified and becomes abundant. The "trampled" mitzvah, far from being minor, is the structural point at which Or Yashar's descent completes and Or Chozer's ascent begins.

### How This Fits the Hemshech

Having established the Shema as Or Chozer at the intellectual-conscious level (Va'etchanan), the hemshech now, in Eikev, establishes Or Chozer at the physical-action level through birur. Together these two maamarim complete the hemshech's picture of Or Chozer as operating through the full range of human service — from the most exalted intellectual unification to the most mundane material mitzvah. This is the first time in the hemshech that the concept of dira betachtonim (divine dwelling below) is directly approached through the mechanism of birur.
