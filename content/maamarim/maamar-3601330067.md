## Context

This maamar was delivered during **Parshat Korach 1903-04** in the year **תרד"ע** (1903–04), as part of the ongoing hemshech (continuous discourse) that Rebbe Shalom DovBer of Lubavitch (the Rashab) delivered over two decades. It forms part of **חלק ראשון (Part 1)** of the hemshech.

The Hemshech Taarav is the longest chassidic discourse ever composed, spanning from 5662 (1901-02) through 5680 (1919-20). Each maamar continues the thread of the previous, building a comprehensive Kabbalistic-Chassidic system centered on the nature of divine light, the structure of the sefirot, and the mission of the Jewish soul.

The specific question focuses on understanding how the language used points to a deeper Kabbalistic reality within the hemshech's ongoing analysis.

**Central Concept**: This maamar continues the hemshech's development of the relationship between Or Yashar (direct divine light) and Or Chozer (reflected light), applying it to the specific occasion of Parshat Korach 1903-04. The Rashab develops the Kabbalistic framework of Sefirot and Partzufim to illuminate the Torah's discussion of the relevant theme.

**Resolution**: The chiddush reached in this maamar shows how the specific quality of divine service associated with ש"פ קרח, תרד"ע corresponds to a specific aspect of the Or Chozer dynamic — revealing how the human soul's action in the lowest world (Asiyah) draws divine light from the highest Keter-level, thereby advancing the hemshech's central theme of divine habitation in the lower realms (dira betachtonim).

## Deep Dive

### The Construct Being Built
This maamar erects a systematic analysis of the mochin-midot (intellect-emotion) relationship, establishing how intellectual illumination from above produces the "weakening" (chalishut) of the midot — not suppression but a productive modulation — and further how this chalishut is itself an initial form of התכללות (mutual interpenetration of opposites) within the sefirotic structure. The maamar then advances to the deeper level of התכללות where the midot not merely tolerate but actively receive from one another.

### The Opening Move
The parasha opens with Korach's rebellion: "In the morning God will make known who is His and who is holy, and He will bring near to Himself the one He chooses" (Numbers 16:5). The Midrash Rabbah records Moses's response: God divided His world just as He divided light from darkness — as the division of light and darkness serves the world's functioning (לתשמישו של עולם), so too the divisions among Israel (Israel from nations, Levites from Israelites, Aaron from Levites) serve the divine purpose. The question: what is the conceptual link between the cosmic division of light and darkness, and the social-spiritual differentiation of priestly grades? Korach challenged the differentiation — is there a principled response that goes beyond mere "God said so"?

### The Argumentative Arc
- The Rashab begins from the hemshech's established point: the sefirot of Tohu were in "full force" (תוקף), meaning each midah expressed itself absolutely without modulation by its opposite. The midot of Tikkun are in "weakened" form — not because they are less inherently powerful but because intellectual illumination (from the mochin) produces a natural softening.
- The mechanism is analyzed: Chesed in its full force is simply boundless giving, with no internal check — it cannot give room to Gevurah (limitation, boundary). But when Chokhmah illuminates Chesed, the Chesed itself becomes bounded — not by an external imposition but from within, because the Chokhmah-light reveals that the totality of divine intention requires both Chesed and Gevurah. This bounded Chesed is Chesed-with-Gevurah-within-it: the first stage of התכללות.
- The Rashab then introduces an important refinement: even the Chesed of Atzilut, in its boundless form, is operating within metziyut (existential presence). When the Chesed at the level of Arich (the very long one — the attribute-system of Keter) expresses itself, it is not in a mode of metziyut at all, so it naturally coexists with Gevurah without conflict. The distinction is: metziyut-Chesed (Tohu-mode and Ze'ir Anpin-mode) clashes with Gevurah; bitul-Chesed (Arich-mode) coexists with it.
- The Rashab now advances to a subtle point about Chesed at the level of Ratzon (Will, corresponding to the aspect of Chesed and Gevurah within Keter/Arich): at this level, the "opposites" are not even truly opposite. Chesed-of-will = desire for the good; Gevurah-of-will = rejection of the bad — these are one continuous act of will, not two competing ones. "Joy on this side and weeping on that side" (חדוה מסט' דא ובכי' מסט' דא — the famous teaching) — the more intensely one desires the divine, the more acutely one feels the pain of distance from it. These are not contradictions but expressions of the same fundamental will.
- The division of light and darkness is now resolved: the cosmic division serves the world precisely because it creates the conditions for Tikkun — for the interplay of giving and receiving, illuminating and concealing, that makes creation meaningful. Korach's error was to think that differentiation (Havdalah) is a deficiency — that a world of undifferentiated holiness would be superior. The Rashab's answer: without differentiation, there is no gift, no service, no meeting. The divisions are not arbitrary hierarchy but the structural requirement for relationship.

### What It Gets To
The maamar concludes with a refined account of chalishut (weakening of midot): it is not that Chesed becomes less than Chesed, but that within Chesed appears a limit — and that limit is the internal presence of Gevurah within Chesed. This is the beginning of real התכללות: not external coexistence but internal mutual containment. Aaron's appointment over the priestly service is the concrete embodiment of this principle: the high priest's role requires holding simultaneously the Chesed of giving blessing and the Gevurah of maintaining boundaries of holiness — not as a compromise between them but as a unified act of priestly service.

### How This Fits the Hemshech
Parshat Korach 5664 is a crucial pivot in the hemshech: it advances from the general analysis of Tohu/Tikkun to a precise account of how התכללות operates mechanically within the sefirot. The distinction between חסד דמ"ה (Chesed of Ze'ir Anpin — within metziyut) and רב חסד (Chesed of Arich — above metziyut) and חסד שברצון (Chesed at the level of Will — fully beyond polarity) provides the hemshech with a three-tiered model of divine generosity that will be used throughout the rest of the discourse. The parasha's political content (Korach's rebellion against priestly differentiation) is read as the human-social eruption of the Tohu-dynamic — the demand to erase distinctions — against which the hemshech's entire sefirotic architecture is the principled philosophical response.
