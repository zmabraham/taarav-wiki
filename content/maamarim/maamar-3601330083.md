## Context

This maamar was delivered during **Shemini Atzeret 1909-10** in the year **העת"ר** (1909–10), as part of the ongoing hemshech (continuous discourse) that Rebbe Shalom DovBer of Lubavitch (the Rashab) delivered over two decades. It forms part of **חלק שני (Part 2)** of the hemshech.

The Hemshech Taarav is the longest chassidic discourse ever composed, spanning from 5662 (1901-02) through 5680 (1919-20). Each maamar continues the thread of the previous, building a comprehensive Kabbalistic-Chassidic system centered on the nature of divine light, the structure of the sefirot, and the mission of the Jewish soul.

**Central Concept**: This maamar continues the hemshech's development of the relationship between Or Yashar (direct divine light) and Or Chozer (reflected light), applying it to the specific occasion of Shemini Atzeret 1909-10.
