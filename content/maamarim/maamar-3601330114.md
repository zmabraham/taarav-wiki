## Context

This maamar was delivered during **Parshat Emor 1909-10** in the year **העת"ר** (1909–10), as part of the ongoing hemshech (continuous discourse) that Rebbe Shalom DovBer of Lubavitch (the Rashab) delivered over two decades. It forms part of **חלק שני (Part 2)** of the hemshech.

The Hemshech Taarav is the longest chassidic discourse ever composed, spanning from 5662 (1901-02) through 5680 (1919-20). Each maamar continues the thread of the previous, building a comprehensive Kabbalistic-Chassidic system centered on the nature of divine light, the structure of the sefirot, and the mission of the Jewish soul.

## Deep Dive

### The Construct Being Built

This maamar — anchored in Parshat Emor's commandment of Sefirat HaOmer (the counting of seven complete weeks from Pesach to Shavuot) — constructs one of the hemshech's most sophisticated Kabbalistic distinctions: the difference between the Sefirot HaGnuzot (the concealed/hidden Sefirot, also called Ayin Beis — the 72 primordial Sefirot of Adam Kadmon prior to Tzimtzum) and the Sefirot HaGeluyot (the revealed Sefirot of Atzilut and below). The central move is to show that the 49 days of Omer-counting correspond to a structured traversal of the seven times seven sub-gradations of the revealed Sefirot, and that this traversal elevates human consciousness from the metziyut-level (existence-awareness) toward the mahut-level (essence-awareness) — from awareness that the divine light exists to actual grasp of its quality.

### The Opening Move

The verse: "וּסְפַרְתֶּם לָכֶם מִמָּחֳרַת הַשַּׁבָּת מִיּוֹם הֲבִיאֲכֶם אֶת עֹמֶר הַתְּנוּפָה שֶׁבַע שַׁבָּתוֹת תְּמִימֹת תִּהְיֶינָה" — "And you shall count for yourselves from the morrow of the Shabbat, from the day you bring the omer wave-offering, seven complete weeks shall there be." The Rashab raises multiple textual difficulties: (1) Why "for yourselves" (lachem) — this seems redundant; (2) why "from the morrow of the Shabbat" when the Torah means the morrow of the first day of Pesach (the Shabbat here being the holy day, not the weekly Sabbath) — why the ambiguous term? (3) Why is the additional marker "from the day you bring the omer wave-offering" needed after "from the morrow of the Shabbat"? (4) Most significantly: why "complete" (temimot) — what makes a week "complete," and the Midrash teaches that the weeks are "complete" only when Israel performs repentance (teshuvah). What is the connection between completeness-of-weeks and teshuvah?

### The Argumentative Arc

- **The prior context — kamayim panim el panim**: The Rashab recalls the framework from d"h "kamayim panim el panim" (as water reflects a face): just as a person looking into still water sees their own face reflected back, so the divine will (ratzon) was formed through the reflection of Israel's future service back toward the Ein Sof — the Ma'N (feminine waters, Or Chozer) that "rose" from the potential of future created beings caused the formation of the divine will (Ratzon), which then descended as Or Yashar. This is the fundamental Ratzo V'Shov at the level of primordial Keter, before creation. The Sefirat HaOmer is the experiential re-enactment of this primordial Or Chozer movement at the human-temporal scale.
- **The Sefirot HaGnuzot — concealment that is not mere hiddenness**: Using the Sefer Yetzirah's formulation "ten Sefirot without anything" (עשר ספירות בלי מה — bli mah, literally "without what"), the Rashab draws the distinction between two types of concealment: (a) concealment that is simply the hidden state of something that could be revealed (like the flame within a coal — the flame is present but not visible, yet it is still a flame); and (b) concealment that is so complete that there is no "thing" there to be revealed at all — it is "without what" because there is no entity present in any mode of existence. The Sefirot HaGnuzot are of the second type: they do not "exist" in any sense that the created world's knowing-apparatus can access. They are known only through their effects — through the Sefirot HaGeluyot that they source.
- **The three levels of concealment — coal, stone, and formless potential**: The Rashab refines the concealment typology: (1) the flame within the coal (aish bagachal) — concealed but in the mode of existence, a "concealment that is in existence" (he'elem sheYeshno b'Metziyut); (2) the potential for fire within flint (koach ha-eish b'Tzur HaChalamish) — not even a flame in concealed form, just the capacity for fire, the potentiality without any current actuality; (3) the capacity for intellect in the raw soul before any experience of thinking — utterly prior to any form of intellectual existence. The Sefirot HaGnuzot are of the third type: utterly prior to metziyut. The Sefirot HaGeluyot are of the first type; they exist, even if they are hidden from lower-world perception.
- **"Without what" — the impossibility of intrinsic existence for Gnuzot**: The critical Kabbalistic claim: the Sefirot HaGnuzot have no "metziyut" (existence in their own right) — they are names and descriptions applied from below, from the perspective of what they produce, not descriptions of what they are in themselves. This is expressed in the Rashab's teaching: it is analogous to a person's name. Before someone is addressed by name, the name is not "present" in them — it is a relational designation, not an intrinsic feature. Calling the person by name creates a correspondence, but the name was not "in" the person as a self-subsisting reality. Similarly, the Sefirot HaGnuzot are called "Chokhmah," "Binah," etc. by virtue of what they produce in the revealed Sefirot, but in themselves they have no such character — they are "Ein Sof" at the Sefirot-level, un-differentiated presence.
- **The Omer-count as traversal of the 7x7 gradient**: The seven weeks of seven days each (49 total) map the seven times seven sub-gradations of the Sefirot HaGeluyot: each week corresponds to one of the seven emotive Sefirot (Chesed, Gevurah, Tiferet, Netzach, Hod, Yesod, Malkhut), and each day within the week corresponds to the same seven Sefirot as they appear within the week's Sefirah. The counting "for yourselves" (lachem) means: this traversal is for the benefit of the counters — it is Or Chozer work, ascending through each Sefirah-level to make the soul's own Kli-structure transparent at each level.
- **Completeness (temimot) through teshuvah**: The Midrash's teaching that the weeks are "complete" when Israel does teshuvah is explained: teshuvah (returning/repentance) is the paradigm of Or Chozer at the human level — it is the soul's return to its divine source, the reversal of the downward-outward movement of Or Yashar back into its own origin. Each week of Omer-counting is "complete" (tamim) only when the Or Chozer movement through that week's Sefirah is completed — when the soul's relationship to, say, Chesed-in-Chesed is fully a returning/drawing-back-toward-source rather than merely an outward-flowing. Teshuvah is the faculty that makes each Sefirah's Or Chozer aspect activate fully.
- **The omer wave-offering and the dimension of Keter**: The "omer ha'tnufa" (wave-offering) involves waving the offering in all six directions (up, down, east, west, north, south) — encompassing all spatial orientation. This waving is the Or Chozer's assertion that it originates from and returns to a source that encompasses all directions simultaneously — the Keter-level that has no orientation because it is prior to the creation of directionality. The "morrow of the Shabbat" (the day after Pesach's rest) is the first day of the traversal — the moment when the Or Chozer's countable/structured ascent begins, having been "charged" by Pesach's supra-temporal Sovev-irruption.

### What It Gets To

The maamar concludes that the Omer-count is not simply a bridging ritual between Pesach and Shavuot but is the human traversal of the entire architecture of the Sefirot HaGeluyot, driven by Or Chozer (teshuvah) toward the revelation of the Sefirot HaGnuzot at Shavuot. The "completeness" of each week is the progressive transparency of the Kli at each Sefirah-level, allowing the Gnuzot-level (primordial, pre-existence) to be "visible" through the Geluyot-level (revealed). Shavuot — the giving of Torah — is the moment when the traversal is complete and the Sefirot HaGnuzot's un-differentiated presence is revealed as Torah's infinite content.

### How This Fits the Hemshech

Parshat Emor's Omer-analysis is the hemshech's most detailed account of how Or Chozer operates through the structured time-sequence of Jewish practice. The 49-day count maps the Or Chozer's graduated ascent (against the Or Yashar's graduated descent) through all levels of the Sefirot HaGeluyot, with the goal of making the Kelim at each level "complete" (tamim/transparent). The distinction between Sefirot HaGnuzot and HaGeluyot will remain active throughout the remainder of the hemshech wherever the Rashab needs to distinguish between the Or's pre-existence dimension (what cannot be received as such, only as trace-effects) and its operative dimension (what the Kelim can actually hold and reveal).
