## Context

This maamar was delivered during **Parshat Naso 1909-10** in the year **העת"ר** (1909–10), as part of the ongoing hemshech (continuous discourse) that Rebbe Shalom DovBer of Lubavitch (the Rashab) delivered over two decades. It forms part of **חלק שני (Part 2)** of the hemshech.

The Hemshech Taarav is the longest chassidic discourse ever composed, spanning from 5662 (1901-02) through 5680 (1919-20). Each maamar continues the thread of the previous, building a comprehensive Kabbalistic-Chassidic system centered on the nature of divine light, the structure of the sefirot, and the mission of the Jewish soul.

## Deep Dive

### Opening Kushya

The maamar opens with a precise textual difficulty: the Torah commands "נשא את ראש בני גרשון גם הם" — "lift the head of the sons of Gershon, also them." The word "גם הם" (also them) implies that the Gershonites are secondary, receiving the elevation of "lifting of the head" (נשיאת ראש) only by extension. The primary recipients must be the sons of Kehat. The Rashab sharpens this: "וצ"ל למה נא' בבני גרשון גם הם דמשמע מזה דעיקר ענין נשיאת ראש הוא בבני קהת" — why does the Torah say "also them," implying that the essential nesi'at rosh belongs to Kehat alone? And the Midrash's answer — that Kehat was placed first only "מפני כבוד התורה" (for the honor of the Torah) — itself demands explanation: what is the inner meaning of kavod haTorah such that it creates this hierarchy?

### Conceptual Architecture

The Rashab builds the answer through a layered metaphysical argument. He begins with the cosmic dynamic of tzimtzum and gilui (contraction and revelation): the tzimtzum exists for the sake of revelation, and the revelation requires the tzimtzum — "הצמצום הוא בשביל... שבהצמצום כלול כוונת הגילוי." This yields the principle of ratzo v'shov (running and returning): all vitality — divine, angelic, and human — moves in an oscillation of histalkut (withdrawal) and hitpashut (expansion). The maamar's central illustration is anatomical: the circulation of blood through the heart. When blood contracts into the heart (כיווץ) and is then expelled outward (התפשטות), it is precisely the contraction that generates the force of the outward flow — "דוקא ע"י הסגירה וההעלם מתחדש כחו ומתפשט בכח ועוז בכל האברים." Remove the contraction, and the expansion loses its power entirely.

This physical dynamic is identified with the neshama's service: the term "נשמה" itself, the Rashab notes, derives from the word for breathing (שאיפה), inhale and exhale — the soul is constitutively defined by ratzo v'shov. The ratzo is the burning desire described as "רשפי אש התשוקה והצמאון ליכלל בעצמות אוא"ס ב"ה," a longing so total that the soul finds no satisfaction in any revelation, however lofty — "גם בהגילוים שבעולם בהאור והחיות האלקי... אין לו בזה שום חיות ועונג כלל." The shov is the subsequent drawing-down of Torah, the inner illumination that quiets the longing and draws divine light into the soul.

### Key Chassidic Concepts Invoked

- **רצוא ושוב** (Ratzo v'Shov) — the oscillating rhythm at every level of reality: blood, neshama, sefirot, Chayot haMerkava
- **צמצום וגילוי** (Tzimtzum and Gilui) — contraction as the precondition for revelation
- **פנימיות וחיצוניות הכתר** (Inner and Outer dimensions of Keter) — Torah draws from the inner Keter, mitzvot from the outer
- **אהבה דרעותא דליבא** (Ahavat Re'uta deLiba) — the essential love arising from the innermost point of the heart, the ratzo of the soul
- **מטי ולא מטי** (Reaching and not reaching) — the mode by which divine light inhabits vessels without being fully absorbed, enabling them to exist

### Resolution and Chiddush

The resolution is precise. Kehat carries the Aron, which contains the Luchot — i.e., Torah. Torah is described as "יקרה היא מפנינים" (more precious than pearls/peninin), because the peninin are set in the outer crown, while Torah draws from "פנימית הכתר," the inner dimension of Keter. Nesi'at rosh means the elevation into the inner Keter. The sons of Kehat, as bearers of the Aron, perform the shov — the drawing-down of inner divine light — and thus uniquely embody nesi'at rosh. The Gershonites carry the curtains (יריעות), which correspond to the or hamakif (surrounding light) and to the ratzo — the love and longing. They too reach the level of Keter, but its outer dimension: "זהו עכ"פ בבחי' הכתר." Hence "גם הם" — they too achieve nesi'at rosh, but derivatively. Kavod haTorah means precisely the primacy of the inner Keter over the outer: the chiddush is that the service of ratzo (love, longing, Gershon) and the service of shov (Torah, inner light, Kehat) are both elevations into Keter, but the drawing-down of Torah touches a deeper interiority — and that is why it takes precedence.
