## Context

This maamar was delivered during **Parshat Pinchas 1903-04** in the year **תרד"ע** (1903–04), as part of the ongoing hemshech (continuous discourse) that Rebbe Shalom DovBer of Lubavitch (the Rashab) delivered over two decades. It forms part of **חלק ראשון (Part 1)** of the hemshech.

The Hemshech Taarav is the longest chassidic discourse ever composed, spanning from 5662 (1901-02) through 5680 (1919-20). Each maamar continues the thread of the previous, building a comprehensive Kabbalistic-Chassidic system centered on the nature of divine light, the structure of the sefirot, and the mission of the Jewish soul.

## Deep Dive

### The Construct Being Built
This maamar uses the figure of Pinchas — who channeled zealousness (kina'ut) for God into an act that halted the plague and earned him a "covenant of peace" — to develop the hemshech's analysis of how the union of apparently opposite qualities (zealousness/violence and peace/covenant) is possible at the sefirotic level, extending the Korach maamar's account of Chesed and Gevurah's integration into the realm of human action. The parasha's subject also includes the detailed enumeration of the festival offerings, which provides a structural parallel to the sefirotic structure of Or Yashar (the downward-flowing light of fixed divine bestowal).

### The Opening Move
The parasha opens: "Pinchas son of Elazar son of Aaron the priest turned My wrath from the children of Israel, acting zealously for My sake among them" (Numbers 25:11). The striking theological question is immediately apparent: God rewards an act of zealous violence — spearing two people in flagrante — with a "covenant of peace" (brit shalom) and the gift of priesthood. How is the most extreme act of Gevurah (forceful boundary-setting, even lethal force) the basis for receiving the Chesed of a perpetual covenant of peace? This is the Tohu-question in reverse: if Tohu's Chesed and Gevurah could not coexist, why does Pinchas's Gevurah produce Chesed?

### The Argumentative Arc
- The hemshech has established that in Tikkun, Chesed contains Gevurah internally and vice versa. Pinchas embodies this principle in action: his kina'ut was not an expression of personal rage or self-assertion (which would be Tohu-mode Gevurah) but of love — specifically, love for the divine and for Israel. The act of force arose from Chesed-consciousness (love for God, love for Israel's integrity) rather than from Gevurah-consciousness (anger at the violators for their affront to his sense of propriety).
- The Rashab deploys the hemshech's category of chalishut: precisely because Pinchas's midah of Gevurah was "softened" by the mochin-light of his love for God, his Gevurah was capable of being an act of peace. Unstipulated Gevurah (Tohu-mode) destroys; Chesed-infused Gevurah (Tikkun-mode) heals.
- The second half of the parasha — the enumeration of the festival offerings — provides the structural counterpart: the musaf (additional) offerings for each festival are described in precise, fixed quantities. This is Or Yashar in its most regularized form: the divine bestowal of light descending according to a fixed, divinely-calibrated schedule (the festival cycle), independent of human initiative. Pinchas's act is Or Chozer — human initiative that draws down divine response; the festival offerings are Or Yashar — divine initiative flowing at fixed times.
- The names of Pinchas's genealogy (son of Elazar, son of Aaron) emphasize his priestly lineage: the priestly consciousness that Aaron embodied (Menorah-directionality from Beha'alotcha) is now incarnated in zealous action. The priest is not merely the performer of ritual but the human locus where Or Yashar (the divine offerings) and Or Chozer (human devotion) meet.

### What It Gets To
The maamar establishes Pinchas as the paradigm of integrated Tikkun-action in the world: when human Gevurah arises from divine Chesed-motivation, the act of boundary-enforcement becomes an act of healing and covenant-making. The brit shalom is not a reward for violence but the natural consequence of a Gevurah that was Chesed all along. The festival offerings then ground this individual exemplum in the cosmic structure: the calendar of divine bestowal is the background against which individual human action (Or Chozer) occurs, and the meeting of the two constitutes the fullest expression of the divine-human relationship.

### How This Fits the Hemshech
Parshat Pinchas 5664 extends the hemshech's analysis of midot-integration into the domain of ethical action and historical event, showing that the sefirotic structure is not merely a theoretical map but a grammar of how human deeds either replicate Tohu's fragmentation or enact Tikkun's integration. This is the hemshech's characteristic movement: from abstract Kabbalistic structure to historical-narrative application to practical spiritual implication. The festival-offerings analysis also advances the hemshech's treatment of Or Yashar as the fixed divine framework within which Or Chozer operates, a theme that will be developed further in the Matot-Masei and Devarim maamarim.
