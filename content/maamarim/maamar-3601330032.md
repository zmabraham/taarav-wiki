## Context

This maamar was delivered during **Parshat Vayeshev, 20 Kislev 1902-03** in the year **תער"ג** (1902–03), as part of the ongoing hemshech (continuous discourse) that Rebbe Shalom DovBer of Lubavitch (the Rashab) delivered over two decades. It forms part of **חלק ראשון (Part 1)** of the hemshech.

The Hemshech Taarav is the longest chassidic discourse ever composed, spanning from 5662 (1901-02) through 5680 (1919-20). Each maamar continues the thread of the previous, building a comprehensive Kabbalistic-Chassidic system centered on the nature of divine light, the structure of the sefirot, and the mission of the Jewish soul.

## Deep Dive

### Opening Question (Kushya)

The maamar opens on the verse פדה בשלום נפשי מקרב לי כי ברבים היו עמדי and the rabbinic teaching that one who engages in Torah, gemilut chasadim, and communal prayer is as if he redeemed God and His children from among the nations. The Rashab then poses two sharp questions: (1) What does redemption (פדייה) mean when applied above — what is there to redeem on the divine level? (2) Why specifically these three acts — Torah, gemilut chasadim, and tefillah be-tzibbur — and not private prayer?

### The Argument, Step by Step

To resolve this, the maamar draws on the earlier hemshech analysis of ויקח יעקב אבן וירימה מצבה. The Zohar states that the matzevah represents the elevation of Malkhut from the state of a single point (נקודה) to a full Partzuf — and that this depends specifically on Yaakov, who is Tiferet. The Pardes explains: מצבה הוא יחוד ת"ת ומל', the matzevah is the union of Tiferet and Malkhut.

The maamar then builds the cosmological picture: the ten sefirot divide into five upper sefirot (פנימיות) and five lower sefirot (חיצוניות). Malkhut similarly has an inner dimension that receives constant light from Ze'ir Anpin, and an outer dimension that is clothed in the worlds of Beri'ah, Yetzirah, and Asiyah — where light reaches only in concealment. The state described as קיימא סיהרא באשלמותא — the moon standing in fullness — means that even Malkhut's outer, lower five sefirot are illuminated with revealed light, not just constricted light. The analogy is the full moon: the half of the sphere facing away from the sun (representing the lower sefirot of Malkhut that face the void of the worlds) shines fully only at the middle of the month.

This fullness occurred in the time of Shlomo (the fifteenth generation from Avraham), in the Beit HaMikdash, and existed before the sin of Adam. It is sourced not from the sequential chain of hishtalshelut but from פנימיות ועצמות אוא"ס — the innermost level of Ein Sof that transcends the chain entirely.

The vehicle of this drawing-down is specifically Yaakov-Tiferet, because Tiferet is the middle line (קו האמצעי) that draws from פנימיות הכתר — the inner Keter — all the way through to the lowest level of Malkhut, without interruption and without changing its essential quality at each stage: כללות הקו היא כמהות אחד ממש. This is the chiddush of Yaakov over Avraham and Yitzchak: the Patriarchs operated in the mode of מקיף (encompassing light), but Yaakov initiated the inner union — יחוד פנימי — drawing the essence of the Infinite into the vessels below.

In practice, this movement has two phases: העלאה (ascent) — the letters of prayer, refined from the natural soul's desires through hitbonenut that transforms bitter into sweet (מהפך מרירא למיתקא); and המשכה (drawing down) — the letters of Torah, pulling divine light from above into material reality, so that even a physical hand giving tzedakah becomes a vessel for Ein Sof, as the arm of Chesed in Atzilut.

### The Chiddush and Resolution

The Rashab concludes: פדי' במל' — the redemption is the redemption of Malkhut itself from its state of concealment in the lower worlds. Torah and gemilut chasadim effect this בשלום, peacefully, through the drawing-down of light, while communal prayer reaches the level of עצמות אוא"ס — as the rabbis say, the community year-round is like an individual on the Days of Awe. The three acts together constitute the complete cycle: ascent through prayer, revelation through Torah, and embodiment through acts of kindness. David said פדה בשלום נפשי because his soul, a כללית נשמה (universal soul), itself functions as a tzibbur — and through Torah and gemilut chasadim, the redemption of Malkhut occurs without struggle, in peace.
