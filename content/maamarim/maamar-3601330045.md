## Context

This maamar was delivered during **Parshat Devarim 1902-03** in the year **תער"ג** (1902–03), as part of the ongoing hemshech (continuous discourse) that Rebbe Shalom DovBer of Lubavitch (the Rashab) delivered over two decades. It forms part of **חלק ראשון (Part 1)** of the hemshech.

The Hemshech Taarav is the longest chassidic discourse ever composed, spanning from 5662 (1901-02) through 5680 (1919-20). Each maamar continues the thread of the previous, building a comprehensive Kabbalistic-Chassidic system centered on the nature of divine light, the structure of the sefirot, and the mission of the Jewish soul.

## Deep Dive

### The Construct Being Built
This maamar — delivered on Shabbat Chazon, Parshat Devarim, which opens the fifth book of Moses — builds a detailed analysis of Adam Kadmon (א"ק, the primordial cosmic structure) as the all-inclusive intermediary between Ein Sof and all subsequent world-structures, using the concept of "place" (מקום / Makom) as the key to understanding how א"ק encompasses all levels without being reducible to any of them. The maamar simultaneously resolves the question of why Devarim (Mishneh Torah) differs from the other four books of Torah in its mode of speech.

### The Opening Move
The maamar's opening question is literary and theological: the four earlier books of the Torah are narrated in the "third person of the recounting" (שלישי המדבר) — a narrator reporting that "God spoke to Moses." Neither Moses nor God is the narrator; an unnamed narrator reports the dialogue. This is the classical prophetic mode. But Devarim opens differently: "אלה הדברים אשר דבר משה" — "these are the words that Moses spoke." Here Moses is the speaker from his own mouth. The difficulty is logical: if Moses is speaking "from his own mouth" in Devarim, how can Devarim be Torah — the word of God? And if it is God's word, why does it present as Moses's own speech? The answer the maamar will develop connects the unique status of Devarim to the unique status of Moses within the Or Yashar / Or Chozer framework.

### The Argumentative Arc
- The maamar begins by analyzing the three-level structure of initial formation: חומר (hyle/matter) → צורה (form) → תיקון (structure/arrangement). Matter is pure potential without actualized essence; form is the essential definition abstracted from matter; structure/arrangement is the actualized, differentiated existence. This tripartite structure is then correlated to the Kabbalistic sequence: א"ק (Adam Kadmon) → עקודים (the "bound" world) → נקודים (the "dotted" world) → אצילות and beyond.
- Why is א"ק specifically called "Adam" (Human)? No other Partzuf (divine configuration) is called by this name — Atzilut is described as having the form of a human (כמראה אדם על הכסא), but א"ק is "Adam" in its essence, not merely in its form. The reason: "Adam" in its deepest sense means "the being that encompasses all levels." The human being in the lower world encompasses all four levels of existence (דצח"מ — Domem/mineral, Tzomeyach/vegetable, Chai/animal, Medaber/speaking): the human body itself is mineral (its mineral composition), the human hair is vegetable (it grows), the human vitality is animal, and the human intellect is medaber. No other creature encompasses all four.
- Moreover, within the human's intellectual faculties themselves, all four levels are present: "domem-intellect" = practical/technical knowledge (external and static), "tzomeyach-intellect" = natural-pattern recognition (organically growing understanding), "chai-intellect" = the intellect expressed in speech (responsive, relational), "medaber-intellect" = pure self-reflective cognition (the intellect knowing itself).
- א"ק encompasses all levels of all worlds — from the highest (the first point of the Kav) to the lowest (the final level of Asiyah) — not as separate zones within it but as undifferentiated potentials. This is the sense in which א"ק is the cosmic "Adam": it includes all that will exist without any of it yet existing in differentiated form.
- The concept of "Makom" (Place): place is not spatial location but ontological "weight" or "standing" — the inherent value and rank of a being within the cosmic order. The divine name "HaMakom" (used in the Midrash on the Patriarchs, and addressed in the previous maamar on Balak) means that God is the "Place of all" — all things have their ontological standing within and through the divine. א"ק is the first "place" within the divine that has a relationship to the lower worlds — it is the cosmic structure that gives all subsequent structures their "place."
- The difference between א"ק and all subsequent Partzufim: עקודים (the next level after א"ק) is the beginning of the actualization of specific vessel-structures — the ten lights are "bound" in one vessel, but they are now specifically ten. א"ק contains the ten-structure in a completely undifferentiated mode — not ten in one vessel but one that is the root of all ten without being ten.
- Mishneh Torah / Devarim: Moses in Devarim speaks "from his own mouth" because at this point in the cosmic drama (the fortieth year, the threshold of the Land), Moses has completed his role as the channel of Or Yashar (the first four books: God → Moses → Israel). Devarim represents the Or Chozer phase: having fully received the divine light through Or Yashar, Moses now embodies it so completely that his own speech is indistinguishable from God's speech. This is the state described in Zohar as "Shekhinah midebberet mitoch gronno" — the divine Presence speaks through Moses's throat. This is the Or Chozer having ascended to the point where it is identical with the Or Yashar at its source: the voice of the human is the voice of the divine, not by merger but by the complete actualization of the divine image within the human vessel.
- The Or Chozer at its maximal state (Moses speaking Devarim) = א"ק at its relationship to the lower worlds: just as א"ק contains all worlds in an undifferentiated mode, Moses's speech in Devarim contains all the Torah in a mode that is simultaneously most personal (his voice, his perspective, his words) and most universal (God's absolute word). The deepest self-expression and the purest divine revelation coincide.

### What It Gets To
The maamar arrives at the hemshech's culminating insight for the year תער"ג: the maximum of Or Chozer (human voice as fully actualized divine channel) and the maximum of Or Yashar (divine will fully present in the finite) are not two different states but the same state — what the hemshech has been calling "dira betachtonim" (divine dwelling in the lower realms). Devarim as a book embodies this: it is Moses's most personal speech and simultaneously the purest Torah. The cosmic structure of א"ק, which encompasses all worlds in undifferentiated simplicity, finds its human analogue in Moses-speaking-Devarim, who encompasses all divine teaching in the simplicity of his own personal voice.

### How This Fits the Hemshech
This maamar closes the first major arc of Hemshech Taarav. Beginning with the initial analysis of Or Yashar and Or Chozer, through the structure of the Sefirot, the hidden Sefirot in the Kav, the Shavuot installation of a permanent channel, the specific modalities of mitzvah-engagement (Menorah, Parah Adumah, vows, zealotry), and the Atzilut-Torah-as-intermediary analysis, the hemshech has arrived at the point where it can name the final state: the Or Chozer that fully ascends is identical to the Or Yashar that fully descends — and this identity is not a philosophical abstraction but an experiential reality, embodied in Moses's voice in Devarim and in the messianic future when this will be the universal condition. The subsequent years of the hemshech will deepen and ramify from this pinnacle.
