## Context

This maamar was delivered during **Parshat Noach 1902-03** in the year **תער"ג** (1902–03), as part of the ongoing hemshech (continuous discourse) that Rebbe Shalom DovBer of Lubavitch (the Rashab) delivered over two decades. It forms part of **חלק ראשון (Part 1)** of the hemshech.

The Hemshech Taarav is the longest chassidic discourse ever composed, spanning from 5662 (1901-02) through 5680 (1919-20). Each maamar continues the thread of the previous, building a comprehensive Kabbalistic-Chassidic system centered on the nature of divine light, the structure of the sefirot, and the mission of the Jewish soul.

## Deep Dive

### Opening Question

The maamar opens with the verse **"צהר תעשה לתיבה ואל אמה תכלנה מלמעלה"** (Bereishit 6:16) and immediately raises a compound kushya. Rashi presents two opinions: *צהר* denotes either a window (חלון) or a precious stone (אבן טובה) that illuminates the ark. The Rashab demands: what is the inner meaning of the ark requiring light at all, and what is the difference between the two opinions? A second difficulty: why does the Torah interrupt the ark's dimensional specifications — already giving its height as thirty cubits — to insert the *tzahar* command, only then resuming with "ואל אמה תכלנה"? The juxtaposition implies a conceptual link: **מהו השייכות** — what is the connection between *tzahar* and the directive about the cubit?

### The Conceptual Argument Step by Step

The Rashab builds on foundations from earlier sections of the hemshech, where מספר (number) was shown to operate on two levels: **מספר הכלים** — the numbering of the vessels (sefirot), which involves genuine limitation and demarcation — and **מספר האורות** — the numbering of the lights, which appears as number but is in reality without limit, since the lights cling to their source in Ein Sof.

From there the maamar enters the Shelah's paradox: the Talmud (Bava Metzia) teaches that blessing does not rest on something measured and counted, yet the Shelah elsewhere cites a midrash that the counting of Israel *causes* the Shekhinah to dwell. The resolution distinguishes two kinds of counting. **מספר דעוה"ז** — the numbering of this world — imposes finite definition and separation; it is the mode of *kol she-darko limnot* (כל שדרכו למנות), rooted in *yesod* as it extends through the left side toward the externals (חיצונים), associated with concealment and limitation. **מספר דעוה"ב** — the numbering of the World-to-Come — is the mode of *es she-darko limnot* (את שדרכו למנות), where *את* signals self-nullification; this numbering corresponds to the letters of Torah and to the light of the sefirot that, though apparently discrete, carry infinite force from their root: **"מספר אשר לא יספר"** — a counting that does not count.

The distinction maps onto divine providence: **השגחה דשם אלקים** operates through natural concealment and intermediaries; **השגחה דשם הוי'** is direct luminous oversight, as expressed in "המוציא במספר צבאם" — God draws out the celestial host *by number*, meaning each individual star is held in existence by a direct act of divine attention, reflecting that their perpetuity is not intrinsic but continuously sustained by Ein Sof will.

The neshamos of Israel are then shown to stand higher than even the celestial host, for the stars are created beings whose permanence is externally maintained, whereas neshamos are **אלקות ממש** — actual divinity — rooted in *shem Havayah* and dwelling in dveikut with their source even when descended into bodies. Their counting therefore draws down *hashraas ha-Shekhinah* in the fullest sense.

### Resolution and Chiddush

The maamar arrives at its resolution of the פסוק. The Baal Shem Tov's teaching — that **תיבה** (the ark) alludes to the *words* (תיבות) of prayer — unlocks everything. The ark's illumination by *even tovah* (precious stone) corresponds to the purification of the *nefesh ha-behamit* through prayer: when the animal soul's materiality is subdued (כפיית החומריות), the letters of prayer become luminous stones, and the illumination they generate is **כצהרים** — like noon-brightness — denoting the revelation of *atzmus ohr ein sof* itself. As the Noam Elimelech notes, prayer transforms the letter-combination of צרה (distress) into צהר (radiance).

**ואל אמה תכלנה** is then read as the dimension of *bitul ha-kelim*: "אמה" alludes to *achas hi yonasi* — the *yichud* of mesiras nefesh be-echad — and "תכלנה" (be completed, converge) describes the minimizing and nullification of the vessels so that they can receive maximal light. This is the principle of *ekudim*: one vessel, no shattering. The chiddush is that the two opinions in Rashi (window / precious stone) map onto two modes of avodah — the *tzaddik* who draws Ein Sof directly through *mesiras nefesh* (חלון, open aperture to the above), and the *beinoni* who achieves luminosity through suppression of the animal soul (אבן טובה, the refined stone). Both routes converge on the same goal: **המספר דעוה"ז** becomes **מספר אשר לא יספר** — the finite, concealed world is permeated with boundless divine light.
