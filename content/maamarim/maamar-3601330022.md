## Context

This maamar was delivered during **2nd day of Rosh Hashana 1902-03** in the year **תער"ג** (1902–03), as part of the ongoing hemshech (continuous discourse) that Rebbe Shalom DovBer of Lubavitch (the Rashab) delivered over two decades. It forms part of **חלק ראשון (Part 1)** of the hemshech.

The Hemshech Taarav is the longest chassidic discourse ever composed, spanning from 5662 (1901-02) through 5680 (1919-20). Each maamar continues the thread of the previous, building a comprehensive Kabbalistic-Chassidic system centered on the nature of divine light, the structure of the sefirot, and the mission of the Jewish soul.

## Summary

**Opening Question**: On Rosh Hashanah (2nd day of Rosh Hashana 1902-03), the Rashab addresses the tension between divine kingship (מלכות, Malchut) and divine transcendence: if God is truly infinite and beyond all relationship with creation, how can there be a meaningful 'coronation' (הכתרה) of God as king? What does it mean to declare 'the Lord reigns' when God transcends all categories of governance?

**Central Concept**: The distinction between God's essential being (עצמות, Atzmus) and God's revelation as king (מלכות, Malchut) — Malchut is the Sefirah through which the infinite becomes relationally accessible to created beings without compromising the divine transcendence. The Rashab develops how the concept of 'king' points precisely to the aspect of divinity that interfaces with those it governs.

**Resolution**: The 'coronation' of Rosh Hashanah refers not to a change in divine being but to a heightened revelation of Malchut — the dimension of divine kingship that is always present but becomes more accessible to finite perception on this day. The Or Chozer of the collective soul's prayer on Rosh Hashanah uniquely draws down this revelation.

## Deep Dive

### The Construct Being Built

Delivered on the *yom* (day) of the second day of Rosh Hashana — a counterpart to the previous evening's maamar — this discourse develops the daytime dimension of the Rosh Hashana Or Mekif revelation. Where maamar 21 analyzed the *leil* (night) as belonging to the hiddenness that allows Or Mekif to be the dominant register, this maamar constructs the *yom* as the mode in which Or Mekif and Or Pnimi *jointly* manifest — and uses this joint manifestation to explain the unique power of Rosh Hashana's shofar-blowing as Or Chozer.

### The Opening Move

The maamar returns to the same paradox of divine kingship raised on the previous night, but approaches it from the daytime perspective: by day, the Or Pnimi of the world is active and differentiated, yet the Rosh Hashana liturgy still insists on declaring God king over everything (*Adon kol ha-neshamot*). The question is whether and how the Or Mekif-dimension of Malchut can be accessed *despite* — or perhaps *through* — the day's Or Pnimi illumination, rather than in its absence.

### The Argumentative Arc

- **Day as the register of Or Pnimi**: Daytime in the kabbalistic framework corresponds to the active manifestation of Or Pnimi through the sefirot and the worlds — the structured, differentiated flow of divine light through the chain of being (*hishtalshelut*). This is the register of *avodah* (divine service), prayer with conscious kavanah, and the performance of mitzvot that draws on the differentiated structure of the sefirot.

- **Shofar as the sound of Or Mekif**: The shofar blast is analyzed as a qualitatively different kind of Or Chozer from ordinary speech-prayer. Articulated speech (the prayers of Rosh Hashana) operates within the Or Pnimi register — it is structured, differentiated, directional. The shofar's raw, pre-linguistic cry, by contrast, bypasses the vessel-structure entirely and rises directly to the Or Mekif level. This is why the shofar, as an *undifferentiated* sound, can access the *undifferentiated* level of the divine.

- **The dual Malchut experience**: On the second day of Rosh Hashana, the joint daytime experience makes possible a dual-mode engagement with Malchut: the Or Pnimi liturgical address to God-as-King operates within the sefirot-structure, while the shofar simultaneously reaches the Or Mekif level of Malchut that transcends all structure. This dual engagement is the specific innovation of the second day over the first.

- **Collective coronation in the day register**: The hemshech's earlier argument (maamar 21) that collective Or Chozer is required to reach Malchut is here developed: on the day of Rosh Hashana, when the full community gathers for the shofar-blowing, the collective voice of the shofar constitutes an Or Chozer of maximum amplitude — reaching precisely the Or Mekif level of Malchut that individual prayer cannot access.

- **Teshuva and Or Chozer**: The season of *Aseret Yemei Teshuva* is introduced as the context within which the Rosh Hashana Or Chozer continues to operate. Teshuva is characterized as the paradigmatic Or Chozer process: the return of the scattered sparks of Or Pnimi to their Or Mekif source, reversing the descent of divine light through the agency of the repentant soul.

### What It Gets To

The maamar establishes that the daytime Rosh Hashana experience integrates both modes of divine light — Or Pnimi through structured liturgy, Or Mekif through the shofar's undifferentiated cry — into a single event of coronation that is more complete than either mode alone could achieve. The shofar is thus not merely a ritual act but the acoustical signature of Or Mekif becoming accessible in the midst of Or Pnimi's active reign.

### How This Fits the Hemshech

Maamar 21 analyzed the night-mode of Or Mekif revelation; this maamar provides the day-mode, completing the full Rosh Hashana cycle. Together they establish a template the hemshech will use repeatedly: every sacred occasion has both an Or Pnimi dimension (structured, differentiated service) and an Or Mekif dimension (undifferentiated, essential contact), and the occasion's full spiritual significance requires both. This template will be applied to Sukkot, Shemini Atzeret, and eventually to Shabbat and the entire calendar cycle.
