## Context

This maamar was delivered during **Parshat Matot 1902-03** in the year **תער"ג** (1902–03), as part of the ongoing hemshech (continuous discourse) that Rebbe Shalom DovBer of Lubavitch (the Rashab) delivered over two decades. It forms part of **חלק ראשון (Part 1)** of the hemshech.

The Hemshech Taarav is the longest chassidic discourse ever composed, spanning from 5662 (1901-02) through 5680 (1919-20). Each maamar continues the thread of the previous, building a comprehensive Kabbalistic-Chassidic system centered on the nature of divine light, the structure of the sefirot, and the mission of the Jewish soul.

## Summary

The specific question focuses on understanding how the language used points to a deeper Kabbalistic reality within the hemshech's ongoing analysis.

**Central Concept**: This maamar continues the hemshech's development of the relationship between Or Yashar (direct divine light) and Or Chozer (reflected light), applying it to the specific occasion of Parshat Matot 1902-03. The Rashab develops the Kabbalistic framework of Sefirot and Partzufim to illuminate the Torah's discussion of the relevant theme.

**Resolution**: The chiddush reached in this maamar shows how the specific quality of divine service associated with ש"פ מטות, תער"ג corresponds to a specific aspect of the Or Chozer dynamic — revealing how the human soul's action in the lowest world (Asiyah) draws divine light from the highest Keter-level, thereby advancing the hemshech's central theme of divine habitation in the lower realms (dira betachtonim).

## Deep Dive

### The Construct Being Built
This maamar builds the hemshech's detailed theory of measurement within the divine light-system by distinguishing two types of "measuring rod" (קו המדה vs. קנה המדה — the measuring line of Gevurah vs. the measuring reed of Chesed), using this to explain why vows (נדרים) in the Torah are addressed to "the heads of the matot" rather than "the heads of the shvatim," and to deepen the analysis of how Torah's illumination differs from the Patriarchs' illumination.

### The Opening Move
The maamar opens with the verse: "וידבר משה אל ראשי המטות לבני ישראל לאמר — זה הדבר אשר צוה ה'" (Moses spoke to the heads of the matot [staffs/tribes]). The Rashab raises three questions: (1) What is the difference between "shvatim" (tribes, the usual word) and "matot" (staffs/rods)? The Torah uses both, and their difference must be meaningful. (2) Why is the subject of vows (נדרים) specifically connected to matot and not shvatim? (3) What is the significance of the preliminary declaration "זה הדבר" (this is the matter) — why not simply begin with the laws of vows?

### The Argumentative Arc
- The hemshech has been building toward a comprehensive theory of divine "measurement" — how the Or Yashar is calibrated and shaped for each level of the world-system. This maamar now provides the most developed treatment of that theory, centered on the distinction between קו המדה (measuring line) and קנה המדה (measuring reed), drawn from the Zohar on Parshat Pekudei.
- The fundamental principle: every divine emanation (every Or Yashar entering the world-system) must be measured — calibrated to the receiving vessel. This measurement operates in two dimensions: (a) the measurement of the Or as it exists within the divine system (i.e., how much of the source-light enters the Kav); (b) the measurement of the Or as it will be received by the created vessel (how the Or is calibrated to the capacity of the specific world-level that will receive it).
- קו המדה (measuring line) = the dimension of Gevurah / restriction: it determines the limit of each Sefirah's expansion so that the next lower Sefirah can emerge. Without this "stop," the upper Sefirah would expand infinitely and no lower Sefirah could exist. This is the principle by which the Keter does not simply overwhelm all of Chochmah, and Chochmah does not simply overwhelm all of Binah — each is "cut off" at the right point to allow the next level to emerge.
- קנה המדה (measuring reed) = the dimension of Chesed / extension: the reed grows in water (a symbol of Chesed, which flows and nourishes), and represents the positive measurement that gives each Sefirah the fullness of its own content. The reed measures not to limit but to ensure completeness.
- This gives the maamar its central distinction: קו המדה and קנה המדה are not two different acts of measurement but two aspects of the same measurement — the Gevurah-aspect that stops the upper from overflowing, and the Chesed-aspect that ensures the lower is fully constituted.
- The human soul analogy: when a person is in a state of passionate divine will (רצון/retzón) — the Keter-level — they cannot simultaneously be in a state of intellectual deliberation (the Chochmah-Binah level). The two states are mutually exclusive in their moment of full expression. The קו המדה creates the "stop" at the Keter level that allows Chochmah to emerge; without this stop, the will would flood out all intellect. Similarly, the Keter must "step back" (withdraw its full expansion) to allow Chochmah to exist as its own entity.
- Shvatim vs. Matot: "שבט" means a tribe — organic, natural, living growth, like a tree-branch that is still alive and growing. "מטה" means a staff/rod — cut from the tree, no longer organically growing, but now rigid, permanent, capable of being used as an instrument of measurement and authority. The matot-state corresponds to the קנה המדה / קו המדה level — the stabilized, measured, instrument-level of divine service, as opposed to the shvatim-level of living organic growth.
- Vows (נדרים) are addressed to the matot-heads because vows are, by definition, acts of measurement: the vow-maker takes an aspect of reality (a permitted object or action) and draws a boundary around it ("I hereby restrict this to holiness"). This is quintessentially the function of the measuring rod — the establishment of a divine boundary within the world. Only the matot-leaders (those who carry the quality of measured, stabilized divine authority) are qualified to loose or bind vows — i.e., to operate the measuring-rod function within the human community.
- The preliminary "זה הדבר" (this is the matter): this phrase is unique in Torah — ordinarily God speaks "וידבר ה' אל משה לאמר" and Moses conveys the message. Here, "זה הדבר" — "this is the matter" — signals that what follows is not a conveyed transmission but a direct embodiment of divine speech in the human speaker (Moshe as navi). The maamar analyzes this as an instance of Or Chozer reaching directly to the level of Shem Havayah — Moses speaking "from his own mouth" (מפי עצמו) in a way that is simultaneously the word of God, because Moses's Or Chozer ascended to the point of identity with the divine will.

### What It Gets To
The maamar arrives at a complete theory of divine measurement: every Or Yashar is simultaneously measured by the Gevurah-restriction of קו המדה and constituted by the Chesed-fullness of קנה המדה, and this dual measurement is what makes the world-system possible — without it, upper lights would overwhelm lower vessels. The distinction between shvatim and matot maps the distinction between organic divine growth (natural Or Yashar / Or Chozer dynamics within hishtalshelut) and measured divine authority (the stabilized instrument-level that can establish binding boundaries within the system).

### How This Fits the Hemshech
This maamar provides the hemshech's most technically detailed treatment of the measurement-theory of divine light, establishing that the Or Yashar / Or Chozer circuit is not a simple two-way flow but is regulated by a dual-measurement system (Gevurah-limit + Chesed-constitution) at every level. This is the theoretical foundation for the subsequent maamar on Masei, which will analyze the "journeys" of Israel through the wilderness as corresponding to the measured steps of Or Yashar's descent and Or Chozer's ascent through the graduated world-system.
