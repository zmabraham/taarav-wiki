## Context

This maamar was delivered during **Parshat Tetzaveh 1909-10** in the year **העת"ר** (1909–10), as part of the ongoing hemshech (continuous discourse) that Rebbe Shalom DovBer of Lubavitch (the Rashab) delivered over two decades. It forms part of **חלק שני (Part 2)** of the hemshech.

The Hemshech Taarav is the longest chassidic discourse ever composed, spanning from 5662 (1901-02) through 5680 (1919-20). Each maamar continues the thread of the previous, building a comprehensive Kabbalistic-Chassidic system centered on the nature of divine light, the structure of the sefirot, and the mission of the Jewish soul.

## Deep Dive

### The Construct Being Built

Building directly on Teruma's taxonomy of Orot, Kelim, and Levushim, this maamar deploys Parshat Tetzaveh — the parasha of priestly garments and the eternal Menorah light — to analyze the specific function of the Levush as the mechanism through which Or Yashar acts upon a reality outside its own essential nature. The Kohen (priest) in his bigdei kehuna (priestly vestments) becomes the paradigm of a being that is itself a Levush: he is not the Or, not the Kli, but the medium through which the Or's power operates in the world beyond its own domain.

### The Opening Move

Parshat Tetzaveh opens with the word "וְאַתָּה תְּצַוֶּה" — "and you shall command." The Rashab marks a textual peculiarity: Moshe's name does not appear anywhere in this entire parasha, yet the parasha is addressed specifically to him ("you"). The absence of name combined with the direct address "you" (אתה) points to something in Moshe's function that transcends the named-individual level — he operates here from a dimension above the identity conferred by a name. This is the supra-rational dimension of Keter, which exceeds all the named Sefirot. Simultaneously the parasha commands the eternal lamp (ner tamid) — light that burns continuously, day and night, without interruption. These two facts — Moshe's anonymous "you" and the ner tamid's continuity — frame the central question: what is the nature of a light that is not contingent on any recipient, and who is the agent that sustains it?

### The Argumentative Arc

- **The Levush as functional medium**: Following from Teruma's fourfold schema, the Levush is that through which the Or acts upon what is beyond the Kli's own essence. The bigdei kehuna are the Torah's most explicit example of this: the priestly garments do not reveal or receive the Or (as a Kli would) but enable the Kohen to perform the Temple service — to act upon the realm of the sacrificial animals, the incense, the showbread — which are all below the Kli-level of Atzilut. The garments are what makes the Kohen's action effective in the lower domain.
- **Moshe as the paradigmatic Levush-operator**: Moshe's name's disappearance from Tetzaveh is explained by the Rashab through this very framework. In all other parashot, Moshe acts as the named mediator — the Kli that receives G-d's word and transmits it. But in Tetzaveh, he acts purely as "you," the anonymous second person — the chitzoniyut-Kli dimension that channels the Or's power outward without personally receiving and revealing it. He is the Levush-function personified: hence no name.
- **The ner tamid and Or Ein Sof's continuous presence**: The ner tamid cannot be explained as a physical lamp alone — the Talmud acknowledges that the lamp's miraculous burning was a sign of the Shekhinah's presence. The "tamid" (continuous) character of this light corresponds to the Or Ein Sof's mode of presence in the world: the Soveiv Kol Almin (encompassing light) is not subject to interruption because it does not pass through the contraction-and-expansion cycle of Or Yashar. The ner tamid is the physical sign that the Levush-dimension of divine presence — the encompassing rather than the penetrating light — sustains reality from moment to moment.
- **Pure olive oil as chokhmah**: The oil for the Menorah must be "shemen zayit zakh" (pure pressed olive oil). In the Rashab's framework, oil — which is the extractable essence of the olive, itself a fruit whose center is hidden beneath layers of flesh — is the standard analogue for Chokhmah: the innermost essence of the intellect, compressed and pure. The Menorah-light thus runs specifically on Chokhmah-energy, not on the distributed light of Binah or the emotive faculties. This means that the ner tamid sustains itself from the highest readily-accessible level of the Seder Hishtalshelut, not from the actualized emotional-behavioral plane.
- **The priestly garments and the structure of Levushim in BYA**: The eight priestly garments of the Kohen Gadol and the four of the ordinary Kohen map the two tiers of Levushim in the worlds below Atzilut. The eight garments (corresponding to the eight letters of "Aharon" in certain gematria traditions, and to the eight categories of the Mishkan's cosmic structure) correspond to the eight levels at which the Or operates in Beriah through its Levush-enclosure. The four garments of the ordinary Kohen correspond to the four worlds and the four letters of the divine Name as they appear in clothed mode.

### What It Gets To

The maamar establishes that the Levush-function is not a deficiency but a distinct ontological capacity: it is specifically through Levushim that Or Yashar is able to act upon the lowest worlds. The Kohen in his garments, Moshe as the anonymous "you," and the ner tamid as continuous Encompassing-light all instantiate this same principle. The practical conclusion for divine service (avodah) is that there exists a mode of relationship with G-d that does not require the full transparency of Kli-unification — it can operate through the bounded, operational mode of the Levush.

### How This Fits the Hemshech

Tetzaveh completes the dyad begun in Teruma: Teruma established what a Kli is and how it differs from a Levush; Tetzaveh establishes what a Levush does and why it is necessary. Together these two maamarim equip the hemshech with the analytic tools — the full Orot/Kelim/Levushim schema — needed for the climactic analysis of the Mishkan's structure and the mechanisms by which divine light reaches into the material world. The disappearance of Moshe's name becomes the hemshech's most concentrated symbol of the Keter-level's supra-named character.
