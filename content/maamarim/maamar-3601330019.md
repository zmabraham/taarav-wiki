## Context

This maamar was delivered during **Parshat Ki Tavo 1901-02** in the year **תער"ב** (1901–02), as part of the ongoing hemshech (continuous discourse) that Rebbe Shalom DovBer of Lubavitch (the Rashab) delivered over two decades. It forms part of **חלק ראשון (Part 1)** of the hemshech.

The Hemshech Taarav is the longest chassidic discourse ever composed, spanning from 5662 (1901-02) through 5680 (1919-20). Each maamar continues the thread of the previous, building a comprehensive Kabbalistic-Chassidic system centered on the nature of divine light, the structure of the sefirot, and the mission of the Jewish soul.

## Deep Dive

### The Construct Being Built

This maamar advances the hemshech's theory of Or Mekif by investigating the mechanism through which encompassing light *generates* inner-world revelation — specifically, how the Or Mekif acts as the generative root (*shoresh*) of the Or Pnimi without itself descending into the vessel-structure of Atzilut. The kabbalistic construct is the relationship between *ratzon* (will) as the functional analogue of Or Mekif and the inner sefirot as its disclosure.

### The Opening Move

The parasha of Ki Tavo (Deuteronomy 26) opens with the bikkurim (first-fruits) ceremony — the Jew brings produce to the Temple and declares a narrative of descent and ascent (*"Arami oved avi…"*). The difficulty the Rashab raises is why the *act of bringing* (biah) is the vehicle for divine blessing, and how physical arrival at a sacred threshold connects to the cosmic structure of the hemshech. The key word *ki tavo* — "when you come" — is the counterpart to *ki tetze* of the previous maamar; both together map the two directions of divine service: going out (Or Mekif engaging the world) and coming in (the return of Or Chozer to its source).

### The Argumentative Arc

- **Or Mekif as root of all inner revelation**: Building directly from maamar 18, the Rashab deepens the proof that the Or Mekif is not simply "external" but is the actual *origin* (*shoresh*) of every inner-world light. All Or Pnimi — the light that clothes itself within vessels and sefirot — derives its existence and its quality from the Or Mekif above it.

- **The bikkurim gesture as Or Chozer**: The act of *bringing* the first fruits maps onto the dynamic of Or Chozer: the lower-world product (physical fruit of Asiyah) is returned to its source (the Temple, site of concentrated Or Pnimi), effecting the upward movement that completes the circuit of Or Yashar's descent. This *biah* — the arrival at the threshold — is precisely the moment when Or Chozer reaches its apex.

- **Ratzon as the connector**: The concept of *ratzon* (divine will) is introduced as the intermediate faculty that bridges Or Mekif and Or Pnimi. Will is not a "thing" that descends into vessels but a directedness toward the desired object — it connects without clothing itself. This makes ratzon the functional analogue of Or Mekif operating at the level of Keter: present everywhere, determining the quality of every inner-world light, yet never becoming one of the sefirot.

- **Bikkurim and dira betachtonim**: The physical performance of bikkurim — bringing produce from the lowest world into the Temple — is shown to be the paradigmatic act of *dira betachtonim*: it demonstrates that the infinite (the divine will behind creation) actually wishes to dwell in the lowest, most particular expressions of reality. The "coming in" is not merely ritual but the world's own Or Chozer ascending.

- **The two directions as a complete system**: Together with maamar 18, this maamar establishes that *tetze* and *tavo* — going out and coming in — are the two poles of a single dynamic: Or Mekif projects downward (enabling *tetze*), and Or Chozer returns upward (constituting *tavo*). The complete circuit is the hemshech's structural spine.

### What It Gets To

The maamar establishes that every act of bringing-in (*biah*) in divine service is an instance of Or Chozer returning to its Keter-source, and that this return is made possible by — and simultaneously *reveals* — the Or Mekif that initiated the original descent. The chiddush is that physical mitzvah performance (here: bikkurim) is not merely an ethical or legal act but a cosmic one: it completes the circuit of divine light and effects actual changes in the structure of the worlds.

### How This Fits the Hemshech

Maamar 18 established the Or Mekif as the generative root of all inner-world vitality. This maamar now provides the *return arc* — how that vitality circles back through human service. Together they constitute the first full iteration of the hemshech's fundamental dynamic: light descends via Or Mekif/Or Yashar and ascends via Or Chozer, with human divine service as the mechanism of the return. All subsequent maamarim will elaborate specific aspects of this circuit.
