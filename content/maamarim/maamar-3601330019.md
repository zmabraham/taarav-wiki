## Context

This maamar was delivered during **Parshat Ki Tavo 1901-02** in the year **תער"ב** (1901–02), as part of the ongoing hemshech (continuous discourse) that Rebbe Shalom DovBer of Lubavitch (the Rashab) delivered over two decades. It forms part of **חלק ראשון (Part 1)** of the hemshech.

The Hemshech Taarav is the longest chassidic discourse ever composed, spanning from 5662 (1901-02) through 5680 (1919-20). Each maamar continues the thread of the previous, building a comprehensive Kabbalistic-Chassidic system centered on the nature of divine light, the structure of the sefirot, and the mission of the Jewish soul.

## Deep Dive

The maamar opens with a cluster of questions on the verse "וְהָיָה כִּי תָבוֹא אֶל הָאָרֶץ אֲשֶׁר ה' אֱלֹקֶיךָ נֹתֵן לְךָ נַחֲלָה וִירִשְׁתָּהּ וְיָשַׁבְתָּ בָּהּ." The kushya is threefold: (1) why does the verse shift register — "כי תבוא" implies arriving by one's own power, yet immediately "ה' אלקיך נותן לך" speaks of a gift from above; (2) what is the difference between "נתינה" (gift) and "ירושה" (inheritance), since both seem to be about possessing the land; and (3) why does "וישבת בה" add anything once gift and inheritance are already stated? Additionally, the Sifri's comment that one should "perform the mitzvah whose reward is entering the land" raises a further question: the land is the reward for all the mitzvot collectively — so what single mitzvah uniquely unlocks it?

The maamar resolves all of this by mapping the verse's three expressions onto three distinct levels of divine light that have been developed throughout the hemshech. The framework is אור פנימי versus אור מקיף, and within אור מקיף a further distinction between טהירו תתאה (the general encompassing light of אדם קדמון, which relates to the worlds after the tzimtzum) and טהירו עילאה (the primordial circle before the tzimtzum, "עיגול הגדול שלפני הקו"). The key move: "ארץ הוא לשון רצון... וזהו ה' אלקיך נותן לך דהתגלות הרצון פשוט זהו שנותנים מלמעלה" — "land" (ארץ) connotes will (רצון), and simple or transcendent will can only be received as a gift from above, not achieved by one's own effort.

The three levels then resolve the three clauses: (1) "נתינה" corresponds to the Or Mekif of Adam Kadmon — the simple will revealed through prayer and intellectual contemplation (עבודה שע"פ טו"ד), which is granted from above but passes when the contemplation ends; (2) "ירושה" corresponds to the deeper Or Mekif of טה"ע, accessed specifically through birur (refinement) of the animal soul — "ביטול החומריות... מגיע להאור מקיף דטה"ע" — which, like an inheritance, is permanent and cannot be lost; (3) "וישבת בה" points to the or mekif becoming or pnimi — the encompassing light radiating inward — which occurs through Torah study after prayer, and corresponds eschatologically to the future revelation when the kav and the surrounding infinite light fully unite.

The chiddush: the single mitzvah the Sifri refers to is precisely this graduated avodah — prayer-based contemplation, followed by refinement of the lower soul, followed by Torah — and each stage unlocks a qualitatively different level of divine light, not merely a quantitative reward. Physical divine service (birur of the body's materiality) uniquely reaches the highest encompassing level because "שרש הכלים הוא מהאו"מ" — the root of vessels lies in the encompassing light — making bodily self-nullification the only path to that register.
