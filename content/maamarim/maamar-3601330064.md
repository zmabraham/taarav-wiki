## Context

This maamar was delivered during **Parshat Naso 1903-04** in the year **תרד"ע** (1903–04), as part of the ongoing hemshech (continuous discourse) that Rebbe Shalom DovBer of Lubavitch (the Rashab) delivered over two decades. It forms part of **חלק ראשון (Part 1)** of the hemshech.

The Hemshech Taarav is the longest chassidic discourse ever composed, spanning from 5662 (1901-02) through 5680 (1919-20). Each maamar continues the thread of the previous, building a comprehensive Kabbalistic-Chassidic system centered on the nature of divine light, the structure of the sefirot, and the mission of the Jewish soul.

## Summary

**Opening Question**: Opening the discourse on Parashat נשא (Parshat Naso 1903-04), the Rashab raises a question about a specific verse or teaching from the portion that connects to the ongoing analysis in the Hemshech Taarav. The specific question addresses a difficulty in understanding the relationship between an aspect of divine service described in the parasha and the Kabbalistic framework being developed throughout the hemshech.

**Central Concept**: The parasha's specific teaching is used as a lens to further develop the hemshech's analysis of Or Yashar and Or Chozer (direct and reflected light), and their practical implications for divine service. The Rashab connects the parasha's narrative or legal content to the cosmic structure of Sefirot, Partzufim, and the chain of worlds (hishtalshelut).

**Resolution**: The resolution integrates the parasha's teaching into the hemshech's ongoing thread: the specific mitzvah or narrative of the portion is shown to correspond to a specific aspect of the divine system being analyzed. The chiddush (novel insight) illuminates how the physical performance of Torah law draws divine light from the highest Keter-level into the lowest practical reality.
