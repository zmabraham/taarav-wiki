## Context

This maamar was delivered during **Parshat Nitzavim 1909-10** in the year **העת"ר** (1909–10), as part of the ongoing hemshech (continuous discourse) that Rebbe Shalom DovBer of Lubavitch (the Rashab) delivered over two decades. It forms part of **חלק שני (Part 2)** of the hemshech.

The Hemshech Taarav is the longest chassidic discourse ever composed, spanning from 5662 (1901-02) through 5680 (1919-20). Each maamar continues the thread of the previous, building a comprehensive Kabbalistic-Chassidic system centered on the nature of divine light, the structure of the sefirot, and the mission of the Jewish soul.

**Central Concept**: This maamar continues the hemshech's development of the relationship between Or Yashar (direct divine light) and Or Chozer (reflected light), applying it to the specific occasion of Parshat Nitzavim 1909-10. The Rashab develops the Kabbalistic framework of Sefirot and Partzufim to illuminate the Torah's discussion of the relevant theme.

**Resolution**: The chiddush reached in this maamar shows how the specific quality of divine service associated with ש"פ נצבים, העת"ר corresponds to a specific aspect of the Or Chozer dynamic — revealing how the human soul's action in the lowest world (Asiyah) draws divine light from the highest Keter-level, thereby advancing the hemshech's central theme of divine habitation in the lower realms (dira betachtonim).

## Deep Dive

### The Construct Being Built

This is the culminating maamar of the autumn-parasha arc, and it builds one of the hemshech's most sophisticated constructs: a three-level typology of ratzo (ascending movement toward Ein Sof) corresponding to three modes of biru'rim (refinements), mapped onto the verse's enumeration of the assembled community's grades (from "ראשיכם" through "חוטב עציך"). The maamar then establishes that "hayom" (today) refers to Rosh Hashana — the day when all levels simultaneously stand (nitzavim) before God — and uses this simultaneity to prove that Or Chozer from the lowest level (woodcutter) is not inferior but structurally necessary to the Or Chozer from the highest level (tribal heads), because it is specifically from below that the divine pleasure (oneg) of dira betachtonim is activated.

### The Opening Move

"Atem nitzavim hayom kulachem lifnei Hashem Elokeichem — You are standing today, all of you, before the Lord your God — your heads, your tribes... from the woodcutter to the water-drawer" (Deut. 29:9-10). The opening presents two interlocked questions from the source text: (1) "Hayom" — "today" — is identified as Rosh Hashana (following the principle that wherever "hayom" appears in Scripture it refers to the Day of Judgment: as the Targum Yonatan renders Job 1:6 "And it was on a day — and the day was the great Day of Judgment of Rosh Hashana"). If it is specifically Rosh Hashana, what is the meaning of "nitzavim kulachem" — standing, all of you — on that day? (2) The verse says "kulachem" (all of you) and then enumerates distinct levels — if they are all equal, why enumerate them? If they are enumerable as distinct, in what sense are they "all" equal?

### The Argumentative Arc

- **Three modes of ratzo (ascending) from the source text**: The Rashab develops a threefold typology from the hemshech's prior analysis (referenced as "d'h. mah she'nomar" from earlier maamarim): (1) The ratzo of the lower worlds (biy"a) through the biru'r of psoles (separating the spark from the dross) — this is the service of the woodcutters and water-drawers, those whose avodah is primarily physical and whose Or Chozer ascends through the laborious extraction of sparks from the densest material; (2) The ratzo of the keilim of Atzilut — a more refined ascent that bypasses the dross-biru'r entirely and operates on the level of zikkuch (purification) of the vessels themselves, corresponding to the re'uat da'at (depth of knowledge) of the inner spiritual faculties; and (3) The ratzo of the Orot (lights of Atzilut) — a still higher ascent that has nothing to do with biru'r at all, since biru'r is a category that applies only to keilim (vessels), not to lights. This corresponds to ahavat ta'anugim (love rooted in delight) that arises from the soul's essential nature (etzem haneshama) and its delight in Ein Sof itself.
- **Three corresponding divine revelations**: Each form of ratzo draws down a corresponding Or Chozer from above: (1) the first draws the Or of Atzilut (the light of the structured emanation, rooted in the external aspect of Atik); (2) the second draws the Or above the chain of worlds (l'ma'alah min hahishtalshelut), specifically the three upper aspects of Atik as they clothe themselves in Binah of Atik; (3) the third draws the pnimiyut of Atik (the innermost aspect of Atik), the light of divine self-contained delight that does not emerge for the sake of worlds at all.
- **"Kulachem" — the simultaneity principle**: The maamar's key move is that on Rosh Hashana, all three forms of ratzo occur simultaneously. This is the meaning of "kulachem" — all of you, together. The woodcutter and the tribal head are not performing hierarchically distinct avodot that happen to coincide; they are performing the same fundamental act of Or Chozer from their respective levels, and the simultaneity generates a collective Or Chozer that draws all three tiers of divine light at once. This is structurally impossible at any moment other than Rosh Hashana, which is why "nitzavim" — standing firm, simultaneously — is specifically associated with "hayom."
- **"Hoday'ta ha'yom v'hashevota el l'vavecha — You shall know today and take it to heart"** (Deut. 4:39, cross-referenced in the source text): The maamar brings this verse as a proof-text for the epistemological structure of Rosh Hashana. "Yadata" (knowing) corresponds to the Or Chozer from the level of da'at (knowledge), which integrates the Or Yashar (Havayah) with the Or of limitation (Elokim = tzimtzum). The verse's formula "ki Havayah hu ha'Elokim" — that the Divine Name of infinite revelation (Havayah) is identical with the Name of limiting governance (Elokim) — is the declaration that Or Yashar and Or Chozer are not opposed but are two aspects of a single divine act. Knowing this "hayom" (on Rosh Hashana) means the soul's da'at penetrates to the level where tzimtzum and giluy (concealment and revelation) are experienced as unified.
- **The Tzimtzum-as-preparation-for-Giluy principle**: The source text develops the crucial argument (following the d'h. v'chol ha'am ro'im) that the tzimtzum (cosmic contraction) precedes the giluy (revelation) not merely chronologically but ontologically: the tzimtzum is structured specifically in order to enable a particular quality of giluy (Or Pnimi — inner light, rather than Or Makif — encompassing light). The darkness (lailah) precedes the light (yom) in the sequence of creation precisely because the intended quality of the light (inner, assimilated, constitutive of reality rather than merely surrounding it) requires the prior darkness as its generative condition. This means: "You shall know today" — the Rosh Hashana revelation of inner light — is only possible because of the year-long preceding process of tzimtzum (the soul's engagement with the concealed lower world).

### What It Gets To

The maamar resolves both opening questions: (1) "Hayom" is Rosh Hashana because only on this day is the Or Pnimi (inner divine light) revealed at the level of the lowest created beings — the woodcutter and water-drawer — meaning the day when Or Chozer from the absolute bottom of the ladder reaches the absolute top. (2) "Kulachem" refers not to equality of level but to equality of access: on Rosh Hashana, the Or Yashar descends to the level of every soul simultaneously (through the structure of "Havayah hu ha'Elokim"), and therefore every soul's Or Chozer — however modest its starting point — reaches the same divine destination. The woodcutter's Or Chozer reaches Pnimiyut Atik on Rosh Hashana not because the woodcutter has climbed but because the divine system descends all the way to the woodcutter's level and draws the Or Chozer upward.

### How This Fits the Hemshech

Nitzavim is the halachic and theological climax of the autumn arc within the second volume of Hemshech Taarav. The three-level ratzo typology synthesizes everything the hemshech has been building: the distinction between biru'r mi'toch psoles (Shoftim's body-gates work), zikkuch hakeilim (the more interior gate-governance), and ahavat ta'anugim (the yechida-level seeing established in Re'eh) is now shown to be a complete system of Or Chozer, with Rosh Hashana as the structural singularity where all three levels fire simultaneously. The maamar also advances the hemshech's longest-running project: the proof that Or Pnimi (inner light that genuinely constitutes created reality) is superior to Or Makif (encompassing light that merely surrounds it) — and that human avodah in the lower world, precisely because it is embedded in material reality, generates Or Pnimi rather than merely Or Makif.
