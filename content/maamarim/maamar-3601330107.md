## Context

This maamar was delivered during **Parshat Vayikra 1909-10** in the year **העת"ר** (1909–10), as part of the ongoing hemshech (continuous discourse) that Rebbe Shalom DovBer of Lubavitch (the Rashab) delivered over two decades. It forms part of **חלק שני (Part 2)** of the hemshech.

The Hemshech Taarav is the longest chassidic discourse ever composed, spanning from 5662 (1901-02) through 5680 (1919-20). Each maamar continues the thread of the previous, building a comprehensive Kabbalistic-Chassidic system centered on the nature of divine light, the structure of the sefirot, and the mission of the Jewish soul.

## Deep Dive

### The Construct Being Built

With Sefer Vayikra and its opening parasha of korbanot (sacrifices), the hemshech enters a new phase: from the construction of the dwelling (Mishkan) to the service performed within it. This maamar develops the thesis that korbanot (sacrifices) are the paradigm of the Or Chozer movement — the upward elevation of the material world toward its divine source — and that the word "korban" itself (from karov, to draw near) encodes the fundamental mechanism: the elevation of lower matter into proximity with what is above it.

### The Opening Move

The parasha opens with G-d calling to Moshe from the Ohel Moed (Tent of Meeting): "ויקרא אל משה וידבר ה' אליו מאהל מועד לאמר" — "And He called to Moses, and the L-rd spoke to him from the Tent of Meeting." The Rashab notes that the divine call (kria) precedes the speech (dibbur). In all other instances where G-d speaks to Moses, the speech is the primary act. Here, uniquely, the calling is mentioned first and separately. What is a kria that is distinct from the subsequent dibbur, and why does it originate specifically from the Ohel Moed — the completed Mishkan — rather than from Sinai or any other location?

### The Argumentative Arc

- **Kria (calling) as the Or Yashar's summons from the Mishkan**: The divine kria is the Or Yashar now speaking from within the completed Mishkan — the vessel that Or Chozer's labor has prepared. The "calling" is the first movement of Or Yashar from its now-established dwelling-point in the lower world: it is the divine light addressing Moses from below (from the Ohel Moed) rather than from above (from Sinai). This is a reversal: or Yashar now speaks from the position it has attained through Or Chozer's construction-work.
- **Korban as Or Chozer in action**: The word "korban" (sacrifice) derives from karov (near). The Rashab develops this: the korban is the mechanism by which the material world draws near to its divine source. An animal offering involves: the selection of an animal (the lowest material level), its slaughter (separation of the life-force from matter), the burning on the Mizbeach (altar — the transformation of matter into fire and smoke), and the ascent of the aroma toward heaven. This is a precise recapitulation of Or Chozer's movement through every level of the Seder Hishtalshelut: from material (animal) through vital-force (blood) through transformation (fire) through the subtle-material (smoke and aroma) toward the transcendent source.
- **The animal-types and their Sefirot-correlates**: The three types of korban mentioned in Vayikra's opening (cattle-bakar, sheep/goats-tzon, and birds-of) correspond to the three tiers of Or Chozer. Cattle represent the Or Chozer that originates from Asiyah's material level; sheep/goats represent the Or Chozer that originates from Yetzirah's formative level; birds (which fly and ascend naturally) represent the Or Chozer that originates from Beriah's conceptual level. All three converge on the same Mizbeach because Or Chozer from all three worlds ultimately unifies in the same divine source.
- **The Mizbeach as the point of Or Yashar/Or Chozer union**: The Mizbeach (altar) is positioned at the entrance to the Mishkan — the threshold between the inner sacred space (where the divine Presence dwells) and the outer court (where Israelites bring their offerings). This threshold position is the hemshech's spatial metaphor for the junction point between Or Yashar (descending into the inner Mishkan) and Or Chozer (ascending from the outer court via the korban). The burning on the Mizbeach is where the two movements meet and become indistinguishable.
- **"Leraitzon lifnei Hashem" — acceptability before G-d**: The repeated phrase "for a pleasing aroma before G-d" (ריח ניחוח לה') is analyzed as a description of what Or Chozer achieves at its destination: the "pleasing" (nicho'ach, from niyach — to rest, to settle) indicates that the ascending Or Chozer brings the divine will to rest. This is Ratzon (divine will, associated with Keter) settling — the completion of Or Chozer's journey.

### What It Gets To

The maamar establishes that the korbanot are not primitive appeasement rituals but the most precise embodiment of the Or Chozer mechanism in physical practice. The opening kria (call) from the Ohel Moed demonstrates that the completed Mishkan has changed the direction of divine address: Or Yashar now speaks from within the world it has entered, having been drawn there by Or Chozer's labor. The korban-service within the Mishkan is what sustains this inversion.

### How This Fits the Hemshech

Vayikra marks the hemshech's transition from the cosmic-structural phase (the Mishkan's construction as model of divine dwelling) to the operational phase (how the divine dwelling is maintained through ongoing service). The korban-analysis provides the hemshech with a detailed functional model of Or Chozer at every world-level, which will be drawn upon throughout the remaining maamarim of Part 2 as the Rashab examines Yom Kippur's avodah, the Omer-count, and the agricultural laws as further instantiations of the ascending Or Chozer movement.
