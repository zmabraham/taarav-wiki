## Context

This maamar was delivered during **Parshat Shemot 1909-10** in the year **העת"ר** (1909–10), as part of the ongoing hemshech (continuous discourse) that Rebbe Shalom DovBer of Lubavitch (the Rashab) delivered over two decades. It forms part of **חלק שני (Part 2)** of the hemshech.

The Hemshech Taarav is the longest chassidic discourse ever composed, spanning from 5662 (1901-02) through 5680 (1919-20). Each maamar continues the thread of the previous, building a comprehensive Kabbalistic-Chassidic system centered on the nature of divine light, the structure of the sefirot, and the mission of the Jewish soul.

## Deep Dive

### Opening Question (Kushya)

The maamar opens with a textual difficulty about the verse "ואלה שמות בני ישראל הבאים מצרימה את יעקב." The Rashab asks two things: (1) this verse largely repeats what was already stated in Parshat Vayigash ("אלה שמות בני ישראל הבאים מצרימה יעקב ובניו") — why the repetition? (2) There is a grammatical shift: in Vayigash, Yaakov appears as part of Bnei Yisrael ("יעקב ובניו"), implying he is included among them, whereas here the Torah writes "את יעקב" — with an את that sets him apart. And a deeper problem: how can Yaakov be among Bnei Yisrael at all, since Yaakov *is* Yisrael — "לא יעקב יאמר עוד שמך כי אם ישראל"?

### The Conceptual Build

To resolve this, the Rashab draws on the hemshech's established teaching about tefillah (prayer) as the soul's journey toward its root. Prayer progresses through three experiential registers — ruach, ra'ash, and aish (wind, earthquake, fire) — mapped onto Pesukei D'Zimra, Birkot Keriat Shema, and Keriat Shema itself.

**Nefesh / Hodaah:** Every Jewish soul, even one distant from G-d, possesses a capacity for hodaah — basic acknowledgment of the Divine. The Rashab illustrates the range: from the lowest level ("גנבא אפום מחתרתא" — the thief who believes yet violates) to genuine bitul. This stage corresponds to nefesh.

**Ruach / Hitpa'alut through Yesh Me'ayin:** The deeper level is hitpa'alut ha-midot (emotional arousal) through hitbonenut. The primary subject of this hitbonenut is yesh me'ayin — creation from nothing. The Rashab argues that every finite, composite, perishable thing must have been brought into being: "א"א להיות יש מיש" — it is impossible for one thing to emerge from another without an ein (nothingness) between them. Contemplation of the divine sustaining power ("לעולם ה' דברך נצב בשמים") produces emotional arousal in the nefesh ha'elokit. Crucially, this engages the chitzoniyut ha-mochin — the outer aspect of intellect that descends toward the emotions.

**The Role of Malchut:** Creation specifically emerges from Malchut because the upper sefirot receive and transmit divine light in modes of kiruv (proximity) — they cannot serve as the medium for yesh me'ayin. Only Malchut, whose atzilut operates in modes of richuk (distance), can produce created existence. Yet within Malchut dwells a koach ein sof more elevated than in the other sefirot — which is why "כח הפועל" (the active power) must be simultaneously immanent in the created and concealed from it in radical hav'dalah. These two poles — "איהו תפיס בכולהו עלמין ולית מאן דתפיס בי'" — constitute the hifla'ah (wondrous paradox) of creation.

**Chitzoniyut vs. Pnimiyut ha-Mochin:** The maamar distinguishes two modes of intellect. Chitzoniyut ha-mochin operates through "לבושי השגה" — conceptual garments drawn from natural faculties — which flows downward toward the emotions. Pnimiyut ha-mochin is direct apprehension of the divine without conceptual mediation; it ascends in devekut rather than descending. The three prayer stages map onto: ruach (hitpa'alut through yesh me'ayin, outer mochin), ra'ash (hitpa'alut through kedushah of Ein Sof), and aish (ahavah berish'fei eish from Binah — burning love arising from fully absorbed comprehension of G-d's greatness, producing klos ha-nefesh and mesirat nefesh in Shema).

### Key Chassidic Concepts

- **חיצוניות / פנימיות המוחין** (chitzoniyut/pnimiyut ha-mochin) — outer vs. inner intellect
- **יש מאין** (yesh me'ayin) — creation from nothing as the primary object of hitbonenut
- **כח הפועל בנפעל** (koach ha-po'el ba-nif'al) — the creator's power embedded in the created
- **הפלאה** (hifla'ah) — the paradox of simultaneous immanence and radical separation
- **רוח רעש ואש** (ruach, ra'ash, aish) — three registers of prayer corresponding to levels of the soul

### The Chiddush and Resolution

The maamar returns to its opening question. Both Yisrael and Yaakov designate the mochin (intellect), but differently: "ישראל" — parsed as "לי ראש" — denotes pnimiyut ha-mochin, the inner intellect above the flow of hishtalshelut. "יעקב" — parsed as "יו"ד עקב" — denotes chitzoniyut ha-mochin, the intellect that does flow into the midot. In Parshat Vayigash, from the perspective of Yisrael (pnimiyut ha-mochin), Yaakov is a "son" — a lower level subsumed within the higher ("יעקב ובניו"). But Parshat Shemot describes a second, deeper descent — into the level of dibbur (speech) rather than merely machshavah (thought). Here the relevant "Yisrael" is Yisrael Zuta (Z"A), a lower manifestation; from that vantage point, Yaakov is no longer a ben (subordinate) but an ach (peer), hence "את יעקב" — set alongside rather than included within. The doubling of the verse across two parshiyot thus encodes two distinct descents: "ירד ירדנו" — one into the concealed worlds of machshavah, one into the revealed worlds of dibbur — each with its corresponding ascent: "אעלך גם עלה."
