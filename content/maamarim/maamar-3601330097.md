## Context

This maamar was delivered during **Parshat Shemot 1909-10** in the year **העת"ר** (1909–10), as part of the ongoing hemshech (continuous discourse) that Rebbe Shalom DovBer of Lubavitch (the Rashab) delivered over two decades. It forms part of **חלק שני (Part 2)** of the hemshech.

The Hemshech Taarav is the longest chassidic discourse ever composed, spanning from 5662 (1901-02) through 5680 (1919-20). Each maamar continues the thread of the previous, building a comprehensive Kabbalistic-Chassidic system centered on the nature of divine light, the structure of the sefirot, and the mission of the Jewish soul.

## Deep Dive

### The Construct Being Built

This maamar — the first of the Shemot-cycle within 5670 — builds the concept of the shem (name) as the disclosure of the Ohr Ein Sof within the structure of the seder hishtalshelut, demonstrating that Shemot (names) as a Kabbalistic category represents the precise point at which the infinite divine reality becomes accessible to finite beings. The names of the twelve sons of Israel descending to Egypt are not merely a genealogical list but the encoding of divine light within the vessels of the bounded world.

### The Opening Move

The parasha begins: "ואלה שמות בני ישראל הבאים מצרימה" — "And these are the names of the children of Israel who came to Egypt." The Rashab notes the double significance of the word "shemot": the Torah is announcing both (1) the names of individuals and (2) something categorical about names themselves as a spiritual reality. The word "these" (eleh) also implies a connection to what came before (ve-eleh adds to what preceded), linking the end of Bereishit's revelations to the beginning of Shemot's concealment. Why does Egypt — the archetypal place of darkness — begin with an emphasis on names?

### The Argumentative Arc

- **A "shem" (name) is not an arbitrary label but an expression of the essence of the named**: in the hemshech's Kabbalistic framework, names function as the disclosure of orot (lights) through the medium of the klei hashemot (vessels of the names) — a finer medium than the sefirot-vessels proper. The shem is what makes the essence knowable without the essence itself being exposed.
- **The descent to Egypt = Or Yashar entering maximum concealment, but naming preserves the connection**: by enumerating the names of the sons, the Torah is showing that even in the deepest descent into galut (Egypt), the essential identity — the shem — is preserved. The name is the thread of Or Chozer that remains active even when Or Yashar is utterly hidden. As long as Israel retains its names (and the Midrash notes that they preserved their names in Egypt), the channel for eventual redemption is maintained.
- **"Shemot" as the dimension of Malchut within the divine names**: the four-letter Name (Havaya) contains within it the shem of Malchut — the heh at the end. In the Rashab's analysis, the names of the sons of Israel descending to Egypt correspond to the letters of the divine Name descending into the lower worlds. Each son/name maps onto a specific dimension of the divine light that is being "named" (disclosed) within the concealment of Egypt.
- **The connection between shem and tzimtzum**: the shem comes into being precisely at the point of tzimtzum — when the infinite light contracts, it leaves behind the reshimu (residual impression), and it is from this reshimu that the structure of names emerges. The shem is thus both a sign of the absence of the full ohr and the vessel that preserves its essential character through that absence.
- **Moshe's own name as the paradigm**: the parasha will introduce Moshe Rabbeinu, and his name — given by Pharaoh's daughter ("ki min ha-mayim m'shithu" — because I drew him from the water) — is central to the hemshech's analysis. Moshe's name is assigned from without (the daughter of the oppressor names the redeemer), which corresponds to the way that Or Chozer is "named" (identified) from the perspective of the lower world even while it carries the essential divine mission.

### What It Gets To

The maamar concludes that the Shemot (names) are the spiritual technology by which Or Yashar maintains its identifying signature even when maximally concealed within Egypt-level darkness. The neshama's task in galut is precisely the preservation and articulation of the shem — the essential divine identity — until the time of redemption when that identity can be fully disclosed.

### How This Fits the Hemshech

Parshat Shemot opens a new phase of the hemshech: having analyzed the structure of Or Yashar and Or Chozer through the Bereishit narratives (Avot, the sefirot-mapping of the twelve tribes, the descent of Or Yashar into concealment via Yosef), the Rashab now turns to the galut/geulah dynamic as the master drama of how Or Chozer operates at the national/historical level. The names that are preserved in Egypt are the structural equivalent of the reshimu that remains after tzimtzum — the thread that will guide the redemption and the eventual revelation of the most hidden levels of Keter.
