# Da'at Elyon and Da'at Tachton on Rosh Hashana

---

## Overview

The apparent contradiction of Rosh Hashana — 'beginning of Your works' vs. 'remembrance of the first day' — is resolved through the distinction between Da'at Elyon (supernal divine knowledge) and Da'at Tachton (lower divine knowledge), explaining why shofar must supplement verbal Malkhuyot.

### Hebrew Terms

- **דעת עליון ודעת תחתון** (supernal and lower divine knowledge)
- **תחלת מעשיך / זכרון ליום ראשון** (beginning of works / remembrance)
- **שופר** (shofar — beyond verbal expression)

## Position in the Conceptual Chain


- **Advances**: [[concepts/atzmus-malchut-structured-interface|Atzmus and Malchut: Infinite Will's Structured Interface]]
The hemshech introduces a two-tier epistemology of divine knowledge: Da'at Elyon (which perceives only divine Yesh ha'amiti) and Da'at Tachton (which perceives created yesh). The shofar bridges them — its wordless sound accessing Da'at Elyon beyond what language (Da'at Tachton) can reach.
