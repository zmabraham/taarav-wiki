# Or vs. Shefa: Light Radiates Without Depletion

---

## Overview

Maamar Shoftim (1901-02) draws a precise distinction between two modes of divine transmission:
**שפע** (*shefa* — flow) and **אור** (*or* — light/radiance). *Shefa* is a substantive transfer in which the giver's own intellect or energy is drawn out and transmitted, causing diminishment in the source. *Or* is pure radiance — a glow that carries no substance from the source, causes no change in the luminous body, and operates automatically (*ממילא*). The sun's light does not deplete the sun. This distinction is foundational to understanding how Or Yashar can descend infinitely from Atzmus without diminishing it.

### Hebrew Terms

- **שפע** — Shefa; flow, substantive transmission that diminishes the source
- **אור** — Or; radiance, light that leaves the source unchanged
- **הארה** — Hara'ah; illumination, a derivative shine
- **ממילא** — Automatically, without effort or cost
- **מאור** — Ma'or; the luminous body, the source of light
