# Kochot vs. Levushim: Soul Powers and Soul Garments

---

## Overview

Maamar Vayishlach (1902-03) constructs the distinction between *kochot ha-nefesh* (the soul's powers/faculties — intellect and emotions) and *levushei ha-nefesh* (the soul's garments — thought, speech, and action). These are two categorically different relationships between the soul and its expressions. The soul's *powers* function as transparent kelim (analogous to the Sefirot of Atzilut — transparent vessels that reveal what they contain). The soul's *garments* function as opaque levushim (analogous to the lower worlds' concealing structures). The malachim/messengers sent by Yaakov to Esav model this: an angel's entire existence is defined by its mission (*shlichut*), just as the soul's powers are entirely in the soul's service with no independent existence.

### Hebrew Terms

- **כחות הנפש** — Kochot ha-Nefesh; soul powers/faculties (intellect, emotions)
- **לבושי הנפש** — Levushei ha-Nefesh; soul garments (thought, speech, action)
- **שליחות** — Shlichut; mission; what defines a malach's existence
- **כלים** — Kelim; vessels; transparent containers of divine light
- **לבוש** — Levush; garment; opaque covering that conceals more than reveals
