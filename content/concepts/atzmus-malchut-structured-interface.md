# Atzmus and Malchut: Infinite Will's Structured Interface

---

## Overview

The paradox of divine coronation (Rosh Hashana) is resolved: Malchut is not a diminution of Atzmus (divine essence) but the precise structural interface through which infinite divine will reaches finite beings — without Malchut, the infinite cannot be 'king' because there are no subjects.

### Hebrew Terms

- **עצמות** (Atzmus — divine essence/selfhood)
- **מלכות** (Malchut — kingship / relational mode)
- **הכתרה** (coronation — the Rosh Hashana act)

## Position in the Conceptual Chain

The hemshech establishes the definitive account of Malchut as the sefirah uniquely suited to interface between infinite and finite: it does not compromise divine transcendence but is the specific structural feature that makes relationship possible. This is the Rosh Hashana thesis.
