# Anochi Anochi: Forgiveness as Divine Self-Regard

---

## Overview

The doubled 'Anochi Anochi' (I, I am He who blots out transgressions) in Isaiah resolves two paradoxes: God forgives 'for My own sake' (l'ma'ani) — not for human merit — and the doubling reveals that divine forgiveness is an act rooted in Atzmus rather than in relational accounting.

### Hebrew Terms

- **אנכי אנכי הוא מוחה פשעיך** (I, I am He who blots out your transgressions)
- **למעני** (for My own sake)
- **תשובה** (teshuva — return)
