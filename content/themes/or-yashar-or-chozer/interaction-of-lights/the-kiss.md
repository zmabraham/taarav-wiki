# The Kiss: Meeting of Or Yashar and Or Chozer

## English Explanation

In the Hemshech Taarav, the Rashab employs one of the most intimate images in
Kabbalistic literature to describe the meeting of Or Yashar and Or Chozer: the
*neshikah* (נְשִׁיקָה, kiss). When descending direct light and ascending reflected
light meet, the result is not a collision but a union — a *yichud* — described as a
kiss between two parties who have been separated and now reunite.

The metaphor of the kiss is profound. A kiss is the touching of two mouths, the most
intimate point of verbal and spiritual expression (פֶּה, peh, the mouth). In Kabbalistic
thought, the mouth is associated with speech, breath, and the inner life of the soul.
Two people kissing share their very breath — their inner spiritual content. Similarly,
when or yashar and or chozer meet, they share their respective "contents": or yashar
brings the infinite divine will downward, or chozer brings the transformed, refined
experience of finite existence upward.

This kiss produces a new state of yichud — unity — that transcends either light
alone. Or yashar by itself cannot achieve this unity because it has no partner from
below. Or chozer alone cannot achieve it because it requires a source. Only in their
meeting — the kiss — does the full purpose of creation become realized: the union of
infinite and finite, upper and lower, giver and receiver.

The Rashab locates this kiss particularly in moments of prayer (davening) when the
worshipper achieves both ratzo (rushing forward) and shov (return) simultaneously.
In that moment, the person's ascending love (or chozer) meets the divine descending
illumination (or yashar) and a kiss-like union occurs.
