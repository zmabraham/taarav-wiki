# The Yichud Produced by the Meeting of Lights

## English Explanation

When Or Yashar and Or Chozer meet, they produce a *yichud* — a state of divine unity
that is qualitatively different from either light alone. The Rashab in Hemshech Taarav
describes this yichud as the purpose toward which the entire process of Tzimtzum,
Kav, and Reshimu was aimed: the union of Infinite and finite in a stable, mutually
enriching relationship.

This yichud is not a dissolution of distinctions. Or Yashar remains essentially
downward-flowing; Or Chozer remains essentially upward-flowing. Their meeting does not
destroy these directions but rather creates a third reality: a dynamic equilibrium in
which both movements are present simultaneously. The Rashab compares this to the
paradoxical unity of masculine and feminine forces (אוֹר פְּנִימִי, inner light, and
אוֹר מַקִּיף, surrounding light) — each distinct, yet together producing something
neither could generate alone.

The most significant yichud of lights occurs at the level of Atzilut, where the
Sefirot themselves embody this interplay. Chochmah (Wisdom) receives pure or yashar
from Keter; Binah (Understanding) generates or chozer back toward Keter and Chochmah;
their meeting in Da'at produces a unified consciousness that then flows down through the
remaining Sefirot as a single coherent divine "personality."

In human terms, the yichud produced by this meeting is what the Rashab calls
*devekut* (דְּבֵקוּת, cleaving to God) — not the loss of self in the divine, but a
dynamic relationship of mutual engagement. The human being gives or chozer; God gives
or yashar; and in their meeting, both are enriched.
