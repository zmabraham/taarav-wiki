# Or Chozer Ascending

## English Explanation

One of the most striking features of Or Chozer in the Hemshech Taarav is its
*ascent* — the reflected light does not merely return to the level from which it was
sent, but can actually rise *higher* than the source of the original or yashar. This
upward movement (עֲלִיָּה, aliyah) of or chozer is a recurring theme in the Rashab's
analysis of prayer, divine service, and the purpose of creation.

The mechanism of this ascent is counterintuitive. Or Yashar descends in a graduated
way: it diminishes as it descends through each successive world. Or Chozer, however,
ascends without that same gradient of diminishment, because it carries with it the
"flavor" (טַעַם, taam) of every level through which the or yashar passed on its way
down. In returning upward, or chozer collects and integrates the spiritual qualities of
each world it traverses.

This is why the Rashab describes or chozer as producing a kind of *yichud* (unity)
between the upper and lower — the ascending reflected light bridges the entire chain of
worlds, bringing the spiritual residue of the lowest realms back into direct
relationship with the Infinite. The souls of Israel, who perform mitzvot in the lowest
world (Asiyah), generate or chozer that ascends all the way to the highest divine
levels, carrying the unique "flavor" of physical existence.

From a practical standpoint, this explains why the Rashab emphasizes the *shov*
movement in the ratzo-veshov dynamic. The return (shov), when properly executed, is
not a retreat but an ascent — the person, having rushed toward God (ratzo), now brings
their earthly experience back upward, enriching the divine encounter.

## Hebrew Key Terms

| Term | Transliteration | Definition |
|------|-----------------|------------|
| עֲלִיָּה | Aliyah | Ascent; the upward movement of or chozer |
| יִחוּד | Yichud | Unity; the union produced by or chozer's return |
| טַעַם | Taam | Flavor; the spiritual quality imprinted on or chozer |
| שׁוֹב | Shov | Return; the downward/grounding movement in divine service |
| רָצוֹא | Ratzo | Rushing forward; the upward longing in divine service |
