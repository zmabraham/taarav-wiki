# Quality of Or Chozer

## English Explanation

The quality of Or Chozer is not uniform — it depends entirely on the nature and
refinement of the vessel that generates it. A coarser vessel produces or chozer that
rises less high; a more refined vessel, capable of greater bitul (self-nullification)
in the face of the divine light, produces or chozer of higher quality that ascends
further and achieves a deeper yichud (union).

The Rashab in Hemshech Taarav distinguishes between the or chozer of the World of
Tohu and that of the World of Tikkun. In Tohu, the lights were intense and the
vessels were weak — there was virtually no or chozer, because the vessels shattered
under the intensity of or yashar before they could reflect it. In Tikkun, the vessels
are modulated and cooperative: they receive or yashar at appropriate levels and
generate sustained or chozer as a result.

This distinction maps onto human spiritual life. A person in a state of spiritual
overwhelm — completely "broken" by the intensity of divine awareness — generates no
or chozer because they cannot maintain the vessel of selfhood needed for reflection.
A person who maintains a healthy, modulated sense of self while engaged in divine
service generates ongoing or chozer that enriches both themselves and the worlds
above.

The highest quality or chozer is associated with Yechida — the innermost level of the
soul. Because Yechida is most directly connected to the Infinite (אֵין סוֹף, Ein Sof),
the or chozer generated from that level is most penetrating and most elevated. The
Rashab notes that fully accessing Yechida in this world is rare, but approaching it
through deep teshuvah (return/repentance) generates or chozer of exceptional quality.
