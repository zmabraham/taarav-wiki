# Generation of Reflected Light

## English Explanation

Or Chozer — reflected light — is generated when the downward flow of Or Yashar
encounters a vessel (כְּלִי, keli) or a boundary and "reflects" back upward toward its
source. In the Hemshech Taarav, the Rashab explains this as more than a mere optical
metaphor: it is a fundamental metaphysical dynamic describing how finite beings can
participate in — and even contribute to — the divine order.

The generation of or chozer depends on the capacity of the receiver. A vessel that is
entirely transparent to or yashar (fully batel, nullified) does not generate or chozer
— the light passes through it unchanged. It is specifically the *boundary* of the
vessel, its capacity to have a defined edge and identity, that causes the reflection.
This is why or chozer is described as originating "from below" — it emerges from the
perspective of the vessel, not the source.

In human terms, the capacity to reflect divine light back to its source is the
spiritual dynamic of prayer (תְּפִלָּה, tefilla) and Torah study performed with *ratzon*
(will). When a person engages with the divine not merely as a passive recipient but as
an active responder — saying, in essence, "I receive this light and send it back to
You" — that is the generation of or chozer in practice.

The Rashab notes that or chozer, while originating from below, can actually ascend
*higher* than the original or yashar descends. This is the paradox of reflected light:
the very act of reception and reflection creates a new dynamic that transcends the
original giving. This is why the souls' service below can, in a mystical sense, affect
even the highest divine levels.

| אוֹר חוֹזֵר | Or Chozer | Reflected light; light that returns upward |
