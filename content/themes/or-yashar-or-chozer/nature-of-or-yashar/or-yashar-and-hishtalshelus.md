# Or Yashar and the Chain of Worlds

## English Explanation

Or Yashar is the energizing principle of the entire *hishtalshelut* — the "chain" or
cascading sequence of spiritual worlds from Atzilut down to Asiyah. Each world in this
chain both receives or yashar from the world above it and serves as a conduit, passing
a diminished version downward. This progressive diminishment is not a flaw but a
feature: it allows each world to sustain its own distinct level of existence.

The Rashab in Hemshech Taarav describes the chain of worlds using the metaphor of a
candle flame (שַׁלְהֶבֶת, shalhevet). A candle's flame does not transfer its own
substance when lighting another candle — the second candle receives illumination from
the first but becomes its own distinct entity. Similarly, or yashar illuminates each
successive world without that world consuming or destroying the source above it.

In Atzilut, or yashar is so intense that the Sefirot themselves are essentially divine
— "Atzilut" comes from the root אֵצֶל, "beside," meaning the world remains immediately
adjacent to the Infinite. Descending into Beriah, Yetzirah, and Asiyah, or yashar
becomes progressively more "clothed" in the language and structure of each world until,
in Asiyah, it animates physical matter.

The significance of this for divine service is that every action performed in the
lowest world (Asiyah) draws upon or yashar that has traveled the entire length of the
chain. The Rashab emphasizes that this makes physical mitzvot uniquely precious: they
draw the very same divine light that originated in the Infinite, now channeled through
the entire sequence of worlds into concrete, material action.

## Hebrew Key Terms

| Term | Transliteration | Definition |
|------|-----------------|------------|
| שַׁלְשֶׁלֶת | Shalshelset | Chain; the linked sequence of spiritual worlds |
| אֲצִילוּת | Atzilut | World of Emanation; closest to Ein Sof |
| בְּרִיאָה | Beriah | World of Creation |
| יְצִירָה | Yetzirah | World of Formation |
| עֲשִׂיָּה | Asiyah | World of Action; the physical world |
