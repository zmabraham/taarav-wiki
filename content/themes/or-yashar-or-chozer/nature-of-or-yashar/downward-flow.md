# Downward Flow of Or Yashar

## English Explanation

Or Yashar — direct light — is the primordial mode of divine illumination that flows
*downward* through the chain of worlds (שַׁלְשֶׁלֶת הַהִשְׁתַּלְשְׁלוּת, hishtalshelut).
It emanates from the Ein Sof and descends progressively through each level of existence:
Atzilut, Beriah, Yetzirah, and Asiyah. At each stage the light becomes more contracted
and clothed, yet it retains its essentially downward, linear trajectory — from above to
below, from cause to effect, from giver to receiver.

The Rashab in the Hemshech Taarav emphasizes that this downward motion is not merely
spatial. It represents the dynamic of *revelation*: the divine will, when it chooses to
give, sends forth a ray (קַו, kav) that penetrates the void left by the Tzimtzum and
progressively illuminates each successive world. Each world receives according to its
capacity, each one acting as both recipient of the light above it and source for the
world below.

This downward cascade is described using the metaphor of a king's glance (מַבָּט הַמֶּלֶךְ):
just as a king's gaze descends from his elevated throne to those far beneath him, or
yashar travels from the infinite height of Ein Sof down into the finite vessels of the
lower worlds. The light does not lose its divine origin in this descent; rather, it
becomes progressively "clothed" in the language of each world.

Understanding or yashar as downward flow clarifies why the created worlds cannot
automatically ascend on their own — they are receivers, not initiators. The initiative
belongs entirely to the divine light that flows down. This asymmetry is the basis for
understanding why or chozer (reflected light) is a remarkable phenomenon: it represents
a movement *against* the natural direction of or yashar.

## Hebrew Key Terms

| Term | Transliteration | Definition |
|------|-----------------|------------|
| אוֹר יָשָׁר | Or Yashar | Direct, downward-flowing divine light |
| הִשְׁתַּלְשְׁלוּת | Hishtalshelut | The chain of worlds; sequential descent |
| קַו | Kav | The line/ray of divine light post-Tzimtzum |
| מַבָּט | Mabat | Gaze; used as a metaphor for divine downward illumination |
| לְבוּשׁ | Levush | Garment; the progressive clothing of light in each world |
