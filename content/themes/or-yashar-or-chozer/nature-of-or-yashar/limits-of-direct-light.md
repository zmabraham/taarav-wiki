# Limits of Direct Light

## English Explanation

While Or Yashar is the primary mode of divine illumination, it carries an inherent
limitation: it cannot, by itself, produce truly independent creatures. A world
illuminated only by or yashar remains entirely absorbed in its divine source — the
more powerfully the direct light shines, the more any created being would be nullified
(בָּטֵל, batel) into the Infinite.

This is one reason the Tzimtzum was necessary. Before the Tzimtzum, the Ein Sof
illuminated everything with direct, undifferentiated or yashar — there was no "room"
for anything other than the divine. The Tzimtzum created a space (מָקוֹם הַפָּנוּי,
makom hapanui) in which finite vessels could exist without being immediately dissolved.

Even after the Tzimtzum, the or yashar that flows through the Kav is carefully
calibrated. Too much direct light would overwhelm the vessels, as occurred during the
Shevirat HaKelim (שְׁבִירַת הַכֵּלִים, breaking of the vessels) in the World of Tohu.
The vessels of Tohu were overwhelmed by the intensity of direct or yashar because they
lacked the capacity to contain or "back-talk" to the light — they had no or chozer.

In the World of Tikkun, this is rectified by the interplay of direct and reflected
light. Or yashar provides the content and energy; the vessels and their capacity to
generate or chozer provide the structure and boundary. Without limits on or yashar,
creation would dissolve back into undifferentiated divinity.

From a spiritual practice perspective, the Rashab notes that a person who experiences
only the "downward" rush of divine enthusiasm — the ratzo, the longing and rush toward
God — without the counterbalancing shov (return and grounding) is in a spiritually
precarious position, analogous to the unstable vessels of Tohu.

## Hebrew Key Terms

| Term | Transliteration | Definition |
|------|-----------------|------------|
| בִּיטוּל | Bitul | Self-nullification in the divine |
| מָקוֹם הַפָּנוּי | Makom HaPanui | The vacated space left by Tzimtzum |
| שְׁבִירַת הַכֵּלִים | Shevirat HaKelim | The breaking of the vessels in Tohu |
| כֵּלִים | Keilim | Vessels; the structural containers of divine light |
| תִּקּוּן | Tikkun | Rectification; the post-shevirah order of worlds |
