# מקיף ופנימי — Encompassing and Internalized Light

**The Distinction Between Or Mekif and Or Pnimi in Hemshech Taarav**

---

## Overview

Closely related to the Or Yashar / Or Chozer dynamic is the distinction between **Or Mekif** (אור מקיף — encompassing/surrounding light) and **Or Pnimi** (אור פנימי — inner/internalized light). The Rashab in Hemshech Taarav develops this distinction as a *second axis* of analysis that complements Or Yashar / Or Chozer, allowing for a much more nuanced description of how divine light relates to created beings.

This distinction is developed throughout the hemshech, particularly in connection with the soul's higher levels (Chaya and Yechida) and the structure of the Sefirot in Atzilut.

---

## The Distinction Defined

**Or Mekif (אור מקיף — Surrounding/Encompassing Light)**:
The aspect of divine light that *transcends* the capacity of the kli (vessel) to absorb or internalize. It "surrounds" the vessel from outside, sustaining it but not entering into it. The Or Mekif is too vast, too intense, or too elevated to be comprehended or contained. Yet it is essential — without it, the vessel would not have the divine "context" that keeps it oriented toward the Infinite.

**Or Pnimi (אור פנימי — Internal/Internalized Light)**:
The aspect of divine light that *enters* the kli and becomes genuinely internalized — the light that the vessel can actually absorb, process, and express. Or Pnimi is the light that the vessel "becomes" — it shapes the vessel's character and function.

---

## The Rashab's Analysis: Three Key Insights

**1. Every kli receives both**
The Rashab teaches that every vessel — every sefirah, every soul — simultaneously receives Or Mekif and Or Pnimi. The Or Pnimi is what it can contain; the Or Mekif is what surrounds it in excess of its containment. Neither can be reduced to the other.

**2. Or Mekif as the source of Or Pnimi**
The Or Pnimi that enters the kli is actually *drawn from* the Or Mekif that surrounds it. Through the soul's work (particularly intense prayer and deep Torah study), the surrounding Or Mekif can gradually become Or Pnimi — what was once beyond comprehension becomes internalized and genuinely possessed. This process is called *hachna'sat ha'or mekif l'pnimiyut* (bringing the encompassing light inside) and is a central goal of Chabad avodah.

**3. The Yechida and Or Mekif**
The soul-level of *Yechida* corresponds to the Or Mekif — it is the aspect of the soul that transcends internalization, that is always "beyond" the person even as it constitutes their deepest identity. Similarly, the soul-level of *Chaya* corresponds to an intermediate Or Mekif that can potentially be internalized in moments of intense prayer or revelation.

---

## Relationship to Or Yashar / Or Chozer

The Mekif/Pnimi distinction cuts across the Or Yashar/Or Chozer axis:

| | Or Mekif | Or Pnimi |
|--|--|--|
| Or Yashar | Divine transcendence surrounding creation | Divine immanence within creation |
| Or Chozer | The soul's yearning beyond its capacity | The soul's internalized relationship with God |

The most refined state of spiritual development is when Or Mekif becomes Or Pnimi — when what was beyond the soul becomes genuinely its own. This is what the Rashab calls *dirah b'tachtonim* at the personal level: God's transcendence becoming immanent, the surrounding light becoming internal.

---

## Application to Sefirot

In the Sefirot:
- **Keter** is Or Mekif relative to Chochma — it transcends the intellectual structure
- **Chochma** is Or Mekif relative to Bina — Bina can internalize some of Chochma but never all of it
- **Malchut** has uniquely both: it is Or Pnimi to all the worlds below it, and Or Mekif to all that transcends it
