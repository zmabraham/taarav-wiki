# טבע האור ישר — Nature of Or Yashar

**The Direct Downward Flow of Divine Light in Hemshech Taarav**

---

## Overview

**Or Yashar** (אור ישר — "straight" or "direct" light) refers to the divine illumination that flows downward from Ein Sof through the successive levels of Atzilut, Beriya, Yetzira, and Asiya. The term *yashar* (ישר — straight, direct) emphasizes the unidirectional nature of this light: it proceeds from higher to lower, from cause to effect, from the Infinite toward the finite.

The Rashab in Hemshech Taarav — particularly in the maamarim of תער"ב-תער"ג (the first years of the hemshech) — develops Or Yashar as far more than a directional description. It represents the entire modality of divine *hashpa'ah* (influence, outpouring), the aspect of the divine that gives, emanates, and descends without requiring anything in return.

---

## The Rashab's Distinctive Analysis

Unlike earlier Kabbalistic treatments that describe Or Yashar primarily in geometric terms (a light that flows downward in a straight line), the Rashab grounds Or Yashar in a deeper ontological principle: **it is the expression of divine *ratzon* (will) to create and sustain worlds**.

Three key dimensions of Or Yashar as developed in the Taarav:

**1. Or Yashar as Active Giving (Hashpa'ah)**
Or Yashar is the light of the *mashpia* (influencer/giver). Just as a teacher who gives Torah to students, or a parent who provides for children, the Or Yashar flows outward without being "diminished" at the source. This is the paradox of divine giving: the Infinite gives without losing anything.

**2. Or Yashar as the Foundation of Each World's Existence**
Each world (*olam*) receives its existence through the Or Yashar flowing into it from the level above. The Or Yashar of Atzilut creates Beriya; the Or Yashar of Beriya creates Yetzira, and so on. The Rashab analyzes how each subsequent world receives a more *contracted* (metzumtzam) form of the Or Yashar.

**3. Or Yashar and the Concealment of the Source**
Crucially, Or Yashar as it descends conceals its source. By the time it reaches the lower worlds, the created being does not perceive the Infinite behind the light it receives. This concealment is necessary — if the full Or Ein Sof were revealed, finite creation could not exist. The Or Yashar is thus both a revelation *and* a concealment.

## Or Yashar in the Sefirot

In the analysis of the Sefirot, Or Yashar flows through each sefirah in a characteristic way:
- In **Keter**: Or Yashar is the will that initiates the whole chain of emanation
- In **Chochma**: Or Yashar is the first *flash* of divine thought — the point (*nekudah*) of the idea before it is developed
- In **Bina**: Or Yashar becomes developed understanding — the *river* fed by Chochma's spring
- In the **Middot** (Chesed through Yesod): Or Yashar is the active giving of each attribute
- In **Malchut**: Or Yashar reaches its most contracted form — the divine presence at the boundary of creation

## Or Yashar and Tohu vs. Tikkun

In *Olam HaTohu* (World of Chaos), Or Yashar was present in excess — the lights were *too intense* for the vessels. The Shevirat HaKelim (Breaking of Vessels) resulted from an overwhelming Or Yashar that could not be balanced by the Or Chozer response of those vessels.

In *Olam HaTikkun*, Or Yashar is calibrated — it descends in a measured, proportional flow that the vessels of Tikkun can sustain. The Partzufim (divine configurations) of Tikkun are precisely structured to receive Or Yashar without shattering.
