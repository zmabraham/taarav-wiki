# Transition from Mekif to Pnimi

## English Explanation

One of the most practically significant teachings in the Hemshech Taarav is the
description of how *mekif* (surrounding light) can become *pnimi* (inner light)
through the process of spiritual growth and internalization. What was once an
overwhelming, unabsorbable divine experience can gradually be integrated into the
personality — transforming from an outer presence into an inner reality.

The Rashab illustrates this with the process of learning (לִמּוּד, limmud) and
contemplation (הִתְבּוֹנְנוּת, hitbonenut). When a student first encounters a profound
Torah concept, it is like an ohr mekif — it surrounds and affects them, but they
cannot yet internalize it. Through repeated study and contemplation, the concept
gradually penetrates deeper into the student's consciousness until it becomes a living
part of their inner world — ohr pnimi.

This transition is not simply intellectual. The Rashab describes it as a genuine
spiritual transformation in which the vessel (the person's mind and character) expands
its capacity to receive more refined light. As the vessel grows, yesterday's mekif
becomes today's pnimi, and new, higher levels of mekif become accessible.

The transition also has a cosmic dimension. The lights that currently exist as mekif
relative to the worlds will become pnimi in the Messianic era — when the capacity of
the worlds expands to receive what was previously too intense to be internalized. The
Rashab sees this as the ultimate purpose of the entire process of creation: the
progressive transformation of mekif into pnimi, until the infinite fully inhabits the
finite without destroying it.

## Hebrew Key Terms

| Term | Transliteration | Definition |
|------|-----------------|------------|
| הִתְבּוֹנְנוּת | Hitbonenut | Deep contemplation; the key to mekif-pnimi transition |
| לִמּוּד | Limmud | Learning; the process that enables internalization |
| כְּלִי | Keli | Vessel; expands through spiritual growth |
| גְּאוּלָּה | Geulah | Redemption; the era when mekif becomes pnimi universally |
| הִתְרַחֲבוּת | Hitrachabut | Expansion; the widening of the vessel's capacity |
