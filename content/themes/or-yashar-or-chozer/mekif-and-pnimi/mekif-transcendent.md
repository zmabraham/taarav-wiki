# Mekif: The Transcendent Surrounding Light

## English Explanation

*Ohr Mekif* (אוֹר מַקִּיף, surrounding light) is divine light that does not enter the
vessel but *surrounds* and encompasses it from without. In the Hemshech Taarav, the
Rashab develops a rich analysis of how mekif differs from pnimi (inner light) and why
both are necessary for the full structure of creation.

The mekif is called "transcendent" because it represents aspects of divinity that
exceed the vessel's capacity to internalize. A finite vessel can only absorb so much
divine light before it is overwhelmed; the light that cannot be absorbed does not
disappear — it remains present as ohr mekif, surrounding the vessel and "pressing in"
from without. This is why the Rashab associates mekif with the concept of Sovev Kol
Almin (סוֹבֵב כָּל עָלְמִין) — the divine light that surrounds all worlds equally without
entering or differentiating itself according to their distinct levels.

Mekif operates at a paradoxical level: precisely because it does *not* enter the
vessel, it has a more powerful and intimate relationship with the vessel's core than
the pnimi light does. The pnimi light, by entering the vessel, becomes limited and
modified by the vessel's shape. The mekif, remaining outside, retains its full,
undiluted intensity and envelops the vessel's very essence.

In human spiritual life, the mekif corresponds to aspects of the divine that a person
can intuit or be moved by without being able to fully articulate or internalize — the
sense of awe (יִרְאָה, yira) before the infinite, the overwhelming feeling of divine
presence in prayer or at a moment of spiritual crisis. These experiences "surround"
the person without being fully processed intellectually.

## Hebrew Key Terms

| Term | Transliteration | Definition |
|------|-----------------|------------|
| אוֹר מַקִּיף | Ohr Mekif | Surrounding/transcendent light |
| סוֹבֵב כָּל עָלְמִין | Sovev Kol Almin | Surrounds all worlds equally |
| יִרְאָה | Yira | Awe; the emotional experience of mekif |
| אֵין סוֹף | Ein Sof | The Infinite; source of mekif |
| עָצְמוּת | Atzmus | Essence; what mekif uniquely contacts |
