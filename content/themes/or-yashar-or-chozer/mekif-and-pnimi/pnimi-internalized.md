# Pnimi: The Internalized Inner Light

## English Explanation

*Ohr Pnimi* (אוֹר פְּנִימִי, inner light) is divine light that actually enters and
animates the vessel from within. In contrast to the surrounding mekif, the pnimi is
the life-force that gives a created being its specific, individual character. In the
Hemshech Taarav, the Rashab associates pnimi with the concept of *Memale Kol Almin*
(מְמַלֵּא כָּל עָלְמִין) — the divine light that fills all worlds, adapting itself to
each world's specific nature and capacity.

Because pnimi enters the vessel and is shaped by it, it necessarily becomes limited
and particularized. The divine light that animates a stone is the same in its source
as the light that animates a human being — but by entering the vessel of stone, it
becomes "stone-light," constrained by the stone's nature. This is why the Rashab
says pnimi is associated with *differentiation* (חִלּוּק, chiluk): the same light
produces radically different manifestations according to the vessel it enters.

This internalization is what gives creation its richness and diversity. Without
pnimi, there would be only mekif — surrounding, undifferentiated divine presence that
could not produce distinct created beings. Pnimi is the divine that "becomes" the
world in some sense, taking on the world's colors and textures while remaining, at
its root, infinite.

In spiritual practice, pnimi corresponds to the aspects of Torah and mitzvot that a
person genuinely understands, feels, and integrates into their character. The wisdom
one truly understands, the love one actually feels in prayer — these are pnimi. They
enrich the person's inner life but, precisely because they are internalized, they are
shaped and limited by the person's spiritual vessel.

## Hebrew Key Terms

| Term | Transliteration | Definition |
|------|-----------------|------------|
| אוֹר פְּנִימִי | Ohr Pnimi | Inner/internalized light |
| מְמַלֵּא כָּל עָלְמִין | Memale Kol Almin | Fills all worlds; pnimi's cosmic function |
| חִלּוּק | Chiluk | Differentiation; what pnimi produces in the vessel |
| כְּלִי | Keli | Vessel; shapes and limits the pnimi |
| חַיּוּת | Chayut | Life-force; the animating function of pnimi |
