# יחס האור ישר והאור חוזר — The Interaction of the Two Lights

**How Direct and Reflected Light Work Together in Hemshech Taarav**

---

## Overview

In Hemshech Taarav, the Rashab does not treat Or Yashar and Or Chozer as merely two separate phenomena — he analyzes the *interaction* between them as the generative engine of all spiritual reality. The relationship between descending and ascending light produces the very structure of the worlds and the possibility of all spiritual growth. This is developed throughout the hemshech but with special depth in the maamarim of חלק ראשון.

---

## The Dynamic Circuit

The relationship between Or Yashar and Or Chozer forms a circuit — or more precisely, a *pulse*:

1. **Or Yashar descends** from Ein Sof into the chalal through the Kav
2. **The Or Yashar strikes a kli** (vessel/sefirah) at the appropriate level
3. **Or Chozer is generated** by the kli's reception and reflection upward
4. **The Or Chozer ascends** back toward the source, carrying the unique "imprint" of its origin in a created being
5. **The Or Chozer's ascent draws further Or Yashar** — the return movement stimulates additional downward flow

This is not a static relationship but a continuous, living dynamic. The Rashab uses the metaphor of *neshima* (breathing) — the worlds "breathe" through this alternation of Or Yashar and Or Chozer.

---

## Complementarity and Asymmetry

A key teaching in the Taarav: while Or Yashar and Or Chozer are complementary, they are *not* symmetrical. Each has qualities the other lacks:

**Or Yashar has**:
- Connection to the source (it comes directly from Ein Sof)
- Richness of divine content (it carries the fullness of the divine will)
- Precedence (it initiates; Or Chozer responds)

**Or Chozer has**:
- Height of destination (it ascends to Atzmut, the divine essence itself)
- The quality of *yesh me'ayin* — something arising from apparent nothing (created beings generating a response that reaches beyond what was given to them)
- The quality of *bechira* (choice) — Or Chozer arises through the free decision of the created being to respond

The Rashab emphasizes that this asymmetry means neither can replace the other. A world of pure Or Yashar without Or Chozer would be like a one-way conversation — divine energy pouring out without any recipient response. A world of pure Or Chozer without Or Yashar would be impossible, since Or Chozer requires Or Yashar to initiate it.

---

## The Meeting Point: The Sefirot

The *sefirot* are the points where Or Yashar and Or Chozer meet. Each sefirah receives Or Yashar from above and generates Or Chozer back upward. The proportion and quality of this exchange defines the character of each sefirah. In the Taarav's analysis:

- **Keter** has the most refined Or Chozer (closest to the source) but the most contracted Or Yashar output downward
- **Malchut** has the richest Or Yashar received (from all sefirot above) but generates Or Chozer from the lowest, most material starting point — making Malchut's Or Chozer uniquely powerful by virtue of the distance it travels

This is why *Dirah B'Tachtonim* (G-d's dwelling in the lowest realms) is the ultimate goal: Malchut's Or Chozer, ascending from the very bottom of creation, reaches the highest level of all.

---

## Practical Implications

The Rashab draws explicit practical consequences from this analysis:

- **Prayer** must contain both movements: the descent of Or Yashar (God's blessing flowing into us) and the ascent of Or Chozer (our sincere response going up)
- **Torah study** with pure intellectual engagement generates Or Chozer; Torah study that is purely passive receiving (without the student's active intellectual response) generates only Or Yashar flow without full Or Chozer return
- **Mitzvot** create the "vessels" (kelim) that make the Or Chozer of the soul stable and sustainable — like proper equipment for the spiritual athlete
