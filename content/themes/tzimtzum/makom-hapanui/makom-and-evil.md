# The Makom HaPanui and the Origin of Evil

## English Explanation

One of the most theologically charged aspects of the Makom HaPanui in the Hemshech
Taarav is its relationship to evil. The Rashab explains that the *klipot* (קְלִיפּוֹת,
husks/forces of impurity) and the *Sitra Achra* (סִטְרָא אָחֳרָא, the Other Side)
derive their existence from the Makom HaPanui — specifically, from the dimension of
the space that is furthest from the divine light and deepest in concealment.

The key insight is that evil is not a positive force with its own independent existence.
Rather, it is a kind of "negative space" — the region of the Makom HaPanui that is
most deeply vacated of divine light, where the concealment of God is most extreme.
Evil arises not from the presence of something divine but from the *absence* of
sufficiently revealed divine light.

This is why the Rashab says the klipot are called "the empty one" (הָרֵיקָנִים, the
empty ones) — they are characterized precisely by their emptiness, their distance from
divine content. They derive their existence from the reshimu — the minimal divine trace
that remains — but they misrepresent themselves as independent realities, claiming
their emptiness is fullness.

The implication for spiritual practice is that combating evil does not require
confronting it directly. The path is to increase divine *light* — revelation, Torah,
mitzvot — in the areas of concealment. Evil, being essentially a vacuum, is dispelled
by the introduction of divine presence, just as darkness is dispelled by light.

## Hebrew Key Terms

| Term | Transliteration | Definition |
|------|-----------------|------------|
| קְלִיפּוֹת | Klipot | Husks; forces of concealment/evil |
| סִטְרָא אָחֳרָא | Sitra Achra | The Other Side; the realm of evil |
| רֵיקָנִים | Reikanim | Empty ones; characterization of evil |
| הֶעְלֵם | He'elem | Concealment; the condition that enables evil |
| גִּילוּי | Giluiy | Revelation; the antidote to evil |
