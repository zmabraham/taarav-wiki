# הרשימו — The Reshimu: The Divine Impression

**The Residual Trace of Or Ein Sof After the Tzimtzum in Hemshech Taarav**

---

## Overview

The **Reshimu** (רשימו — impression, residue, trace) is what remained within the *chalal* after the Or Ein Sof withdrew in the Tzimtzum. It is one of the most important and philosophically subtle concepts in Hemshech Taarav. The Rashab gives the Reshimu an analysis that surpasses in depth and precision anything found in earlier Kabbalistic or Chabad literature.

The Reshimu is the thinnest possible presence of Or Ein Sof — yet it is this minimal divine trace that provides the very foundation upon which the entire structure of the worlds is built.

---

## The Rashab's Analogies

The Rashab offers several analogies for the Reshimu in the Taarav:

**The fragrance in an empty vessel**: After a perfumed substance is removed from a bottle, the bottle retains a faint trace of the fragrance. The fragrance is not the substance itself but a trace that testifies to its former presence. So too the Reshimu is not the Or Ein Sof but the faint trace that remained after its withdrawal.

**The impression of a seal in wax**: After a signet ring is pressed into wax and removed, the impression remains — it has the exact contour of the ring even though the ring itself is gone. The Reshimu has the "shape" of the Or Ein Sof — it encodes the template of all the worlds — even though the Or Ein Sof itself has withdrawn.

**Warmth after heat**: After the source of heat is removed from a room, warmth lingers. The Reshimu is the "warmth" of the Or Ein Sof that remains in the chalal.

---

## Why the Reshimu is Essential

The Reshimu performs a structural function that nothing else can:

**Without the Reshimu**: The chalal would be absolute nothingness — completely cut off from the divine. This would mean complete non-existence, since nothing can exist without some divine sustaining presence. A true void could produce nothing.

**With the Reshimu**: The chalal has a minimal but genuine divine presence. This presence serves as the "soil" from which the worlds grow when the Kav comes to activate it. The Reshimu is the *template* — it encodes the possibility of all the distinctions and structures of the created worlds.

The Rashab teaches: **The Reshimu is the potential of all the worlds in their most contracted possible form**.

---

## The Reshimu and the Sefirot of Tohu

The world of *Tohu* (Chaos) — the first world that emerged after the Tzimtzum — was built primarily on the Reshimu. The Sefirot of Tohu arose from the Reshimu's potential being activated by the Kav. Because the Reshimu represents the most contracted, minimum form of Or Ein Sof, the Sefirot of Tohu were in a sense "minimally supported" — they had the intensity of the Kav's energy but the fragile containment of the Reshimu's template.

This is why the vessels of Tohu shattered: the Reshimu's template was insufficient to contain the Kav's light.

See: [[themes/tohu-and-tikkun/world-of-tohu|World of Tohu]] and [[themes/tohu-and-tikkun/vessels-of-tohu|Vessels of Tohu]].

---

## The Reshimu and Human Consciousness

The Rashab draws an analogy between the Reshimu and *zikaron* (memory) in human consciousness. When an experience passes, we do not retain the full experience but an impression of it — a *reshimu* that shapes our future without being the original experience itself. In spiritual life, the *reshimu* of a previous encounter with divine consciousness — even after the acute experience has passed — continues to shape and sustain the person's relationship with God.
