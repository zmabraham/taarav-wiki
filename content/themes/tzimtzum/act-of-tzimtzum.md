# מעשה הצמצום — The Act of Tzimtzum

**What the Primordial Contraction Actually Entailed, in the Rashab's Analysis**

---

## Overview

Before creation as we know it, the Or Ein Sof — the infinite divine light — filled all of "existence" without differentiation, leaving no space for a finite world to emerge. The Tzimtzum (צמצום — contraction, withdrawal) is the primordial act by which the Ein Sof "withdrew" its light to create a *chalal* (empty space) within which finite worlds could come into being.

The Rashab's analysis in Hemshech Taarav is distinctive in treating the Tzimtzum not as a historical event in a naive sense but as a metaphysical principle that describes the fundamental relationship between the Infinite and the finite.

---

## What the Tzimtzum Was Not

The Rashab is emphatic about what the Tzimtzum was *not*:

**Not a spatial withdrawal**: Ein Sof is not spatial; the "space" of the chalal is not physical space. The Tzimtzum must be understood as a conceptual/metaphysical withdrawal, not a physical relocation of divine presence.

**Not an absence of God**: After the Tzimtzum, the chalal is not a place where God is absent. Rather, the Or Ein Sof is present but *concealed* — its presence does not manifest in a way that would overwhelm or prevent finite existence. See: [[themes/tzimtzum/tzimtzum-literal-vs-nonliteral|Literal vs. Non-Literal Tzimtzum]].

**Not a diminishment of Ein Sof**: The withdrawal does not change the Ein Sof in any way — Ein Sof remains infinite, unchanged, and fully present. The Tzimtzum is entirely from the "perspective" of creation, not of the Creator.

---

## What the Tzimtzum Was

The Rashab identifies the Tzimtzum as a **withdrawal of the Or Ein Sof's revealed aspect** — specifically, the aspect of Or Ein Sof that would actively and overwhelmingly fill all existence. What withdraws is the *revealed* divine radiance, not the divine essence (Atzmut) itself.

Three elements of the act:

**1. The Withdrawal (Histalkut)**
The Or Ein Sof "gathered itself" — the Hebrew term used is *kibbetz atzmo* (gathered itself to itself). This contraction created the conceptual space for a differentiated, finite reality to emerge.

**2. The Leaving of the Reshimu**
The withdrawal was not total — a *reshimu* (impression, residue) of Or Ein Sof remained in the chalal. See: [[themes/tzimtzum/reshimu|The Reshimu]]. Without the reshimu, the chalal would be absolute nothingness; with it, there is the minimal divine presence needed as a foundation for worlds.

**3. The Drawing of the Kav**
After the Tzimtzum established the chalal, a *kav* (line) of Or Ein Sof was drawn into the chalal from the surrounding Or Ein Sof. See: [[themes/tzimtzum/kav|The Kav]]. The kav is the "active" divine presence within the chalal — providing the energy for the construction of the worlds from the template of the reshimu.

---

## Why the Tzimtzum? The Divine Purpose

A question the Rashab addresses directly in the Taarav: why did Ein Sof perform the Tzimtzum? The answer has two levels:

**The proximate reason**: To create space for finite worlds — for created beings who have their own apparent existence and can therefore engage in genuine service of God.

**The deeper reason**: To enable Or Chozer. The Tzimtzum is the enabling condition for the most valued thing in all of creation: a created being choosing, from within its limited, contracted existence, to return to and reveal the Infinite. Without the Tzimtzum, there can be no genuine Or Chozer.

This makes the Tzimtzum not a tragedy (loss of the undifferentiated Or Ein Sof) but the very mechanism of creation's *raison d'être*.
