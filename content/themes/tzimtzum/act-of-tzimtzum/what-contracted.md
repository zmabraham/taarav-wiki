# What Contracted in the Tzimtzum

## English Explanation

The *Tzimtzum* (צִמְצוּם, contraction/withdrawal) is one of the most debated
concepts in Kabbalistic thought: what exactly "contracted"? In the Hemshech Taarav,
the Rashab develops a nuanced position building on the Chabad tradition established
by the Alter Rebbe: the Tzimtzum was not a contraction of the divine *Essence* (עַצְמוּת,
Atzmus) — which by definition is omnipresent and immutable — but of the *Ohr Ein Sof*,
the divine light or radiating energy.

Before the Tzimtzum, Or Ein Sof filled all space (מָלֵא כָּל הָאָרֶץ, the earth is
full of His glory — in a pre-creation, metaphysical sense). There was no "room" for
created beings because the divine radiance was present everywhere at infinite intensity.
The Tzimtzum was a kind of "dimming" or "withdrawal" of this radiance from a central
point, creating a metaphysical space (מָקוֹם הַפָּנוּי) in which worlds could exist.

The Rashab emphasizes that what contracted was specifically the *revelatory* aspect
of Ein Sof — the aspect that makes the divine *present and felt* in a given space.
The divine Essence did not move or contract; only its radiating light withdrew. This
is why the Rashab can say that even after the Tzimtzum, "there is no place empty of
Him" (לֵית אֲתַר פָּנוּי מִנֵּיהּ) — the Essence remains omnipresent. What is absent
from the Makom HaPanui is the active radiance, not the Essence itself.

## Hebrew Key Terms

| Term | Transliteration | Definition |
|------|-----------------|------------|
| צִמְצוּם | Tzimtzum | Contraction/withdrawal of divine light |
| עַצְמוּת | Atzmus | Divine Essence; did not contract |
| אוֹר אֵין סוֹף | Ohr Ein Sof | Infinite divine light; what contracted |
| מָקוֹם הַפָּנוּי | Makom HaPanui | The vacated space; result of Tzimtzum |
| לֵית אֲתַר פָּנוּי | Leit Atar Panui | "No place is empty of Him" |
