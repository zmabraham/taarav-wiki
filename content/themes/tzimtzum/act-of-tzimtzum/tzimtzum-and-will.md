# Tzimtzum and the Divine Will

## English Explanation

In the Hemshech Taarav, the Rashab situates the Tzimtzum specifically in relation to
the divine *ratzon* (will) — and more precisely, in the Sefirah of *Keter* (כֶּתֶר,
Crown). Keter represents the divine will in its most primordial form: not yet revealed
as a specific intention, but as the pure, undifferentiated will that precedes all
specific content. It is in this dimension of divine will that the "decision" to perform
the Tzimtzum is located.

The Rashab draws a distinction between the essential divine will (which is eternal and
unchanging) and the *gilu'i ha-ratzon* (גִּילוּי הָרָצוֹן, revelation of will) — the
aspect of will that actively expresses itself in specific acts. The Tzimtzum is a kind
of contraction of the revealed will: instead of actively expressing the divine
presence everywhere, the will "withholds" its expression in the Makom HaPanui.

This analysis has significant implications. It means the Tzimtzum is not a physical
event or even a change in the divine reality — it is a modulation of *how much* divine
will is actively expressed in a given domain. The Ein Sof did not "decide" to contract
in a temporal sense; rather, the Tzimtzum describes an eternal aspect of how divine
will relates to creation.

The connection between Tzimtzum and will also explains why human *teshuvah* (return)
can "reverse" elements of the Tzimtzum: when a human being engages their own deepest
will (rooted in Yechida) in the direction of God, they are connecting to the same
level of divine will from which the Tzimtzum proceeded, and thereby accessing a
reality "above" the Tzimtzum.

## Hebrew Key Terms

| Term | Transliteration | Definition |
|------|-----------------|------------|
| רָצוֹן | Ratzon | Will; the divine faculty associated with Keter |
| כֶּתֶר | Keter | Crown; the Sefirah of will; location of Tzimtzum |
| גִּילוּי הָרָצוֹן | Giluiy HaRatzon | Revelation of will; what the Tzimtzum contracts |
| תְּשׁוּבָה | Teshuva | Return; accesses the level above the Tzimtzum |
| יְחִידָה | Yechida | Innermost soul-level; connected to Keter |
