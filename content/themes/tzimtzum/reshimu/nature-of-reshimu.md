# Nature of the Reshimu

## English Explanation

The *Reshimu* (רְשִׁימוּ, residue or trace) is the faint divine impression that
remains in the Makom HaPanui after the Tzimtzum. In the Hemshech Taarav, the Rashab
devotes considerable analysis to the nature of this residue, emphasizing that without
it, no world could come into existence at all.

When the Or Ein Sof contracted, it did not leave the vacated space in complete
darkness. A residue — like the faint fragrance remaining in a vessel after its
perfume has been poured out — persisted. This reshimu is so attenuated that it allows
for the existence of genuinely finite beings, yet it contains enough divine "flavor"
to prevent the vacated space from being utterly empty (and therefore non-existent).

The Rashab emphasizes that the reshimu is distinct from both the full Or Ein Sof
and from nothing. It occupies a unique middle position: it is genuine divine light
(or), but in its most contracted, minimal expression. This is why the reshimu can
serve as the foundation for finite existence — it is "just enough" divinity to support
being without overwhelming it.

An important characteristic of the reshimu is that it retains the *qualities* of all
the divine light that was present before the Tzimtzum, but in a latent, undifferentiated
form. This is why all the diversity of creation can eventually unfold from the reshimu:
it carries the "genetic code" of all that was, compressed into a minimal trace.
