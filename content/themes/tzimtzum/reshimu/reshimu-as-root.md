# Reshimu as Root of Created Worlds

## English Explanation

The Reshimu functions as the root (*shoresh*, שֹׁרֶשׁ) of all subsequent creation in
the Hemshech Taarav's account. It is the "soil" into which the Kav (the renewed ray
of divine light) enters to begin the process of world-building. Without the reshimu,
the Kav would have nothing to interact with — it would re-illuminate the Makom HaPanui
with full Or Ein Sof intensity, eliminating the possibility of finite worlds.

The Rashab explains that the reshimu serves as the "root of the vessels" (שֹׁרֶשׁ
הַכֵּלִים, shoresh hakelim). The capacity for finite, bounded existence — the very
principle of *kelim* (vessels) — derives from the reshimu. After the Tzimtzum, the
reshimu represents the aspect of "form" or "limitation" that will structure all
subsequent creation. The Kav provides the energy; the reshimu provides the blueprint
for how that energy will be structured.

The reshimu also serves as the root of the souls of Israel in the Rashab's analysis.
Because the reshimu retains the "impressions" of all the divine light that was present
before the Tzimtzum, it contains the spiritual DNA of all souls. The souls, which are
"portions of God from above" (חֵלֶק אֱלוֹקַּ מִמַּעַל), are rooted in this residual
divine impression, giving them a connection to the pre-Tzimtzum Ein Sof that cannot
be severed.
