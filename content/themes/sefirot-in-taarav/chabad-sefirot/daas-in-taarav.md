# Da'at in the Hemshech Taarav

## English Explanation

*Da'at* (דַּעַת, Knowledge) is the third of the *Chabad* intellectual Sefirot, and
in many ways the most practically significant in the Hemshech Taarav. Da'at is not
ordinary factual knowledge but rather the *connection* (קֶשֶׁר, kesher) that bridges
intellectual understanding (Chochmah and Binah) with emotional experience (the Middot).
Without Da'at, even the most profound intellectual understanding of divine matters
remains abstract and fails to produce genuine emotional engagement.

The Rashab describes Da'at using the biblical language of "knowing" as intimate union:
"And Adam *knew* his wife Eve" (Genesis 4:1). This knowing is not information
acquisition but deep personal connection — the penetrating, engaged knowing that
transforms both knower and known. When a person "knows" God through Da'at, they do
not merely possess information about God; they are *in relationship* with God in a
living, felt way.

Da'at is therefore the hinge-point of the entire spiritual process in the Hemshech
Taarav's system. The process flows: Chochmah (flash) → Binah (expansion through
hitbonenut) → Da'at (lived connection and emotional integration) → Middot (love,
awe, joy in divine service). Without Da'at, the contemplation of Binah never reaches
the heart. With Da'at, intellectual understanding transforms into emotional reality
and ultimately into behavioral expression.

The Rashab also emphasizes that Da'at is associated with *mesirut nefesh* (מְסִירַת
נֶפֶשׁ, self-sacrifice) — the willingness to transcend the self in service of a
known and deeply felt divine connection.

## Hebrew Key Terms

| Term | Transliteration | Definition |
|------|-----------------|------------|
| דַּעַת | Da'at | Knowledge/connection; bridges intellect and emotion |
| קֶשֶׁר | Kesher | Connection; what Da'at essentially is |
| מִדּוֹת | Middot | Emotional attributes; what Da'at activates |
| מְסִירַת נֶפֶשׁ | Mesirut Nefesh | Self-sacrifice; the product of deep Da'at |
| יִחוּד | Yichud | Union; what Da'at achieves between knower and known |
