# Binah in the Hemshech Taarav

## English Explanation

*Binah* (בִּינָה, Understanding) is the second intellectual Sefirah and the one that
receives the "flash" of Chochmah and expands it into a developed, structured
understanding. In the Hemshech Taarav, the Rashab describes Binah using the metaphor
of the *palace* (הֵיכָל, heichal): while Chochmah is like a single bright point of
light, Binah is like the palace that houses and structures that light, giving it form,
dimension, and relationship.

Binah is associated with the divine Mother (*Ima Ila'ah*, אִמָּא עִלָּאָה) in
Kabbalistic Partzuf terminology — the womb (*rechem*, רֶחֶם) in which divine concepts
gestate and develop. The raw flash of Chochmah enters Binah like a seed entering a
womb, and emerges from Binah as the developed, articulated intellectual content of
the lower Sefirot.

In the Hemshech Taarav, Binah plays a particularly important role in the process of
*hitbonenut* (deep contemplation). True hitbonenut is a Binah-level activity: it
takes the Chochmah-flash of divine understanding and dwells on it, turns it over,
examines it from multiple angles, and expands it until it becomes a fully formed
intellectual-emotional reality. This is why hitbonenut is the Rashab's primary
recommended practice: it is the activation of Binah's expanding, developing faculty
in the service of divine understanding.

## Hebrew Key Terms

| Term | Transliteration | Definition |
|------|-----------------|------------|
| בִּינָה | Binah | Understanding; the second intellectual Sefirah |
| הֵיכָל | Heichal | Palace; the metaphor for Binah |
| אִמָּא עִלָּאָה | Ima Ila'ah | Supernal Mother; the Partzuf of Binah |
| רֶחֶם | Rechem | Womb; Binah's gestating function |
| הִתְבּוֹנְנוּת | Hitbonenut | Contemplation; the primary Binah activity |
