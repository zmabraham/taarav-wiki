# Chochmah in the Hemshech Taarav

## English Explanation

*Chochmah* (חָכְמָה, Wisdom) is the first of the intellectual Sefirot and occupies
a central position in the Hemshech Taarav. The Rashab develops a nuanced understanding
of Chochmah that distinguishes it from ordinary human wisdom: true Chochmah is the
divine faculty of *instantaneous, undivided flash* (בְּזַק, bezak) — the first
spark of a concept before it has been developed, articulated, or analyzed.

In the chain of divine faculties, Chochmah receives from Keter (the level of pure
divine will) and transmits to Binah (Understanding). The transmission from Keter to
Chochmah represents the divine "flash" — the moment when the undifferentiated divine
will becomes the first, most primal form of divine content. This content is still
"nothing" (אַיִן, ayin) in the sense that it is not yet formed into anything specific,
but it is already the seed of everything that Binah will develop.

A key concept the Rashab develops in the hemshech is *bitul b'Chochmah* — the
self-nullification inherent in the Chochmah experience. Because Chochmah is the
first flash of divine content emerging from the infinite Ein Sof, it retains more
bitul (nullification to the divine) than any other Sefirah. When a person operates
from Chochmah — in a state of genuine divine flash and inspiration — they experience
a profound self-transparency, a sense of the divine thought "thinking through them."

## Hebrew Key Terms

| Term | Transliteration | Definition |
|------|-----------------|------------|
| חָכְמָה | Chochmah | Wisdom; the first intellectual Sefirah |
| בְּזַק | Bezak | Flash; the instantaneous quality of Chochmah |
| אַיִן | Ayin | Nothing; the nullified quality of Chochmah |
| בִּיטוּל | Bitul | Self-nullification; inherent in Chochmah |
| כֶּתֶר | Keter | Crown; Chochmah's source |
