# The Relationship Between Light and Vessel

## English Explanation

The relationship between *or* (אוֹר, light) and *kli* (כְּלִי, vessel) is one of
the foundational structural principles of the entire Hemshech Taarav. The Rashab
devotes extensive analysis to how these two elements relate, why both are necessary,
and what happens when their relationship is properly configured versus when it breaks
down.

Light (*or*) represents the divine energy, quality, and presence that inhabits the
vessel. It is dynamic, flowing, and inherently expansive — it does not naturally
limit itself. Vessel (*kli*) represents the structure, form, and boundary that gives
the light a defined space within which to manifest. Without a vessel, light has no
specific form or location; without light, a vessel is dark and empty.

The Rashab describes the ideal or-kli relationship as *mida k'neged mida* (מִדָּה
כְּנֶגֶד מִדָּה, measure for measure) — the vessel must be perfectly sized and
configured to receive the particular light it is meant to hold. Too large a vessel
for the light means the light cannot fill it; too small a vessel means the light
overwhelms and breaks it (as in the World of Tohu's vessels).

This relationship is dynamic, not static. As a person grows spiritually, their
*vessels* expand, allowing them to receive more *or*. What was formerly a mekif
(surrounding, un-received light) becomes pnimi (internalized light) as the vessel
develops the capacity to receive it. The entire process of spiritual development is
essentially the process of vessel-expansion to accommodate ever greater divine light.
