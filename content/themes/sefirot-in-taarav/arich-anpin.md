# ארך אנפין — Arich Anpin: The Long/Patient Face

**The Keter-Partzuf in Hemshech Taarav**

---

## Overview

**Arich Anpin** (ארך אנפין — Aramaic for "Long/Patient Face," also abbreviated as A"A) is the Partzuf corresponding to *Keter* (Crown), the highest Sefirah, and is the most transcendent of the five Partzufim of Atzilut. In Hemshech Taarav, the Rashab gives Arich Anpin special attention as the interface between the Or Ein Sof/Adam Kadmon level and the structured Partzufim of Atzilut.

---

## Why "Long Face"?

The term *arich* (ארך — long) refers to *erech apayim* (ארך אפים — patience, literally "long/slow to anger"). Arich Anpin is characterized by divine *patience* — the capacity to withhold judgment and extend grace for an extended period. This reflects its Keter-quality: Keter is the divine will that transcends the emotional reactions of the Middot — it does not "anger" quickly, does not rush to judgment, but sustains creation with patience.

The contrast is with *Ze'ir Anpin* (Small/Impatient Face) — the Partzuf of the Middot that is more immediately responsive (and potentially more reactive) to events in the created world.

---

## Arich Anpin as the Source of Ta'anug (Delight)

The Rashab develops in the Taarav a distinctive analysis of Arich Anpin as the source of *ta'anug* (תענוג — divine delight/pleasure). Within Keter are two qualities:
- **Ratzon (Will)**: The active divine will that initiates creation — the "lower" level of Keter
- **Ta'anug (Delight)**: The divine pleasure in creation — the "higher" level of Keter, the quality of pure divine enjoyment that is prior even to will

Arich Anpin embodies primarily the Ta'anug dimension — the divine "long face" of profound, transcendent delight that underlies all of creation. This is why Arich Anpin can be "patient" — its fundamental quality is not urgency (as Will might imply) but the deep satisfaction of divine delight.

---

## Arich Anpin and the Soul

The Rashab connects Arich Anpin to the *Yechida* soul-level — the most transcendent dimension of the soul that parallels Arich Anpin's divine transcendence. When a person acts from their deepest spiritual core (from the Yechida level), they express something of Arich Anpin's quality: patience, deep delight, transcendence of immediate reactions.

The spiritual practice of *ta'anug b'Elokus* (divine delight — finding genuine pleasure in Torah and mitzvot beyond merely performing them out of obligation) is the human expression of Arich Anpin's quality.
