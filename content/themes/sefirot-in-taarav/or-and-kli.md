# אור וכלי — Or and Kli: Light and Vessel in the Sefirot

**The Inner Structure of Each Sefirah in Hemshech Taarav**

---

## Overview

Each of the ten Sefirot has a dual structure: an inner divine light (*or*, אור) and an outer vessel (*kli*, כלי) that contains and expresses the light. This *or/kli* distinction is foundational to the Rashab's analysis of the Sefirot in Hemshech Taarav — it explains how the divine light can exist in a differentiated, structured form without losing its essential divine quality, and how the Sefirot can serve as interfaces between the Infinite and the finite.

---

## The Or: The Light Within Each Sefirah

The *or* (light) of each sefirah is its inner divine quality — the specific aspect of Or Ein Sof that this sefirah expresses. For example:

- The *or* of Chesed is the divine quality of unlimited giving — pure, undiluted lovingkindness as it exists in God
- The *or* of Gevurah is the divine quality of absolute strength and boundary — not "harsh" in a negative sense but the divine capacity for necessary limit

The *or* is essentially infinite — it is a genuine aspect of Or Ein Sof, contracted for expression through this particular sefirah, but remaining essentially divine and unlimited at its source.

---

## The Kli: The Vessel of Each Sefirah

The *kli* (vessel) is what *limits* and *shapes* the or — giving it a specific, bounded form that can interact with the levels below. The kli is what makes the or *usable* for creation — without the kli, the or would flow without structure, overwhelming what receives it (as happened in Tohu).

The kli of each sefirah corresponds to:
- The specific *form* of the sefirah's expression (how Chesed gives, how Gevurah limits, etc.)
- The *name* of the sefirah — the name is the "outer garment" that captures the kli's quality
- The *letter-combinations* associated with each sefirah in Kabbalistic tradition

---

## The Rashab's Key Analysis: The Inner vs. Outer Dimensions

The Rashab in the Taarav goes deeper than the or/kli distinction to distinguish:

**Pnimiyut HaSefirah (Inner dimension)**: The or's own essential quality — what the sefirah is in itself, before it interacts with the levels below it

**Chitzoniyut HaSefirah (Outer dimension)**: The sefirah's functional expression — what it does in relation to the sefirot below it and the worlds it produces

This distinction matters because: the same sefirah (say, Chesed) has a different quality at its pnimiyut level than at its chitzoniyut level. At pnimiyut, Chesed is the pure divine attribute of lovingkindness as it exists in God. At chitzoniyut, Chesed is the specific way God gives to creation — calibrated, limited, adapted to what creation can receive.

---

## Or/Kli and Or Yashar/Or Chozer

The or/kli distinction maps onto the Or Yashar/Or Chozer dynamic:

- **Or** (within the sefirah) corresponds to **Or Yashar** — the divine light entering the sefirah from above
- **Kli** (the sefirah's vessel) generates **Or Chozer** — the reflected light that ascends back upward from the sefirah's engagement with the or

The better calibrated the kli (the better the vessel fits the or), the more robust the Or Chozer generated. This is why the Tikkun world's kelim — better calibrated to their orot — generate more robust Or Chozer than the Tohu world's kelim did.
