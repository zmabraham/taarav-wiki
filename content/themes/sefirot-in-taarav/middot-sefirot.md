# ספירות המידות — The Middot Sefirot

**Chesed, Gevurah, Tiferet and the Emotional Attributes in Hemshech Taarav**

---

## Overview

The *Middot* (מידות — attributes, measures) are the six emotional Sefirot: **Chesed** (Lovingkindness), **Gevurah** (Strength/Severity), **Tiferet** (Beauty/Harmony), **Netzach** (Victory/Eternity), **Hod** (Splendor), and **Yesod** (Foundation). Together they comprise *Ze'ir Anpin* (the Small Face) — the primary Partzuf of emotional divine attributes. In Hemshech Taarav, the Rashab analyzes the Middot as the divine "character" — the personality of the divine as it engages with creation.

---

## The Structure of the Middot

The six Middot form a coherent emotional structure:

**Chesed (חסד — Lovingkindness)**: The capacity for unconditional giving, love, and expansion. The right arm of Ze'ir Anpin — the divine quality of reaching out to the "other" with generosity.

**Gevurah (גבורה — Strength/Severity)**: The capacity for necessary boundary, discipline, and contraction. The left arm — the divine quality of withholding when giving would be harmful.

**Tiferet (תפארת — Beauty/Harmony)**: The mediation between Chesed and Gevurah — the synthesis that creates genuine beauty through the integration of opposites. The torso/heart of Ze'ir Anpin.

**Netzach (נצח — Victory/Eternity)**: The enduring, persistent quality — the divine capacity for sustained, patient engagement with the world. The right hip.

**Hod (הוד — Splendor)**: The receptive, responsive quality — the divine capacity for genuine acknowledgment and gratitude. The left hip.

**Yesod (יסוד — Foundation)**: The channeling, connecting sefirah — the "pipe" through which Chesed through Hod's energy is gathered and transmitted to Malchut. The covenant (*brit*) of Ze'ir Anpin.

---

## The Rashab's Analysis: Middot and ChaBaD

A central contribution of the Taarav: the Middot are not independent — they emerge from and are shaped by the ChaBaD sefirot above them. Chesed is not raw benevolence but *intellectually grounded benevolence* — love that understands why giving is appropriate. Gevurah is not raw harshness but *intellectually grounded discipline* — restriction that understands why restriction is necessary.

The quality of a person's Middot thus depends directly on the quality of their ChaBaD — their intellectual understanding of divine reality. Shallow understanding produces immature emotional responses; deep understanding produces refined, nuanced emotional attributes that are genuinely responsive to the divine will.

This is the basis for the Chabad practice of *avodah through ChaBaD*: cultivating the intellectual sefirot in order to refine and elevate the emotional Middot.

---

## The Middot and the Animal Soul's Elements

The Rashab draws a correspondence between the Sefirot of Middot and the animal soul's four elements:

- **Chesed** corresponds to the *water* element (flowing, giving, pleasure-seeking turned toward the divine)
- **Gevurah** corresponds to the *fire* element (intense, boundary-setting, passionate)
- **Tiferet** corresponds to the *air* element (harmonizing, integrating, expansive)
- **Malchut** (not exactly a Middah but the receptive base) corresponds to *earth* (stable, grounded, receptive)

Refining the animal soul's elements involves specifically cultivating the corresponding Middot through ChaBaD-grounded meditation and practice.
