# Zeir Anpin: The Short-Faced One

## English Explanation

*Zeir Anpin* (זְעִיר אַנְפִּין, "the short-faced one" or "the small face") is the
Partzuf comprising the six emotional Sefirot: Chessed, Gevurah, Tiferet, Netzach,
Hod, and Yesod. In the Hemshech Taarav, Zeir Anpin represents the divine
*personality* — the aspect of God that engages with creation through the full
spectrum of relational qualities: love, strength, beauty, victory, splendor, and
the capacity for transmission.

Zeir Anpin is the "son" of Abba and Ima in the Partzuf family — it receives the
intellectual content generated by Chochmah and Binah and expresses it as emotional
engagement with the world. While Abba and Ima operate in a relatively abstract,
intellectual domain, Zeir Anpin is the divine aspect that is most directly involved
in the *relationship* with created beings.

The Rashab emphasizes that Zeir Anpin's six components work as an integrated whole
— not six separate qualities operating in isolation (which would be Tohu-mode) but
a cooperative emotional personality that modulates its expression according to the
situation. Chessed and Gevurah, though polar opposites, are both present in Zeir
Anpin and balance each other through Tiferet (Beauty/Harmony).

In the broader divine structure, Zeir Anpin corresponds to the divine name *HVYH*
(יהוה) and is associated with the dimension of divine governance (*hashgacha*) that
actively manages and relates to creation.
