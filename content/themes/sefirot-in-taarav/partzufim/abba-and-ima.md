# Abba and Ima: The Parental Partzufim

## English Explanation

*Abba* (אַבָּא, Father) and *Ima* (אִמָּא, Mother) are two of the five primary
*Partzufim* (divine "faces" or personalities) in Kabbalistic thought, corresponding
respectively to the Sefirot of Chochmah and Binah. In the Hemshech Taarav, the
Rashab develops the relationship between Abba and Ima as a model for understanding
how divine creativity operates.

Abba represents the initial, undeveloped "seed" of divine thought — the flash of
Chochmah-level insight. Ima represents the gestating, developing, expanding faculty
of Binah — the "womb" in which Abba's seed develops into the structured content
that will be transmitted to the lower Partzufim and worlds. Together, Abba and Ima
constitute the divine "parental" pair from which the rest of the Sefirot-world
emerges.

The Rashab notes that the relationship between Abba and Ima is one of the deepest
divine unions (*yichudim*, יִחוּדִים) in the Kabbalistic system. Their ongoing
"union" is what continuously generates the divine content of the lower Sefirot. This
is why prayer, which seeks to re-establish divine union and flow, is described as
promoting the *yichud Abba v'Ima* (union of Father and Mother).

In human spiritual terms, Abba and Ima correspond to the flashing inspiration of an
idea and the sustained contemplative work that develops it into a full understanding —
together constituting the complete intellectual process that underlies genuine divine
service.
