# Arich Anpin: The Long-Faced One

## English Explanation

*Arich Anpin* (אַרִיךְ אַנְפִּין, "the long-faced one" or "the extended face") is
the highest of the Partzufim, corresponding to the Sefirah of Keter. In the
Hemshech Taarav, Arich Anpin represents the divine *patience* and *long-suffering*
— the aspect of God that extends time and space to allow creation the opportunity
to develop and return. The "long face" is the face of one who does not rush to
judgment, who extends infinite patience.

Arich Anpin is also associated with the divine *will* (*ratzon*) at its most
primordial level — the will before it has any specific content or direction. It is
the "will to will" — the divine capacity to desire and intend before any particular
desire has formed. This makes Arich Anpin the root of all subsequent divine
expression: every specific divine quality (represented by the lower Partzufim)
ultimately derives from this foundational, undirected divine will.

The Rashab develops in the hemshech the important concept that Arich Anpin contains
within it both *Ta'anug* (תַּעֲנוּג, divine pleasure) and *Ratzon* (will) as its two
primary dimensions. Ta'anug is the most interior, hidden dimension — the divine
pleasure in existence itself, prior to any specific pleasure. Ratzon is the outer
expression of that interior pleasure as directed desire.

Understanding Arich Anpin is important for grasping the Rashab's teaching about
Yechida: the soul's Yechida level connects directly to Keter/Arich Anpin, which is
why Yechida accesses divine reality at its most primordial and most transcendent.
