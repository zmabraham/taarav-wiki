# Nukva: The Feminine Partzuf

## English Explanation

*Nukva* (נוּקְבָּא, feminine) is the Partzuf corresponding to the Sefirah of Malchut
(Kingdom) and represents the divine feminine principle in the Kabbalistic system.
In the Hemshech Taarav, the Rashab develops Nukva's role as the Partzuf that
interfaces most directly with the created worlds — the divine "face" turned downward
toward creation.

Nukva is described as the *Shechinah* (שְׁכִינָה, divine presence) — the aspect of
God that "dwells" within creation rather than remaining transcendent above it. The
Shechinah is the divine that is immanent, accessible, and directly experienced in
the world. In human experience, it is the Shechinah-Nukva that one encounters in
prayer, in the performance of mitzvot, and in the felt sense of divine presence.

The relationship between Zeir Anpin and Nukva is one of the central dynamics of
the entire Kabbalistic system: their *yichud* (union) is what generates the flow
of divine blessing into the created worlds. Prayer, mitzvot, and Torah study all
contribute to and promote this yichud — which is why these activities are described
as *l'shem yichud* (לְשֵׁם יִחוּד, for the sake of unification).

The Rashab notes that Nukva has a unique property: she *receives* the divine content
of Zeir Anpin and transmits it to the worlds below. This makes Nukva both the
ultimate receiver (within the divine system) and the ultimate giver (in relation
to creation) — the model for the Jewish people, who receive divine light from above
and transmit it to the world below.
