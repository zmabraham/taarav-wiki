# הרשימו כיסוד — Reshimu as Foundation

**The Reshimu as the Structural Template of All Created Reality in Hemshech Taarav**

---

## Overview

While the Reshimu is the minimal divine presence in the chalal, it functions as the *yesod* (foundation) of all created reality — the structural template that determines what the worlds will be like even before the Kav activates them. In Hemshech Taarav, the Rashab develops the Reshimu's foundational role as a key to understanding the nature of creation and the persistence of divine structure throughout all the worlds.

---

## The Reshimu as the "Plan" of Creation

The Rashab draws on an analogy from human creativity: a master architect, before building, develops a complete *takhnit* (plan) for the building — dimensions, structure, room arrangement, everything. The actual building then follows this plan. Similarly, the Reshimu is the divine "plan" for creation — encoded in the minimum divine trace remaining in the chalal. When the Kav comes, it "builds" according to this plan.

This makes the Reshimu the source of all the *distinctions* and *orders* within creation:
- The distinction between the worlds (why Atzilut is different from Beriya)
- The order of the Sefirot (why Chochma precedes Bina)
- The nature of each world's limitations (why the physical world is specifically as it is)

All of this is "encoded" in the Reshimu — the blueprint that the Tzimtzum left behind.

---

## The Reshimu and the Consistency of Creation

A profound implication: because the Reshimu is the stable template underlying all the worlds, the structure of the worlds is *consistent* — the same Sefirot appear at every level (though in increasingly contracted forms), the same principles (Or Yashar/Or Chozer, Mashpia/Mekabel, etc.) operate throughout.

This consistency is not accidental — it reflects the fact that all the worlds emerge from the same Reshimu-template, activated by the same Kav. The Rashab uses this to ground the Kabbalistic principle of *histakkelut* (analogy/correspondence) between worlds: what is true at the cosmic level has its analogue at the human level, and vice versa, because both emerge from the same fundamental template.

---

## The Reshimu and Human Spiritual Structure

The Rashab applies the Reshimu concept to human spiritual psychology: every person has a *reshimu* of their soul's highest level permanently inscribed in their spiritual makeup. Even when the person is far from spiritual awareness, the reshimu of the Yechida (the soul's highest level) continues to sustain their fundamental divine connection.

This reshimu of the Yechida is what enables *teshuvah* (return) — the path back is always present because the foundation (reshimu) of the highest soul-level is always there, even if completely concealed.

---

## The Reshimu as the Basis for Dirah B'Tachtonim

Finally, the Rashab teaches that the ultimate divine goal — the *dirah b'tachtonim* (divine dwelling in the lowest realms) — is grounded in the Reshimu. The physical world is not foreign to the divine because it was built on the Reshimu — the divine template that permeates even the most contracted level of creation. When the divine presence is fully revealed in the physical world (the ultimate Tikkun), it will be revealed *through* the Reshimu's template — the divine blueprint that was always there, waiting to be uncovered.
