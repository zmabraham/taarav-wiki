# הקו — The Kav: The Directed Line of Or Ein Sof

**A Detailed Analysis of the Kav in Hemshech Taarav**

---

## Overview

The **Kav** (קו — line) is the thin, directed beam of Or Ein Sof that was extended into the *chalal* (empty space) after the Tzimtzum. It is the active, generative principle within the chalal — the divine energy that "builds" the spiritual worlds by interacting with the passive *Reshimu* (divine impression). See also: [[themes/tzimtzum/kav|Kav in the Tzimtzum context]] for the basic definition.

This page provides the deeper analysis of the Kav as developed in Hemshech Taarav.

---

## The Kav's Double Character

A key insight from the Rashab in the Taarav: the Kav has a double character — it is simultaneously:

**1. Or Ein Sof in contracted form**
The Kav is not a separate entity from Or Ein Sof — it *is* Or Ein Sof, but Or Ein Sof in its most directed, most contracted expression within the chalal. The Kav carries the full infinite quality of Or Ein Sof — but *focused* into a single sequential flow rather than the undifferentiated omnidirectional fullness of pre-Tzimtzum Or Ein Sof.

**2. The activating principle within the chalal**
Relative to the Reshimu, the Kav is the "active" principle — the energy that engages with the Reshimu's template and actualizes it. The Kav is like the teacher who transmits knowledge; the Reshimu is like the student's pre-existing capacity to receive.

---

## The Kav's Sequential Nature

The most distinctive quality of the Kav is its *sequential* (rather than simultaneous) character. Before the Tzimtzum, Or Ein Sof "existed" in all places and all levels simultaneously — there was no before and after, no higher and lower, no sequence. The Kav introduces sequence:

- It begins at the uppermost boundary of the chalal (closest to the surrounding Or Ein Sof)
- It extends downward through the chalal in successive stages
- Each stage of the Kav produces a different level of the spiritual worlds

This sequential character is the origin of *time* as we know it — the "before and after" of creation. Time is, at its root, the sequential unfolding of the Kav through the chalal.

---

## The Kav and the Igulim (Circles)

The Rashab analyzes the relationship between the *Kav* (linear, sequential) and the *Igulim* (עגולים — concentric circles, representing a different model of post-Tzimtzum light). These two models — linear and circular — represent two different aspects of divine self-expression:

- **Igulim**: The divine light as *surrounding* all worlds equally — an Or Mekif that does not differentiate between worlds (analogous to Sovev Kol Almin)
- **Yosher/Kav**: The divine light as *entering into* the sequential structure of the worlds — graduated, directional (analogous to Memaleh Kol Almin)

The Rashab in the Taarav develops the relationship between these two models as fundamental to understanding how the Ein Sof relates to the worlds both as transcendent (Igulim) and as immanent (Yosher/Kav).

---

## The End of the Kav: The Physical World

The Kav descends through Adam Kadmon, Atzilut, Beriya, Yetzira, and Asiya — its energy diminishing at each successive level — until it reaches the very bottom of the chalal: the physical world. The physical world is thus not the "furthest" from God in the sense of being "abandoned" — it is the *endpoint* of the Kav's journey. God's creative energy reaches all the way to the physical world.

The Rashab uses this to address the apparent paradox of physical existence: if God is infinite, how can there be a "lowest" world? His answer: the Kav's endpoint is the final expression of Or Ein Sof's self-limitation — not an absence of God but the fulfillment of God's creative intention to have a world at the furthest possible remove from the source.
