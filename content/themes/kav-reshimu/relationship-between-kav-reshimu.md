# היחס בין הקו לרשימו — The Relationship Between Kav and Reshimu

**How the Two Post-Tzimtzum Principles Work Together in Hemshech Taarav**

---

## Overview

The relationship between the Kav and the Reshimu is the generative engine of all spiritual structure in the post-Tzimtzum world. In Hemshech Taarav, the Rashab provides the most philosophically developed analysis of this relationship in Chabad literature, revealing it as a microcosm of the fundamental relationship between giver and receiver, or between Or Yashar and Or Chozer, at the deepest possible level.

---

## Active and Passive: Mashpia and Mekabel

The fundamental characterization: the Kav is *mashpia* (influencer/giver) and the Reshimu is *mekabel* (receiver). This is the most primordial instance of the Or Yashar / Or Chozer dynamic:

- **Kav (Mashpia)**: Provides the energy, the light, the divine vitality that "enters" the chalal and activates the potential within the Reshimu
- **Reshimu (Mekabel)**: Provides the template, the receptive capacity, the "form" that shapes how the Kav's energy is expressed

Neither alone is sufficient:
- Kav without Reshimu = raw energy without structure = potentially destructive (similar to Tohu)
- Reshimu without Kav = template without energy = inert potential, never actualized

Together they produce the ordered, structured spiritual worlds.

---

## The Interaction Creates the Sefirot

The Rashab's analysis: when the Kav's energy enters the Reshimu's template at a specific level of the chalal, the result is a *sefirah* — a structured divine attribute. The particular quality of each sefirah is determined by:

1. The level of the Kav (how much energy / what quality of light at that point)
2. The quality of the Reshimu at that level (what template is encoded)

Different combinations of Kav-energy and Reshimu-template produce the different sefirot. The ten sefirot thus emerge as natural consequences of the Kav/Reshimu interaction across different levels of the chalal.

---

## The Analogy: Teacher and Student

The Rashab uses an extended analogy: the Kav is like a teacher who has profound knowledge; the Reshimu is like a student with particular capacities. The teacher's teaching is shaped by what the student can receive — the teacher does not simply pour in all their knowledge but adapts to the student's template. Yet without the teacher's knowledge, the student's capacity remains unrealized.

The spiritual worlds that emerge from the Kav/Reshimu interaction are like the content that emerges when the teacher's knowledge meets the student's capacity — neither the pure knowledge alone nor the pure capacity alone, but the particular expressed understanding that results from their interaction.

---

## The Relationship's Implication for Human Avodah

The Rashab draws a profound practical conclusion: every human being also has a *Kav* dimension and a *Reshimu* dimension in their spiritual life:

- The *Kav* dimension of a person: the divine vitality and divine teachings (Torah and mitzvot) that enter from above
- The *Reshimu* dimension of a person: the accumulated spiritual impressions, habits, and capacity for reception that prior avodah has built up

Genuine spiritual growth requires both: the person must build their *reshimu* capacity (through regular study and practice) so that when divine inspiration (*kav*) comes, it has a vessel to be received into. Inspiration without capacity is wasted; capacity without inspiration is barren.
