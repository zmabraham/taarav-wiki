# Reshimu and Free Choice

## English Explanation

One of the more subtle teachings in the Hemshech Taarav connects the Reshimu to
the *bechirah* (בְּחִירָה, free choice) of human beings. The Rashab explains that
genuine free choice requires an environment in which the divine is neither absent
(which would make meaningful existence impossible) nor overwhelmingly present (which
would make genuine choice impossible). The Reshimu creates precisely this environment.

In a domain filled with full or-Ein-Sof, there would be no freedom — the divine
would be so obviously, overwhelmingly present that all creatures would be compelled
toward it. In a domain with no divine presence at all, there would also be no freedom —
nothing could exist. The Reshimu creates a "middle space" — a domain where the divine
is genuinely present but not coercively evident.

This is the spiritual dimension of the Makom HaPanui: it is not merely an empty space
in which worlds happen to be created. It is specifically the space of *free choice* —
the domain where created beings can genuinely choose to seek or ignore the divine,
because the divine presence (Reshimu) is real but subtle.

The Rashab notes that the entire history of human spiritual development is the story
of the interaction between the Reshimu (the subtle divine presence) and human choice:
each human action either activates and elevates the Reshimu within them and their
world, or suppresses and ignores it. Torah and mitzvot are specifically the technologies
for activating the Reshimu — for making the latent divine potential become manifest.

| הַסְתָּרָה | Hastara | Concealment; the Reshimu's "hidden" quality |
