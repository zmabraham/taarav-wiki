# הרשימו — The Reshimu: The Divine Impression

**A Detailed Analysis of the Reshimu in Hemshech Taarav**

---

## Overview

The **Reshimu** (רשימו — impression, residue, trace) is the minimal divine presence that remained within the *chalal* after the Or Ein Sof withdrew in the Tzimtzum. It is the *passive* principle within the chalal — the template or "mold" within which the Kav's energy is channeled and structured. See also: [[themes/tzimtzum/reshimu|Reshimu in the Tzimtzum context]] for the basic definition.

This page provides the deeper analysis of the Reshimu as developed in Hemshech Taarav.

---

## The Reshimu as Information

One of the Rashab's most penetrating insights in the Taarav: the Reshimu functions as *encoded information* — it contains within its minimal form the entire structure of what the worlds will be. The Reshimu is, in this sense, the *code* or *blueprint* of creation, encoded in the most compressed possible form.

This analogy is helpful: just as a seed contains within it all the information needed to produce a specific plant — without yet being that plant — the Reshimu contains within it all the differentiated structure of the spiritual worlds, without yet being those worlds. The Kav "reads" and actualizes this encoded information.

---

## The Reshimu's Minimal but Essential Quality

The Rashab emphasizes a paradox: the Reshimu is the *minimum* possible presence of Or Ein Sof — yet it is precisely this minimum quality that enables the worlds. If the Reshimu were more intense, it would not be compatible with finite worlds; if it were entirely absent, there would be no foundation for worlds.

This "minimum necessary" quality of the Reshimu is itself a product of divine wisdom: the Tzimtzum was calibrated precisely to leave the exact minimum of divine presence needed to serve as a foundation for creation. The Reshimu is thus an expression of *chochmah* (divine wisdom) in its most contracted and precise form.

---

## The Reshimu and Human Memory

The Rashab develops an extended analogy between the Reshimu and *zikaron* (memory) in human cognition. When an experience passes, we retain not the full experience but an impression — a reshimu of it. This impression:

- Is less vivid than the original experience
- Yet contains the essential "shape" and meaning of that experience
- Serves as the basis for the memory's influence on future thought and action

In spiritual life, the reshimu of a previous encounter with divine consciousness — a moment of deep prayer, an experience of divine love or awe — shapes the person's spiritual orientation even after the acute experience has passed. Learning to maintain and develop these *reshimot* (impressions) is a form of spiritual practice that the Rashab identifies in the Taarav.

---

## The Reshimu and Tohu's Vessels

The Rashab analyzes why the vessels of the Tohu world were inadequate. His answer involves the Reshimu: the Tohu world was built *primarily* on the template of the Reshimu, without sufficient mediation by the Kav. The Reshimu's template, while encoding all the worlds' potential, was designed to hold the Kav's energy in a *balanced* way — when the Kav's energy came in at full force without proper mediation through the Reshimu's containing structure, the vessels shattered.

The Tikkun world represents a more refined relationship between the Reshimu's template and the Kav's energy — the proper calibration that prevents another shattering.
