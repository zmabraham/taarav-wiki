# Kav as Divine Will: The Line of Purpose

## English Explanation

In the Hemshech Taarav, the Rashab interprets the *Kav* (קַו, ray/line) not merely
as a geometrical concept — a thin line of light entering the Makom HaPanui — but as
the expression of *divine will* (רָצוֹן, ratzon) in its most directed and purposeful
form. The Kav represents the divine "decision" to create worlds and the directional
intention behind that decision.

Before the Tzimtzum, the divine will was completely undifferentiated — it was the
Infinite's eternal, non-directed quality of being-and-radiating. The Tzimtzum was the
contraction of that undifferentiated radiation. The Kav is the *re-emergence* of
divine will, but now in a specifically directed form: it enters the Makom HaPanui
with a defined purpose (creating ordered worlds) and a defined direction (from above
downward, along the axis that will become the chain of hishtalshelut).

The Rashab notes that the Kav's directionality is itself a form of *tzimtzum* (self-
limitation): the divine will, by focusing itself into a specific direction and purpose,
is voluntarily limiting its infinite, omnidirectional quality. This is the "inside"
of the Tzimtzum — not just the withdrawal of light but the *purposeful, directed*
quality of the post-Tzimtzum divine will.

Understanding the Kav as directed divine will explains why the worlds it produces are
*goal-oriented*: they are not accidental by-products of divine overflow but the
intended products of a specific divine intention.

| Term | Transliteration | Definition |
|------|-----------------|------------|
| קַו | Kav | Ray/line; the directed divine will |
| רָצוֹן | Ratzon | Will; what the Kav expresses |
| כַּוָּנָה | Kavana | Intention; the purposeful quality of the Kav |
| כִּיוּוּן | Kiwun | Direction; the Kav's defining quality |
| תַּכְלִית | Tachlit | Purpose/goal; what directs the Kav |
