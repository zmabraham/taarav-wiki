# Kav and Adam Kadmon

## English Explanation

*Adam Kadmon* (אָדָם קַדְמוֹן, Primordial Human) is the first and highest spiritual
structure that the Kav produces upon entering the Makom HaPanui. In the Hemshech
Taarav, the Rashab treats Adam Kadmon as the divine "template" in human form — the
original divine personality (אִישׁ, ish) from whose spiritual structure all subsequent
worlds and souls are derived.

The Kav enters the Makom HaPanui and immediately begins to organize the reshimu into
a structured form. The highest level of this organization is Adam Kadmon — a complete,
integrated spiritual personality with all ten Sefirot in perfect, harmonious proportion.
Adam Kadmon is the "first human" in a cosmic, not biological, sense: the first
instance of the divine organizing itself into a relational, personality-structured form.

Adam Kadmon occupies the highest level of the created/emanated order. It is closer
to the Ein Sof than any subsequent world — it is sometimes described as being "inside"
the Makom HaPanui in a different sense than the lower worlds, which are "further down"
the Kav. The light of Adam Kadmon is so intense that it would overwhelm everything
below it if it were to radiate downward without further tzimtzumim (contractions).

The subsequent spiritual worlds (Atzilut, Beriah, Yetzirah, Asiyah) are each the result
of further tzimtzumim applied to the light of Adam Kadmon — progressive dimmings that
make the divine presence accessible to successively lower levels of existence.
