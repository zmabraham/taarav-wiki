# Kav Differentiating the Worlds

## English Explanation

One of the Kav's most important functions in the Hemshech Taarav is the
*differentiation* of spiritual worlds. Before the Kav, the Makom HaPanui was
relatively undifferentiated — it contained only the reshimu, which carried the
encoded potential for all spiritual levels but without any actual hierarchy. The
Kav's directed penetration of the Makom HaPanui is what creates the actual
differentiation of levels — the "above" and "below" of the hishtalshelut.

As the Kav penetrates deeper into the Makom HaPanui, different levels of light-
intensity produce different spiritual worlds. At the shallowest depth of penetration
(closest to the source of the Kav), the light is most intense — this produces Adam
Kadmon, then Atzilut. At deeper penetration, the light diminishes in intensity,
producing Beriah, then Yetzirah, then Asiyah, and finally the physical world.

The Rashab uses the metaphor of a ray of light passing through successively denser
media: each medium reduces the intensity of the light while giving it a specific
"color" or quality. The Kav passing through the reshimu produces different "colors"
at each depth, which are the distinct qualities of each spiritual world.

This differentiation is purposeful, not accidental: each level of the hishtalshelut
serves a specific function in the overall divine plan. The lower worlds are not
failures of the upper worlds — they are necessary stages in the complete expression
of divine creative intentionality.

| הִבְדָּלוּת | Hivdalut | Differentiation; what the Kav creates |

| הִשְׁתַּלְשְׁלוּת | Hishtalshelut | Chain of worlds; the Kav's structure |
