# נשמות באדם קדמון — Souls in Adam Kadmon

**The Soul's Highest Root in the Primordial Man**

---

## Overview

At the very highest level of the soul's cosmic grounding, Hemshech Taarav reveals that Jewish souls are rooted not only in Atzilut but ultimately in *Adam Kadmon* (אדם קדמון — Primordial Man) — the highest world within the chalal, above even Atzilut. This is the most elevated dimension of the soul's divine connection, and it is developed particularly in connection with the *Yechida* level and the Rashab's teaching about the ultimate purpose of Jewish spiritual life.

---

## The Soul as a Microcosm of Adam Kadmon

The name *Adam Kadmon* — Primordial Man — is significant. The human form (*tzelem Adam*) is not modeled after the biological human but the reverse: the biological human is modeled after the cosmic structure of Adam Kadmon. The human soul, in its deepest root, participates in the *tzelem Elohim* (divine image) that is Adam Kadmon.

The Rashab develops this in the Taarav: the *ten Sefirot* of Adam Kadmon are the ultimate "blueprint" for the structure of the Jewish soul. Each soul is a *microcosm* — a small-scale reflection — of Adam Kadmon's vast structure. This means:

- The soul's Yechida corresponds to Adam Kadmon's Keter (at its most transcendent)
- The soul's Chaya corresponds to Adam Kadmon's Chochma
- The soul's Neshama corresponds to Adam Kadmon's Bina
- etc.

---

## The Root Before Tohu and Tikkun

A crucial teaching: the soul's root in Adam Kadmon is *above the Tohu/Tikkun distinction*. Adam Kadmon precedes both the world of Tohu (which emerged from Adam Kadmon's lower aspects) and the world of Tikkun (which emerged in response to Tohu). Therefore, the soul's root in Adam Kadmon is a root in a level that was never broken, never shattered, and needs no repair.

This has a profound practical implication: at the deepest level of the soul, every Jewish person is connected to a level that is wholly intact, wholly divine, wholly beyond the historical drama of breaking and repair. The work of *teshuvah* (repentance and return) is not repairing the soul's Adam Kadmon root — which was never damaged — but revealing it more fully at the lower levels.

---

## Souls in Adam Kadmon and the Era of Moshiach

The Rashab connects the revelation of the soul's Adam Kadmon root to the era of *Moshiach* (the Messiah). In the messianic era, the Yechida level — the soul's Adam Kadmon root — will be fully revealed not only in individual moments of *mesirut nefesh* but as the constant, manifest reality of every Jewish soul. The concealment of this root, which characterizes our current era, will be dissolved.

This is why the Rashab emphasizes the importance of studying and internalizing the concepts of the Hemshech Taarav: learning about the soul's Adam Kadmon root is itself a form of beginning to reveal that root — *itaruta d'le'ela* (arousal from above) that prepares for the fuller messianic revelation.

---

## The Lights of Adam Kadmon as the Source of Torah

The Rashab develops a connection in the Taarav between the *orot d'Adam Kadmon* (lights of Adam Kadmon) and the deepest level of Torah. Just as the soul's root in Adam Kadmon transcends the Tohu/Tikkun distinction, the Torah's root in Adam Kadmon transcends the distinction between the Torah as it exists now (in our world of Tikkun) and the Torah as it will be revealed in the messianic era.

The study of *Torat HaSod* (the hidden dimension of Torah, including Chassidus) is specifically aimed at accessing the Adam Kadmon dimension of Torah — drawing down the pre-Tohu/Tikkun Torah-light into our Tikkun-world.
