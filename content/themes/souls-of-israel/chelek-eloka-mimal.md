# חלק אלוקה ממעל — Chelek Eloka Mima'al

**The Soul as a Literal Portion of Divinity in Hemshech Taarav**

---

## Overview

The statement that the Jewish soul is *chelek Eloka mima'al mamash* (חלק אלוקה ממעל ממש — "a literal portion of divinity from above") — drawn from Job 31:2 and developed extensively in Chabad literature — is taken by the Rashab in Hemshech Taarav to its most radical and philosophically precise conclusion. The word *mamash* (ממש — literally, in actuality) is key: the soul is not merely *like* a portion of divinity, or *compared to* divinity — it is *actually* a part of the divine.

---

## The Rashab's Analysis

The Rashab in the Taarav confronts the philosophical challenge directly: How can a finite human soul be a "literal portion" of the Infinite? Doesn't this either compromise the divine infinity (by suggesting the Infinite can be divided into "portions") or fail to genuinely describe the soul (by describing it as something it cannot be)?

His resolution involves the distinction between the *Atzmut* (divine essence) and the *Or Ein Sof* (divine light). The soul is a *chelek* (portion) not of the Atzmut in its absolute transcendence but of the *Or Ein Sof as it manifests in Atzilut*. Specifically:

- The soul is a portion of the *Sefirot of Atzilut*
- The Sefirot of Atzilut are themselves divine — they are not created but emanated
- Therefore, a portion of the Sefirot is genuinely divine

But the Rashab goes further: the soul is also connected, through the Yechida level, to the Atzmut itself — making the chelek relationship not merely with the emanated Sefirot but with the very essence of divinity.

---

## Implications of the Chelek Relationship

**1. The soul cannot be permanently severed from God**
If the soul is genuinely a chelek of the divine, it cannot ultimately be cut off from its source, no matter how far it has descended or how much it has been covered by sin and distraction. The chelek relationship is ontological — it cannot be destroyed, only concealed. This is the basis for *teshuvah* (repentance) — the path back is always open because the connection was never truly broken.

**2. The soul's return to God is its nature**
Just as a piece of a stone will, if given the opportunity, gravitate toward joining other stones of the same kind, the soul — as a chelek of divinity — naturally gravitates toward its divine source. *Ratzo* (running toward God) is not an external obligation imposed on the soul but the natural expression of what the soul inherently is.

**3. The elevation of physical acts**
When the chelek-soul performs a physical mitzvah, it draws the physical world into contact with the divine — because the soul's chelek-quality means it carries divine reality with it wherever it goes. This is the ground for the transformative power of mitzvot.

---

## The Word Mamash: Why "Literally"?

The Rashab emphasizes the word *mamash* (literally, actually) as a guard against two possible misreadings:

- Against **allegorical reading**: The soul is not *as if* a portion of divinity, not metaphorically divine — it actually is divine in a real, non-figurative sense
- Against **pantheist reading**: The soul is a *portion* (chelek, a specific, individuated aspect) — it is not identical with the entire divine, but genuinely participates in the divine nature
