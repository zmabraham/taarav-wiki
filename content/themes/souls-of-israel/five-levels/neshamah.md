# Neshamah: The Breath of Intellect

## English Explanation

*Neshamah* (נְשָׁמָה, breath) is the third level of the soul, corresponding to the
World of Beriah (creation) and to the intellectual faculties — specifically the *Chabad*
capacities of Chochmah (Wisdom), Binah (Understanding), and Da'at (Knowledge). The
name *neshamah* — breath — suggests the soul's most intimate connection to the divine:
"And He breathed into his nostrils the breath of life" (Genesis 2:7), the personal,
direct divine gift.

In the Hemshech Taarav, the Neshamah level is associated with *sekhel* (שֵׂכֶל,
intellect) — not intellectual ability in the ordinary sense, but the capacity to
comprehend divine concepts (*elokut*, אֱלֹקוּת) and integrate them into one's
understanding of reality. A person whose Neshamah is developed genuinely *understands*
God's greatness, the meaning of Tzimtzum and Or Ein Sof, the structure of the Sefirot
— not as abstract concepts but as living realities that inform their entire perspective.

The Rashab emphasizes that Neshamah-level development is achieved primarily through
sustained *hitbonenut* — deep contemplative study of divine concepts that moves from
abstract understanding to genuine intellectual-emotional integration. When a person
truly grasps (at the Neshamah level) the reality of divine unity, it naturally
transforms their emotional relationship to God (Ruach) and their actions (Nefesh).

The Neshamah is sometimes said to be the soul-level that "never sins" — even when a
person sins, the Neshamah remains untouched by the sin, though it may be temporarily
more hidden.
