# Yechida: The Singular Soul Unified with Ein Sof

## English Explanation

*Yechida* (יְחִידָה, singular/unique) is the highest of the five soul-levels, and in
the Hemshech Taarav it receives special attention as the most central concept of the
entire hemshech. Yechida corresponds to the level of *Keter* (Crown) and to the
divine *Atzmus* (Essence) — it is the point in the soul that is most directly
connected to, and in some sense identical with, the Infinite.

The word *yechida* means "singular" or "unique" — it is the point of absolute
uniqueness in the soul, the element that cannot be replicated or shared because it
is the specific, singular divine spark that constitutes the essence of this particular
soul's connection to its divine source. While all Jewish souls share the same
Neshamah-level substance (they are all "portions of God"), each soul's Yechida is
absolutely unique to that soul.

Yechida transcends even the Chaya level in that it is not merely close to the divine
but is, in a metaphysical sense, *of the divine* — not a created thing that received
divine light but a literal extension of the divine being itself. The Rashab uses the
analogy of a flame (Chaya) versus an ember of fire (Yechida): the flame is separate
from its source even while connected to it; the ember is literally a piece of the
fire itself.

In the current state of exile (גָּלוּת, galut), Yechida is largely inaccessible
to ordinary consciousness — it sleeps, covered by the layers of Nefesh, Ruach,
Neshamah, and Chaya. The Messianic era is described as the awakening of Yechida
on a collective level.

| Term | Transliteration | Definition |
|------|-----------------|------------|
| יְחִידָה | Yechida | Singular soul-level; directly connected to Atzmus |

| עַצְמוּת | Atzmus | Essence; the divine level Yechida touches |
| גָּלוּת | Galut | Exile; Yechida sleeps during exile |
| יְחִידוּת | Yechidut | Singularity; the quality of Yechida |
