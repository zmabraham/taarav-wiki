# Ruach: The Wind-Spirit of Emotion

## English Explanation

*Ruach* (רוּחַ, wind/spirit) is the second level of the soul in the five-level
schema, corresponding to the World of Yetzirah (formation) and to the emotional
faculties. The name *ruach* — wind — suggests movement, flow, and the capacity to
"blow" in different directions: the emotions of the soul are dynamic, variable, and
powerful, capable of transforming a person's experience and motivation.

In the Hemshech Taarav, the Rashab associates Ruach with the *middot* (מִדּוֹת,
character traits and emotional attributes) — love, fear, joy, compassion, and the
other emotional sefirot (Chessed through Yesod). At the Ruach level, divine service
becomes experiential rather than merely behavioral: a person not only *performs*
mitzvot (Nefesh) but *feels* love of God, awe before the divine, joy in serving Him.

The Ruach is what makes spiritual experience rich and textured. Without developed
Ruach, divine service can become rote and mechanical — correct in its external form
but lacking inner vitality. The Rashab emphasizes that the proper development of Ruach
requires first grounding oneself in the Nefesh level (correct action) and then
cultivating emotional connection through *hitbonenut* (contemplation) of divine
greatness, which naturally produces emotional response.

The Zoharic teaching is that "there is no good deed without a good heart" (עֲבוֹדַת
הַלֵּב, avodat halev — service of the heart): Ruach is the "heart" that gives the
body's actions their ultimate worth.

## Hebrew Key Terms

| Term | Transliteration | Definition |
|------|-----------------|------------|
| רוּחַ | Ruach | Wind/spirit; the emotional soul-level |
| יְצִירָה | Yetzirah | World of Formation; corresponds to Ruach |
| מִדּוֹת | Middot | Character traits/emotions; the Ruach's domain |
| עֲבוֹדַת הַלֵּב | Avodat HaLev | Service of the heart; Ruach in action |
| הִתְבּוֹנְנוּת | Hitbonenut | Contemplation; develops Ruach connection |
