# Chaya: The Living Soul Beyond Intellect

## English Explanation

*Chaya* (חַיָּה, living being) is the fourth level of the soul in the Hemshech
Taarav's schema, corresponding to the World of Atzilut. The Chaya transcends the
ordinary faculties of intellect and emotion — it is a level of the soul so close to
the divine source that its experience cannot be easily articulated or understood
through the normal categories of consciousness.

The Rashab describes the Chaya as the soul's "face" (פַּרְצוּף, partzuf) that is
turned toward God rather than toward the world. While Nefesh, Ruach, and Neshamah
are all, in various degrees, *within* the body and engaged with the world, Chaya
transcends this inward engagement. It is the level of soul that never fully "descends"
into the body — it hovers above, encompassing the body from without (like an ohr mekif)
rather than animating it from within.

Because Chaya operates at the Atzilut level (the world that is essentially divine),
a person's contact with their Chaya-level soul is not ordinary intellectual or
emotional experience. It is closer to mystical *hitbatlelut* (self-nullification) —
the moment when the self becomes transparent to the divine. The ecstatic experiences
of the great mystics and tzaddikim are associated with Chaya-contact.

In ordinary life, Chaya makes itself felt primarily in those moments of uncaused
divine joy (*simcha*, שִׂמְחָה) or uncaused awe (*yira*, יִרְאָה) that arise
spontaneously, without any particular intellectual trigger — the sudden, wordless
sense of divine presence.
