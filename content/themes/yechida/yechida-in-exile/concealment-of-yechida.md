# The Concealment of Yechida in Exile

## English Explanation

In the Hemshech Taarav, the Rashab describes the current historical period as a time
when *Yechida* is largely in a state of *hester* (הֶסְתֵּר, concealment) or *sheina*
(שֵׁינָה, sleep). The collective Yechida of the Jewish people — the dimension of the
Jewish soul most directly connected to divine Atzmus — does not radiate openly into
consciousness or into the world. It waits, dormant but undiminished, beneath the
layers of the lower soul-levels.

The Rashab identifies several reasons for this concealment. The primary reason is
the *galut* (גָּלוּת, exile) — the condition in which the Shechinah and the Jewish
people are removed from their natural spiritual home. Exile is not merely geographical;
it is a spiritual condition in which the highest levels of divine connection (Yechida
and its corresponding levels of or Ein Sof) are not accessible. The exile is a kind
of "Tzimtzum" at the level of the Jewish collective soul.

The concealment of Yechida explains several phenomena the Rashab addresses in the
hemshech: why even spiritually sincere Jews may feel distant from God; why the highest
levels of divine inspiration (nevua, prophecy) are absent; and why the generation is
spiritually weaker than earlier generations even when it is more knowledgeable.

Despite this concealment, the Rashab emphasizes that Yechida is never destroyed and
never diminishes. The divine Atzmus it participates in is eternal and immutable.
The concealment is a concealment of *experience*, not of reality.

| גָּלוּת | Galut | Exile; the condition that produces Yechida-concealment |
