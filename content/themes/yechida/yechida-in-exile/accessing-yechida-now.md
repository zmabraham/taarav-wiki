# Accessing Yechida Now: Flashes of the Deepest Level

## English Explanation

Despite Yechida's general concealment during the exile period, the Rashab in the
Hemshech Taarav identifies several specific pathways through which a person can
access flashes (*brizot*, בְּרִיזוֹת) of Yechida-level experience even in the current
period. These moments of Yechida-access are brief — they do not constitute the full,
sustained revelation of Yechida that will characterize the Messianic era — but they
are genuine and spiritually transformative.

The primary pathway is *mesirut nefesh* (מְסִירַת נֶפֶשׁ, self-sacrifice or
self-surrender): moments when a person transcends all personal calculations and
surrenders completely to the divine. In historical times, this was most clearly
manifested in the readiness of Jews to give their lives rather than violate the
Torah (*kiddush Hashem*, sanctification of God's name). But the Rashab teaches that
mesirut nefesh has a daily, internal dimension — the willingness to surrender one's
ego, preferences, and comfort in the service of God.

A second pathway is *teshuvah* (תְּשׁוּבָה) in its deepest form — not merely
behavioral return to mitzvot but the soul's fundamental movement back toward its
divine source. Because Yechida is the point in the soul that was never truly
separated from God, deep teshuvah accesses this eternal connection and momentarily
makes it felt.

Prayer with complete concentration (כַּוָּנָה, kavanah) at the moment of *Shema Yisrael*
is a third pathway: the declaration of divine unity is simultaneously the soul's
declaration of its own unity with the divine.

## Hebrew Key Terms

| Term | Transliteration | Definition |
|------|-----------------|------------|
| מְסִירַת נֶפֶשׁ | Mesirut Nefesh | Self-surrender; the primary pathway to Yechida |
| תְּשׁוּבָה | Teshuva | Return; a pathway to Yechida-access |
| קִדּוּשׁ ה' | Kiddush Hashem | Sanctification of God's name; Yechida in action |
| כַּוָּנָה | Kavanah | Intention; enables momentary Yechida-access |
| שְׁמַע יִשְׂרָאֵל | Shema Yisrael | Hear O Israel; the declaration of unity/Yechida |
