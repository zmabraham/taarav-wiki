# יחידה ואין סוף — Yechida and Ein Sof

**The Soul's Singular Point of Contact with the Infinite**

---

## Overview

Of all the soul-levels, Yechida is the one that stands in the most direct relationship to *Or Ein Sof* — the Infinite Divine Light. The lower soul-levels (Nefesh, Ruach, Neshama, Chaya) each relate to specific Sefirot and Partzufim within the structured worlds; Yechida alone transcends the structured worlds entirely and touches the Or Ein Sof that precedes all structure. In Hemshech Taarav, the Rashab analyzes this relationship with precision: Yechida is not identical with Ein Sof, but it is the point in the soul that is closest to — and most reflective of — the divine Infinity.

---

## The Soul as a "Portion of God"

The classical description of the Jewish soul — *chelek Eloka mimal* (חלק אלוק ממעל — a portion of God from above) — is understood differently at each soul-level:

- At the **Nefesh** level, the soul is a "portion of God" in the sense that it is divinely created and animated
- At the **Ruach** level, the divine quality is expressed through the soul's emotional responsiveness to holiness
- At the **Neshama** level, the divine quality is expressed through the soul's intellectual capacity for divine understanding
- At the **Chaya** level, the divine quality is expressed through the soul's capacity for divine life-force beyond the self
- At the **Yechida** level, the soul is a "portion of God" in the most literal sense — it touches the divine Infinity itself, not a mediated or contracted form of it

The Rashab's contribution in the Taarav is to articulate exactly *how* Yechida relates to Ein Sof — not through the mediation of Sefirot, Partzufim, or any contracted form, but through the *essence* of Or Ein Sof as it exists before Tzimtzum.

---

## Yechida and the Pre-Tzimtzum Reality

Ein Sof before Tzimtzum is characterized by absolute, undifferentiated Infinity — no structure, no boundaries, no "here" and "there." After Tzimtzum, the divine light takes on structure through the Kav, the Sefirot, and the Partzufim. Yechida of the soul is the dimension that corresponds to *before* Tzimtzum — it is the soul's "pre-Tzimtzum point."

This means Yechida carries within it a quality of divine *infinitude* that no other soul-level possesses. When a person accesses their Yechida level (through profound states of self-nullification, intense mystical experience, or the complete transcendence of self that sometimes accompanies martyrdom or overwhelming divine love), they are accessing something that genuinely corresponds to the pre-Tzimtzum Or Ein Sof.

---

## The Practical Asymmetry

There is a significant asymmetry in the soul's relationship with Ein Sof at the Yechida level:

**Downward flow**: Or Ein Sof flows *into* Yechida continuously — it is this flow that sustains the soul's most fundamental existence. Without the constant influx of Or Ein Sof at the Yechida level, the soul would lose its most essential divine quality.

**Upward return**: The soul's *return* to Ein Sof at the Yechida level is rare and exceptional — it requires extraordinary spiritual states that transcend ordinary consciousness. The Rashab does not suggest that ordinary spiritual practice regularly activates the Yechida-Ein Sof dynamic in consciousness; rather, that dynamic is present *structurally* even when not consciously experienced.

---

## Yechida and Emunah

One practically important expression of the Yechida-Ein Sof relationship, in the Rashab's analysis, is *emunah* (faith). Emunah at its deepest level — the simple, unquestioned faith that transcends rational analysis — is an expression of Yechida. It is the soul's most direct "knowing" of God, which precedes and transcends the intellect's knowing. When a person has unshakeable faith in God despite being unable to rationally justify it, they are drawing on Yechida's direct contact with Ein Sof.
