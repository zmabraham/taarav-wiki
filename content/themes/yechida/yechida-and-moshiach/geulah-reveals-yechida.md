# Geulah Reveals Yechida: The Messianic Awakening

## English Explanation

One of the Hemshech Taarav's most eschatologically significant teachings is that the
*Geulah* (גְּאוּלָּה, redemption/Messianic era) is specifically and uniquely the
period of *Yechida's* revelation. While the earlier eras of history have been
characterized by the progressive revelation of lower soul-levels, the Messianic era
is when the highest level — Yechida — will be openly revealed.

The Rashab maps the historical eras onto the five soul-levels: each major era of
Jewish history corresponds to the revelation of one of the five levels. The current
era, the era of *galut* (exile), corresponds to the deeper concealment of all five
levels. The Messianic era is the era when all five, and especially the highest,
will be fully revealed.

This means the Messianic era is not merely a political or material improvement —
it is a qualitative transformation of human and divine relationship. When Yechida
is openly revealed, the direct divine-human connection at the level of Atzmus will
be accessible to all, not just the extraordinary mystic. The prophet's vision that
"all flesh will see" the divine (כָּל בָּשָׂר יִרְאוּ, Kol Basar Yir'u) refers to
this Yechida-revelation becoming universally accessible.

The Rashab uses this teaching to explain why the Messianic era requires a qualitatively
new level of Torah — the *pnimiyut haTorah* (inner dimension of Torah) that has been
progressively revealed through Kabbalah and Chassidus. This inner Torah corresponds
to the Yechida-level of the divine wisdom — it must be revealed precisely because
the Messianic era requires Yechida-level spiritual engagement.
