# יחידה — Yechida: The Singular Soul-Level

**Hub Page for the Yechida Theme in Hemshech Taarav**

---

## Overview

**Yechida** (יחידה — "singular," "unique") is the highest of the five levels of the Jewish soul, and one of the most profound and original topics in Hemshech Taarav. While the four lower soul-levels — Nefesh, Ruach, Neshama, and Chaya — correspond to various Sefirot and Partzufim of Atzilut, Yechida transcends Atzilut itself. It corresponds to *Keter* (specifically to *Arich Anpin*) and, in the Rashab's analysis, to the very level of *Adam Kadmon* — the cosmic divine template that precedes the four worlds.

The Rashab's treatment of Yechida in Hemshech Taarav is distinctive: unlike earlier sources that mention Yechida as a largely inaccessible abstraction, the Rashab analyzes Yechida as a dynamic, practically relevant dimension of the soul that becomes increasingly accessible in the later generations of Chassidic history — and particularly in the era approaching Moshiach.

---

## Yechida's Essential Character

Yechida means "singular" — that which is absolutely one, without division or multiplicity. The lower soul-levels each have differentiated qualities (emotions, understanding, vitality), but Yechida is prior to all differentiation: it is the soul as pure unity, the point at which the soul is most completely one with its divine source.

The Rashab connects this to the divine attribute of *Yachid* (יחיד — the One, the Singular) — Yechida of the soul corresponds to the divine singularity itself.
