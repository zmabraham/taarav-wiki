# Ratzo in Shemoneh Esrei

## English Explanation

*Ratzo* (רָצוֹא, running/rushing) in the context of the Hemshech Taarav refers to
the soul's dynamic of ascending, reaching forward, rushing toward the divine. The
Rashab provides a detailed analysis of where this ratzo dynamic manifests in the
structured prayer of *Shemoneh Esrei* (שְׁמוֹנֶה עֶשְׂרֵה, the Eighteen Blessings,
also called the Amidah).

The structure of Shemoneh Esrei itself embodies the ratzo-veshov dynamic. The initial
three blessings are characterized by a particular quality of upward reaching — the
worshipper approaches the divine, first acknowledging the God of the Patriarchs, then
praising divine power, then declaring divine holiness. This ascending movement is
the *ratzo* of the prayer structure.

The Rashab notes that within the prayer itself, certain meditations produce the ratzo
state more intensely: particularly the *Kedushah* (קְדוּשָּׁה, the sanctification of
God), where the worshipper joins the angelic choir in declaring "holy, holy, holy"
(*kadosh kadosh kadosh*). This moment is described as the most elevated *ratzo*-moment
in the prayer service — the human soul momentarily achieving the angelic state of
continuous ascent.

The Rashab cautions that ratzo in prayer must be balanced by *shov* — the worshipper
must not lose themselves entirely in the ascending movement but must maintain the
capacity to return to earth, to continue their embodied divine service.
