# Ratzo and Fire: The Element of Ascent

## English Explanation

In the Hemshech Taarav, the Rashab connects the dynamic of *ratzo* (rushing/ascending)
with the element of *fire* (אֵשׁ, esh). Fire is the natural symbol of ratzo because
fire naturally rises: its flame always tends upward, it is self-consuming in its
intensity, and it transforms what it touches. These qualities map precisely onto the
ratzo dynamic in the soul.

The connection is made explicit in the *Seraphim* (שְׂרָפִים, "burning ones") — the
angelic beings described in Isaiah's vision (Isaiah 6) who are in a state of continuous
fiery ascent, crying "Holy, holy, holy" in an endless upward movement. The Seraphim
are pure ratzo — they are so entirely in the ascending state that they have no capacity
for *shov* (return). This is why they are not human: human beings are precisely defined
by the combination of ratzo AND shov.

The Rashab develops the fire-ratzo connection through the concept of *ahavat Hashem*
(love of God): genuine divine love burns with the quality of fire — it is consuming,
rising, self-transcending. The great mystics who experienced intense divine love
describe it in precisely these terms — a fire that consumed all other desires and
drove the soul upward.

The caution the Rashab adds: fire without containment destroys rather than warms.
The vessel that channels fire (like a candle or a furnace) makes fire useful and
life-giving. Similarly, ratzo contained within the vessel of Torah and mitzvot
becomes a positive, life-giving spiritual force rather than a consuming one.

## Hebrew Key Terms

| Term | Transliteration | Definition |
|------|-----------------|------------|
| אֵשׁ | Esh | Fire; the element corresponding to ratzo |
| שְׂרָפִים | Seraphim | Burning ones; angels of pure ratzo |
| אַהֲבַת ה' | Ahavat Hashem | Love of God; the ratzo-fire in the soul |
| הִתְלַהֲבוּת | Hitlahavut | Fiery enthusiasm; the ratzo-quality in prayer |
| כְּלִי | Keli | Vessel; needed to make fire/ratzo productive |
