# רצוא ושוב — Ratzo v'Shov: Running and Returning

**Hub Page for the Ratzo v'Shov Theme in Hemshech Taarav**

---

## Overview

**Ratzo v'Shov** (רצוא ושוב — "running and returning") is one of the most dynamic and pervasive structural principles in Hemshech Taarav. Derived from the vision of Ezekiel (1:14 — "and the living creatures *ran and returned* like the appearance of lightning"), Ratzo v'Shov describes a fundamental rhythm of the divine-soul relationship: an alternating movement between *ratzo* (rushing forward, yearning, ascending) and *shov* (returning, drawing back, descending).

The Rashab in Hemshech Taarav develops Ratzo v'Shov far beyond its earlier Kabbalistic uses, analyzing it as:
- A structural feature of the divine reality itself (the Sefirot and Partzufim pulse in a Ratzo/Shov rhythm)
- A description of the soul's fundamental relationship with God
- A practical spiritual principle for understanding prayer, Torah study, and all divine service
- A cosmic principle that explains the alternation between concealment and revelation throughout history

---

## The Two Movements

**Ratzo** (רצוא — running, yearning): The impulse of the soul to transcend its current state and merge with the divine source — to "run" toward God beyond all limits, to annihilate the self in divine infinity. This is the ecstatic, ascending, transcending movement.

**Shov** (שוב — returning): The movement back — the soul returning to its embodied, bounded existence; accepting the limits of form, remaining in the world, continuing its task in physical reality. This is the grounding, descending, world-accepting movement.

Neither movement alone is the goal. Both are essential, and their alternation — their sustained rhythm — is what constitutes healthy spiritual life.
