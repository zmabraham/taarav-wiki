# Shov as Fulfillment: The Return That Completes

## English Explanation

While *shov* (שׁוֹב, return) might seem like the "lesser" movement in the ratzo-veshov
dynamic — after all, ratzo is the exciting, ascending movement — the Hemshech Taarav
reveals that shov is in a deep sense the *completion* and *fulfillment* of the entire
dynamic. Ratzo without shov is incomplete; shov is what gives ratzo its meaning and
purpose.

The Rashab explains this through the concept of the divine *dira betachtonim* (dwelling
in the lower realms). The divine purpose is not to draw creation upward into the
Infinite and absorb it there. The purpose is to bring the Infinite *down* into the
finite and have it dwell there. Ratzo serves this purpose by establishing the connection
between above and below; shov fulfills it by bringing that connection *downward* into
embodied reality.

In terms of or yashar and or chozer: ratzo corresponds to or chozer (reflected light
ascending) and shov corresponds to or yashar (direct light descending). The complete
dynamic requires both: the soul rushes upward (ratzo/or chozer) and then returns
downward (shov/or yashar), bringing with it the divine connection it established above.

The person who has experienced deep prayer (ratzo) and then *returns* from that prayer
to their ordinary life with the quality of the divine encounter integrated into their
personality has completed the full cycle. The shov is what makes the ratzo *useful*
for the world — it translates spiritual experience into embodied reality.
