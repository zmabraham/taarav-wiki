# רצוא — Ratzo: The Yearning/Running

## Literal and Spiritual Meaning

The Hebrew word *ratzo* (רצוא) means literally "running" or "rushing forward." It shares a root with *ratzon* (רצון — will, desire), hinting that the movement is not mere locomotion but the expression of innermost desire. In Ezekiel's vision of the divine chariot (1:14), the *Chayot HaKodesh* — the holy living beings — "ran and returned like the appearance of lightning." This image of a flame that surges upward, straining toward its source, becomes the master metaphor for *ratzo* throughout Chassidic literature.

Spiritually, *ratzo* denotes the soul's drive to transcend its current condition and dissolve into G-dliness. It is the impulse of *bitul* — self-nullification — taken to its logical extreme: not merely subduing the ego but longing to shed the very boundaries of selfhood and merge with the infinite divine light. The soul experiences an almost gravitational pull toward its source, an ache to return to the state of pure union it knew before descending into the body.

## Ratzo in Prayer — Shemoneh Esrei

The *Amidah* (Shemoneh Esrei) is the supreme expression of *ratzo* in the liturgical structure. The worshiper stands with feet together — recalling the angels whose feet appear as one unified foot — in a posture of being wholly gathered upward. The bowing at the opening and closing blessings enacts the soul's forward surge. The silent, whispered quality of the *Amidah* reflects the quality of *ratzo*: speech that disappears into silence, language straining beyond its own capacity. The Rashab notes that at the peak of the *Amidah*, the worshiper momentarily touches a dimension of consciousness that transcends the ordinary structure of petition and response.

## The Fire Metaphor

The flame is the *ratzo* metaphor par excellence. A candle flame perpetually strains upward, attempting to detach from the wick, to become pure fire rejoining the element above. Yet it cannot — it must remain connected to the wick and the wax, or it is extinguished. This tension is not a flaw but the very nature of the flame: its beauty and warmth arise from the upward yearning held in productive tension with its earthly anchor.

## The Rashab's Development in Taarav

In Hemshech Taarav, the Rashab (Rabbi Shalom DovBer Schneersohn) elevates *ratzo* from a descriptive concept into a structural principle of divine reality itself. He demonstrates that the *Sefirot* pulse in a *ratzo/shov* rhythm, that the soul's experience of *ratzo* corresponds to the dimension of [[themes/yechida]] — the highest soul-level, which retains awareness of its essential unity with G-d. The *ratzo* impulse, when properly channeled, is the engine that drives genuine *tefillah* and propels the soul toward the highest revelations described in Taarav, including the downward flow of [[themes/or-yashar-or-chozer]] (the straight and returning lights).

## Key Hebrew Terms

- **רצוא** (*ratzo*) — running, yearning, the ascending movement
- **ביטול** (*bitul*) — self-nullification, the inner stance of *ratzo*
- **רצון** (*ratzon*) — will/desire, sharing the root of *ratzo*
- **כלות הנפש** (*klot hanefesh*) — the soul's "expiration" in longing, the extreme of *ratzo*
- **חיות הקודש** (*Chayot HaKodesh*) — the holy living beings of Ezekiel's vision
