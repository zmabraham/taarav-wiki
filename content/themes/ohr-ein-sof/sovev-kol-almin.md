# סובב כל עלמין — Sovev Kol Almin: Encompassing All Worlds

**The Transcendent Mode of Or Ein Sof in Hemshech Taarav**

---

## Overview

**Sovev Kol Almin** (סובב כל עלמין — "encompassing all worlds") is one of the two primary modes in which Or Ein Sof relates to the created worlds, and it is the more transcendent of the two. In contrast to *Memaleh Kol Almin* (which fills and animates from within), Sovev Kol Almin *surrounds* and *transcends* the worlds — it is the divine presence that is beyond all created reality, sustaining it from outside rather than from within.

In Hemshech Taarav, the Rashab gives this concept considerable attention, particularly in relation to the soul's higher levels (Chaya and Yechida) and to the structure of the Sefirot.

---

## The Rashab's Analysis

The term *sovev* (סובב) means "surrounding" or "encompassing" — a presence that is not *within* something but *around* it. Applied to divine reality, Sovev Kol Almin describes the mode of Or Ein Sof that does not enter into and differentiate according to the capacity of each world. Instead, it relates to all worlds equally — transcending the distinctions between them.

**Key characteristics of Sovev Kol Almin**:

**1. Transcendence of differentiation**: Sovev Kol Almin does not change or adapt to each world. It encompasses Adam Kadmon and the physical world with equal, undifferentiated presence. This is its defining quality — it does not diminish or intensify according to the vessel below it.

**2. Or Mekif**: Sovev Kol Almin is the supreme Or Mekif (encompassing light) — the light that is always beyond the vessel's capacity to internalize. Even the highest sefirot of Atzilut cannot "absorb" the Sovev; it always remains "outside."

**3. The source of *bitachon* (trust)**: The Rashab teaches that the spiritual quality of *emunah* (faith) and *bitachon* (trust) — the soul's sense that God is fundamentally in control regardless of circumstances — derives from Sovev Kol Almin. Since Sovev is not conditioned by the state of the lower worlds, it provides an unconditional divine presence that the soul can access even in the most difficult circumstances.

---

## Sovev Kol Almin and Human Experience

The Rashab connects Sovev Kol Almin to the experience of *yirah tata'ah* (lower fear/awe) — the basic sense that God is present and observing. But at a deeper level, Sovev corresponds to *yirah ila'ah* (higher awe) — the sense of divine vastness that transcends comprehension.

When a person experiences moments of pure awe — a sense of the infinite that cannot be contained in any thought or feeling — they are touching the Sovev Kol Almin dimension of divine reality. The Rashab identifies such experiences as genuine contact with the Or Mekif of the soul's highest levels.

---

## The Relationship Between Sovev and Memaleh

The Rashab develops the relationship between Sovev and Memaleh as a complementary pair:

- **Sovev** = the Ein Sof's transcendence; unchanging, unconditional, present equally everywhere
- **Memaleh** = the Ein Sof's immanence; present *within* each being according to its capacity to receive

Together, they express the full theological picture: God is both the transcendent ground that is beyond all worlds and the immanent vitality that animates every created thing from within. Neither alone is sufficient for a complete understanding of divine reality.
