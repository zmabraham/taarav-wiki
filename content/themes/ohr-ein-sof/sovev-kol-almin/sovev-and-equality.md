# Sovev and Divine Equality

## English Explanation

*Sovev Kol Almin* (סוֹבֵב כָּל עָלְמִין, "surrounds all worlds") is one of the two
fundamental modes of divine presence described in the Hemshech Taarav. The defining
characteristic of Sovev is its *equality* — it relates to all worlds equally, without
distinction, because it is too transcendent to be differentiated by the varying levels
of existence.

This equality is not indifference but a form of supreme divine presence that is, in
a sense, "blind" to the differences between worlds. From Sovev's perspective, the
loftiest spiritual world and the grossest material reality are equally close to —
and equally far from — the Infinite. Sovev does not descend into any world and is
therefore not subject to the gradations of the chain of worlds.

The Rashab uses the metaphor of the sun's light compared to its source. The sun
illuminates a palace and a trash heap equally — not because they are equal, but
because the sun's light is not affected by the dignity or indignity of what it shines
upon. Similarly, Sovev illuminates all realities equally because its transcendence
makes the differences between realities irrelevant to it.

This equality has profound spiritual implications. It means that no matter how far a
person has descended spiritually, they remain equally "surrounded" by divine presence
at the level of Sovev. The Rashab uses this to explain how teshuvah (return) is always
possible: the Sovev-level of divine presence never withdrew, regardless of how deep
the concealment became.
