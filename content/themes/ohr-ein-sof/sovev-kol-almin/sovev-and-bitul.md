# Sovev and Bitul: The Experience of Transcendent Presence

## English Explanation

The experience of *Sovev Kol Almin* in human consciousness is associated in the
Hemshech Taarav with *bitul* (בִּיטוּל, self-nullification) — the experience of
dissolving one's sense of separate selfhood in the overwhelming awareness of divine
transcendence. When a person genuinely contacts the Sovev-level of divinity, the
inevitable response is a profound humbling of the ego.

The Rashab explains this connection: because Sovev is equally present to all things
and not differentiated according to the levels of the chain of worlds, it makes no
distinctions based on individual identity. A person who opens to the Sovev-level
experience finds that their own individual particularity — their specific talents,
accomplishments, status, opinions — all become trivially small in the face of the
All-Encompassing. This is the root of genuine *yira* (יִרְאָה, awe/fear of God):
not fear of punishment but the awe-struck recognition of infinite divine presence
that makes the self seem vanishingly small.

The Rashab carefully distinguishes two types of bitul: *bitul hayesh* (בִּיטוּל הַיֵּשׁ,
nullification of existence) — the dissolution of selfhood experienced in Sovev
contact — and *bitul bimtziut* (בִּיטוּל בִּמְצִיאוּת, nullification within existence)
— the integration of that awareness into ongoing, functioning selfhood. The goal of
divine service is not permanent *bitul hayesh* (which would make normal life
impossible) but rather a life flavored by the *bitul bimtziut* that comes from regular
contact with the Sovev level through prayer, Torah study, and meditation.

## Hebrew Key Terms

| Term | Transliteration | Definition |
|------|-----------------|------------|
| בִּיטוּל | Bitul | Nullification; the response to Sovev-contact |
| יִרְאָה | Yira | Awe; the emotional experience of Sovev |
| בִּיטוּל הַיֵּשׁ | Bitul HaYesh | Nullification of existence |
| בִּיטוּל בִּמְצִיאוּת | Bitul BeMtziut | Nullification within existence |
| עַצְמִי | Atzmi | Self; what bitul humbles |
