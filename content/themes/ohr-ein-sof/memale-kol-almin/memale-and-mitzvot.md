# Memale and the Power of Mitzvot

## English Explanation

A central theme in the Hemshech Taarav is the relationship between *Memale Kol Almin*
and the performance of *mitzvot* (divine commandments). The Rashab teaches that
mitzvot are uniquely effective precisely because they engage with the Memale-level of
divine presence — the divine energy that is *within* the world, accessible through
physical action.

The logic is as follows: Memale is the divine energy that animates all creation from
within. Mitzvot, being commandments given by the divine will (Ratzon Elyon), have a
special affinity with Memale because they are expressions of the same divine will that
created the world through Memale in the first place. When a person performs a mitzvah,
they align their action with the divine will that continuously creates the world —
they are, in a sense, "going with the grain" of creation's deepest structure.

The Rashab also teaches that mitzvot draw *additional* Memale-energy into the world.
A mitzvah performed with proper intention (kavanah) and joy (simcha) is not merely
conforming to creation's existing structure — it actively draws new divine energy from
above into the world. The mitzvah "opens a channel" (פּוֹתֵחַ צִינּוֹר, potei'ach
tzinor) through which divine light flows into the specific dimension of reality engaged
by the mitzvah.

This is why the Rashab views the performance of physical mitzvot as the highest
spiritual activity: not despite their physicality, but because of it. Physical action
in the lowest world draws divine energy from the highest levels down into the most
concrete reality — achieving a more comprehensive unification of divine and created
than any purely spiritual practice can.

## Hebrew Key Terms

| Term | Transliteration | Definition |
|------|-----------------|------------|
| מִצְווֹת | Mitzvot | Commandments; the mechanism for drawing Memale |
| כַּוָּנָה | Kavanah | Intention; required for mitzvot to draw Memale optimally |
| שִׂמְחָה | Simcha | Joy; amplifies the mitzvah's drawing power |
| צִינּוֹר | Tzinor | Channel; what a mitzvah opens for divine light |
| רָצוֹן עֶלְיוֹן | Ratzon Elyon | Supreme Will; mitzvot align with it |
