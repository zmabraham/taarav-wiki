# Memale and the Natural World

## English Explanation

The Rashab in the Hemshech Taarav develops a sophisticated understanding of how
*Memale Kol Almin* relates to the natural world (*teva*, טֶבַע). Nature is not
something separate from the divine — it is the *expression* of Memale at the level
of the physical world. The regularities of nature (the rising of the sun, the
growth of plants, the laws of physics) are the patterns according to which Memale
flows into the physical world.

This means that every natural object and process has an inner divine dimension:
the divine energy (Memale) that animates it. A tree is not merely wood and leaves
organized according to botanical laws — it is a specific, individual expression of
divine creative energy in arboreal form. The laws of nature describe *how* Memale
operates at the physical level; they don't explain *what* Memale is or *why* it
operates at all.

The Rashab uses this framework to explain the spiritual significance of physical
mitzvot (commandments performed with physical objects). A mitzvah performed with a
physical object directly engages the Memale within that object — the divine energy
that gives the object its existence. When a person waves the Four Species on Sukkot,
for example, they are drawing on and elevating the Memale within the lulav, etrog,
myrtle, and willow. The physical action is simultaneously a spiritual one.

This also explains why the study of nature can be a form of divine service: it is
studying the patterns of Memale's flow into the physical world, which is a form of
Torah study in the broad sense.
