# Memale as Life-Force of Creation

## English Explanation

*Memale Kol Almin* (מְמַלֵּא כָּל עָלְמִין, "fills all worlds") is the divine mode
that actively animates creation from within. In the Hemshech Taarav, the Rashab
describes Memale as the *chayut* (חַיּוּת, life-force) that sustains every created
being in existence moment by moment. Without the continuous flow of Memale, creation
would simply cease to exist — not merely weaken or degrade, but return to absolute
nothingness.

The Rashab explains that the created world does not have intrinsic, self-sustaining
existence. It was created *ex nihilo* (יֵשׁ מֵאַיִן, something from nothing) and
continuously requires a renewal of that creative act to remain in being. Memale is
the divine energy that performs this continuous renewal — what the Alter Rebbe calls
in the Tanya, "the letters of divine speech through which the world was created
continuously recreate it at every moment."

This teaching has significant implications for understanding physicality. The physical
world, which appears to be entirely self-sustaining according to natural laws, is in
fact perpetually dependent on Memale for its existence. Natural laws are, in this
view, simply the regularized pattern of how Memale flows into the material world —
they are divine habit, not divine independence.

The Rashab also uses this to address the perception of divine absence (*hester panim*,
הֶסְתֵּר פָּנִים). Even when God's face seems hidden, Memale continues to sustain
existence. The concealment affects only the *revelatory* experience of the divine,
not the fundamental existential dependence of creation on Memale.

## Hebrew Key Terms

| Term | Transliteration | Definition |
|------|-----------------|------------|
| חַיּוּת | Chayut | Life-force; what Memale provides to creation |
| יֵשׁ מֵאַיִן | Yesh Me'Ayin | Something from nothing; creation's origin |
| הִתְחַדְּשׁוּת | Hitchadshut | Renewal; the continuous act Memale performs |
| הֶסְתֵּר פָּנִים | Hester Panim | Hiding of the divine face; doesn't affect Memale |
| אוֹתִיּוֹת | Otiyot | Letters; the medium through which Memale flows |
