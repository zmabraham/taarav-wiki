# אור אין סוף שלפני הצמצום — Or Ein Sof Before the Tzimtzum

**The Pre-Creation State of Or Ein Sof in Hemshech Taarav**

---

## Overview

Before the Tzimtzum, the Or Ein Sof filled all of "existence" in an undifferentiated, unlimited manner — there were no worlds, no sefirot, no distinctions of any kind. This primordial state is both the starting point for all of the Rashab's analysis in Hemshech Taarav and the horizon that all spiritual work aims to reveal. Understanding what Or Ein Sof was *before* the Tzimtzum helps illuminate why the Tzimtzum was necessary and what the spiritual goal of creation is.

---

## The Pre-Tzimtzum State: Key Characteristics

**Complete undifferentiation**: Before the Tzimtzum, the Or Ein Sof had no within or without, no higher or lower, no beginning or end. All of these distinctions — which define the structure of the post-Tzimtzum worlds — were absent. The Or Ein Sof was *echad pashut* (simple, undivided oneness).

**No room for "other"**: Because Or Ein Sof filled all of existence, there was no conceptual "space" for a separate created being. Any finite entity would instantly be dissolved in the infinite radiance. The Tzimtzum was thus necessary not merely to create physical space but to create the *conceptual possibility* of otherness.

**Pure light without vessels**: Before the Tzimtzum, the distinction between light (*or*) and vessel (*kli*) did not exist. The Or Ein Sof was both light and vessel simultaneously, as it were — a complete, self-contained divine reality without the externality that the or/kli distinction requires.

---

## The Language of "Before"

The Rashab notes a crucial philosophical problem: using the word "before" (*lifnei*) the Tzimtzum implies temporal priority — as if the Tzimtzum happened at a specific moment in time. But time itself is a created reality that emerged *after* (or through) the Tzimtzum. "Before the Tzimtzum" is therefore not a temporal claim but a *conceptual* or *ontological* priority claim.

The Or Ein Sof she'lifnei HaTzimtzum is the ontologically prior state — the condition that grounds and precedes all created reality, in a non-temporal sense. Even now, after the Tzimtzum and the creation of all the worlds, the Or Ein Sof "before the Tzimtzum" remains as the transcendent ground of all existence.

---

## Pre-Tzimtzum Or Ein Sof as the Goal of Spiritual Work

The Rashab teaches a remarkable insight in the Taarav: the ultimate goal of all spiritual work — all Torah study, all prayer, all mitzvot — is not merely to ascend to the highest post-Tzimtzum levels (Adam Kadmon, Atzilut) but to reveal the *Or Ein Sof she'lifnei HaTzimtzum* — the pre-Tzimtzum infinite light — *within the post-Tzimtzum world*.

This is the ultimate meaning of *dirah b'tachtonim* (God's dwelling in the lower realms): the revelation of the pre-Tzimtzum Or Ein Sof — with its quality of complete, undifferentiated infinity — within the differentiated, finite world of creation.
