# ממלא כל עלמין — Memaleh Kol Almin: Filling All Worlds

**The Immanent Mode of Or Ein Sof in Hemshech Taarav**

---

## Overview

**Memaleh Kol Almin** (ממלא כל עלמין — "filling all worlds") is the immanent aspect of Or Ein Sof — the divine vitality that enters *into* each world and each being, animating it from within according to its capacity to receive. Unlike *Sovev Kol Almin* (which encompasses from without), Memaleh Kol Almin pervades from within — it is the divine life-force that makes each created being what it is.

The Rashab in Hemshech Taarav analyzes Memaleh Kol Almin as the theological ground for understanding why physical creation is not merely "empty" of the divine — it is actively sustained by divine vitality at every moment.

---

## The Nature of Memaleh Kol Almin

The term *memaleh* (ממלא) means "filling" or "pervading." Memaleh Kol Almin is the divine presence that *enters into* each world and being, *adapting itself* to the capacity of the vessel. Unlike Sovev, which relates to all worlds equally, Memaleh gives each world its characteristic "measure" of divine vitality:

- Adam Kadmon receives the most intense form of Memaleh
- Atzilut receives a somewhat contracted form
- Beriya receives a further-contracted form
- The physical world receives the most contracted Memaleh — but it is still divine vitality, still Or Ein Sof, still infinite at its source

**The key principle**: Even the most contracted form of Memaleh Kol Almin is, at its root, the same Or Ein Sof that fills Adam Kadmon. The contraction is a *revelation-concealment*, not a genuine diminishment of the source.

---

## Memaleh and the Chain of Being (Seder Hishtalshelut)

The Rashab connects Memaleh Kol Almin to the entire *seder hishtalshelut* (chain of being) — the successive contraction of divine vitality from the highest worlds to the lowest. The Memaleh Kol Almin is what *flows through* this chain:

At each level, the Memaleh:
1. Enters the world in the appropriate, contracted form
2. Animates every being within that world from within
3. Is the "inner life" of that world — its reason for existing moment to moment

Without Memaleh Kol Almin, every created thing would instantly cease to exist. The Rashab draws on the famous teaching: "If God's life-force were to depart from a stone for even a moment, the stone would cease to exist."

---

## Memaleh, Or Pnimi, and the Nefesh HaElohit

The Rashab connects Memaleh Kol Almin to the *Or Pnimi* (internal light) and ultimately to the *Nefesh HaElohit* (divine soul). The divine soul of the Jew is a particularized expression of the Memaleh — the Or Ein Sof that fills this specific person from within, giving them their unique divine vitality.

This connection enables a powerful spiritual practice: by meditating on the divine vitality within oneself — recognizing the Memaleh as the source of one's life and consciousness — one can experience a direct sense of divine immanence. This is the *hisponenut* (contemplative meditation) on Memaleh Kol Almin that the Rashab describes as a central Chabad practice.
