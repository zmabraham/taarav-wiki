# אור אין סוף שלאחר הצמצום — Or Ein Sof After the Tzimtzum

**How Or Ein Sof Relates to Creation in Hemshech Taarav**

---

## Overview

After the Tzimtzum, the Or Ein Sof's relationship to the created worlds takes on a more structured, mediated form. In Hemshech Taarav, the Rashab analyzes the post-Tzimtzum Or Ein Sof through its three primary modes: as *Sovev Kol Almin* (encompassing), as *Memaleh Kol Almin* (permeating), and as the source of the *Kav* (the directed line of Or Ein Sof drawn into the chalal). Understanding the post-Tzimtzum Or Ein Sof is essential for understanding the entire structure of the spiritual worlds.

---

## The Paradox of Post-Tzimtzum Or Ein Sof

After the Tzimtzum, a fundamental paradox arises: if Or Ein Sof is truly infinite, how can it be said to have "withdrawn"? Doesn't a genuine infinite remain everywhere?

The Rashab's resolution in the Taarav: the post-Tzimtzum Or Ein Sof remains fully present — but its *mode of presence* changes. Before the Tzimtzum, Or Ein Sof's presence was *manifest and overwhelming* — no finite being could arise. After the Tzimtzum, Or Ein Sof's presence in the chalal is *concealed* — it is present but not manifestly so.

This concealment is not a genuine withdrawal of Or Ein Sof's infinity — it is a change in the relationship between Or Ein Sof's infinity and the created worlds. The infinite remains infinite; what changes is the degree to which that infinity is revealed.

---

## Three Modes of Post-Tzimtzum Or Ein Sof

**1. Sovev Kol Almin (Encompassing)**
The Or Ein Sof surrounds all the worlds from "outside" — transcending all distinctions between worlds, relating to all equally, not entering into the limitations of any specific world. See: [[themes/ohr-ein-sof/sovev-kol-almin|Sovev Kol Almin]].

**2. Memaleh Kol Almin (Permeating)**
The Or Ein Sof animates each world from within, adapting to the specific capacity and limitation of each level. The physical world receives a deeply contracted form of Or Ein Sof as its life-force; Atzilut receives a less contracted form. See: [[themes/ohr-ein-sof/memale-kol-almin|Memaleh Kol Almin]].

**3. The Kav (The Line)**
A specific, directed ray of Or Ein Sof extended into the chalal — the generative force that builds the structures of Adam Kadmon and all subsequent worlds. The Kav is Or Ein Sof in its most directed, most contracted form within the chalal. See: [[themes/kav-reshimu/the-kav|The Kav]].

---

## The Continuity of Or Ein Sof Through All Modes

The Rashab's key teaching: despite the Tzimtzum and the emergence of the post-Tzimtzum modes, **the Or Ein Sof remains fundamentally the same**. There is no genuine change in Or Ein Sof itself — only a change in how it relates to and is perceived by the created worlds.

This is the Rashab's application of the Chabad principle of *Achdus HaShem* (divine unity): the God of creation is absolutely identical with the God who exists beyond creation. The Tzimtzum changes nothing at the level of Atzmut; it only creates the context within which the worlds can perceive and relate to the divine.
