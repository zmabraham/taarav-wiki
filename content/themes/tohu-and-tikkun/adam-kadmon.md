# אדם קדמון — Adam Kadmon: Primordial Man

**The Highest Spiritual World in Hemshech Taarav**

---

## Overview

**Adam Kadmon** (אדם קדמון — Primordial Man) is the highest world within the *chalal* (the space created by the Tzimtzum), the first and most exalted structure that emerged from the interaction of the *Kav* (the line of Or Ein Sof) with the *Reshimu* (the impression remaining after the Tzimtzum). In Hemshech Taarav, the Rashab analyzes Adam Kadmon as the transcendent level that is *above* the distinction between Tohu and Tikkun — it precedes both worlds and is the source from which both emerge.

---

## Adam Kadmon's Position in the Cosmic Structure

The order of the spiritual worlds from highest to lowest:

1. **Or Ein Sof** (surrounding the entire chalal)
2. **Adam Kadmon** (the uppermost world within the chalal — entirely within the chalal but at its upper edge)
3. **Akudim, Nekudim, Berudim** (three stages of development within Adam Kadmon)
4. **Atzilut** (the world of Tohu, then the world of Tikkun)
5. **Beriya, Yetzira, Asiya** (the three lower worlds)

Adam Kadmon thus stands between the Or Ein Sof and all subsequent worlds. It is the *first expression* of the Kav's energy — the first differentiated spiritual reality.

---

## The Name "Adam Kadmon"

The term *Adam* (אדם) refers to the human form — the structure of *tzelem Elohim* (divine image) in which all spiritual worlds are organized. The *Sefirot* of Adam Kadmon are understood as the "limbs" of a cosmic human form — a structure that is *anthropomorphic* not in a physical sense but in the sense that the divine structure of reality corresponds to and is the archetype of the human form.

*Kadmon* (קדמון) means "primordial" or "first" — Adam Kadmon is the primordial human form, the divine archetype that underlies all subsequent structures, including the human being itself. Every human being is, in a sense, a *tzelem* (image) of Adam Kadmon.

---

## Adam Kadmon and the Tohu/Tikkun Distinction

The Rashab emphasizes in the Taarav that Adam Kadmon is *above* the distinction between Tohu and Tikkun:

- The world of Tohu (*Nekudim*) emerged from the *lower* aspects of Adam Kadmon
- The world of Tikkun emerged as the correction/response to Tohu's breaking
- Adam Kadmon itself was never subject to the breaking — it transcends both worlds

This makes Adam Kadmon the level at which Tohu and Tikkun are reconciled and unified — the level at which the apparent "failure" of Tohu and the "correction" of Tikkun are seen as part of a single divine plan.

---

## Adam Kadmon and the Jewish Soul

The Rashab draws a profound connection between Adam Kadmon and the root of Jewish souls. In the Taarav's analysis, the *shoresh haneshama* (root of the soul) of Israel is located in the level of Adam Kadmon — specifically in the *Ten Sefirot of Adam Kadmon* at their most transcendent level.

This gives Jewish souls a connection to a level that is above Tohu and Tikkun, above the breaking and the repair. The Yechida (the highest soul-level) specifically corresponds to the Adam Kadmon level — it is the aspect of the soul that shares the transcendence of Adam Kadmon.

See: [[themes/souls-of-israel/souls-in-adam-kadmon|Souls in Adam Kadmon]] and [[themes/yechida|Yechida]].

---

## The Ten Sefirot of Adam Kadmon

Even at the level of Adam Kadmon, the ten Sefirot exist — but in a form so transcendent that they are barely distinguishable from the Or Ein Sof. The Rashab analyzes the *orot* that emerge from Adam Kadmon through the "sense organs" of Adam Kadmon's cosmic form:

- From the **ears, nose, and mouth** of Adam Kadmon: the lights of Tohu (*Nekudim*) emerged
- From the **eyes** of Adam Kadmon: the lights of Tikkun (*Berudim*) emerged

This image — of Tohu and Tikkun as lights emerging from different "organs" of Adam Kadmon — expresses the idea that both worlds are purposeful expressions of Adam Kadmon's transcendent plan.
