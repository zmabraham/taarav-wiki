# The Result of the Shattering

## English Explanation

The Shevirat HaKelim produced several distinct results, each of which has ongoing
spiritual significance in the Hemshech Taarav's worldview. Understanding these results
is essential for grasping the entire system the Rashab builds.

First, the broken vessel-fragments (שְׁבָרִים, shvarim) fell downward into the lower
worlds, where they became the structural basis for the *klipot* (קְלִיפּוֹת, husks) —
the forces of spiritual concealment. The klipot are not independently created evil;
they are the remnants of broken vessels, structures that once had a holy function
but, having shattered, now exist in a degraded form that conceals the divine rather
than revealing it.

Second, trapped within these broken fragments are *nitzotzot* (נִיצוֹצוֹת, divine
sparks) — portions of the divine light that were within the vessels when they broke.
These sparks constitute the divine content of the material world: the hidden
holiness within every physical object, creature, and situation.

Third, the shattering created the conditions necessary for the World of Tikkun to
arise. The energy released by the shattering was reorganized into the Partzufim
(פַּרְצוּפִים, divine faces/personalities) — integrated, cooperative divine structures
that could sustain stable relationships between lights and vessels.

Finally, the shattering created the human mission: to recover and elevate the fallen
sparks through Torah and mitzvot, gradually restoring the wholeness that was broken.

## Hebrew Key Terms

| Term | Transliteration | Definition |
|------|-----------------|------------|
| שְׁבָרִים | Shvarim | Broken fragments; became the basis of klipot |
| קְלִיפּוֹת | Klipot | Husks; forces of concealment |
| נִיצוֹצוֹת | Nitzotzot | Sparks; trapped in the broken fragments |
| פַּרְצוּפִים | Partzufim | Divine faces; emerged from the reorganized energy |
| תִּקּוּן | Tikkun | Rectification; the human mission to restore the sparks |
