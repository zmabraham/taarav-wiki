# Why the Vessels Shattered

## English Explanation

The Shevirat HaKelim (שְׁבִירַת הַכֵּלִים, the Breaking of the Vessels) is the
catastrophic event in the World of Tohu when the vessels, unable to contain the
intensity of the divine lights, shattered. In the Hemshech Taarav, the Rashab
provides a multilayered explanation of *why* this occurred.

The primary reason, as discussed elsewhere, is the mismatch between the intensity of
the lights and the capacity of the vessels. But the Rashab goes deeper: the vessels
shattered because they refused to cooperate with each other. Each Sefirah-vessel
in Tohu was entirely self-referential — it received divine light as pure Chessed, or
pure Gevurah, or pure Tiferet, with no acknowledgment of the others. This radical
independence was structurally incompatible with sustained existence.

An additional reason the Rashab offers: the shattering was, on the deepest level,
*intended*. The Ein Sof "knew" that the Tohu vessels would shatter, and the shattering
was the divine mechanism for distributing divine sparks (נִיצוֹצוֹת, nitzotzot) into
the lower worlds. Without the shattering, the divine presence could not descend into
the detailed particularity of material existence. The shattering was the means of
seeding the lower worlds with divine energy.

This means the breaking of the vessels was not a tragedy but a necessary stage in
the divine plan — painful from within the perspective of the broken vessels, but
purposeful from the perspective of the overall creative process.

## Hebrew Key Terms

| Term | Transliteration | Definition |
|------|-----------------|------------|
| שְׁבִירָה | Shevirah | Breaking/shattering |
| כֵּלִים | Keilim | Vessels; the structures that broke |
| עַצְמָאוּת | Atzmaiyut | Self-referentiality; what caused the shattering |
| נִיצוֹצוֹת | Nitzotzot | Sparks; scattered by the shattering |
| כַּוָּנָה | Kavana | Intention; the divine purpose behind the shattering |
