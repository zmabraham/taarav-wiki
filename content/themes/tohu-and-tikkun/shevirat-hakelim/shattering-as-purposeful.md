# Shattering as Purposeful: Divine Design in the Breaking

## English Explanation

In the Hemshech Taarav, the Rashab takes a position that might seem shocking at first:
the Shevirat HaKelim was not a cosmic accident or a divine failure. It was purposeful,
even necessary. This position — already developed in earlier Chabad texts — is
elaborated in depth throughout the hemshech.

The purpose of the shattering, on one level, was the descent of divine sparks into the
lower worlds, enabling the material world to have genuine divine content. Without the
shattering, the divine light would have remained "above" in the spiritual worlds, and
the material world would have been a completely Godless void. The shattering was the
mechanism for *investing* the material with the spiritual.

On a deeper level, the shattering was necessary for the emergence of genuine human
freedom (בְּחִירָה, bechirah) and human spiritual effort (עֲבוֹדָה, avodah). If divine
light were openly present everywhere, human beings would have no meaningful choice
and no genuine spiritual work to do. The concealment produced by the shattered vessels
creates the very conditions under which human free choice and divine service become
possible and meaningful.

The Rashab also notes that the shattering enabled the divine light to enter a more
complex and nuanced relationship with creation than was possible in Tohu. The
reorganized sparks, embedded within the structures of the material world, can be
elevated through human action in ways that pure Tohu-light never could be — because
they are now *within* the world rather than above it.

## Hebrew Key Terms

| Term | Transliteration | Definition |
|------|-----------------|------------|
| תַּכְלִית | Tachlit | Purpose; what the shattering ultimately serves |
| בְּחִירָה | Bechirah | Free choice; enabled by the shattering's concealment |
| עֲבוֹדָה | Avodah | Divine service; the human response to the shattering |
| הֶעְלֵם | He'elem | Concealment; produced by the shattered vessels |
| עֲלִיַּת הַנִּיצוֹצוֹת | Aliyat HaNitzotzot | Elevation of the sparks; the human mission |
