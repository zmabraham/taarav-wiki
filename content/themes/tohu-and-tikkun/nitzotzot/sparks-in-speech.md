# Divine Sparks in Speech

## English Explanation

Speech (*dibbur*, דִּבּוּר) is one of the most powerful vehicles for the elevation of
divine sparks, according to the Hemshech Taarav. The Hebrew letters (אוֹתִיּוֹת, otiyot)
themselves are understood as spiritual vessels that contain divine sparks — compressed
divine energy in the form of linguistic structure. When a person speaks words of Torah,
prayer, or holiness, they release and elevate the sparks contained in those letters.

The Rashab builds on the Kabbalistic tradition (developed extensively in the Tanya and
earlier sources) that the letters of the Hebrew language are not conventional symbols
but rather the actual building blocks of creation — the divine words through which God
spoke the world into existence. "And God said, 'Let there be light'" — this speech
was not merely communication; it was the creative act itself. The divine sparks within
creation are, in a sense, the residual energy of these creative divine words.

This explains the Rashab's emphasis on the power of speech in Torah study: when a
person articulates Torah thoughts aloud, they are not merely communicating information
but actually working with the divine creative medium of language, releasing sparks
that the words contain.

Conversely, harmful speech (לְשׁוֹן הָרָע, leshon hara) represents a severe spiritual
problem because it takes the divine sparks embedded in the holy letters and channels
them into destructive purposes, further entrapping the sparks rather than elevating
them.

## Hebrew Key Terms

| Term | Transliteration | Definition |
|------|-----------------|------------|
| דִּבּוּר | Dibbur | Speech; the primary vehicle for spark elevation |
| אוֹתִיּוֹת | Otiyot | Letters; vessels of divine sparks |
| לִמּוּד הַתּוֹרָה | Limmud HaTorah | Torah study; the ultimate speech-based tikkun |
| לְשׁוֹן הָרָע | Leshon HaRa | Harmful speech; traps sparks further |
| יְצִירָה | Yetzirah | World of Formation; the world of speech/letters |
