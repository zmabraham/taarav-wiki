# Divine Sparks in Food

## English Explanation

One of the most tangible applications of the nitzotzot (divine sparks) doctrine in
the Hemshech Taarav is the teaching about sparks embedded in food. When a person
eats food with proper intention — for the sake of health to serve God, and/or as
part of a holy occasion like Shabbat or Yom Tov — they are performing a *tikkun*
(rectification) of the divine sparks trapped within that food.

The mechanism is as follows: the physical food, like all material objects, contains
divine sparks from the Shevirat HaKelim. These sparks are what give the food its
nutritive and life-sustaining properties — they are the divine energy compressed into
physical form. When a person eats and uses the energy for holy purposes, the spark
is "released" from its material vessel and ascends, rejoining the supernal divine
light. When the energy is used for base purposes, the spark remains trapped.

The Rashab emphasizes that this is not merely symbolic. The physical act of eating
genuinely affects metaphysical reality: sparks are literally elevated or further
degraded depending on how the energy from the food is used. This is why the laws
of kashrut (כַּשְׁרוּת, kosher) are so significant — they govern which foods are
accessible to tikkun and which are not.

Food that is non-kosher contains sparks that cannot be elevated through eating —
they belong to the domain of the three "impure" klipot that have no connection to
holiness. Only foods within the category of kelipat nogah (נֹגַהּ, the partially
luminous husk) contain sparks that can be elevated.
