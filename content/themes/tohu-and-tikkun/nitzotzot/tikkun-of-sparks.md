# The Tikkun of Sparks: Human Mission in Creation

## English Explanation

The elevation of divine sparks (*birur nitzotzot*, בֵּירוּר נִיצוֹצוֹת) is, in the
Hemshech Taarav, nothing less than the central mission of human existence and the
engine of cosmic redemption. The Rashab teaches that every positive human action
— eating, speaking, thinking, performing mitzvot, studying Torah — has the potential
to elevate one or more of the sparks that fell during the Shevirat HaKelim.

The process of tikkun is cumulative. Across generations of human spiritual effort,
sparks have been gradually elevated until, in the Messianic era, all the sparks will
have been recovered and restored to their divine source. This is what the Rashab
means when he describes the Messianic era as the completion of the work begun by
the Shevirat HaKelim: the entire divine content that was dispersed through the
shattering will have been gathered and unified.

The Rashab notes that this mission has a paradoxical quality: the very act of
performing a mitzvah with a physical object (eating, wearing tefillin, giving
tzedakah) is what elevates the spark within that object. The divine spark is elevated
*through* its physical engagement, not despite it. This is why the Rashab insists that
physical mitzvot have supreme cosmic importance — they are the mechanism by which
the lowest levels of material existence are redeemed.

The Hemshech Taarav also notes that toward the end of history, the remaining
un-elevated sparks become increasingly embedded in difficult and complex situations,
which is why each generation's spiritual challenges become more subtle and nuanced
than the last.

| Term | Transliteration | Definition |
|------|-----------------|------------|
| בֵּירוּר נִיצוֹצוֹת | Beirur Nitzotzot | Elevation/refinement of sparks |
| מִצְווֹת | Mitzvot | Divine commandments; the mechanism of tikkun |
| גְּאוּלָּה | Geulah | Redemption; the completion of birur nitzotzot |
| מְשִׁיחַ | Mashiach | The Messiah; reveals the completion of tikkun |
