# אורות התיקון — Lights of Tikkun

**The Calibrated, Proportional Divine Lights of Olam HaTikkun**

---

## Overview

The *orot* (lights) of the World of Tikkun are fundamentally different in character from those of the World of Tohu — not because Tikkun's lights come from a lesser source, but because they are *mediated, graduated, and distributed* through the Partzuf structure in a way that makes them sustainable and functional. In Hemshech Taarav, the Rashab analyzes the distinctive quality of Tikkun's lights as a model for understanding how divine energy can be beneficially integrated into created reality.

---

## The Key Difference: Mediation Through Partzufim

The orot of Tikkun do not enter the sefirot directly — they are mediated through the *Partzufim* (divine configurations). This mediation involves:

**Inter-partzuf giving and receiving**: Abba (Father) gives to Imma (Mother) before the light reaches Ze'ir Anpin; Ze'ir Anpin gives to Nukva before it reaches the lower worlds. Each transfer moderates and calibrates the light.

**Intra-partzuf integration**: Within each Partzuf, the light of the highest sefirot is moderated by the lower ones before reaching expression. Chochma's intense flash is received into Bina's expansive processing; only after this integration does light emerge as developed understanding.

**Or Chozer feedback**: Unlike in Tohu, the integrated Partzufim of Tikkun generate substantial Or Chozer — the upward response that calibrates and moderates the downward Or Yashar flow.

---

## Quantity vs. Quality

The Rashab makes a striking comparison: **the orot of Tohu were greater in quantity and intensity; the orot of Tikkun are greater in quality and functionality**.

Tohu's lights were more powerful — but power without containment accomplishes nothing except destruction. Tikkun's lights are calibrated to what the vessels can actually absorb and express. The functional effectiveness of a lesser light that genuinely illuminates is greater than the "greater" light that only burns.

This distinction has implications for spiritual life: the Rashab cautions against spiritual excesses that overwhelm the person's vessels, however intense the experience may be. Sustainable, calibrated spiritual growth — the approach of Tikkun — is ultimately more productive than intense flashes that cannot be integrated.

---

## The Lights of Tikkun and the Four Worlds

The orot of Tikkun are distributed across the four worlds:
- **Atzilut**: The most intense Tikkun-orot — but still proportional to Atzilut's vast vessels (the Partzufim)
- **Beriya**: A contracted form of the Atzilut-orot, calibrated for Beriya's structure
- **Yetzira**: Further contracted
- **Asiya**: The most contracted Tikkun-light — sustaining the physical world as divine life-force
