# Edom and Esav: Spiritual Dimensions

## English Explanation

The connection between Edom (the kingdom of the seven kings) and Esav (Jacob's twin
brother, who is identified in the Torah with Edom) is deeply developed in the
Hemshech Taarav's Kabbalistic framework. The Rashab follows the traditional Kabbalistic
reading in which Esav-Edom represents a spiritual archetype of *unchecked force*
(גְּבוּרוֹת, gevurot) without the tempering of kindness — precisely the configuration
of the World of Tohu.

Esav in the Torah is characterized by physicality, impulsiveness, and a kind of raw
vitality that is powerful but ultimately destructive. These traits, in the Rashab's
reading, are not accidental biographical details but spiritually significant markers
of the Tohu-energy that Esav embodies. He lives in the moment, acting without
consideration of consequences or long-term relationships — again, the Tohu-vessel
dynamic of self-referential intensity.

The transition from Edom to Israel (from Esav to Jacob) is the transition from Tohu
to Tikkun. Jacob represents the integrated, cooperative, long-suffering approach to
life that is the antidote to Edom's fragmented intensity. The Rashab sees the
historical struggle between Esav-Edom and Israel as the ongoing cosmic drama of Tohu
and Tikkun being played out in human history.

Ultimately, the Rashab notes, even Edom's sparks will be elevated in the Messianic
era — the forces of Tohu, properly absorbed into the framework of Tikkun, will
contribute their extraordinary intensity in a purified form.
