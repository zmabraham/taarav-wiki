# Kings as Archetypes: The Sefirot in Tohu Configuration

## English Explanation

The Seven Kings of Edom function in the Hemshech Taarav not merely as historical
figures but as *archetypes* — prototypical forms of divine quality that existed before
the rectified configuration of the Sefirot emerged. The Rashab uses their stories
to illuminate the essential nature of each Sefirah when it exists in an unmodulated,
non-cooperative, Tohu-like state.

Each king's name, place of origin, and associated wives carry symbolic freight in the
Rashab's reading. These details encode information about the inner nature of each
Sefirah in its Tohu state: how it related (or rather failed to relate) to the Sefirot
adjacent to it, and why it shattered rather than transmitting its qualities downward.

The transition from the Kings of Edom to the Partzufim of Tikkun is the transition
from *archetypes* to *personalities*. Archetypes are abstract, pure expressions of a
single quality — like a color in its pure, unmixed form. Personalities (Partzufim)
are complex, multi-faceted configurations in which multiple qualities are integrated
into a coherent whole. The failure of the Kings was their inability to move from
archetype to personality.

In human terms, the Rashab uses this to discuss the difference between a person who
has one outstanding virtue in isolation versus a person of integrated character. The
archetype-person (Tohu-mode) burns brightly in one quality but inevitably "dies" —
their single quality eventually consumes itself without the balancing influence of
other qualities.
