# The Intensity of Lights in the World of Tohu

## English Explanation

The World of Tohu (עוֹלַם הַתֹּהוּ, the World of Chaos) is characterized in the
Hemshech Taarav by an overwhelming excess of divine light relative to the capacity of
its vessels. The Rashab describes the lights of Tohu as possessing extreme intensity
(עָצְמָה, otzmah) — they were vast, undiluted emanations of Or Ein Sof that far
exceeded what any vessel could contain or transmit.

This excessive intensity was not a flaw in the light itself but in the configuration
of the World of Tohu as a whole. The Sefirot in Tohu existed in a state of radical
independence from one another — each Sefirah was an absolute point of pure divine
quality with no modulation, no blending, no cooperation with the others. Chessed
(loving-kindness) was absolute, undiluted loving-kindness. Gevurah (strength) was
absolute, undiluted strength. Without the moderating influence of other qualities,
each light was overwhelming in its uncompromising intensity.

The Rashab draws an analogy to a king who is so completely identified with his kingly
majesty that he cannot relate to ordinary subjects at all. His royal essence is
profound, but because it is unmediated and unmodulated, it destroys any relationship
with lesser beings. Similarly, the lights of Tohu were divine in the highest sense but
incapable of sustaining a productive relationship with their vessels.

This excess of light relative to vessel is the root of the Shevirat HaKelim — the
breaking of the vessels — that defines the transition from Tohu to Tikkun.
