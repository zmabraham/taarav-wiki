# ארבעה יסודות — The Four Elements of the Animal Soul

**Fire, Water, Air, and Earth in Hemshech Taarav's Psychology**

---

## Overview

The animal soul in Chabad thought is not a monolithic force — it is composed of four *yesodot* (יסודות — elements): **fire** (אש, *esh*), **water** (מים, *mayim*), **air** (רוח, *ruach*), and **earth** (עפר, *afar*). These four elements, drawn from classical philosophy and integrated into Kabbalah, are developed by the Rashab in Hemshech Taarav as a detailed psychological framework for understanding human nature, passion, and spiritual work.

---

## The Four Elements and the Soul's Qualities

Each of the four elements produces a distinct cluster of psychological qualities in the animal soul:

**Fire (אש — Esh)**:
Fire's quality is upward movement and intensity. In the animal soul, fire produces *ga'ava* (pride, arrogance) — the sense that one rises above others; also *ka'as* (anger) — the explosive, destructive heat of rage. Fire is also the source of passionate desire — the burning quality of intense wanting.

**Water (מים — Mayim)**:
Water's quality is downward flow and pleasure-seeking. Water flows toward the lowest point; in the animal soul, water produces desire for *ta'anug* (physical pleasure) — food, drink, physical comfort — and for wealth (*kishuf*). Water is also the source of *ahavas mamon* (love of money) — the drive to accumulate.

**Air (רוח — Ruach)**:
Air's quality is expansion and distribution. In the animal soul, air produces *shtut* (foolishness, levity) — the dispersal of mental energy in frivolous pursuits; also *boastfulness* and the love of empty talk (*lashon hara*).

**Earth (עפר — Afar)**:
Earth's quality is heaviness and inertia. In the animal soul, earth produces *atzlut* (laziness), *atzvut* (depression), and *kelimut* (shame/inhibition). Earth is also associated with *sikhlit* — an excessive focus on calculating rational self-interest without spiritual aspiration.

---

## The Rashab's Distinctive Analysis

Earlier Chabad sources (particularly the Tanya) describe the four elements of the animal soul in terms of their negative expressions. The Rashab in the Taarav adds a deeper layer: each element has a *koach* (power) that, when properly channeled, becomes a spiritual resource:

- **Fire** channeled through Torah and prayer becomes burning enthusiasm (*hitlahavut*) for holiness
- **Water** channeled through mitzvot becomes the deep satisfaction (*menucha*) of fulfillment and the love of giving
- **Air** channeled through speech becomes the eloquence of Torah teaching and the expansiveness of sharing wisdom
- **Earth** channeled through *avodah* becomes humility (*anavah*) and groundedness — the stability of a person who has genuinely integrated their spiritual work

This analysis grounds the Rashab's teaching that the animal soul is not to be destroyed but *transformed* — its four elemental energies converted from self-serving to God-serving.

---

## The Four Elements and Kelipat Nogah

The four elements of the animal soul operate specifically through *Kelipat Nogah* — the semi-transparent husk that can be either elevated or degraded. The nitzotzot (divine sparks) embedded within the four elements are what give them their power; the elements themselves are the "packaging" of these sparks in a form that can interface with the physical world.
