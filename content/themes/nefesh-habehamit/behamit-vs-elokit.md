# נפש הבהמית ונפש האלוקית — The Two Souls

**The Animal Soul and the Divine Soul in Hemshech Taarav**

---

## Overview

The framework of **two souls** (שתי נפשות — shtei nefashot) is foundational to Chabad psychology. In Hemshech Taarav, the Rashab develops this framework with greater metaphysical depth than the Tanya's treatment, revealing the cosmic roots of both souls and explaining their relationship in terms of the Or Yashar / Or Chozer dynamic, Tohu and Tikkun, and the structure of the Sefirot.

---

## The Divine Soul (Nefesh HaElohit)

The *Nefesh HaElohit* (נפש האלוקית — divine soul) is rooted in *Atzilut* — the highest of the four worlds, the world of divine emanation. Specifically, in the Taarav's analysis, it is rooted in the *ten Sefirot of Atzilut* (and at its highest, in Adam Kadmon). This grounding in Atzilut means the divine soul is literally an extension of the divine — not merely "like" God but actually partaking of Atzilut's divine quality.

The divine soul's essential nature: *bitul* (self-nullification before the Infinite) — it inherently tends toward union with its divine source.

The divine soul's goal: to reveal the *Or Ein Sof* within the material world through Torah and mitzvot, generating the Or Chozer that is the purpose of creation.

---

## The Animal Soul (Nefesh HaBehamit)

The *Nefesh HaBehamit* (נפש הבהמית — animal soul) is rooted in *Kelipat Nogah* — the translucent husk that mediates between the fully impure kelipot and the realm of holiness. Its essence is *yesh* (selfhood, independent existence) — it asserts itself as a genuine, independent entity, not as a transparent vessel for the divine.

The animal soul's essential nature: *ga'ava* (pride/self-assertion) — it inherently tends toward its own satisfaction and self-perpetuation.

The animal soul's potential: to become the vehicle through which the divine soul's work is carried out in the physical world. The animal soul's passion and intensity, when redirected, become the most powerful fuel for spiritual work.

---

## The Relationship: Not Enemies but Partners

A crucial teaching in the Taarav: the two souls are not simply opposed as good vs. evil. The Rashab develops the idea that the *purpose* of the divine soul's descent into a body with an animal soul is precisely to transform the animal soul — and this transformation is the most valued spiritual achievement.

If the divine soul could fulfill its mission without the animal soul, there would have been no need for the soul to descend. The descent of the divine soul into a physical body with an animal soul is the *greatest possible* context for generating Or Chozer — because the distance traveled (from animal-soul-level existence back to divine-level awareness) is the greatest possible, and therefore the Or Chozer generated is the most powerful possible.

---

## The Two Souls in the Body: Where Each Resides

The Rashab analyzes the location of each soul in the body:

**Divine soul** — its primary location is the *brain* (the seat of intellect and chabad-faculties: chochma, bina, da'at). Its secondary expression is in the right chamber of the heart (*tzad yamin shebalev*) — the seat of divine love.

**Animal soul** — its primary location is the *left chamber of the heart* (*tzad smol shebalev*) — the seat of animal desire and passion. From there it spreads through the blood to all organs.

The heart is thus the primary *arena* of the two-soul dynamic — the battleground where the divine soul's love for God and the animal soul's love for self meet and contend. Prayer (*tefillah* — which means "to connect"), centered in the heart, is the primary practice for shifting the heart's dominant force from animal to divine.
