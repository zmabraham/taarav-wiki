# שלושת לבושי הנפש — Three Garments of the Soul

**Thought, Speech, and Action in Hemshech Taarav**

---

## Overview

Both the *Nefesh HaBehamit* (animal soul) and the *Nefesh HaElohit* (divine soul) express themselves through three *levushim* (garments, לבושים): **thought** (מחשבה, *machshava*), **speech** (דיבור, *dibur*), and **action** (מעשה, *ma'aseh*). These are called "garments" rather than "parts" of the soul because they clothe and express the soul without being identical with its inner essence. In Hemshech Taarav, the Rashab analyzes the garments with special attention to how they relate to the cosmic structure of Or Yashar and Or Chozer.

---

## The Rashab's Framework

In Hemshech Taarav, the Rashab develops a systematic mapping between the soul's three garments and the structural categories of the spiritual worlds:

**Thought (Machshava)** corresponds to the level of *Beriya* (the world of creation) — the most refined and "interior" of the three garments. Thought is invisible, immediate, and most closely connected to the soul's inner reality. The animal soul's thoughts of desire, calculation, and self-interest all operate at this level.

**Speech (Dibur)** corresponds to the level of *Yetzira* (the world of formation) — more externalized than thought but still not fully materialized. Speech is the bridge between the inner world of thought and the outer world of action.

**Action (Ma'aseh)** corresponds to the level of *Asiya* (the world of action) — the most external and materialized expression of the soul. Physical actions in the world are the "lowest" expression of the soul's vitality.

---

## The Two Souls and Their Garments

A crucial insight: both the animal soul and the divine soul use the same three garments — thought, speech, and action. The "battle" between the two souls is not about who owns the garments but about *which soul's content* fills them:

- **Animal soul's garments**: Thoughts of physical desire, speech of mundane or impure content, actions driven by ego and appetite
- **Divine soul's garments**: Thoughts of Torah and divine wisdom, speech of prayer and sacred learning, actions of mitzvot

The Rashab teaches that the three garments of the divine soul, when fully occupied with Torah content, form a *barrier* (mehitzah) that prevents the animal soul from accessing them. Conversely, when the garments are given over to the animal soul's content, the divine soul is effectively "clothed" in foreign garments.

---

## The Special Status of the Torah Garments

The Rashab gives particular attention to the garments of the *divine* soul in relation to Torah:

When a person studies Torah (*machshava*), speaks Torah (*dibur*), and acts according to Torah's mitzvot (*ma'aseh*), their three garments become one with the *three garments of the Ein Sof* — because Torah is the divine wisdom itself. This is what the Talmudic statement means: "The Torah and the Holy One Blessed Be He are one" — when the human garments become Torah-garments, they literally unite with the divine.

This analysis gives a metaphysical grounding for the commandment to study Torah and to perform the mitzvot with the body: the physical action of a mitzvah is not merely symbolic but literally connects the physical world (through the ma'aseh garment) to the Or Ein Sof.
