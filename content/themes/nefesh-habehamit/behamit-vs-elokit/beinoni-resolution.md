# The Beinoni Resolution: Living with Both Souls

## English Explanation

The *beinoni* (בֵּינוֹנִי, the intermediate person) is the central spiritual type in
the Tanya and remains central in the Hemshech Taarav's practical teachings. The
beinoni has not transformed the animal soul into holiness (as the complete tzaddik
has) but maintains consistent divine-soul governance over thought, speech, and action
through ongoing effort and discipline.

The Rashab's remarkable teaching is that the beinoni level is *available to everyone*
and is in fact the realistic spiritual ideal for most people. The complete tzaddik is
an exceptional achievement that cannot be simply willed into being — it requires
divine grace and extraordinary spiritual work over a lifetime. But the beinoni, who
exercises control over their thought, speech, and action *even while the animal soul
continues to function internally*, is a level anyone can choose to inhabit at any
given moment.

The resolution the beinoni achieves is not a synthesis that eliminates conflict but
a *functional harmony* — both souls continue to exist and operate, but the divine
soul's faculty of choice (בְּחִירָה, bechirah) consistently chooses divine-soul
expression in the external world. The animal soul's desires and emotions remain
present as inner experience but do not control behavior.

This model is practically significant: it means that a person does not need to
"feel" holy to act holy, and does not need to have eliminated all internal conflict
to be considered spiritually successful. The beinoni's heart may still feel the
pull of the animal soul — but their hands, mouth, and mind remain in the service
of the divine soul.

## Hebrew Key Terms

| Term | Transliteration | Definition |
|------|-----------------|------------|
| בֵּינוֹנִי | Beinoni | Intermediate person; the realistic spiritual ideal |
| בְּחִירָה | Bechirah | Free choice; the beinoni's primary tool |
| שְׁלִיטָה | Shelita | Governance; the beinoni's achievement |
| הַרְגָּשָׁה | Hargasha | Feeling; the beinoni controls action despite feeling |
| צַדִּיק | Tzaddik | Righteous; has transformed the animal soul entirely |
