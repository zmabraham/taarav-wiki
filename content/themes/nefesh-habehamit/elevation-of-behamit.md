# העלאת נפש הבהמית — Elevation of the Animal Soul

**The Process of Transforming the Animal Soul in Hemshech Taarav**

---

## Overview

The elevation of the *Nefesh HaBehamit* — transforming the animal soul's energies from self-serving to God-serving — is the practical mission at the center of all of Hemshech Taarav's psychology. The Rashab teaches that the divine soul does not merely *suppress* or *override* the animal soul; rather, through avodah (divine service), the animal soul's own energy becomes the fuel for spiritual ascent. This is *hafichat hayetzer* (הפיכת היצר — transformation of the evil inclination, literally "turning" it).

---

## Three Stages of Engagement with the Animal Soul

The Rashab identifies three stages in the relationship between the divine soul and the animal soul:

**Stage 1: Beinoni (בינוני — the intermediate person)**
The Beinoni does not allow the animal soul's content to express itself through thought, speech, or action — the divine soul's "garments" are not given over to the animal soul. However, the animal soul itself remains unchanged in its inner nature; it continues to desire what it desires. The Beinoni maintains control of the garments but has not transformed the soul.

**Stage 2: Tzaddik Gamur (צדיק גמור — the complete righteous person)**
In the complete tzaddik, the animal soul has been so thoroughly elevated that it no longer desires what it previously desired. Its very desires have been transformed — it now genuinely desires holiness. The four elements have been converted: fire becomes enthusiasm for holiness, water becomes the pleasure of giving, etc. This is the goal of the complete *hafichat hayetzer*.

**Stage 3: The Ultimate Elevation — Itkafya and Ithafcha**
The Rashab describes two forms of engagement:
- *Itkafya* (אתכפיא — suppression): Controlling the animal soul through discipline without transforming its inner desires. Necessary and valuable, but not the highest level.
- *Ithafcha* (אתהפכא — transformation): Actually converting the animal soul's desires into holy desires. This is the ultimate and most valued form of elevation.

The Rashab emphasizes in the Taarav that *ithafcha* — genuine transformation — is the higher achievement, though it requires greater investment of avodah.

---

## The Mechanism of Transformation

How does transformation actually occur? The Rashab's answer in the Taarav:

**1. Through sustained hisponenut (contemplative meditation)**
Deep, sustained meditation on the divine greatness — particularly on the Sovev Kol Almin and the fact that all creation is sustained by Or Ein Sof — changes the animal soul's perception of reality. When the animal soul genuinely perceives that physical pleasures are sustained by and exist within the divine (rather than being independent of it), its desire for those pleasures becomes a desire for the divine within them.

**2. Through the practice of Torah and mitzvot with inner intention**
Each mitzvah performed with genuine *kavanah* (intention) creates a spiritual "impression" on the animal soul — gradually reshaping it. The cumulative effect of a lifetime of Torah, prayer, and mitzvot with inner intention is the transformation of the animal soul.

**3. Through using the animal soul's own energy for holiness**
The Rashab teaches that the most powerful Or Chozer is generated when the *animal soul's own energy* — its passions, drives, and intensity — is channeled toward the divine. This is why *da'at* (internalized knowledge, דעת) is crucial: when the divine concepts genuinely enter the animal soul's emotional center, the animal soul's emotional energy becomes the fuel for the ascent.

---

## The Elevated Animal Soul and the World to Come

The Rashab teaches that the elevated animal soul — one that has been fully transformed through a lifetime of avodah — becomes a genuine *kli* (vessel) for a higher level of Or Ein Sof in the World to Come. The body that was the home of the animal soul, and which participated in the elevation process through physical mitzvot, will be resurrected and elevated to a level higher than even the divine soul's pre-elevation state.
