# Thought of the Animal Soul

## English Explanation

The *nefesh habehamit* (animal soul) expresses itself through three *levushim*
(לְבוּשִׁים, garments): thought, speech, and action. In the Hemshech Taarav, the
Rashab examines each garment in detail, beginning with *machshava* (מַחֲשָׁבָה,
thought) — the innermost garment and the one most directly connected to the soul's
essential faculties.

The animal soul's thought is characterized by *self-interest* and *corporeality*: its
natural tendency is to think about physical needs, social status, pleasures, fears,
and resentments — anything that serves the interests of the physical self. These
thoughts arise spontaneously, without the person's conscious choice, because they
reflect the animal soul's natural orientation toward survival and gratification.

The Rashab notes that the thought-garment of the animal soul is the primary arena
of the spiritual battle (מִלְחֶמֶת הַיֵּצֶר, the war against the evil inclination).
The yetzer hara (evil inclination) primarily operates through thought: it suggests
fantasies, distractions, resentments, and temptations in the realm of consciousness
before they have a chance to become speech or action. A person who can intercept the
animal soul's thoughts before they solidify into words or deeds has won the most
crucial battle.

The divine soul's task is to replace the animal soul's thought-content with *Torah
thoughts* and *divine contemplation* (הִתְבּוֹנְנוּת, hitbonenut) — redirecting the
mental faculty from animal-oriented to divine-oriented content.

## Hebrew Key Terms

| Term | Transliteration | Definition |
|------|-----------------|------------|
| מַחֲשָׁבָה | Machshava | Thought; the innermost garment of the animal soul |
| לְבוּשׁ | Levush | Garment; the mode through which the soul expresses itself |
| יֵצֶר הָרָע | Yetzer HaRa | Evil inclination; operates through thought |
| הִתְבּוֹנְנוּת | Hitbonenut | Contemplation; the divine soul's counter to animal thought |
| מִלְחָמָה | Milchama | Battle; the conflict between divine and animal souls |
