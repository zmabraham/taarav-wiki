# Action of the Animal Soul

## English Explanation

*Maase* (מַעֲשֶׂה, action) is the outermost garment of the animal soul and the one
most directly engaged with the physical world. In the Hemshech Taarav, the Rashab
gives particular attention to the action-garment because it is the arena in which the
divine-animal soul dynamic has the most concrete consequences.

The animal soul's actions are primarily oriented toward physical survival, comfort,
pleasure, and the assertion of personal interests in the social world. These include
eating, sleeping, physical pleasure, financial acquisition, and social competition.
The Rashab does not consider these impulses intrinsically evil — the physical body has
genuine needs, and the divine created these needs. The question is whether the animal
soul's action-garment serves these needs within the framework of divine will (Torah
and mitzvot) or outside it.

A critical teaching in the Hemshech Taarav is that *action* has priority in the
cosmic order — "action is the primary thing" (הַמַּעֲשֶׂה הוּא הָעִקָּר, hama'aseh hu
ha'ikar). This means that the divine soul's highest expression is not in the realm
of thought or feeling but in concrete, physical action — specifically, the performance
of mitzvot. A mitzvah performed with the physical body in the lowest world is more
significant cosmically than the most exalted meditation, because it draws divine
energy into the most concealed domain of reality.

This teaching redeems the animal soul's action-garment: properly directed, the very
physicality that characterizes the animal soul's actions becomes the vehicle for the
most powerful divine service.
