# Kelipat Nogah in Daily Life

## English Explanation

The domain of *Kelipat Nogah* encompasses the vast majority of ordinary human daily
life. In the Hemshech Taarav, the Rashab provides concrete examples of the activities
that operate within this domain and explains how each can be elevated or degraded
depending on the person's orientation.

Eating permitted (kosher) food is the paradigmatic example. The food contains divine
sparks (nitzotzot) embedded within its Kelipat Nogah shell. When eaten with the
intention of sustaining the body to serve God, or in honor of Shabbat, the spark is
elevated and the Kelipat Nogah is "refined" (מְבוּרָר, mevurar). When eaten purely
for pleasure or sensual gratification, the spark remains trapped and the Kelipat
Nogah potentially descends toward the three impure klipot.

Sleep is another prime example. Sleeping the necessary amount to maintain health and
enable one to serve God the next day is an activity within Kelipat Nogah that can be
elevated — even sanctified. Sleeping excessively for laziness or sensual indulgence
degrades the Kelipat Nogah.

Business and commercial activity, performed honestly and for the purpose of
providing for one's family and enabling Torah study and charitable giving, operates
through Kelipat Nogah in its elevated mode. The same commercial activity, pursued
for wealth as an end in itself, operates through Kelipat Nogah in its degraded mode.

The Rashab's consistent teaching is that intention (*kavanah*) is the key variable:
the same activity can be either elevating or degrading depending on the person's
orientation toward it.
