# Elevating Kelipat Nogah

## English Explanation

The elevation (*birur*, בֵּירוּר) of Kelipat Nogah is, according to the Hemshech
Taarav, one of the central tasks of the *beinoni* (בֵּינוֹנִי, the intermediate
person — neither completely righteous nor wicked). While the complete tzaddik has
already transformed their animal soul into holiness, the beinoni's task is precisely
to navigate the domain of Kelipat Nogah and elevate it through proper intention and
use.

The mechanism of elevation is threefold. First, *kavanah* (intention): the person
performs the neutral activity with conscious awareness that it serves a holy purpose.
Second, *simcha* (joy): performing the activity with genuine positive engagement,
not grudging compliance. Third, *Torah and mitzvot*: using the energy gained from
Nogah-activities (food, sleep, recreation) in the service of Torah study and mitzvot.

The Rashab notes that the elevation of Kelipat Nogah is not merely about extracting
the divine spark from the food or object used. It is also about transforming the
*person's* relationship to the material world. Each time a person successfully uses a
neutral activity for holy purposes, they strengthen the divine soul's governance over
the animal soul and its domain of Kelipat Nogah.

Over time, this cumulative process of elevating Kelipat Nogah builds toward the
redemption of the material world as a whole — each elevated Nogah-activity contributes
to the broader *birur nitzotzot* (refinement of divine sparks) that is the cosmic
purpose of human existence.

## Hebrew Key Terms

| Term | Transliteration | Definition |
|------|-----------------|------------|
| בֵּירוּר | Beirur | Refinement/elevation; the key task in Nogah |
| בֵּינוֹנִי | Beinoni | Intermediate person; operates primarily in Nogah |
| צַדִּיק | Tzaddik | Righteous person; has transformed Nogah entirely |
| שִׂמְחָה | Simcha | Joy; a key element in elevating Nogah |
| עֲבוֹדָה | Avodah | Divine service; the goal of Nogah-elevation |
